# Glossary

**Terms, definitions, and abbreviations used across company documentation.**

---

## OKR Terms

**Aspirational OKR**
An OKR with a stretch target. Expected achievement is 60-70%. Labelled [Aspirational] when set.

**Baseline**
The starting measurement before improvement effort begins. The "from" in "from X to Y."

**BLUF**
Bottom Line Up Front. Communication style where the key message comes first, context second.

**Cadence**
The rhythm at which OKRs are set and reviewed. Company OKRs are annual. Team OKRs are quarterly.

**Cascading**
The process of aligning lower-level OKRs to higher-level ones. Team KRs support Company KRs.

**Committed OKR**
An OKR with high confidence of achievement (80%+). Labelled [Committed] when set.

**DRI**
Directly Responsible Individual. The single person accountable for an outcome. Every KR has one DRI.

**Initiative**
A major body of work that supports one or more KRs. Tracked in project management, not OKR system.

**Key Result (KR)**
A quantitative measure of progress toward an Objective. Format: metric from baseline to target.

**KPI**
Key Performance Indicator. A health metric to maintain within a threshold. Different from KR (which measures change).

**Lagging Indicator**
An outcome metric that measures what has already happened. Revenue is a lagging indicator.

**Leading Indicator**
A predictive metric that indicates future outcomes. Pipeline value is a leading indicator of revenue.

**Milestone**
A checkpoint within an initiative marking completion of a significant deliverable. Binary: done or not done.

**Objective (O)**
A qualitative statement of direction. Inspirational, directional, achievable but ambitious.

**Operational OKR**
A Team OKR focused on internal improvement (team health, process) rather than direct Company KR support. Labelled [Operational].

**Score**
The measured achievement of a KR at end of period. Scale: 0.0 to 1.0. Success is 0.7-0.9.

**Target**
The desired end state. The "to" in "from X to Y."

**Zombie KR**
A KR that is clearly going to miss significantly but has not been formally addressed. Requires CEO decision: Close, Carry Forward, or Restructure.

## Infrastructure Terms

**AI Factory**
A data centre facility optimised for artificial intelligence workloads. Characterised by high power density, liquid cooling, and GPU-heavy compute.

**BESS**
Battery Energy Storage System. Large-scale battery installations for grid stabilization and energy arbitrage.

**Anchor Tenant**
The primary customer in a facility, typically contracting the majority of capacity. Critical for project financing.

**Capacity (in Growth context)**
The demand side of the business. Customers who consume data centre capacity.

**COD**
Commercial Operation Date. The date when a facility begins commercial operations and is handed over to customers.

**Co-location**
A data centre model where customers rent space, power, and cooling while owning their own IT equipment.

**DEC**
Data centre. Used in project codes (e.g., AGRIPORT-02-DEC).

**EPC**
Engineering, Procurement, and Construction. A contract type where a single contractor delivers a complete facility.

**Grid Connection**
The electrical infrastructure connecting a facility to the public power grid. Critical path for data centre development.

**HoT**
Heads of Terms. A binding term sheet outlining key commercial terms that forms the basis for full contract negotiation.

**Hyperscaler**
Large-scale cloud providers (Microsoft, Google, Amazon, Meta) who operate massive data centre portfolios.

**Interconnection**
In electrical context: the process of connecting a facility to the grid. Requires application, study, and agreement with grid operator.

**IFC**
Issued for Construction. The final approved version of engineering drawings.

**IOI**
Indication of Interest. Initial expression of interest from a potential customer or partner, preceding formal LOI.

**LOI**
Letter of Intent. A non-binding document expressing intention to proceed with a transaction.

**MW**
Megawatt. Unit of power. Data centre capacity is typically measured in MW of IT load.

**PPA**
Power Purchase Agreement. A contract to buy electricity, typically from renewable sources, at agreed terms.

**PUE**
Power Usage Effectiveness. Ratio of total facility power to IT equipment power. Lower is better. 1.0 is theoretical perfect.

**RFS**
Ready for Service. The point at which infrastructure is operational and available for customer use.

**Supply (in Growth context)**
The supply side of the business. Sites, power, and infrastructure that enable data centre development.

**Term Sheet**
A document outlining the key terms of a proposed transaction. May be binding or non-binding.

## Netherlands / EU Terms

**ACM**
Autoriteit Consument & Markt. The Dutch competition and consumer authority.

**Bestemmingsplan**
Zoning plan. The local land use plan that determines what can be built where.

**GWh**
Gigawatt-hour. Unit of energy. 1 GWh = 1,000 MWh.

**Netbeheerder**
Grid operator. In the Netherlands: TenneT (high voltage), regional operators (medium/low voltage: Liander, Stedin, Enexis).

**Omgevingsvergunning**
Environmental permit. Required for most significant construction projects.

**SDE++**
Stimulering Duurzame Energieproductie. Dutch subsidy scheme for renewable energy production.

**TenneT**
The Dutch high-voltage grid operator. Manages the national electricity transmission network.

**Warmtenet**
District heating network. Infrastructure that distributes heat from centralised sources to buildings.

## Project Management Terms

**Backlog**
The list of work items to be completed. Sprint backlog is the subset for the current sprint.

**Gate**
A decision point in stage-gate methodology. Requires deliverables review and Go/No-Go decision.

**Retrospective**
A meeting to review what worked, what did not, and what to improve. Held at end of each sprint.

**Sprint**
A fixed time period (one week in our methodology) during which a set of work is completed.

**Stage-Gate**
A project management methodology with defined phases (stages) separated by decision points (gates).

**Standup**
A brief daily meeting (15 min) where team members share progress and blockers.

## Company-Specific Terms

**The 60/40 Rule**
60% committed OKRs, 40% aspirational. Our balance between reliability and ambition.

**The Newspaper Test**
Quality check for KRs: Could an outsider verify this was achieved? If not, it is not measurable enough.

**Two-Way Door**
A reversible decision. Can be made quickly with 60% confidence.

**One-Way Door**
An irreversible decision. Requires more deliberation, 80% confidence minimum.

**48-Hour Rule**
Conflict resolution deadline. All cross-team conflicts must be resolved within 48 hours or escalate to CEO.
