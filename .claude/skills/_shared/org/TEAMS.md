## 2. Team Definitions

### 2.1 Projects (PROJ)

**Mission:** Deliver data centre projects from term sheet to COD.

**Leader:** COO

**Scope:**
- Site development (post-handoff from Growth)
- Permitting
- Engineering and design
- Construction management
- Commissioning
- Handover to operations

**Does NOT own:**
- Site identification (Growth - Supply)
- Site control/LOI negotiation (Growth - Supply)
- Customer relationship (Growth - Capacity)
- Product definition (Product)

**Receives from Growth:**
- Signed term sheet (binding)
- Site due diligence package
- Customer requirements
- Commercial terms summary

**Delivers to Growth (at COD):**
- Completed facility
- As-built documentation
- Operations manual
- Customer handover support

### 2.2 Growth (GRTH)

**Mission:** Build pipeline and close deals on both sides of the platform.

**Leader:** CBDO

**Sub-teams:**

**Capacity (Demand Side)**
- Customer acquisition
- Pipeline development
- Contract negotiation
- Customer relationship management (entire lifecycle)

**Supply (Site Side)**
- Site identification
- Site screening and feasibility
- Landowner/grower relationships
- LOI/option negotiation
- Power procurement
- Site control until term sheet

**Scope:**
- All commercial activity
- Customer relationships (pre-sale through post-COD)
- Site origination and control
- Partnership development
- Marketing

**Does NOT own:**
- Technical development of sites (Projects)
- Product definition (Product)
- Construction or permitting (Projects)

**Delivers to Projects:**
- Signed binding term sheet
- Complete due diligence package
- Customer requirements document

**Receives from Projects (at COD):**
- Completed facility for customer handover
- Documentation for ongoing relationship

### 2.3 Product (PROD)

**Mission:** Define what we offer and how it creates value.

**Leader:** CPO

**Sub-teams:**

**Capacity (Customer-Facing Product)**
- Data centre product specifications
- Pricing structure
- Service level definitions
- Customer-facing documentation

**Supply (Site Identification Product)**
- Heat offering definition
- Grower value proposition
- Site qualification criteria
- Integration with other business lines

**Scope:**
- Product strategy and roadmap
- Product specifications and packaging
- Pricing frameworks (CEO approves final pricing)
- Internal tools and platform
- Product documentation

**Does NOT own:**
- Selling the product (Growth)
- Building the product/facility (Projects)
- Customer relationships (Growth)

**Collaboration:**
- Works with Growth on deal-specific customisation
- Provides product expertise during sales process
- Iterates based on market feedback from Growth

### 2.4 Finance (FIN)

**Mission:** Ensure financial health and enable informed decisions.

**Leader:** Finance Manager

**Scope:**
- Financial planning and analysis
- Accounting and reporting
- Cash management
- Project finance support
- Investor relations support
- Board reporting
- Budgeting and forecasting

**Does NOT own:**
- Strategic decisions (CEO)
- Pricing (Product + CEO)
- Commercial terms (Growth)

**Provides to all teams:**
- Financial data and analysis
- Budget tracking
- Project economics
- Cash flow visibility

## 3. Handoffs

### 3.1 Growth → Projects

**Trigger:** Binding term sheet signed securing power and plot.

**Handoff Package:**
- Signed term sheet
- Site due diligence (environmental, geotechnical, title)
- Customer requirements document
- Commercial terms summary
- Key contact information
- Relationship history and notes

**Meeting:** 60 minutes with Growth lead, Projects lead, relevant team members.

**Post-Handoff:**
- Growth retains customer relationship ownership
- Projects leads all technical delivery
- Weekly sync on customer-facing matters

### 3.2 Projects → Growth (Post-COD)

**Trigger:** COD achieved, commissioning complete, punch list closed.

**Handoff Package:**
- As-built documentation
- Operations manual
- Warranty information
- Lessons learned
- Customer feedback from construction phase

**Meeting:** 60 minutes with Projects lead, Growth lead, optionally customer.

**Post-Handoff:**
- Growth owns ongoing customer relationship
- Projects provides technical support as needed

## 4. Decision Rights

### 4.1 CEO-Reserved Decisions

These decisions always require CEO approval:

- Hiring and termination
- Pricing and commercial terms (final approval)
- Capital allocation above €1K
- Strategic pivots or major direction changes
- External commitments (contracts, partnerships, investors)
- OKR abandonment
- Cross-team conflict resolution (on appeal)
- Board communications
- Fundraising decisions

### 4.2 Team Lead Decisions

Team leads decide autonomously within their domain:

- How to achieve their OKRs
- Day-to-day task prioritisation
- Vendor selection under €1K
- Internal process and workflow
- Tool and methodology choices
- Sprint planning and execution
- Team meeting cadence

### 4.3 The 5-Minute Rule

If a decision can be reversed with less than one day of work, make it immediately. Do not wait for approval.

### 4.4 Two-Way vs One-Way Doors

| Type | Threshold | Action |
|------|-----------|--------|
| Two-way door (reversible) | 60% confidence | Decide fast, correct later |
| One-way door (irreversible) | 80% confidence | Gather data, cap deliberation at 48-72 hours |

## 5. RACI Matrix

### 5.1 Key: RACI Definitions

- **R = Responsible** — Does the work
- **A = Accountable** — Makes final decision, one per row
- **C = Consulted** — Provides input before decision
- **I = Informed** — Notified after decision

### 5.2 Strategic Decisions

| Decision | CEO | COO | CBDO | CPO | FIN |
|----------|-----|-----|------|-----|-----|
| Company strategy | A | C | C | C | C |
| Annual OKRs | A | C | C | C | C |
| Team OKRs (PROJ) | I | A | C | C | I |
| Team OKRs (GRTH) | I | C | A | C | I |
| Team OKRs (PROD) | I | C | C | A | I |
| Team OKRs (FIN) | A | I | I | I | R |
| Hiring | A | C | C | C | C |
| Budget allocation | A | C | C | C | R |
| Fundraising | A | I | I | I | C |

### 5.3 Commercial Decisions

| Decision | CEO | COO | CBDO | CPO | FIN |
|----------|-----|-----|------|-----|-----|

| Customer contract terms | A | I | R | C | C |
| Site LOI terms | A | I | R | C | C |

## 7. Communication Channels

### 7.1 Default Channels

| Purpose | Channel |
|---------|---------|
| Urgent/blocking | Direct message or call |
| Team coordination | Team Slack channel |
| Cross-team coordination | Project-specific channel |
| Documentation | ClickUp + shared drive (see OKR-PROJECT-MANAGEMENT.md Section 5 for ClickUp structure) |
| Decisions | Document in relevant system |

### 7.2 Escalation Path

1. Direct conversation with relevant person
2. Team lead involvement
3. Cross-team lead discussion
4. CEO decision (48-hour resolution deadline)

- Punch list closure rate

**Product (PROD)**
- Feature delivery rate
- Internal tool adoption
- Product feedback score
- Documentation completeness

**Finance (FIN)**
- Reporting accuracy
- Cash forecast accuracy
- Close cycle time
- Audit readiness
