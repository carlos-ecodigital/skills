## Section 3: Founder & Team

### 3.1 Founder Biographies
`ANS` | `P0` | `[NARRATIVE]` | `[BOTH]` | Feeds: `SF` `CS` `DR`

For each founder:
- Full name, age, nationality, tax residence
- Current role and title at {company}
- Professional background: 3-5 most relevant prior roles with company name, title, tenure, and specific achievements (quantified where possible)
- Education: degrees, institutions, relevant certifications
- Domain-specific experience: years in energy, infrastructure, technology, finance, or relevant sector
- Notable achievements: exits, companies built, projects delivered, patents, publications

**Gate:** Each founder bio must include verifiable achievements. Generic descriptions ("experienced leader") without specifics do not pass.

---

### 3.2 Co-Founder Relationship
`ANS` | `P0` | `[NARRATIVE]` | `[SEED]` | Feeds: `SF`

If multiple founders:
- How and when did the co-founders meet?
- Prior working history together (projects, companies, years)
- Complementary skills: what does each founder bring that the other lacks?
- Decision-making process between founders
- Any prior conflicts and how they were resolved

**Gate:** Co-founders must demonstrate prior working relationship or clear complementary value. "We met at a conference last month" is a concern for seed investors.

---

### 3.3 Equity Split & Vesting
`ANS` | `P0` | `[EXACT]` | `[SEED]` | Feeds: `SF` `LC`

Current equity ownership:
- Each founder's percentage (current, pre-funding)
- Rationale for the split (equal? Contribution-based? Role-based?)
- Vesting schedule: cliff period, total vesting period, acceleration triggers
- Any shares already vested vs. unvested
- Reverse vesting or buyback provisions for unvested shares

**Gate:** Equity split must be documented. Unequal splits must have a clear rationale. Absence of vesting at seed stage is a red flag.

---

### 3.4 Full-Time Commitment
`ANS` | `P0` | `[EXACT]` | `[SEED]` | Feeds: `SF`

For each founder:
- Full-time committed? If not, what percentage of time?
- Any other business interests, board seats, or consulting engagements?
- Expected transition to full-time (if part-time): timeline and trigger
- Personal financial runway: months without salary (sensitive — restrict to lead investor only)

**Gate:** Lead founders must be full-time or committed to transitioning within 3 months of funding. Part-time lead founder is a significant investor concern.

Non-founder team members:
- Name, role, start date, full-time/part-time
- Background and relevance to the role
- Compensation: salary, equity (if any), vesting terms
- Employment type: employee, contractor, advisor

### 3.6 Hiring Plan

Post-funding hiring plan:

- Sourcing strategy (network, recruiters, job boards)

### 3.7 Key Person Risk
`ANS` | `P0` | `[NARRATIVE]` | `[BOTH]` | Feeds: `SF` `PF`

Key person dependency assessment:
- Which individuals are critical to {company}'s operations?
- What happens if the CEO is unavailable for 3 months?
- Succession plan: who steps in for each key person?
- Key person insurance: in place or planned?
- Non-compete enforceability for key persons

**Gate:** Key person risk must be identified with at least a conceptual mitigation plan.

---

### 3.8 ESOP / Option Plan
`ANS` | `P0` | `[EXACT]` | `[SEED]` | Feeds: `SF` `LC`

Employee option pool:
- Pool size (% of fully diluted cap table)
- Approved by board/shareholders?
- Grants issued to date vs. available
- Standard vesting terms for new grants
- Exercise price and valuation basis
- Tax treatment in the relevant jurisdiction(s)

**Gate:** ESOP pool must be established or committed before fundraise. Investors expect 10-15% pool at seed stage.

---

### 3.9 Employment Agreements
`DOC` | `P0` | `[DOC-REQUIRED]` | `[SEED]` | Feeds: `LC` `DR`

For each founder and key employee:
- Written employment agreement in place?
- IP assignment clause included?
- Non-compete and non-solicitation provisions?
- Notice period and termination provisions?
- Jurisdiction of employment and applicable labor law?

**Gate:** All founders and key employees must have written agreements with IP assignment. Absence creates IP ownership risk.

### 3.11 Team Gaps & Weaknesses
`ANS` | `P0` | `[NARRATIVE]` | `[BOTH]` | Feeds: `SF` `SO`

Honestly assess team gaps:
- What functional areas are weakest?
- What experience is missing that investors will notice?
- How are you compensating for gaps today (advisors, contractors, partners)?
- What's the plan to close gaps over the next 12 months?

**Gate:** Must identify at least 2 genuine gaps. "Our team has no weaknesses" signals lack of self-awareness.

### 3.12 Advisory Board
`ANS` | `P0` | `[NARRATIVE]` | `[SEED]` | Feeds: `SF` `CS` `DR`

List all advisors:
- Name, background, and specific relevance to {company}
- Engagement terms (equity, cash, hybrid)
- Frequency of engagement (monthly call, quarterly meeting, ad hoc)
- Specific value delivered to date (introductions made, decisions influenced, expertise provided)

**Gate:** Each advisor must have demonstrable relevance. "Name-drop" advisors with no engagement are a negative signal.

### 3.15 Compensation Structure
`ANS` | `P1` | `[EXACT]` | `[SEED]` | Feeds: `SF` `LC`

Current and proposed compensation:
- Founder salaries (current — pre-funding)
- Founder salaries (proposed — post-funding)
- Any deferred compensation arrangements
- Comparison to market rate (what discount are founders taking?)

**Gate:** If founders plan to pay themselves market rate from Day 1 post-funding, flag for investor discussion. Early-stage founders typically take 50-70% of market rate.

### Founder-Market Fit

#### 3.19 Domain Credibility
`ANS` | `P0` | `[NARRATIVE]` | `[BOTH]` | Feeds: `SF` `SO`

Demonstrate domain expertise relevant to your company's sector:
- Years of direct experience in the relevant industry
- Specific projects delivered (name, size, role, outcome)
- Relationships with key stakeholders (regulators, suppliers, customers, industry bodies)
- Technical certifications or qualifications
- Publications, patents, or industry recognition

**Gate:** Founders must demonstrate domain expertise with specific evidence. At least one founder must have hands-on project delivery experience in the relevant sector.

---

#### 3.20 Technical Depth
`ANS` | `P0` | `[NARRATIVE]` | `[SEED]` | Feeds: `SF` `SO`

Can each founder explain — without notes or prepared slides — the core technical mechanics of their business?
- Rate each founder: Deep technical / Working knowledge / Conversational / Cannot explain
- Which technical areas does each founder own?
- Where are the gaps in technical understanding?

**Gate:** At least one founder must be rated "Deep technical" in the core technology areas. If all founders are "Conversational" across the board, flag as technical depth risk.

#### 3.21 Replacement Scenario
`ANS` | `P0` | `[NARRATIVE]` | `[SEED]` | Feeds: `SF`

If the CEO were to become unavailable tomorrow:
- Is there a COO-ready person in the team or advisory board?
- Timeline to hire a replacement CEO/COO?
- What decisions would be blocked without the CEO?
- What governance mechanism exists for transition?

**Gate:** Must have at least a conceptual succession plan.

#### 3.22 Personal Financial Runway
`ANS` | `P0` | `[EXACT]` | `[SEED]` | Feeds: `SF`

For each founder (sensitive — restrict to lead investor only):
- How many months can you sustain without salary?
- What is your personal burn tolerance threshold?
- Are you financially dependent on this venture for living expenses?
- Any personal guarantees or liabilities that could create pressure?

#### 3.23 Failure Definition
`ANS` | `P0` | `[NARRATIVE]` | `[SEED]` | Feeds: `SF` `SO`

Answer honestly:
- What constitutes failure for this venture?
- At what specific metrics or milestones would you consider pivoting?
- At what point would you shut down and return capital?
- What's the difference between "needs more time" and "isn't working"?

**Gate:** Must demonstrate self-awareness about failure scenarios.
