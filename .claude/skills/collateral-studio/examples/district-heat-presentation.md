## Slide 1: De Wcw verandert de warmtemarkt fundamenteel

**Title:** Kostengebaseerde tarieven. Publiek eigendom. Nieuwe bronnen nodig.

**Content:**
Three Wcw changes with effective dates:
1. **Kostengebaseerde tarieven** — einde aan gasgebonden pricing (2026-2027)
2. **Gemeentelijke regie** — aanwijzing warmtebedrijf door gemeente
3. **>50% publiek eigendom** — 7-14 jaar transitieperiode

Key implication: Heat utilities that secure cheap, stable heat sources now will have structural advantage under the new regime.

**Key data:** Wcw effective 2026-2027; cost-based tariffs; >50% public ownership transition
**Source footnote:** Wcw; VNG Jul 2025; Dirkzwager Dec 2025

**Speaker notes:** Open with regulation, not technology. District heating executives live in a regulatory world. The Wcw is their biggest strategic concern right now. Show you understand their reality before introducing your solution.

## Slide 2: Uw bronnenportfolio staat onder druk

**Title:** Gasafhankelijkheid wordt duurder — elk jaar structureel

**Content:**
- Gas cost trajectory: EUR 35-45/MWh + ETS2 from 2027 (EUR 10-20/MWh additional)
- Over-reliance on single heat source = supply and price risk
- Wcw demands: cost-based tariffs mean margins compress if source costs rise
- Chart: Cost trajectory of gas heating vs. alternative sources (2025-2035)

**Key data:** EUR 35-45/MWh gas; EUR 10-20/MWh ETS2 from 2027
**Source footnote:** TTF market data; EU ETS2 framework

**Speaker notes:** Frame the problem as portfolio risk, not just cost. A heat network with one source is vulnerable. Diversification isn't optional under the Wcw — it's survival.

**Title:** Ontwerp op >99% beschikbaarheid — met contractuele backup-voorzieningen

**Content:**
- AI data center infrastructure designed for high availability (redundant systems)
- Diversified tenant base: no single-tenant dependency
- Contract provisions: backup heat source activation triggers, cure periods
- Design life: 30+ years for infrastructure; IT hardware refreshed every 3-5 years
- Insurance: comprehensive coverage including business interruption

**Key data:** >99% availability target; 30-year design life; contractual backup provisions
**Source footnote:** DE facility design specification

**Speaker notes:** Address the "what if the data center shuts down?" concern head-on. Infrastructure lasts decades. Tenants rotate. Backup provisions are contractual. Compare with geothermal (geological uncertainty) or industrial waste heat (factory closure risk).
