### Section 1: Company Overview

**Digital Energy Group AG** develops, finances, and operates integrated AI infrastructure across Europe. Headquartered in Zug, Switzerland, with operations in the Netherlands, the company co-locates AI data centers on industrial and agricultural sites, recovering waste heat for greenhouses, district heating networks, and industrial users.

Each project is structured as an independently financed Dutch BV with integrated battery energy storage. Digital Energy's development pipeline includes multiple projects in the Netherlands, supported by technology partnerships with Lenovo and Nokia.

### Section 2: What We Build — The Digital Energy Center (DEC)

A Digital Energy Center is an integrated AI data center designed for sovereign European compute with full waste heat recovery.

**Core components:**

| Component | Specification |
|---|---|
| AI compute infrastructure | Liquid-cooled, 40-140 kW/rack, GPU-optimized |
| Waste heat recovery | 97% of IT load recovered at 45-60°C |
| Battery energy storage (BESS) | Co-located via cable pooling; grid services + power quality |
| Network | Nokia infrastructure; AMS-IX proximity; sub-2ms to Frankfurt/London |
| PUE | 1.2 target |
| Security | Physical + logical isolation per tenant |

**What makes a DEC different from a standard data center:**
1. **Heat is a product, not waste.** 97% of thermal energy is captured and sold to adjacent users.
2. **Grid connection is shared.** Cable pooling (Energiewet 2026) enables DEC + BESS + existing user on one connection.
3. **Infrastructure, not venture.** Each project is non-recourse financed with contracted revenue streams.
