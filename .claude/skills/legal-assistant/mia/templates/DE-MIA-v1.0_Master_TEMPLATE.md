2.1 **Purpose.** This MIA establishes a master framework under which the Introducer may introduce Customers and/or Investors to the Provider, and under which the Provider may pay Fees to the Introducer for Qualifying Introductions made under an activated Annex.

2.2 **Activation.** The following Annex(es) are activated by the Parties' execution of this Master (and by execution of the corresponding Annex schedule):

| Annex | Activated? | Subject Matter |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Annex A — Commercial Introductions | [ANNEX_A_ACTIVE] | Introductions of prospective Customers |
| Annex B — Capital Introductions | [ANNEX_B_ACTIVE] | Introductions of prospective Investors |

2.3 **Independence and severability of Annexes.** Each activated Annex operates independently of the other. The termination, suspension, expiry, or invalidity of one Annex does not affect the validity or operation of the other Annex or of this Master, except as expressly provided. In particular, any automatic suspension or termination of Annex B under Clause B.12 (Automatic Suspension on Regulatory Change) or Clause B.13 (Enhanced Severability) has no effect on the Master or Annex A.

2.4 **Master is binding.** The provisions of this Master are binding on the Parties from the Effective Date.

## 6. Non-Circumvention (BINDING)

6.1 **Non-contact obligation.** During the term of this MIA and for the duration set out in Clause 6.2, the Introducer shall not, directly or indirectly, without the prior written consent of the Provider:
(a) contact, solicit, deal with, or enter into any business relationship with any Introduced Party in connection with subject matter materially similar to that covered by an activated Annex; or
(b) circumvent, avoid, or bypass the Provider in order to deal directly or indirectly with any Introduced Party in such subject matter; or
(c) attempt to divert or appropriate any business opportunity disclosed by the Provider to the Introducer in connection with this MIA.

6.2 **Duration.** The obligations in this Clause 6 continue for [NC_DURATION] after the earlier of (a) the expiry or termination of this MIA, or (b) in respect of a particular Introduced Party, the date on which the relevant Qualifying Introduction has been completed and any Fee due in respect of it has been paid in full.

6.3 **Independent Knowledge Exception.** This Clause 6 does not restrict the Introducer from dealing with any party with whom the Introducer can demonstrate (with contemporaneous evidence) it had a substantive prior relationship independent of any disclosure by the Provider.

6.4 **MSA / Investment Supersession.** Where an MSA is signed between the Provider and a Customer, or where capital is committed by an Investor to the Provider, the obligations of this Clause 6 in respect of that Customer or Investor are subsumed by, and superseded by, the relevant Qualifying Introduction record and any onward arrangements between the Parties.

## 7. Pipeline Carve-Out (BINDING)

7.1 **No Fee where pre-existing relationship demonstrable.** No Fee is due to the Introducer in respect of an Introduced Party where the Provider can demonstrate, with contemporaneous evidence, that:
(a) the Provider had a direct relationship (including but not limited to an open commercial discussion, contractual relationship, or active investor engagement) with the Introduced Party at the date of the relevant Introduction; or
(b) the Provider had been in substantive contact with the Introduced Party (including any of its directors, officers, or beneficial owners acting in that capacity) at any time within the [LOOKBACK_MONTHS] preceding the date of the relevant Introduction.

7.2 **Evidence.** The Provider's records (including its customer relationship management system, email and messaging archives, calendar entries, meeting notes, and correspondence) constitute presumptive evidence under this Clause 7. The burden of demonstrating the existence of the pre-existing relationship or contact is on the Provider.

7.3 **Notification.** Where the Provider intends to rely on this Clause 7 in respect of a particular Introduction, the Provider shall notify the Introducer in writing within fifteen (15) Business Days of receiving the Introduction, identifying the Introduced Party and the basis on which Clause 7.1 applies. Failure to notify within that period (absent fraud or material misrepresentation by the Introducer) waives the Provider's reliance on this Clause 7 in respect of that Introduction.

7.4 **Application across Annexes.** This Clause 7 applies to Introductions under both Annex A and Annex B.

## 8. General Provisions (BINDING)

8.1 **Notices.** Notices under this MIA must be in writing and sent to the addresses on the signature page (or such other address as a Party notifies the other in writing). Notices are deemed received on (a) the second Business Day after dispatch by courier, (b) the date of delivery if hand-delivered, or (c) the Business Day after transmission if sent by email to the recipient's stated email address with no automated bounce.

8.2 **Counterparts.** This MIA may be executed in counterparts, including by electronic signature within the meaning of the eIDAS Regulation (EU) No 910/2014. Each counterpart constitutes an original.

8.3 **Term.** This MIA takes effect on the Effective Date and continues in force until terminated in accordance with Clause 8.4 or, in respect of an activated Annex, until that Annex terminates in accordance with its own terms.

8.4 **Termination.** Either Party may terminate this MIA on thirty (30) days' written notice to the other Party. Termination of the Master terminates each activated Annex. Termination of an Annex does not, of itself, terminate the Master or any other activated Annex.

8.5 **Termination for cause.** A Party may terminate this MIA (and all activated Annexes) immediately on written notice to the other Party if the other Party (a) commits a material breach of this MIA or any activated Annex which is not capable of remedy, or which is capable of remedy but is not remedied within thirty (30) days of written notice requiring remedy; or (b) becomes insolvent, enters into liquidation (other than for solvent reorganisation), or has an administrator or receiver appointed over the whole or a material part of its assets.

8.6 **Survival.** Clauses 5 (Confidentiality), 6 (Non-Circumvention), 7 (Pipeline Carve-Out), 8.7 (Governing Law), 8.8 (Jurisdiction), and any provision of an activated Annex expressed to survive, survive the termination of this MIA in accordance with their terms.

8.7 **Governing Law.** This MIA is governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the Netherlands. The United Nations Convention on Contracts for the International Sale of Goods (CISG) is expressly excluded.

8.8 **Jurisdiction.** The courts of Amsterdam (*Rechtbank Amsterdam*) have exclusive jurisdiction over any dispute arising out of or in connection with this MIA.

8.9 **Severability.** If any provision of this MIA is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the remainder of this MIA continues in full force, and the Parties shall negotiate in good faith a replacement provision that gives effect to the original commercial intent so far as legally possible. The severability of Annex B specifically is governed by Clause B.13 (Enhanced Severability).

8.10 **Entire Agreement.** This MIA (including any activated Annex) and any Existing Confidentiality Agreement referenced in Clause 5A.1 constitute the entire agreement between the Parties in respect of its subject matter and supersede all prior discussions, understandings, and agreements in respect of that subject matter.

8.11 **Assignment.** Neither Party may assign this MIA without the other's prior written consent (not to be unreasonably withheld), save that the Provider may assign by way of security to bona fide financing parties without consent.

8.12 **Variation.** No variation of this MIA is effective unless in writing and signed by both Parties.

8.13 **No partnership.** Nothing in this MIA constitutes a partnership, joint venture, agency, or employment relationship between the Parties.
