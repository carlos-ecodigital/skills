# DE-LOI-SS-v1.0 — Strategic Supplier LOI/NCNDA (Reference Template)

**Status:** v1.0 reference template, 2026-04-16. Generator engine produces cover + Recital A + Recital B automatically; clauses 1–8 below are drafted from this markdown.

**Use for:** Supply-chain partners providing equipment, services, or build capabilities to DEC deployments — modular infrastructure manufacturers, EPCs, cooling/power OEMs, key vendors with capacity reservation / design integration / preferred-supplier relationships.

**Do NOT use for:** ecosystem partnerships without commercial flow (use DE-LOI-EP-v1.0). Customer capacity purchase (use DE-LOI-Wholesale-v3.2 for neoclouds, DE-LOI-EndUser-v3.2 for enterprises). Distribution / channel (use DE-LOI-Distributor-v3.2).

---

## Strategic purpose selector (REQUIRED — choose 1 or 2)

The intake must specify 1–2 strategic purposes. Each drives which Cl. 3 / Cl. 4 sub-clauses fire.

| ID | Purpose | Drives |
|---|---|---|
| `capacity_lock_in` | Secure equipment / service capacity in a constrained market | Cl. 3.2 Capacity Reservation, Cl. 3.3 Lead-Time Targets, optional Cl. 4.4 Exclusivity |
| `pricing_volume` | Preferred pricing, volume tiers, commercial bands | Cl. 3.4 Pricing Framework, Cl. 3.5 Volume Tiers |
| `supply_chain_de_risking` | Dual-source optionality, component substitution, delivery SLAs | Cl. 3.6 Dual-Source + Continuity Commitments |
| `engineering_integration` | Design co-development, IP allocation, interface specs | Cl. 3.7 Design Integration + IP Allocation, Cl. 4.3 Joint-Development Governance |
| `pipeline_visibility` | Placement on Provider vendor panel for named DEC projects | Cl. 3.8 Preferred-Supplier / ROFR (20-BD window), Cl. 4.1 Project Introduction Process |

**Always-on clauses (regardless of purposes):** Cl. 3.1 Capability Contribution (responsibility matrix); Cl. 4.2 Contractual Sequence; Cl. 4.5 Implementation Roadmap; Cl. 5 Project Finance; Cl. 6 Confidentiality (Tier B); Cl. 7 Non-Circumvention (light supply-side variant); Cl. 8 General.

This letter of intent and non-circumvention non-disclosure agreement (the "LOI") reflects both Parties' strategic intent and is intended to form the basis for further technical and commercial negotiation toward a Framework Agreement and statements of work for named Provider projects.

**(D) Confidentiality rationale** —

## 1. Definitions

Include: `Affiliate`, `Associated Counterparty` (with supplier-scope note), `Business Day`, `Confidential Information` (metadata protection on), `DEC`, `Designated Site`, `Financing Party`, `Framework Agreement` ("the definitive Framework Agreement and accompanying statements of work to be negotiated between the Parties"), `Protected Business Information` (optional — include only if `engineering_integration` selected), `Purpose`, `Ready-for-Service`, `Representatives`, `Site Identifier`.

Omit: `Competitor` (supplier-specific; use Cl. 4.4 Change of Control mechanic instead), `DEC Block` (dropped v3.2), `Services` (supplier provides goods/capability, not colo services), `Transaction` (not a single deal — multiple SOWs contemplated).

## 2. Purpose and Scope

**2.1** This LOI records the Parties' mutual interest in establishing a strategic supply partnership under which the Partner would contribute [capability summary] to the Provider's DEC platform, on the indicative terms set out in Clauses 3 and 4.

**2.2** The Parties intend this LOI to provide a framework for further technical and commercial discussion toward a definitive Framework Agreement. The commercial terms in Clauses 3 and 4 are non-binding expressions of intent.

**2.3** The confidentiality, non-circumvention, and general provisions in Clauses 5 through 8 are legally binding and enforceable from the date of execution.

## 3. Partnership Scope (NON-BINDING — per Cl. 5.1)

### 3.1 Capability Contribution (ALWAYS)

Under the envisaged partnership:

(a) the Provider would contribute the site, energy procurement, grid connection, land and regulatory compliance, long-term operational responsibility, and integration into the Provider's broader DEC platform for each Designated Site; and

(b) the Partner would contribute [CAPABILITY DESCRIPTION — e.g., modular DEC infrastructure design and engineering, off-site prefabrication and factory manufacturing, on-site assembly and integration, commissioning support, design optimisation toward a Ready-for-Service target of [RFS_TARGET], preferred-customer status for hardware and component procurement, and ongoing design iteration to accommodate next-generation accelerator architectures].

The precise scope boundaries, responsibility matrix, and commercial terms will be defined in the Framework Agreement and accompanying SOWs.

### 3.2 Capacity Reservation — IF `capacity_lock_in`

The Provider has indicated a projected demand for [N units / MW / services] across its active development pipeline. Subject to commercial agreement, the Partner will reserve capacity in its manufacturing / service delivery plan to support the Provider's indicative pipeline, with specific volumes and delivery windows set out in the Framework Agreement.

### 3.3 Lead-Time Targets — IF `capacity_lock_in`

The Partner targets a Ready-for-Service lead time of [X] from [site-preparation completion / purchase order / design freeze]. The Framework Agreement will set the binding lead-time commitments, performance measurement, and remedies for delay.

### 3.4 Pricing Framework — IF `pricing_volume`

Pricing framework under discussion: [cost-plus / unit economics / volume tier model]. Specific pricing, volume thresholds, and escalation mechanisms will be set out in the Framework Agreement.

### 3.5 Volume Tiers — IF `pricing_volume`

Indicative volume tiers contemplated: [Tier A: N–M units, pricing principle X; Tier B: M–N units, pricing principle Y]. Non-binding; final tiers in Framework Agreement.

### 3.6 Dual-Source and Continuity Commitments — IF `supply_chain_de_risking`

The Partner acknowledges the Provider's requirement for supply-chain resilience. The Parties will discuss, and document in the Framework Agreement, (a) component substitution and second-source provisions; (b) continuity commitments (Partner obligation to provide advance notice of discontinuation and to support orderly transition); (c) delivery SLAs and remedies.

### 3.7 Design Integration and IP Allocation — IF `engineering_integration`

The Parties will collaborate on design integration across [SPECIFIED INTERFACES — e.g., modular DEC outer shell, cooling distribution, power distribution]. IP allocation: (a) each Party retains its background IP; (b) foreground IP developed jointly will be allocated per the `joint_ip` choice (`none` / `background` / `foreground`) specified in the Framework Agreement; (c) no Party assigns pre-existing IP by reason of this LOI or any discussions under it.

### 3.8 Preferred-Supplier / ROFR — IF `pipeline_visibility`

Subject to commercial agreement in the Framework Agreement, the Provider intends to grant the Partner a right of first refusal on the procurement of [SCOPE — e.g., modular infrastructure equipment and prefabricated components] across the Provider's active development pipeline. The right of first refusal requires the Partner to submit a compliant proposal within 20 Business Days of the Provider's invitation. This LOI does not create any binding ROFR; the terms will be set out in the Framework Agreement.

## 4. Pipeline Engagement (NON-BINDING — per Cl. 5.1)

### 4.1 Project Introduction Process — IF `pipeline_visibility`

Following execution of this LOI, the Provider intends to maintain a pipeline register of Designated Sites. The Partner will be invited to participate in named project opportunities on the cadence and criteria set out in the Framework Agreement.

### 4.2 Contractual Sequence (ALWAYS)

The Parties acknowledge the intended progression of commercial instruments:

(a) this LOI, setting out non-binding commercial intent and binding confidentiality / non-circumvention;

(b) a Framework Agreement, setting out the definitive commercial and operational framework for the supply relationship;

(c) one or more Statements of Work (SOWs) for named Provider projects, each executed under the Framework Agreement; and

(d) site-specific deliverables, schedules, or operational annexes executed under each SOW.

### 4.3 Joint-Development Governance — IF `engineering_integration`

The Parties intend to establish a joint design / engineering working group to coordinate interfaces, design reviews, and lifecycle decisions. Governance structure, decision rights, and escalation paths will be set out in the Framework Agreement.

### 4.4 Change of Control (ALWAYS)

If, during the term of this LOI, a direct competitor of the Provider (as determined by reference to its primary business being the development, ownership, or operation of colocation facilities for high-density compute workloads) acquires Control of the Partner, the Provider may terminate this LOI by written notice with immediate effect. Upon such termination, the confidentiality and non-circumvention obligations in Clauses 6 and 7 shall continue for their stated survival periods.

### 4.5 Exclusivity — IF `capacity_lock_in` and `choices.exclusivity=true`

Subject to execution of the Framework Agreement and achievement of the reservation commitments set out therein, the Parties intend to agree a scope of mutual exclusivity covering [DEFINED SCOPE]. This LOI does not create any exclusivity; the terms will be agreed in the Framework Agreement.

### 4.6 Implementation Roadmap (ALWAYS)

Following execution of this LOI, the Parties intend to proceed as follows:

(a) Technical and commercial scoping (target: 30 days post-LOI) — detailed discovery on scope, capability, lead times, pricing framework, IP allocation.

(b) Draft Framework Agreement (target: 60 days post-LOI) — the Provider will issue a draft Framework Agreement incorporating agreed terms.

(c) Framework Agreement negotiation and execution (target: 90 days post-LOI) — the Parties will negotiate and execute the Framework Agreement.

These timelines are indicative and non-binding.

## 7. Non-Circumvention (BINDING — light supply-side variant)

**7.1** The Partner shall not, directly or indirectly, without the prior written consent of the Provider:

(a) contact, solicit, deal with, or enter into any business relationship with any Associated Counterparty introduced by the Provider in connection with this LOI or any Provider project; or

(b) circumvent, avoid, or bypass the Provider in order to deal directly or indirectly with any Associated Counterparty in connection with the development, ownership, or operation of colocation or energy infrastructure on or adjacent to a site identified by the Provider.

**7.2 Duration.** 24 months from expiry or termination (default; adjustable 18–36 months per DE policy).

**7.3 Deemed Introduction.** Provider's sharing of any Site Identifier constitutes a deemed introduction of all Associated Counterparties for that site.

**7.4 Scope Limitation.** The non-circumvention obligations are limited to the Provider's supply-side relationships. Nothing in this Clause 7 restricts the Partner's own business with its existing or future customers, including other colocation operators, cloud providers, or enterprise customers.

**7.5 Independent Knowledge Exception.** Does not apply to any Associated Counterparty with whom the Partner can demonstrate by contemporaneous written evidence a pre-existing relationship or substantive commercial contact.

**7.6 Framework Agreement Supersession.** Upon execution of the Framework Agreement, the non-circumvention provisions in the Framework Agreement supersede this Clause 7, except that the survival period in 7.2 continues to apply to any introduction made before the Framework Agreement effective date.
