# Positioning Canvas: Waste Heat to Growers

A complete positioning exercise using the Obviously Awesome framework for DE's waste heat supply to Dutch greenhouse operators.

---

## Component 1: Competitive Alternatives

**Question:** What would a Dutch greenhouse operator do if Digital Energy didn't exist?

| Alternative | Likelihood | Why They'd Choose It |
|---|---|---|
| **Stay on natural gas** (most likely) | High | Familiar, no action required, immediate availability |
| **Geothermal (aardwarmte)** | Medium | Government support (SDE++), deep decarbonization |
| **Industrial waste heat** | Low | Only if a factory is nearby; opportunistic |
| **Biomass boiler** | Low | Moderate decarbonization; some subsidy options |
| **Do nothing** (variant of gas) | High | Inertia — "it works today, why change?" |

**Primary competitive alternative:** Natural gas (staying on the status quo)
**Secondary competitive alternative:** Geothermal (for forward-looking growers)

---

## Component 2: Unique Attributes

**Question:** What does DE have that natural gas and geothermal don't?

| Attribute | vs. Natural Gas | vs. Geothermal |
|---|---|---|
| Price: EUR 10-15/MWh, CPI-indexed | Gas: EUR 35-45 + ETS2 (volatile) | Geo: EUR 20-30 (post-boring, subsidized) |
| Zero CAPEX from grower | Gas: ongoing boiler CAPEX | Geo: EUR 10M+ co-investment |
| 12-18 month timeline | Gas: already there | Geo: 8-15 years |
| No commodity price exposure | Gas: TTF volatility | Geo: stable (but SDE++ dependent) |
| No geological risk | N/A | Geo: not every boring succeeds |
| No subsidy dependency | N/A | Geo: relies on SDE++ |
| Grid connection monetization | Gas: doesn't use grid | Geo: doesn't use grid |
| 24/7 availability (8,760 hrs) | Gas: on demand | Geo: 6,000-7,500 hrs |
| CPI indexation (not commodity) | Gas: TTF-linked | Geo: mix |

**Top 3 differentiating attributes:**
1. **60-80% cost reduction with zero investment** (vs. both alternatives)
2. **12-18 months to delivery, no risk** (vs. geothermal)
3. **Grid connection becomes a revenue-generating asset** (unique to DE model)

## Component 4: Best-Fit Customer

**Question:** Which growers care MOST about this value?

### Ideal Profile
- **Size:** 10-50 hectares under glass
- **Location:** Grid-congested area (Westland, Oostland, Noord-Holland, Noord-Brabant)
- **Grid connection:** Existing oversized connection (10-25 MW) from WKK era
- **Gas bill:** > EUR 1M/year (pain is acute enough to act)
- **Business stage:** Family business with succession planning (30-year horizon is relevant)
- **Current heating:** Gas-dependent (no existing geothermal commitment)
- **Mindset:** Pragmatic, numbers-driven, peer-influenced (LTO network)

### Disqualifying Characteristics (not best-fit)
- Already committed to geothermal project (sunk cost; unlikely to switch)
- Too small (< 5 hectares — heat demand doesn't justify DEC)
- No grid connection (no cable pooling opportunity)
- Lease-operated (can't grant recht van opstal)
- Located far from grid congestion zones (grid connection less scarce, less leverage)

### Best-Fit Trigger Events
- Gas bill increase (TTF spike or contract renewal)
- Succession conversation (son/daughter taking over)
- LTO event where another grower shares their experience
- Grid capacity review (discovering their connection is underutilized)
- ETS2 awareness (realizing costs will increase again in 2027)

## Component 5: Market Category

**Question:** What frame makes DE's value immediately obvious to a grower?

### Category Analysis

| Category Option | Grower's Mental Model | Works? |
|---|---|---|
| "Datacenter" | "Servers? On my land? Noise, electricity, weird." | No — wrong associations |
| "AI infrastructure" | "Ik snap er niets van" (I don't understand it) | No — intimidating |
| "Warmtebedrijf" (heat company) | "Like Eneco or Vattenfall? Too corporate." | Partial — misses uniqueness |
| "Restwarmteleverancier" (waste heat supplier) | "Ah, like industrial heat — I know some who use that." | Better — familiar concept |
| "Verwarmingsalternatief" (heating alternative) | "Something instead of gas? Tell me more." | Best entry point — problem-framed |

### Recommended Category
**"Restwarmteleverancier"** (waste heat supplier) — because:
- Growers already understand waste heat conceptually (some know about factory heat)
- It positions DE as a HEAT provider, not a TECHNOLOGY company
- It avoids the AI/datacenter framing that confuses and intimidates
- It naturally leads to the cost comparison conversation

### Category Context Statement
"We are a waste heat supplier. We build the heat source on your land, finance it entirely, and deliver heat to your greenhouse at 60-80% below your current gas cost. The heat comes from AI computers, but your experience is simple: cheaper, stable heating."

## Positioning Statement

```
For Dutch greenhouse operators with 10+ hectares and oversized grid connections
who face structurally rising gas costs and approaching EU ETS2 carbon pricing,
Digital Energy is the waste heat supplier
that reduces heating costs by 60-80% with zero investment and 12-18 month delivery
because we build and finance AI data centers on your land that recover 97% of
waste heat at EUR 15/MWh, CPI-indexed.
Unlike natural gas, our price doesn't follow commodity markets.
Unlike geothermal, we deliver in months, not decades, with zero geological risk.
```

## Message Testing Checklist

Before deploying this positioning in the field:

- [ ] Does a grower understand it in 30 seconds without explanation?
- [ ] Does the value statement survive the "so what?" test? ("60-80% gas cost reduction" → "So what?" → "You save EUR [X]K/year" → "So what?" → "Your business survives the energy transition")
- [ ] Can the grower explain it to their spouse/accountant in one sentence?
- [ ] Does it differentiate clearly from gas AND geothermal?
- [ ] Is every number verifiable from the proof points library?
- [ ] Does it work in Dutch? (Translate and test with native speakers)
