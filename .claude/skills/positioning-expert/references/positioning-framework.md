# Positioning Framework — Obviously Awesome Adapted for Digital Energy

April Dunford's 5-component positioning framework, adapted for DE's multi-segment infrastructure platform. Use this framework every time you need to define or refine positioning for any DE product or segment.

---

## The 5-Component Framework

### Component 1: Competitive Alternatives

**Question:** What would our target buyer do if Digital Energy didn't exist?

This is NOT "who are our competitors?" It's broader: what is the buyer's realistic status quo or next-best option?

#### Competitive Alternatives by Segment

| Segment | Alternative 1 | Alternative 2 | Alternative 3 | Alternative 4 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Grower** | Stay on natural gas | Geothermal (aardwarmte) | Industrial waste heat (if nearby) | Biomass boiler |
| **District Heating** | Gas-fired baseload | Geothermal | Biomass | Industrial waste heat |
| **Neocloud** | Nordic colocation (cheap power, high latency) | Frankfurt incumbents (low latency, no capacity) | Self-build (full control, 5-10 year wait) | US hyperscaler infrastructure |
| **Enterprise** | US hyperscaler (AWS/Azure/GCP) | On-premises GPU cluster | Nordic sovereign cloud | Managed service provider |
| **Energy Partner** | Standalone BESS project | Grid leasing to third party | Self-develop DC on own grid | Do nothing (wait for grid queue) |
| **Institutional Investor** | Direct infrastructure fund | Listed DC REITs | Green bonds / ESG allocation | Traditional energy infrastructure |

#### How to Use This
1. List the alternatives specific to the deal you're working on
2. For each alternative, identify what the buyer gets AND what they give up
3. Your positioning must explain why DE is better than their most likely alternative — not all alternatives
4. The most dangerous alternative is usually "do nothing" — address inertia directly

### Component 2: Unique Attributes

**Question:** What capabilities does DE have that no single alternative provides?

Unique attributes must be **true, demonstrable, and relevant to the buyer.** "We have great technology" is not a unique attribute. "We have secured grid connections in the Netherlands where 60 GW of applications are queued" is.

#### DE's Unique Attributes

| Attribute | What It Is | Why It's Unique |
|---|---|---|
| **Secured grid connections** | Grid capacity already approved in congested areas | 60 GW queue; up to 10-year wait; we bypassed the queue |
| **Integrated three-stream model** | DC + waste heat + BESS on one connection | No competitor combines all three; cable pooling enables it |
| **Waste heat as product** | 97% recovery, sold at EUR 10-15/MWh CPI-indexed | Standard DCs dump heat; we monetize it |
| **BESS as cash flow bridge** | BESS deploys first, generates returns during DC construction | Eliminates the "no revenue during construction" problem |
| **Speed to deployment** | 12-18 months from FID (vs. 5-10 years for new grid) | Existing grid = no grid wait; pre-approved connections |
| **Non-recourse project finance** | Each site is a separate Dutch BV with isolated risk | Institutional-grade structure; not venture-funded risk |
| **European sovereignty** | Dutch BV, European hardware (Lenovo), Dutch law | Not US hyperscaler; not offshore entity; verifiable European jurisdiction |
| **Cable pooling** | Up to 4 parties sharing one grid connection | Energiewet 2026; enables co-location without new grid |
| **Zero buyer CAPEX (heat)** | DE finances all infrastructure; buyer pays per MWh consumed | No investment required from grower/utility; pure opex |

### Component 3: Value (What the Attributes Create)

**Question:** What value does each unique attribute actually deliver to the buyer?

This is the translation layer. Attributes are features; value is what the buyer gets. Buyers don't buy attributes — they buy outcomes.

### Component 4: Best-Fit Customers

**Question:** Within each segment, who cares MOST about our specific value?

Not all growers are equal. Not all neoclouds want the same thing. Best-fit customers are the ones whose pain is most acute and whose alternative options are worst.

#### Best-Fit Customer Profiles

**Grower — Best fit:**
- 10-50 hectares (large enough for meaningful heat demand)
- Located in congested grid areas (Westland, Oostland, Noord-Holland)
- Has existing oversized grid connection (from WKK era)
- Gas bill > EUR 1M/year (pain is acute)
- Next generation is thinking about succession (30-year decision is relevant)
- NOT currently committed to geothermal project

**District Heating — Best fit:**
- Network in 4th/5th generation design or upgrade cycle
- Municipal ownership structure (Wcw transition pressure)
- Currently over-reliant on gas-fired baseload
- Located near potential DEC site (heat transport < 5 km)
- In Wcw designated heat company selection process

**Neocloud — Best fit:**
- European expansion on their roadmap (not US-only)
- Inference-heavy workloads (latency-sensitive)
- Mid-scale (5-50 MW range — too big for retail colo, too small for hyperscale)
- Selling to European enterprises (sovereignty is a commercial advantage)
- Timeline pressure: need capacity in 12-18 months, not 3-5 years

**Enterprise — Best fit:**
- EU-headquartered or EU-regulated
- AI-forward (training or deploying models, not just experimenting)
- Data sovereignty is a board-level concern (financial services, defense, government)
- Current hyperscaler dependency creates compliance or strategic risk

**Investor — Best fit:**
- Infrastructure mandate (not venture/growth equity)
- Dutch/European allocation available
- Familiarity with project finance / non-recourse structures
- ESG/sustainability alignment (heat recovery, energy transition)
- Ticket size: EUR 5-50M per project

### Component 5: Market Category

**Question:** What context makes our value immediately obvious to the buyer?

Market category is the frame. When you say "we're a [category]," the buyer instantly forms expectations about what you do, who your competitors are, and what you're worth.

#### Category Options for DE

| Category Option | Pros | Cons | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| "Integrated energy infrastructure" | Captures all three streams; signals project finance | Vague; doesn't differentiate | Investors, energy partners |
| "AI infrastructure with heat recovery" | Unique; no competitor claims this | Confusing if buyer doesn't know AI or heat markets | General positioning |
| "Sovereign AI data center" | Clear for neocloud/enterprise; EU narrative | Doesn't capture heat or BESS | Neoclouds, enterprises |
| "Sustainable data center" | ESG appeal; heat recovery as differentiator | Overused; every DC claims sustainability | Press, general marketing |
| "Waste heat supplier for horticulture" | Clear for growers; speaks their language | Misses the AI/DC part entirely | Growers only |
| "Energy transition infrastructure" | Policy-aligned; ESG; broad | Too broad; doesn't say what you actually do | Institutional investors |

#### Recommended Category by Context

| Context | Recommended Category | Positioning Statement |
|---|---|---|
| Talking to growers | "Restwarmteleverancier" (waste heat supplier) | "We build the heat source that replaces your gas boiler — for free." |
| Talking to neoclouds | "European sovereign AI infrastructure" | "We have European megawatts for your GPUs — secured, liquid-cooled, available in 12-18 months." |
| Talking to district heating | "Baseload warmtebron" (baseload heat source) | "24/7 waste heat at CPI-indexed prices — the cheapest baseload in your portfolio." |
| Talking to enterprises | "Sovereign European AI platform" | "European compute on Dutch soil, European hardware, Dutch law." |
| Talking to investors | "Integrated energy infrastructure platform" | "Three contracted revenue streams per project, non-recourse financed, on scarce grid connections." |
| General marketing | "Digital Energy Centers" | "AI infrastructure that turns waste heat into value." |

## How to Run a Positioning Exercise

### Step-by-Step Process

1. **Pick one segment.** Don't try to position for all segments simultaneously.
2. **List their realistic alternatives.** What would they ACTUALLY do? Not theoretical — practical.
3. **For each alternative, list what the buyer gets and gives up.** Be honest about where alternatives are strong.
4. **List DE's unique attributes relevant to this segment.** Only attributes that matter to THIS buyer.
5. **Translate each attribute to a buyer-facing value statement.** Features → outcomes.
6. **Narrow to best-fit customer.** Within the segment, who has the most acute pain?
7. **Choose the market category.** What frame makes the value immediately obvious?
8. **Write the positioning statement.** One sentence that captures: For [best-fit customer] who [pain], DE is the [category] that [key value] because [unique attribute].
9. **Test it.** If it doesn't resonate in a real sales conversation, iterate.

### Positioning Statement Template

```
For [BEST-FIT CUSTOMER]
who [PRIMARY PAIN POINT],
Digital Energy is the [MARKET CATEGORY]
that [PRIMARY VALUE DELIVERED]
because [UNIQUE ATTRIBUTE THAT ENABLES THE VALUE].
Unlike [PRIMARY COMPETITIVE ALTERNATIVE],
DE [KEY DIFFERENTIATOR].
```

### Example (Grower):
```
For Dutch greenhouse operators with 10+ hectares
who face structurally rising gas costs and underutilized grid connections,
Digital Energy is the waste heat supplier
that reduces heating costs by 60-80% with zero investment
because we build and finance AI data centers on your land that recover 97% of
waste heat at EUR 15/MWh, CPI-indexed.
Unlike natural gas, our price doesn't follow commodity markets.
Unlike geothermal, we deliver in 12-18 months, not 8-15 years.
```
