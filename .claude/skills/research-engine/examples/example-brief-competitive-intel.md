## Adversarial Debate: GIGA Buffalo's "2+ GWh" Pipeline Claim

### Pro Position (Agent A)

GIGA Buffalo's website and press materials consistently reference a 2+ GWh pipeline. The Moerdijk project alone accounts for 200 MWh with a valid omgevingsvergunning. If Venlo and Terneuzen reach similar scale, the cumulative figure is plausible. Dutch RVO records show multiple SDE++ applications under GIGA Buffalo B.V. entities, supporting the breadth claim. The company's hiring pattern (5 new project development roles in H2 2025) is consistent with scaling a large pipeline.

### Contra Position (Agent B)

"Pipeline" is undefined in GIGA Buffalo's materials — it likely includes everything from site identification to permitted projects. The 2 GWh figure would require ~500 MW of 4-hour systems, but only Moerdijk has a permit. Grid connection applications (Venlo, Terneuzen) are not permits — TenneT queue times are 3–5 years in congested zones. No financing has been announced for any project. By contrast, Giga Storage's 500 MW pipeline includes multiple projects already under construction or permitted. Comparing the two pipeline numbers at face value is misleading.

### Judge Resolution

**Assessment: Contra position is better supported.** GIGA Buffalo's 2 GWh figure likely includes projects at stages where attrition rates are high (40–60% of early-stage BESS projects in NL do not reach FID, per market estimates). The number is not fabricated — it reflects aspirational pipeline breadth — but should not be compared directly with Giga Storage's more advanced, partially operational portfolio. **Confidence in GIGA Buffalo delivering 2 GWh by 2028: LOW.** Confidence in Giga Storage delivering 500 MW by 2028: **MEDIUM-HIGH.**
