# Sample Contact Rationales

Each contact in the output requires a rationale explaining why this person is the right target. Here are 5 examples showing good rationale writing.

## Example 1: Champion at Large Enterprise (High Confidence)

**Company:** ING Group | **Contact:** [Name], VP Cloud Platform Engineering
**Rationale:** Champion — directly owns cloud infrastructure and compute platform decisions at ING. ING runs a hybrid multi-cloud strategy (AWS + Azure + private). [Name] spoke at AWS re:Invent 2025 on financial services cloud migration, indicating active involvement in infrastructure scaling decisions. Reports to CTO. High confidence: verified on ING careers page and LinkedIn (title current as of January 2026).

## Example 2: Economic Buyer at University Medical Center (Medium Confidence)

**Company:** UMC Utrecht | **Contact:** [Name], Chief Information Officer
**Rationale:** Economic Buyer — as CIO, holds budget authority for all IT infrastructure investments at UMC Utrecht. UMCs typically centralize IT procurement under the CIO. Found via UMC Utrecht annual report 2024 (management board section). Medium confidence: title confirmed in annual report, LinkedIn profile shows UMC Utrecht as current employer but profile not recently updated.

## Example 3: Single Contact at Tier 3 Company (Medium Confidence)

**Company:** Plukon Food Group | **Contact:** [Name], IT Director

IT Director is the most senior IT role identified via LinkedIn search. As a food processing company with 25+ facilities across Europe, Plukon likely manages significant on-prem compute. ## Example 4: Champion with Uncertainty Flag (Low Confidence)

**Company:** Royal Vopak | **Contact:** [Name], Head of Digital & IT
**Rationale:** Champion — most likely owner of infrastructure and compute decisions. However, Vopak's IT organization may be restructuring — a March 2025 press release announced a "digital transformation program" with a new CDO appointment. The Head of Digital & IT role may have changed scope. Low confidence: title from 2024 LinkedIn profile, needs verification. Recommend checking Vopak.com/about before outreach.

## Example 5: Economic Buyer at Government Entity (Medium Confidence)

**Company:** Rijkswaterstaat | **Contact:** [Name], Directeur Informatievoorziening
**Rationale:** Economic Buyer — as Director of Information Services, owns the IT budget for Rijkswaterstaat (Dutch infrastructure ministry, ~12,000 employees). Government IT procurement follows PIANOo framework — this role typically has final authority on infrastructure vendor selection within the ministry. Found via rijksoverheid.nl organizational chart. Medium confidence: government appointments are public record, but organizational restructuring is common. Title verified on rijksoverheid.nl as of 2025.

## What Makes a Good Rationale

1. **State the role** (Champion / Economic Buyer / Blocker)
2. **Explain WHY this person** — what decision authority do they have?
3. **Note the company's IT context** — centralized vs decentralized, known cloud strategy, recent changes
4. **Cite your source** — where did you find this person?
5. **Flag uncertainty** — if title is old, if org is restructuring, if you're inferring from job postings
6. **Keep it to 2-3 sentences** — dense and actionable, not a paragraph
