# Vertical Playbooks — Per-Vertical Research Strategies

## Overview

Each vertical has distinct definitions of "largest," different registry sources, and unique research sequences. This guide covers six verticals across the Benelux region (NL/BE/LU) with specific named sources and practical research steps.

---

## 1. Healthcare

### What Constitutes "Largest"
- **Primary metric**: Annual revenue (omzet)
- **Secondary metrics**: Number of beds (for hospitals), FTE count, patient volume, number of locations
- **Threshold for Tier 1**: >500M EUR revenue or >1,000 beds
- **Threshold for Tier 2**: 100-500M EUR revenue or 300-1,000 beds
- **Threshold for Tier 3**: 25-100M EUR revenue or 100-300 beds

### Sub-Segments
- University Medical Centers (UMCs) — 7 in NL
- General hospitals (algemene ziekenhuizen)
- Specialty clinics (categorale ziekenhuizen)
- Mental health institutions (GGZ)
- Elderly care / nursing homes (VVT sector)
- Rehabilitation centers
- Home care organizations
- Health insurers (zorgverzekeraars)
- Diagnostic centers and laboratories
- Pharmaceutical distributors

### Registry / Database Sources

**Netherlands**
- **NZa (Nederlandse Zorgautoriteit)**: Regulator; publishes financial data for all healthcare providers, annual jaarverslagen database
- **CIBG**: Maintains BIG-register (healthcare professionals), AGB-register (healthcare organizations)
- **Zorgatlas (RIVM)**: Geographic mapping of healthcare facilities
- **CBS (Centraal Bureau voor de Statistiek)**: Healthcare expenditure statistics, sector employment data
- **KVK (Kamer van Koophandel)**: Company filings, annual reports for larger entities
- **Jaarverslagenzorg.nl**: Mandatory annual reports for healthcare institutions
- **Zorgkaart Nederland**: Patient-facing directory with organization details
- **NFU (Nederlandse Federatie van UMCs)**: All 7 UMC details
- **NVZ (Nederlandse Vereniging van Ziekenhuizen)**: Hospital association member list
- **ActiZ**: Elderly care and home care association

**Belgium**
- **FOD Volksgezondheid / SPF Sante Publique**: Federal public health data
- **KCE (Kenniscentrum voor de Gezondheidszorg)**: Healthcare knowledge center reports
- **RIZIV/INAMI**: National health insurance institute
- **Zorgnet-Icuro**: Flemish hospital and care network (~110 hospitals)
- **Belfius Hospital Barometer**: Annual hospital financial benchmarking

**Luxembourg**
- **Ministere de la Sante**: Healthcare regulation and licensing
- **CNS (Caisse nationale de sante)**: National health fund
- **FHL (Federation des Hopitaux Luxembourgeois)**: Hospital federation
- Note: LU has ~4 major hospital groups (CHL, HRS, CHEM, Bohler/CHL)

### Industry Associations & Member Lists
- NVZ — ~60 hospital members, publishes member directory
- ActiZ — ~400 elderly/home care organizations
- GGZ Nederland — mental health institutions
- ZN (Zorgverzekeraars Nederland) — all health insurers
- Zorgnet-Icuro (BE) — Flemish care network

### Common IT Infrastructure Patterns
- Epic, HiX (ChipSoft), SAP for ERP in large hospitals
- Heavy on-premise due to patient data sovereignty
- Growing cloud adoption for non-clinical workloads
- PACS/imaging systems are compute-intensive
- AI/ML adoption accelerating (radiology, pathology)
- Data lake initiatives for population health

### Research Sequence
1. Pull NZa financial database for revenue ranking
2. Cross-reference with NVZ/ActiZ member lists
3. Check jaarverslagenzorg.nl for detailed financials
4. KVK filings for corporate structure and subsidiaries
5. Website review for IT leadership mentions, digital strategy publications
6. LinkedIn for CIO/CTO/CISO identification
7. Conference speaker lists (Zorg & ICT, HIMSS Europe)
8. Annual report technology sections

## 2. Agriculture

### What Constitutes "Largest"
- **Primary metric**: Revenue (omzet)
- **Secondary metrics**: Hectares under management, livestock count, cooperative membership size, export volume
- **Threshold for Tier 1**: >1B EUR revenue (major cooperatives/traders)
- **Threshold for Tier 2**: 100M-1B EUR revenue
- **Threshold for Tier 3**: 25-100M EUR revenue

### Sub-Segments
- Agricultural cooperatives (e.g., Royal Agrifirm, ForFarmers, FrieslandCampina)
- Livestock farming (dairy, pork, poultry)
- Arable farming (cereals, potatoes, sugar beets)
- Agricultural trading houses
- Feed manufacturers
- Seed companies
- Agricultural machinery/equipment
- Agri-tech / precision farming
- Food processing (linked to agriculture)
- Agricultural finance and insurance

### Registry / Database Sources

**Netherlands**
- **KVK**: Company registration, SBI codes for agriculture (01.xxx)
- **CBS**: Agricultural census data, farm counts by type and size
- **RVO (Rijksdienst voor Ondernemend Nederland)**: Agricultural subsidies, farm registrations
- **NVWA (Nederlandse Voedsel- en Warenautoriteit)**: Food safety authority, registered producers
- **LEI Wageningen (now WEcR)**: Agricultural economics research, farm size data
- **Boerenbusiness.nl**: Agricultural news, company profiles
- **Agri&Food Top 50 (various publications)**: Annual rankings

**Belgium**
- **Boerenbond**: Flemish farmers union, largest agricultural association
- **ABS (Algemeen Boerensyndicaat)**: Alternative farmers union
- **VLAM (Vlaams Centrum voor Agro- en Visserijmarketing)**: Flemish agri-marketing
- **FOD Economie / Landbouwtelling**: Agricultural census
- **Departement Landbouw & Visserij**: Flemish agriculture department

**Luxembourg**
- **ASTA (Administration des Services Techniques de l'Agriculture)**: Agricultural services
- **Chambre d'Agriculture**: Agricultural chamber, member directory
- **SER (Service d'Economie Rurale)**: Rural economy service
- Note: LU agriculture is small; ~2,000 farms, focus on dairy and wine

### Industry Associations
- LTO Nederland — largest Dutch farmers organization
- ZLTO, LLTB — regional branches
- NCR (Nationale Cooperatieve Raad) — cooperative council
- BO Akkerbouw — arable farming board
- ZuivelNL — dairy industry organization
- Boerenbond (BE) — major Flemish agricultural union

### Common IT Infrastructure
- ERP systems (SAP in large cooperatives, specialized agri-ERP for mid-market)
- IoT/sensor networks for precision farming
- GPS/satellite data processing
- Supply chain management platforms
- Cold chain monitoring systems
- Growing cloud adoption for data analytics
- Cooperative IT shared services

### Research Sequence
1. CBS agricultural statistics for sector sizing
2. KVK filings for large cooperatives and agri-businesses
3. Agri&Food rankings and industry publications
4. LTO/Boerenbond member directories
5. RVO subsidy data for scale indicators
6. Website and annual report review
7. LinkedIn for IT/digital leadership
8. Wageningen University connections and events

## 3. Horticulture (NL-Specific Focus)

> **IMPORTANT**: There are 147 existing deals in HubSpot for this vertical. Before adding ANY company, check the HubSpot export for duplicates by company name, domain, and KVK number.

### What Constitutes "Largest"
- **Primary metric**: Revenue (omzet) or auction turnover
- **Secondary metrics**: Hectares under glass, number of greenhouses, production volume, employee count
- **Threshold for Tier 1**: >100M EUR revenue or >50 hectares under glass
- **Threshold for Tier 2**: 25-100M EUR revenue or 10-50 hectares
- **Threshold for Tier 3**: 10-25M EUR revenue or 5-10 hectares

### Sub-Segments
- Cut flowers growers
- Potted plants growers
- Vegetable growers (tomatoes, peppers, cucumbers)
- Tree nurseries (boomkwekerij)
- Bulb growers (bollenteelt)
- Seed breeding companies
- Horticultural trading companies
- Auction/marketplace operators (Royal FloraHolland)
- Greenhouse technology suppliers
- Horticultural cooperatives

### Registry / Database Sources

**Netherlands (Primary Market)**
- **Glastuinbouw Nederland**: Industry association for greenhouse horticulture, member directory
- **Royal FloraHolland**: Cooperative flower auction; member/grower data, annual reports with top grower data
- **KVK**: SBI code 01.1x (horticulture), company filings
- **CBS**: Greenhouse area statistics by municipality, production volumes
- **RVO**: Greenhouse energy data, EIA/MIA investments
- **Greenport databases**: Regional cluster organizations (Westland, Aalsmeer, Venlo, etc.)
- **Plantscope / Floridata**: Industry directories
- **Groenten & Fruit / Vakblad voor de Bloemisterij**: Trade publication rankings
- **LTO Glaskracht (now part of Glastuinbouw Nederland)**: Historical data

**Belgium**
- **VLAM**: Flemish horticultural marketing
- **REO Veiling**: Belgian fruit/vegetable auction
- **Boerenbond Tuinbouw**: Horticultural division

**Luxembourg**
- Very limited horticulture; not a focus market for this vertical

### Industry Associations
- Glastuinbouw Nederland — primary association (~3,000 members)
- Royal FloraHolland — cooperative with ~3,500 member growers
- VGB (Vereniging van Groothandelaren in Bloemkwekerijproducten) — wholesalers
- Plantum — seed and propagation material
- GroentenFruit Huis — fresh produce trade association

### Common IT Infrastructure
- Climate control systems (Priva, Hoogendoorn, Ridder)
- Energy management systems (CHP, heat storage, geothermal)
- ERP (often specialized: Mprove, Mprise, or custom)
- Crop registration and planning software
- Logistics and auction integration systems
- Growing adoption of AI for crop optimization
- High energy consumption = high compute affinity

### Research Sequence
1. **CHECK HUBSPOT FIRST** — export and cross-reference 147 existing deals
2. Glastuinbouw Nederland member directory
3. Royal FloraHolland top grower/member data
4. CBS greenhouse statistics for area-based ranking
5. KVK filings for revenue data
6. Greenport regional directories
7. Trade publication rankings
8. Website review for technology adoption signals
9. LinkedIn for management and IT contacts
10. Energy consumption data as compute propensity proxy

## 4. Finance

### What Constitutes "Largest"
- **Primary metric**: Total assets (for banks), Assets Under Management (for asset managers), Gross Written Premium (for insurers)
- **Secondary metrics**: Revenue, employee count, number of branches/offices
- **Threshold for Tier 1**: >50B EUR total assets (banks) or >10B AUM or >5B GWP
- **Threshold for Tier 2**: 5-50B EUR assets or 1-10B AUM
- **Threshold for Tier 3**: 1-5B EUR assets or 100M-1B AUM

### Sub-Segments
- Universal/retail banks
- Private banks and wealth managers
- Asset management firms
- Insurance companies (life, non-life, health)
- Pension funds
- Payment service providers (PSPs)
- Fintech companies
- Leasing and factoring companies
- Stock exchanges and market infrastructure
- Compliance/regtech

### Registry / Database Sources

**Netherlands**
- **DNB (De Nederlandsche Bank)**: Register of supervised institutions, all licensed banks/insurers/pension funds
- **AFM (Autoriteit Financiele Markten)**: Register of licensed investment firms, advisors
- **KVK**: Company filings
- **CBS**: Financial sector statistics
- **NVB (Nederlandse Vereniging van Banken)**: Banking association member list
- **Verbond van Verzekeraars**: Insurance association
- **Pensioenfederatie**: Pension fund federation
- **Betaalvereniging Nederland**: Payment association
- **MSc Top 100 Verzekeraars (AM)**: Annual insurance rankings

**Belgium**
- **NBB/BNB (Nationale Bank van Belgie)**: Supervised entity register
- **FSMA (Financial Services and Markets Authority)**: Licensed firms register
- **Febelfin**: Belgian financial sector federation
- **Assuralia**: Belgian insurance association
- **Trends Top 100.000**: Company rankings including financial sector

**Luxembourg**
- **CSSF (Commission de Surveillance du Secteur Financier)**: Primary financial regulator, comprehensive register of all licensed entities
- **ALFI (Association of the Luxembourg Fund Industry)**: Fund industry association
- **ABBL (Association des Banques et Banquiers, Luxembourg)**: Banking association
- **ACA (Association des Compagnies d'Assurances)**: Insurance association
- Note: LU is a major financial center; ~130 banks, ~3,700 investment funds, ~270 insurance companies

### Industry Associations
- NVB, Betaalvereniging (NL banking/payments)
- Verbond van Verzekeraars (NL insurance)
- Febelfin (BE financial sector)
- CSSF registers, ALFI, ABBL (LU)

### Common IT Infrastructure
- Core banking platforms (Temenos, FIS, Finastra)
- Heavy regulatory compute (risk models, stress testing)
- Trading infrastructure (low-latency)
- Data warehousing and analytics
- Cloud migration underway but security-constrained
- AI/ML for fraud detection, credit scoring

### Research Sequence
1. DNB/CSSF/NBB supervised entity registers for comprehensive lists
2. AFM/FSMA for investment firms
3. Industry association member lists
4. Annual reports for asset/revenue sizing
5. KVK filings for corporate structure
6. Regulator publications for technology adoption signals
7. LinkedIn for CTO/CIO/CISO identification
8. Conference lists (Money20/20, Sibos, Dutch Financial Markets)

## 5. Logistics

### What Constitutes "Largest"
- **Primary metric**: Revenue (omzet)
- **Secondary metrics**: Fleet size, warehouse square meters, number of employees, TEU volume (ports)
- **Threshold for Tier 1**: >500M EUR revenue
- **Threshold for Tier 2**: 100-500M EUR revenue
- **Threshold for Tier 3**: 25-100M EUR revenue

### Sub-Segments
- Road transport and trucking
- Warehousing and distribution (3PL)
- Port operations and container terminals
- Freight forwarding
- Express and parcel delivery
- Cold chain logistics
- Bulk/tanker transport
- Inland waterway shipping
- Air cargo
- Supply chain management / 4PL

### Registry / Database Sources

**Netherlands**
- **TLN (Transport en Logistiek Nederland)**: Largest Dutch transport association, member directory
- **evofenedex**: Shippers association
- **KVK**: Company filings, SBI codes 49-53 (transport/logistics)
- **CBS**: Transport statistics, fleet data
- **NIWO (Nationale en Internationale Wegvervoer Organisatie)**: Road transport licenses
- **Logistiek.nl / MT Top 100 Logistiek**: Industry rankings
- **Port of Rotterdam Authority**: Tenant and operator data
- **Port of Amsterdam**: Operator directories
- **Schiphol Cargo**: Air cargo operator data

**Belgium**
- **Port of Antwerp-Bruges**: Major European port, operator data
- **Febetra**: Belgian road transport federation
- **VIL (Vlaams Instituut voor de Logistiek)**: Flemish logistics institute
- **TLV (Transport en Logistiek Vlaanderen)**: Flemish transport association
- **Trends Top 100.000**: Company rankings

**Luxembourg**
- **CLC (Cluster for Logistics Luxembourg)**: Logistics cluster
- **Luxembourg for Finance / LuxCargo**: Cargo community
- **Cargolux**: LU-based cargo airline (major employer)
- Note: LU logistics centered around Cargolux and EU institutions supply chain

### Industry Associations
- TLN (~5,000 members) — primary NL transport association
- evofenedex — shipper/logistics buyers
- HIDC (Holland International Distribution Council)
- Febetra / VIL (BE)
- CLC (LU)

### Common IT Infrastructure
- TMS (Transport Management Systems)
- WMS (Warehouse Management Systems)
- Fleet management and telematics
- EDI and supply chain integration
- IoT for tracking and monitoring
- Route optimization (compute-intensive)
- Growing demand for real-time analytics

### Research Sequence
1. TLN/evofenedex member directories
2. Industry rankings (Logistiek Top 100)
3. KVK filings for revenue data
4. Port authority tenant lists
5. NIWO license data for fleet sizing
6. CBS transport statistics
7. Website and annual report review
8. LinkedIn for IT/digital leadership
9. Conference attendee lists (Logistica, Transport & Logistics)

## 6. Defense / Government

### What Constitutes "Largest"
- **Primary metric**: Contract value, budget allocation, strategic importance
- **Secondary metrics**: Employee count, clearance level requirements
- **Threshold approach**: Different from commercial — focus on prime contractors, system integrators, and government agencies with significant IT budgets

### Sub-Segments
- Defense ministries and armed forces
- Defense prime contractors
- Defense system integrators
- Cybersecurity firms (defense-grade)
- Government IT service providers
- Intelligence and security services
- NATO and EU defense agencies
- Defense logistics and maintenance
- Space and satellite defense
- Critical infrastructure operators

### Registry / Database Sources

**Netherlands**
- **Defensie.nl**: Ministry of Defence information, organizational structure
- **PIANOo (Expertisecentrum Aanbesteden)**: Public procurement platform, tender data
- **TenderNed**: Government tender publication system
- **NIDV (Nederlandse Industrie voor Defensie en Veiligheid)**: Defense industry association
- **KVK**: Company filings for defense contractors
- **AIVD/MIVD**: Intelligence services (organizational data only)
- **DMO (Defensie Materieel Organisatie)**: Defense materiel organization

**Belgium**
- **Belgian Defence**: defense.belgium.be
- **Egov.be**: Government procurement
- **BSDI (Belgian Security and Defence Industry)**: Industry association

**Luxembourg**
- **Luxembourg Defence**: Government portal
- **NATO Support and Procurement Agency (NSPA)**: Headquartered in LU
- **SatCen / SES**: Satellite operations (LU-based)
- Note: NSPA being in LU makes it a unique opportunity

### Industry Associations
- NIDV — primary NL defense industry association (~400 members)
- BSDI (BE) — Belgian defense industry
- ASD (AeroSpace and Defence Industries Association of Europe)

### Common IT Infrastructure
- Classified networks (separate from commercial)
- High-security data centers
- Simulation and modeling (compute-intensive)
- C4ISR systems
- Cybersecurity operations centers
- Satellite communication systems
- AI/ML for intelligence analysis

### Research Sequence
1. PIANOo/TenderNed for active and historical defense tenders
2. NIDV member directory
3. Defensie.nl organizational structure
4. KVK filings for defense contractors
5. NATO procurement data
6. Annual defense budget publications
7. Defense industry events (NIDV symposium, Euronaval)
8. LinkedIn for program managers, CTOs (note: many defense contacts have limited LinkedIn presence)
9. Public procurement award notices

### Special Considerations for Defense
- Security clearances may limit contact accessibility
- Longer sales cycles (18-36 months)
- Procurement often through framework agreements
- NATO interoperability requirements
- ITAR/EAR considerations for US-origin technology
- Government fiscal year timing affects budgets
- Often require sovereign/on-premise deployment options

## Cross-Vertical Notes

### Research Prioritization
When working across verticals, prioritize by:
1. Data availability (Finance and Healthcare have best public data)
2. Existing pipeline (check HubSpot for any vertical)
3. Compute propensity (Healthcare imaging, Finance risk models, Defense simulation, Horticulture climate control)
4. Deal size potential (Finance and Defense typically largest)

### Common Pitfalls
- Confusing holding companies with operating entities
- Missing subsidiary relationships (check KVK for group structures)
- Outdated revenue figures (always note the year of data)
- Assuming NL patterns apply to BE/LU (regulatory environments differ)
- Not checking for existing HubSpot records before research
