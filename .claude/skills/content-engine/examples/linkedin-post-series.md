## Theme 2: Waste Heat Economics

### Post 3 — The heat revelation
```
Everyone's talking about AI infrastructure.

Nobody's talking about where the heat goes.

Every MW of AI compute produces roughly a MW of thermal energy. Most data
centers dump it into the atmosphere through cooling towers.

We recover it. 97% efficiency. And sell it to greenhouses at EUR 15/MWh.

For context: natural gas costs Dutch growers EUR 35-45/MWh. And that's
before EU ETS2 adds EUR 10-20/MWh from 2027.

Waste heat isn't a byproduct of our business model. It IS the business
model. The AI compute and the heat recovery are inseparable.

That's what makes a Digital Energy Center different from a data center.
```

## Theme 4: Project Finance / Infrastructure

### Post 7 — Why infrastructure, not tech
```
We're not a tech company.

I know that sounds strange for a company that builds AI data centers.

But here's the thing: our core assets are grid connections, land rights,
heat contracts, and project finance structures. The technology inside the
building is important — but it changes every 3-5 years.

The grid connection lasts decades. The heat supply contract is 30 years.
The recht van opstal (land rights) is perpetual.

That's why we structure every project as a separate Dutch BV with
non-recourse project finance. It's infrastructure, not venture capital.

The investors who understand this aren't VCs. They're pension funds,
infrastructure debt providers, and insurance companies.

Different asset class. Different risk profile. Different return
expectations. Different conversation entirely.
```

### Post 8 — The BESS bridge
```
The hardest part of infrastructure development is the gap between
investment and revenue.

For data centers: 18-30 months of construction before the first tenant
pays rent.

We solved this with a bridge: BESS (battery energy storage).

Our model:
1. Deploy BESS on day one using the grid connection (6-12 months to COD)
2. BESS generates standalone returns immediately (energy arbitrage + FCR)
3. Data center develops in parallel (permitting, financing, construction)
4. When DC goes live, BESS equity converts to DC equity

The BESS doesn't just de-risk the project. It generates cash while we
build. It proves operational capability to institutional lenders. And it
shares the grid via cable pooling.

This is what revenue stacking looks like in practice.
```

### Post 10 — The problem with "game-changing"
```
I've banned the word "game-changing" from our company.

Also banned: revolutionary, cutting-edge, synergy, unlock potential, and
world-class.

Here's why:

When you say your product is "game-changing," you're asking the reader to
trust your adjective. They won't.

When you say "we reduce greenhouse heating costs from EUR 35-45/MWh to
EUR 15/MWh," you're giving them a number they can verify.

Numbers build trust. Adjectives build skepticism.

Every claim in our marketing materials has a source. Every financial
projection has three scenarios. Every regulatory reference names the
specific act and year.

If we can't cite it, we don't say it.

This isn't modesty. It's strategy. Our buyers are sophisticated. They've
seen every pitch deck and every "revolutionary solution." They respond
to specificity.

Precision > promotion. Every time.
```
