# Channel Playbooks — Best Practices per Marketing Channel

---

## 1. LinkedIn

### CEO Personal Account (Primary thought leadership channel)
- **Frequency:** 3-4 posts/week
- **Format:** Text-only posts perform best for reach. Carousel documents for deeper topics. Video for site updates and events.
- **Length:** 1,000-1,300 characters (sweet spot for engagement without "see more" penalty)
- **Voice:** First person. Personal observations, not corporate announcements. "I just learned..." > "Digital Energy is pleased to announce..."
- **Hook formula:** First line must stop the scroll. Use: surprising number, counterintuitive statement, or direct question.
  - "60 GW of battery storage is sitting in a queue in the Netherlands. 60. Gigawatts."
  - "We tell growers their gas bill can drop 60-80%. Most don't believe us at first."
  - "Everyone's talking about AI infrastructure. Nobody's talking about where the heat goes."
- **Engagement triggers:** End with a question. Tag relevant people. Reply to every comment within 2 hours.
- **Hashtags:** Max 3. Use #energietransitie #AIinfrastructure #datacenter or segment-specific tags.
- **Posting time:** Tuesday-Thursday, 7:00-9:00 CET (Dutch professional audience) or 14:00-16:00 CET (international).

### Company Page
- **Frequency:** 2-3 posts/week
- **Format:** Mix of: company news (20%), industry data (30%), thought leadership reposts (20%), team/culture (15%), event promotion (15%)
- **Voice:** Third person, professional, data-led
- **CTA:** Link to website, portal, or resource download

### LinkedIn Ads
- **Headline:** Max 150 characters. Benefit-first: "Cut Greenhouse Gas Costs 60-80% — No Investment Required"
- **Intro text:** Max 70 characters visible before "read more"
- **Image:** Clean, professional. Avoid stock photos. Use data visualizations or facility renderings.
- **Lead Gen Forms:** Pre-fill as many fields as possible (LinkedIn auto-fills from profile). Ask for: name, email, company, job title. Nothing more.
- **A/B test:** Always run 2 variants (different headline or image). Kill underperformer after 1,000 impressions.

## 2. Email

### Cold Email
- **Subject line:** Under 40 characters. Personalized when possible. No caps, no exclamation marks.
  - Good: "[Company] + waste heat?"  |  "Your grid connection in [region]"  |  "Quick question about [topic]"
  - Bad: "AMAZING OPPORTUNITY!"  |  "You won't believe..."  |  "Digital Energy Partnership Proposal"
- **Body:** Under 150 words. Three paragraphs max:
  1. Why you (personalized — reference their company, situation, or recent news)
  2. What we do (one sentence, benefit-oriented, with one number)
  3. Ask (one clear CTA: reply or book a call)
- **Signature:** Name, title, company. No logos, no banners, no quotes. Plain text looks personal.
- **Timing:** Tuesday-Thursday, 8:00-10:00 local time. Avoid Monday morning and Friday afternoon.
- **Follow-up cadence:** Day 0, Day 3, Day 7, Day 14, Day 21 (5-email sequence, then stop).
- **Deliverability:** Warm up new domains for 2+ weeks. Max 30-50 emails/day per mailbox. Use SPF, DKIM, DMARC.

### Warm Email
- **Subject:** Reference the mutual contact or previous interaction: "Following up from [event]" or "[Name] suggested I reach out"
- **Body:** Same brevity rules as cold, but lead with the relationship: "We met at [event]" or "[Mutual contact] mentioned you're exploring..."
- **Timing:** Within 48 hours of event/introduction. Relevance decays fast.

### Newsletter
- **Frequency:** Monthly
- **Structure:** 1 original insight/article (300 words) + 3 curated industry news items (50 words each) + 1 CTA (event, resource, or portal signup)
- **Subject line:** Specific and value-clear: "Dutch grid queue hits 60 GW + 3 things we're watching" (not "Digital Energy Monthly Update")
- **Segment:** By persona interest (heat, compute, BESS, general)

## 3. Blog / Website

### Blog Articles
- **Length:** 800-1,500 words
- **Structure:** Hook → Context → Insight → Evidence → Implication → CTA
- **SEO:** Target one primary keyword per article. Include in H1, first paragraph, and 2-3 subheadings.
- **Frequency:** 2x/month
- **Topic categories:**
  - Market analysis ("Dutch grid scarcity: what the 60 GW queue means for your business")
  - How-it-works explainers ("How waste heat from AI compute heats a greenhouse")
  - Regulatory updates ("Wcw explained: what it means for heat network operators")
  - Thought leadership ("Why Europe needs sovereign AI infrastructure")

### Landing Pages
- **Structure:** Headline → 3-5 bullet benefits → Social proof → CTA → FAQ
- **One page per persona.** Grower landing page in Dutch. Neocloud landing page in English.
- **CTA:** One per page. Portal signup (growers) or "Book a call" (neocloud/enterprise).
- **No navigation links** above the fold. Reduce exit paths.

## 4. Press Releases

- **Structure:** Inverted pyramid
  1. Headline (factual, specific: "Digital Energy Secures 25.5 MW Grid Connection for AI Data Center in Noord-Holland")
  2. Lead paragraph (who, what, when, where, why — in one paragraph)
  3. Quote from CEO
  4. Supporting details (2-3 paragraphs)
  5. Boilerplate (from `de-brand-bible/examples/boilerplate-paragraphs.md`)
  6. Contact information
- **Language:** Factual. No superlatives. No "pleased to announce" (just announce).
- **Distribution:** NL: Energeia, Duurzaam Gebouwd, AGF.nl, Glastuinbouw.nl. International: Data Center Knowledge, DCD, Capacity Media.
- **Versions:** One EN, one NL. Not a direct translation — adapt emphasis per audience.

## 5. Event Invitations

### Conference Speaking
- **Pre-event:** LinkedIn post announcing the talk (2 weeks before). Email to target contacts ("I'm speaking at [event] — happy to connect in person").
- **At event:** Collect contacts (badge scan/QR). Take 2-3 photos for social content.
- **Post-event:** Thank-you email with slides + resource link (within 48 hours). LinkedIn post with key takeaway from the talk.

### Site Visit Invitations
- **Format:** Personal email from BD or CEO. Not marketing blast.
- **Tone:** "Would you like to see [what we're building / the facility / the grid connection] in person?"
- **Details:** Date options, duration (60-90 min), what they'll see, who they'll meet.
- **Follow-up:** Confirm 3 days before. Send directions + parking. Follow up within 48 hours with summary + next steps.

### Webinar Invitations
- **Registration page:** Title, date/time, 3-bullet description of what they'll learn, speaker bio.
- **Promotion:** LinkedIn post + email to segment list + LinkedIn ad (if budget).
- **Follow-up:** Recording + slides to all registrants (attendees and no-shows). Segment by attendance for different follow-up sequences.

## 6. Advertising (Paid)

### Google Ads (Search)
- **Keywords:** "colocation Netherlands," "data center NL," "GPU hosting Europe," "AI infrastructure Netherlands," "glastuinbouw verwarming," "restwarmte datacenter"
- **Ad copy:** Headline 1: benefit. Headline 2: proof/specificity. Description: one sentence + CTA.
- **Landing page:** Dedicated per keyword group. Match the search intent exactly.

### Trade Press Ads
- **Grower publications:** Kas Magazine, Onder Glas, Vakblad voor de Bloemisterij
  - Format: quarter-page or half-page
  - Message: Dutch language, problem-first: "Uw gasrekening 60-80% omlaag?"
  - CTA: Phone number + portal URL (growers prefer phone for initial contact)
- **DC/tech publications:** Data Center Knowledge, DCD, Capacity Media
  - Format: sponsored content or banner
  - Message: English, tech-specific: "Liquid-cooled AI colocation on secured Dutch grid"
  - CTA: Website or contact form
