# Tone & Style Guide — Content Engine Writing Standards

Supplements the brand voice rules in `de-brand-bible/references/voice-rules.md` and `de-brand-bible/references/banned-phrases.md` with content-specific production standards.

---

## Grammar & Mechanics

- **Oxford comma:** Yes, always. "Heat, compute, and BESS."
- **Numbers:** Spell out one through nine in prose. Use digits for 10+ and all measurements/money. "We have five projects" but "25.5 MW."
- **Currency:** Always "EUR" prefix, no symbol: "EUR 15/MWh" not "€15/MWh"
- **Abbreviations:** Define on first use in each document. Exception: common ones (EU, NL, UK, US, AI) don't need expansion.
- **Capitalization:** Sentence case for headings (not Title Case). Exception: formal names (Digital Energy Center, Grower Portal, ProjectBV).
- **Dates:** Day Month Year in prose ("13 February 2026"). YYYY-MM-DD in filenames and technical contexts.
- **Paragraphs:** Max 3 sentences per paragraph in digital content. Max 5 in formal documents.
- **Bullet points:** Parallel structure. Each bullet starts the same way (all verbs, all nouns, etc.).

## Content Length Standards

| Deliverable | Target Length | Hard Limit |
|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn post | 150-200 words | 1,300 characters |
| Cold email body | 80-120 words | 150 words |
| Blog article | 800-1,200 words | 1,500 words |
| Press release | 400-500 words | 600 words |
| Event invitation | 100-150 words | 200 words |
| Newsletter issue | 400-600 words total | 800 words |
| One-pager | 300-400 words | Fits one A4 page |
| Ad headline | 5-10 words | 150 characters |

## Technical Precision Standards

- **Regulatory citations:** Always name the act and year on first use. "Wcw (Wet collectieve warmtevoorziening, effective mid-2026 to 1 Jan 2027)."
- **Market data:** Always include the source in parentheses in blog/thought leadership. "[CBRE Q1 2025]" or "[BNEF Dec 2025]."
- **Financial claims:** Always qualify with "indicative" or "base case" in investor/partner-facing content. Social media can state the number directly.
- **Forward-looking statements:** In formal documents, include a qualifier: "Subject to definitive agreements and market conditions."
- **Conversion rates:** Use current EUR/USD unless the source uses a specific rate. Note the basis if material.

## Readability Standards

- **Flesch-Kincaid target:** Grade 10-12 for English content. Professional but accessible.
- **Avoid jargon for growers.** Use "grid connection" not "netaansluiting" (unless writing in Dutch). Use "waste heat" not "thermal energy recovery."
- **Use jargon for neoclouds.** "40-140 kW/rack DLC" is appropriate. They expect technical density.
- **Use jargon for investors.** "Non-recourse senior secured debt at EURIBOR + 200-250 bps" is expected. Don't simplify for this audience.

## Content Review Checklist

Before publishing any content, verify:

- [ ] Follows brand voice rules (no banned phrases, active voice, numbers-first)
- [ ] Correct buyer persona addressed (pain points match the segment)
- [ ] All numbers sourced (from proof-points library or project-financing references)
- [ ] Single CTA (not multiple competing asks)
- [ ] Correct language (NL for growers, EN for neocloud/enterprise/investor)
- [ ] Correct terminology (DEC not "data center," Grower not "farmer," etc.)
- [ ] Length within channel limits
- [ ] No confidential information (no internal financial models, negotiation positions, or partner-specific terms)
