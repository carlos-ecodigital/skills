# Copywriting Frameworks for B2B Energy & AI Infrastructure

---

## 1. PAS (Problem — Agitate — Solve)

**Best for:** Cold emails, LinkedIn hooks, ad headlines

### Structure
1. **Problem:** Name the specific pain (not generic)
2. **Agitate:** Make it feel urgent — what happens if they don't act?
3. **Solve:** Present DE's solution with one proof point

### DE Examples

**Grower cold email (PAS):**
```
Problem: Your greenhouse gas bill is EUR [X] per year — and EU ETS2 will add
EUR 10-20/MWh from 2027.

Agitate: That's a 20-40% cost increase on top of already volatile gas prices.
Every year you wait, the carbon surcharge grows.

Solve: We co-locate an AI data center on your property and supply you waste
heat at EUR 15/MWh — CPI-indexed, not gas-linked. No investment from you.
```

**Neocloud LinkedIn ad (PAS):**
```
Problem: European GPU capacity is sold out. Grid connections take 10 years.

Agitate: Your enterprise customers want EU sovereignty. You can't deliver if
you can't build.

Solve: Liquid-cooled AI colocation in NL on secured grid. First capacity
available in 4-6 months.
```

## 2. AIDA (Attention — Interest — Desire — Action)

**Best for:** Landing pages, longer LinkedIn posts, blog introductions

### Structure
1. **Attention:** Surprising fact, bold statement, or question
2. **Interest:** Explain why this matters to them specifically
3. **Desire:** Show the outcome with proof
4. **Action:** Clear single CTA

### DE Example (Landing Page — Neocloud)

```
ATTENTION: 60 GW of grid connections are queued in the Netherlands.
We already have ours.

INTEREST: If you're a GPU cloud provider looking for European capacity,
power is the bottleneck — not space. Most new connections take 5-10 years.

DESIRE: Digital Energy offers liquid-cooled AI colocation on secured Dutch
grid connections with AMS-IX proximity. PUE target: 1.2. Power density:
40-140 kW/rack. First capacity available 2027.

ACTION: Book a 15-minute technical briefing. →
```

## 3. BAB (Before — After — Bridge)

**Best for:** Case studies, testimonials, transformation narratives

### Structure
1. **Before:** What the buyer's world looks like now (the pain state)
2. **After:** What it looks like after DE (the transformed state)
3. **Bridge:** How DE gets them there

### DE Example (Grower case study narrative)

```
BEFORE: [Grower name] was spending EUR 800K/year on natural gas. Price
volatility made budgeting impossible. EU ETS2 was going to make it worse.

AFTER: Today, 70% of their heating comes from waste heat at EUR 15/MWh —
CPI-indexed, predictable. Their gas bill dropped EUR 500K/year. They earn
additional revenue from heat sold to neighboring operations.

BRIDGE: Digital Energy co-located a DEC on their property, using their
existing grid connection. The grower invested zero. Construction took
[X] months. Heat delivery started [date].
```

## 4. 4Ps (Promise — Picture — Proof — Push)

**Best for:** Sales emails, partner proposals, follow-ups

### Structure
1. **Promise:** Make a specific, credible claim
2. **Picture:** Help them visualize the outcome
3. **Proof:** One specific proof point or testimonial
4. **Push:** Clear ask with urgency

### DE Example (Energy Partner email)

```
PROMISE: Your 25.5 MW grid connection can generate EUR 23M+ in partnership
value over 10 years (base case).

PICTURE: Imagine: BESS returns starting 2027. Heat revenue from 2029.
Your grid — currently generating nothing — becomes the foundation of a
EUR 250M infrastructure asset.

PROOF: We've modeled three scenarios (conservative EUR 18M, base EUR 28M,
optimistic EUR 40M) using CBRE pricing, BNEF BESS costs, and Alantra
valuation multiples. Every assumption is sourced.

PUSH: Can we schedule 90 minutes next week to walk through the numbers?
I'd like to bring our financial modeling lead.
```

## 5. Hook Formulas (LinkedIn / Social Media)

### Scroll-Stopping First Lines

| Formula | Example |
|---|---|
| **Surprising number** | "60 GW. That's how much battery storage is waiting for a grid connection in the Netherlands." |
| **Counterintuitive claim** | "The most valuable thing about an AI data center isn't the compute. It's the heat." |
| **Direct challenge** | "If you're a greenhouse operator still on gas, here's what your competitors are doing." |
| **Personal observation** | "We just signed our third grower. The conversation that convinced them wasn't about technology." |
| **Question** | "What if your cooling cost became your biggest revenue stream?" |
| **Confession** | "I was wrong about how fast growers would adopt this. Here's what happened." |
| **List tease** | "Three things I've learned about selling waste heat to greenhouses:" |

### Post Structure (LinkedIn)
```
[Hook — 1 line, stops the scroll]

[Space]

[Context — 2-3 sentences, sets up the insight]

[Space]

[Insight — the core point, 2-4 sentences with specific data]

[Space]

[CTA or question — invites engagement]
```

## 6. Email Subject Line Formulas

| Formula | Example | Use For |
|---|---|---|
| **[Company] + [topic]?** | "Van Gogh Greenhouses + waste heat?" | Cold email to growers |
| **Quick question about [topic]** | "Quick question about your grid connection" | Cold email to energy partners |
| **[Mutual contact] suggested I reach out** | "Arco suggested I reach out" | Warm intro email |
| **[Number] that matters for [role]** | "One number that matters for greenhouse economics" | Sequence email #2 |
| **Following up from [event]** | "Following up from LTO Zuid-Holland" | Post-event warm email |
| **RE: [previous topic]** | *(Only if there was a real previous conversation)* | Follow-up — never fake a RE: |

## 7. CTA Formulas

| CTA Type | When to Use | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Low-commitment** | First touch, cold audience | "Would a 15-minute call make sense?" |
| **Resource-based** | Content marketing, lead capture | "Download the grid scarcity report" |
| **Social-based** | LinkedIn posts | "Comment 'heat' and I'll send you the numbers" |
| **Direct** | Warm audience, repeat touch | "Book a site visit" |
| **Urgency (real)** | When capacity is genuinely limited | "We're allocating 3 grid connections in Q2. First come, first served." |
| **Curiosity** | Blog, newsletter | "Here's what the numbers look like →" |

## 8. Bilingual Writing Rules (NL/EN)

For grower-facing content:
- **Primary language:** Dutch
- **Legal/technical terms:** English in parentheses on first use, then Dutch only
  - Example: "recht van opstal (right of superficies)" → thereafter "recht van opstal"
- **Numbers:** Use European format (EUR 1.000,00) in NL content, international format (EUR 1,000.00) in EN content
- **Tone in Dutch:** Slightly more formal than English. Use "u" not "je" for grower-facing materials (professional respect).
- **Avoid direct translation.** Adapt the emphasis and cultural framing, not just the words.
