# Eigen Vermogen Structuren / Equity Structures for Dutch Infrastructure Project Finance

## 1. Overzicht / Overview

Equity structuring for Dutch (NL) infrastructure special purpose vehicles (SPVs) encompasses BV share capital design, shareholder agreements (aandeelhoudersovereenkomsten), co-investment and joint venture (JV) structures, institutional investor requirements, and exit mechanisms. The Dutch BV framework, reformed by the Flex-BV Act (Wet vereenvoudiging en flexibilisering bv-recht, effective 1 October 2012), provides significant flexibility for tailored governance and economic arrangements in project finance contexts.

This reference covers equity structures relevant to data centers (DC), battery energy storage systems (BESS), AI factories, and related Dutch infrastructure projects. It addresses the full lifecycle from incorporation and initial equity raise through operational distributions and eventual exit.

### Key Themes

- **Flex-BV flexibility**: No minimum capital, multiple share classes, customizable transfer restrictions, and tailored distribution rules enable project-specific structuring.
- **Institutional capital**: Dutch pension funds (pensioenfondsen) and insurers (verzekeraars) are increasingly active in domestic infrastructure, driven by SFDR and EU Taxonomy requirements.
- **JV prevalence**: Most large infrastructure projects involve joint ventures combining different capabilities (grid access, capital, operational expertise).
- **Lender overlay**: Equity structures must accommodate senior lender requirements including distribution lock-ups, consent rights, and security packages.

## 2. BV Aandelenstructuur / BV Share Structure

### 2.1 Aandelenkapitaal / Share Capital

The Flex-BV Act eliminated the EUR 18,000 minimum capital requirement for BVs, providing maximum flexibility for project finance structuring.

| Parameter | Requirement | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Minimum nominal value | EUR 0.01 per share | No statutory minimum since Flex-BV [Source: Art. 2:178 BW] |
| Share type | Aandelen op naam (registered) | Bearer shares (toonderaandelen) abolished for BVs |
| Share register | Aandeelhoudersregister | Maintained by bestuur; not public [Source: Art. 2:194 BW] |
| Share transfer | Notariele akte (notarial deed) | Required for valid transfer [Source: Art. 2:196 BW] |
| Issuance at premium | Agio (share premium) | Common in project finance; premium to reserves |
| Multi-class shares | Permitted | Letteraandelen (lettered shares) for differentiated rights |

#### Share Classes in Infrastructure SPVs

| Class | Dutch Term | Typical Use | Rights |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ordinary shares | Gewone aandelen | Base equity participation | Voting + economic |
| Preference shares | Preferente aandelen | Priority return structures | Preferred dividend; limited voting |
| Lettered shares (A/B/C) | Letteraandelen | Different investor tranches | Customized per class |
| Profit-sharing certificates | Winstbewijzen | Management/sponsor incentive | Economic only; no voting |
| Priority shares | Prioriteitsaandelen | Governance control | Enhanced voting on specific matters |

#### Typical SPV Capital Structure

| Layer | Instrument | Typical Range | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Share capital | Nominal + agio | EUR 1-10M | Thin equity layer |
| Shareholder loans | Achtergestelde lening | 5-15% of total funding | Subordinated; quasi-equity |
| Senior debt | Bankfinanciering / bonds | 60-80% of total funding | Project finance leverage |
| Mezzanine (if any) | Achtergestelde lening (external) | 5-10% of total funding | Gap financing |

[Source: NautaDutilh, Stibbe, De Brauw project finance practice guides]

### 2.2 Inbreng in Natura / Contribution in Kind

Non-cash contributions are common in infrastructure SPVs where parties contribute assets, rights, or services rather than (or in addition to) cash.

| Requirement | BV (Flex-BV) | NV | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Accountantsverklaring (auditor's statement) | NOT required since 1 Oct 2012 | Still required | Art. 2:204a BW (old) / Flex-BV |
| Inbrengbeschrijving (description of contribution) | Required | Required | Art. 2:204a BW |
| Content of inbrengbeschrijving | Description of contribution, valuation, valuation method | Same + auditor verification | NBA Handreiking 1129 |
| Signed by | All founders or directors | Same | Art. 2:204a BW |
| Maximum age | 6 months before incorporation/issuance | Same | Art. 2:204a BW |
| Director liability | Bestuurders liable if overvalued (Art. 2:9 BW) | Same + auditor liability | BW Book 2 |

#### Common In-Kind Contributions in Infrastructure Projects

| Contribution Type | Valuation Approach | Key Considerations |
|---|---|---|
| Grid connection rights (netaansluiting) | Market value of connection; queue position value | TenneT/Stedin connection agreements; transferability |
| Land rights (recht van opstal / erfpacht) | Independent appraisal (taxatie) | Duration, conditions, zoning (bestemmingsplan) |
| Existing infrastructure | Replacement cost less depreciation | Technical due diligence required |
| Permits and licenses | Value of time-to-permit; comparable transactions | Omgevingsvergunning, SDE++ beschikking |
| IP / know-how | DCF of projected economic benefit | Transfer pricing considerations |

[Source: NBA Handreiking 1129; Kooijman Autar Notarissen; Van Doorne]

### 2.3 Verwatering en Voorkeursrecht / Dilution and Pre-emption Rights

The Flex-BV Act introduced statutory pre-emption rights for existing shareholders, providing built-in anti-dilution protection.

| Mechanism | Statutory Basis | Application |
|---|---|---|
| Voorkeursrecht (pre-emption right) | Art. 2:206a BW | On new share issuance; 4-week exercise period |
| Exclusion of voorkeursrecht | GM resolution (algemene vergadering) | Can be excluded for specific issuance |
| Contractual anti-dilution | SHA provisions | Full ratchet, weighted average, or broad-based |
| Pay-to-play | SHA provisions | Lose anti-dilution if decline to participate |
| Statutory transfer restriction | Art. 2:195 BW | Blokkeringsregeling (optional since Flex-BV) |

#### Anti-Dilution Mechanisms in SHA

| Type | Description | Typical Use |
|---|---|---|
| Full ratchet | Conversion price adjusts to lowest subsequent price | Aggressive; rare in infra |
| Weighted average (broad-based) | Adjustment based on total shares outstanding | Standard institutional protection |
| Weighted average (narrow-based) | Adjustment based on preferred shares only | Less common |
| Pay-to-play | Anti-dilution lost if investor does not participate in new round | Encourages follow-on investment |
| Percentage maintenance | Right to maintain percentage through additional investment | Common in JV structures |

[Source: BolderLaunch; wetboekplus.nl; Allen & Overy NL]

### 2.4 Uitkeringen / Distributions

The Flex-BV distribution regime introduces a dual test that is central to project finance equity structuring.

#### Statutory Distribution Tests (Art. 2:216 BW)

| Test | Description | Responsible Party | Consequence of Failure |
|---|---|---|---|
| Balanstest (balance sheet test) | Distribution may not exceed equity minus legal/statutory reserves | Algemene vergadering (GM) | Resolution is void |
| Liquiditeitstest (liquidity test) | Company must be able to pay debts as they fall due after distribution | Bestuur (management board) | Personal liability of directors |
| Director refusal right | Bestuur may refuse distribution even if balanstest passed | Bestuur | Must refuse if liquidity test fails |
| Clawback | Recipients who knew/should have known distribution was improper must repay | Shareholders | Up to amount received |

#### Project Finance Distribution Overlay

| Lender Requirement | Typical Threshold | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Lock-up DSCR | 1.10x - 1.20x | No distributions below threshold |
| Default DSCR | 1.05x | Cash sweep activated below threshold |
| DSRA funding | 6 months debt service | Must be fully funded before distribution |
| Maintenance reserve | Per technical advisor | Must be fully funded before distribution |
| Cash sweep | 50-75% of excess cash flow | Reduces distributable amount |
| Distribution holiday | 12-24 months post-COD | No distributions in ramp-up period |

[Source: Art. 2:216 BW; NautaDutilh; Loyens & Loeff project finance guides]

## 3. Aandeelhoudersovereenkomst (SHA) / Shareholders Agreement

### 3.1 Kernbepalingen / Key Provisions

The SHA (aandeelhoudersovereenkomst) is the primary contractual framework governing the relationship between equity investors in an infrastructure SPV.

| Provision | Dutch Term | Description | Dutch Practice |
|---|---|---|---|
| Board composition | Bestuurssamenstelling | Representation rights; nomination per share class | Bestuur (executive) + optional RvC (supervisory) |
| Reserved matters | Goedkeuringsbesluiten | Supermajority/unanimity for key decisions | Budget, CAPEX > threshold, new debt, M&A, related-party transactions |
| Information rights | Informatierechten | Financial reporting, budget, inspection | Quarterly reporting; annual audit; monthly management accounts |
| Transfer restrictions | Overdrachtsbeperkingen | Lock-up, ROFR, tag-along, drag-along | Lock-up 2-5 years typical for infra |
| Deadlock resolution | Geschillenregeling | Escalation mechanism for disputes | Binding advice followed by NAI arbitration |
| Non-compete | Concurrentiebeding | Scope and duration restrictions | 2-3 years post-exit; geographic/sector limitations |
| Exit rights | Uittreedrechten | Put/call options, IPO rights, trade sale | Liquidity event provisions; IRR-based triggers |
| Distribution waterfall | Uitkeringswaterval | Priority of distributions | IRR hurdles with promote structure |
| Funding obligations | Financieringsverplichtingen | Capital call mechanics | Pro rata; default penalties |
| Confidentiality | Geheimhouding | Information restrictions | Survives termination; carve-outs for lenders/advisors |

### 3.2 Bestuur en Toezicht / Board Governance

#### One-Tier vs. Two-Tier Board

| Structure | Dutch Term | Composition | Use in Project Finance |
|---|---|---|---|
| Two-tier (dualistisch) | Bestuur + RvC | Executive board + supervisory board | Traditional; large projects |
| One-tier (monistisch) | Bestuur met uitvoerende en niet-uitvoerende bestuurders | Executive and non-executive directors on one board | Increasingly used; Anglo-Saxon alignment |

#### Board Appointment Rights (Typical 50/50 JV)

| Role | Party A Nomination | Party B Nomination | Joint/Independent |
|---|---|---|---|
| Director 1 | Yes | -- | -- |
| Director 2 | -- | Yes | -- |
| Chairman (if any) | -- | -- | Independent or rotating |
| RvC member 1 | Yes | -- | -- |
| RvC member 2 | -- | Yes | -- |
| RvC chairman | -- | -- | Independent |

### 3.3 Reserved Matters (Goedkeuringsbesluiten)

#### Typical Thresholds

| Category | Decision | Approval Required |
|---|---|---|
| Financial | Annual budget / business plan | Unanimous board + shareholder approval |
| Financial | CAPEX exceeding EUR 500K-2M | Shareholder approval |
| Financial | New borrowing / guarantees | Shareholder approval + lender consent |
| Financial | Distributions | Board + shareholder approval (Art. 2:216 BW) |
| Corporate | Amendment of statuten | GM resolution (notarial deed) |
| Corporate | Share issuance / buyback | GM resolution |
| Corporate | Merger / demerger / dissolution | GM resolution |
| Operational | Material contracts (> EUR 250K-1M) | Board approval; shareholder notification |
| Operational | Key personnel appointment/removal | Board decision; shareholder consultation |
| Related party | Any related-party transaction | Independent director/shareholder approval |
| Dispute | Settlement > EUR 100K-500K | Shareholder approval |

### 3.4 Deadlock Resolution (Nederlandse Praktijk)

Infrastructure JVs require robust deadlock mechanisms given the long asset life and limited exit options.

| Step | Mechanism | Timeline | Description |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Escalation to senior management | 15-30 business days | CEOs or designated senior representatives meet |
| 2 | Mediation (optional) | 30-60 days | Voluntary; mediator appointed by agreement or NAI |
| 3 | Bindend advies (binding advice) | 60-90 days | Independent expert determines outcome |
| 4 | NAI chairman appointment | If parties cannot agree on expert | Voorzitter NAI appoints bindend adviseur |
| 5 | Decision period | 2 months from bindend advies | Parties implement or trigger buyout |
| 6 | Buyout / dissolution | Per SHA mechanism | Texas shoot-out, Russian roulette, or winding-up |

[Source: IBA Guide to Shareholders' Agreements Netherlands 2024; NAI Arbitration Rules]

#### Buyout Mechanisms

| Mechanism | Description | Typical Use |
|---|---|---|
| Texas shoot-out | One party names price; other decides buy or sell | Equal JVs; clean exit |
| Russian roulette | One party offers to buy or sell at stated price; other must accept one | Simpler variant |
| Fair value buyout | Independent valuation; mandatory buy/sell at fair value | Institutional preference |
| Auction (sealed bid) | Both parties submit sealed bids; highest bidder buys | Competitive; less common |
| Winding-up | Dissolution and liquidation of SPV | Last resort; value destructive |

### 3.5 Verhouding SHA en Statuten / Relationship SHA and Articles

| Aspect | SHA | Statuten |
|---|---|---|
| Legal nature | Contractual (obligatoire overeenkomst) | Corporate law (vennootschapsrechtelijk) |
| Binding on | Parties to SHA only | Company, organs, shareholders |
| Enforcement | Damages; specific performance | Corporate law remedies |
| Prevalence in conflict | SHA subordinate for corporate actions | Statuten prevail |
| Third-party effect | No direct effect on third parties | Binding on all shareholders |
| Amendment | Per SHA amendment provisions | Notarial deed; GM resolution |
| Best practice | Mirror key SHA terms in statuten | Ensures corporate law enforceability |

- Redelijkheid en billijkheid (reasonableness and fairness, Art. 2:8 BW) applies to all corporate relationships and can override strict contractual/statutory provisions in exceptional circumstances.
- Enqueterecht (Art. 2:345 BW): shareholders holding 10%+ can petition the Ondernemingskamer (Enterprise Chamber) of the Amsterdam Court of Appeal for an inquiry into mismanagement (wanbeleid).

[Source: Asser/Maeijer/Van Solinge & Nieuwe Weme 2-II*; HR Cancun (ECLI:NL:HR:2014:797)]

## 4. Joint Venture Structuren / JV Structures

### 4.1 50/50 Joint Venture (Gelijkwaardige Samenwerking)

The 50/50 JV is the most common structure for Dutch infrastructure projects where two parties combine complementary capabilities.

#### Typical 50/50 JV Contributions

| Contribution | Party A (Developer/Operator) | Party B (Financial Investor) |
|---|---|---|
| Grid connection rights | Yes | -- |
| Site / land rights | Yes (often) | -- |
| Development expertise | Yes | -- |
| Permitting track record | Yes | -- |
| Equity capital | Partial | Primary |
| O&M capability | Yes | -- |
| Offtake relationships | Yes | Possible |
| Balance sheet / credit | -- | Yes |
| Institutional network | -- | Yes |

#### Governance Framework

| Element | Typical Arrangement |
|---|---|
| Board | 2 directors (1 per party) or 2 + independent chairman |
| Voting | Unanimous for reserved matters; majority for ordinary business |
| Deadlock | Escalation + binding advice + buyout mechanism |
| Management fee | 5-8% of OPEX or cost-plus basis to operating partner |
| Development fee | 1-3% of CAPEX to developer (if applicable) |
| Reporting | Monthly management accounts; quarterly board meetings |

### 4.2 Meerderheid/Minderheid JV / Majority/Minority JV

| Aspect | Majority Partner | Minority Partner |
|---|---|---|
| Typical stake | 51-80% | 20-49% |
| Operational control | Full day-to-day management | Limited to reserved matters |
| Board seats | Proportional majority | At least 1 seat; observer rights |
| Reserved matters | Subject to minority veto on key items | Veto on: budget, CAPEX, debt, M&A, related-party |
| Information rights | Full access | Quarterly reporting; inspection right |
| Exit rights | Drag-along (if >75% typically) | Tag-along on any transfer |
| Distribution | Pro rata; but waterfall may differentiate | Protected by preferred return |

#### Minority Protection Checklist

| Protection | Description | Importance |
|---|---|---|
| Board representation | At least 1 director or RvC seat | Critical |
| Reserved matters veto | Supermajority on key decisions | Critical |
| Anti-dilution | Pre-emption right on new issuance | High |
| Tag-along | Right to sell alongside majority on same terms | Critical |
| Information rights | Regular reporting + inspection | High |
| Put option | Right to sell back at fair value (trigger events) | Medium-High |
| Dividend policy | Minimum distribution policy | Medium |
| Non-compete | Restrict majority's competing activities | Medium |
| Related-party controls | Independent approval of affiliated transactions | High |

### 4.3 Equity Conversion Mechanics

Infrastructure projects often involve equity structures that evolve over time as development milestones are achieved.

| Mechanism | Description | Typical Application | Valuation Approach |
|---|---|---|---|
| Convertible loan | Debt converts to equity at predetermined terms | Bridge financing; development phase | Discount to next equity round or formula |
| Earn-in | Equity stake increases with milestone achievement | Developer earns equity through permitting, COD | Pre-agreed valuation at each milestone |
| Ratchet | Equity percentage adjusts based on performance | IRR-based adjustment post-stabilization | Actual vs. target IRR comparison |
| Warrant/option | Right to acquire additional shares | Management incentive; step-up equity | Strike price based on base case model |
| Sweat equity | Equity for services rendered | Developer/operator incentive | Fair value of services |
| Deferred consideration | Additional equity if performance targets met | Acquisition structures | Earn-out based on EBITDA or other KPI |

#### Milestone-Based Equity Conversion Table

| Milestone | Developer Equity | Investor Equity | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Development phase | 100% (via DevCo) | 0% | Developer bears all risk |
| FID (Final Investment Decision) | 49-51% | 49-51% | Investor enters at FID valuation |
| Construction completion (COD) | 30-49% | 51-70% | Developer may sell down |
| Stabilization (12-24 months) | 20-30% | 70-80% | Performance-based adjustment |
| Long-term hold | 10-20% (retained or sold) | 80-90% | Developer may fully exit |

[Source: InfraDeals; IJGlobal project database; Rebel Group]

## 5. Uitkeringswaterval / Distribution Waterfall

### 5.1 Standaard Waterval / Standard Waterfall

The distribution waterfall defines the priority and allocation of cash distributions from the SPV to equity holders.

| Priority | Distribution | Rate/Amount | Recipient | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Return of capital | 100% of contributed equity | All equity holders pro rata | Pari passu return of investment |
| 2 | Preferred return (hurdle) | 8-10% IRR compounding | All equity holders pro rata | Accrues if unpaid |
| 3 | Catch-up | 100% to promote party | Sponsor/GP | Until promote party reaches target share of total distributions |
| 4 | Residual split | 80/20 or 70/30 | Investors 80% / Sponsor 20% | Continues until next hurdle or exit |

### 5.2 Meervoudige Drempels / Multiple Hurdle Waterfall

Sophisticated institutional investors typically negotiate multi-tier waterfalls aligned with return expectations.

| Tier | IRR Hurdle | LP/Investor Share | GP/Sponsor Share | Cumulative to Sponsor |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0% - 8% | 100% | 0% | 0% |
| 2 | 8% - 12% | 80% | 20% | ~4-5% of total |
| 3 | 12% - 15% | 70% | 30% | ~8-10% of total |
| 4 | >15% | 60% | 40% | Varies by performance |

#### Waterfall Variants

| Variant | Description | Typical Use |
|---|---|---|
| European (whole-fund) | Promote calculated on entire fund/portfolio return | Fund structures |
| American (deal-by-deal) | Promote calculated per individual investment | Co-investment; single-asset SPV |
| Hybrid | Deal-by-deal with fund-level true-up | Compromise approach |
| Catch-up (full) | 100% to sponsor until target split achieved | Standard in NL infra |
| Catch-up (partial) | 50-80% to sponsor in catch-up tier | Investor-friendly variant |

### 5.3 Lender Overlay on Distributions

All equity distributions from a project-financed SPV are subject to lender-imposed constraints that override the equity waterfall.

| Lender Test | Typical Threshold | Frequency | Effect on Distributions |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lock-up DSCR | 1.10x - 1.20x | Semi-annual / annual | No distributions below threshold |
| Default DSCR | 1.05x | Continuous | Cash sweep; potential acceleration |
| DSRA (Debt Service Reserve Account) | 6 months debt service | Continuous | Must be fully funded |
| Maintenance reserve account | Per technical advisor assessment | Continuous | Must be fully funded |
| Cash sweep | 50-75% of excess cash flow | Annual | Reduces distributable amount |
| Distribution holiday | 12-24 months post-COD | One-time | No distributions during ramp-up |
| No Event of Default | -- | Continuous | No distributions during default |
| No Material Adverse Effect | -- | Continuous | Subjective; lender discretion |

#### Distribution Mechanics

| Step | Action | Responsible |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Calculate available cash after OPEX, debt service, reserves | SPV management / financial model |
| 2 | Confirm DSCR above lock-up threshold | Lender's agent / model auditor |
| 3 | Apply cash sweep (if applicable) | Per credit agreement waterfall |
| 4 | Confirm all reserve accounts fully funded | Account bank |
| 5 | Obtain lender consent (if required) | Agent bank |
| 6 | Board approval (liquiditeitstest) | Bestuur |
| 7 | Shareholder resolution | Algemene vergadering |
| 8 | Distribute per equity waterfall | SPV management |

[Source: LMA project finance facility agreement templates; NautaDutilh; KPMG]

## 8. Exit-mechanismen / Exit Mechanisms

### 8.1 Uitstapstrategieen / Exit Routes

| Route | Dutch Term | Typical Timing | Considerations |
|---|---|---|---|
| Trade sale | Verkoop aan derde | 3-7 years post-COD | Auction (veiling) process; EV/EBITDA multiples |
| Secondary sale | Secundaire verkoop | 5-10 years | Infrastructure fund lifecycle; LP secondaries market |
| Refinancing + recap | Herfinanciering + dividend recap | Post-stabilization (2-5 years) | Cash-out via dividend recapitalization; partial exit |
| IPO | Beursgang | Rare for single asset | Platform/portfolio IPO more common; AEX or Euronext |
| Put/call options | Put/call opties | Per SHA triggers | Pre-agreed valuation mechanism; fair value or formula |
| Management buyout | Management buy-out | Varies | Developer buys out institutional investor or vice versa |
| Asset sale (vs. share sale) | Activa-verkoop | Varies | Tax considerations; asset deal vs. share deal |

## 10. Belastingaspecten / Tax Aspects of Equity Structuring

### 10.1 Key Dutch Tax Considerations

| Aspect | Treatment | Reference |
|---|---|---|
| Deelnemingsvrijstelling (participation exemption) | Dividends and capital gains exempt if 5%+ shareholding | Art. 13 Wet VPB 1969 |
| Fiscale eenheid (tax unity) | Consolidated taxation if 95%+ ownership | Art. 15 Wet VPB 1969 |
| Thin capitalization | No statutory thin cap rules (repealed 2013); but ATAD interest limitation applies | ATAD implementation |
| ATAD interest limitation | Net interest deduction limited to 20% of EBITDA or EUR 1M | Art. 15b Wet VPB 1969 |
| Infrastructure project exemption | Infrastructure projects may qualify for ATAD exemption | Art. 15b(6) Wet VPB 1969 |
| Withholding tax on dividends | 15% (reduced by treaty or EU Parent-Subsidiary Directive) | Wet DB 1965 |
| Conditional withholding tax | 25.8% on interest/royalties to low-tax jurisdictions | Wet bronbelasting 2021 |
| Transfer pricing | Arm's length standard for related-party transactions | Art. 8b Wet VPB 1969 |
| CIT rate | 19% (first EUR 200K) / 25.8% (excess) - 2024/2025 rates | Wet VPB 1969 |

### 10.2 Equity Structuring Tax Optimization

| Structure | Tax Benefit | Consideration |
|---|---|---|
| Holding BV above SPV | Deelnemingsvrijstelling on dividends/gains | Substance requirements |
| Cooperative (cooperatie) | No dividend withholding tax | Anti-abuse provisions; substance |
| CV/BV structure | Tax transparency for foreign investors | Hybrid mismatch rules (ATAD2) |
| Shareholder loan vs. equity | Interest deductibility (subject to ATAD) | Recharacterization risk |
| ATAD infra exemption | Full interest deductibility for qualifying projects | Long-term public infrastructure |

[Source: Belastingdienst; Loyens & Loeff; Meijburg & Co; ATAD Directive (EU) 2016/1164]
