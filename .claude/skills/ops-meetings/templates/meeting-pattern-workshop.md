# Pattern: Workshop

## Purpose

Deep collaborative work. Problem-solving, design, planning, or technical deep-dives that require focused, uninterrupted group thinking. Workshops produce an artifact — a document, a design, a decision framework, a plan.

Use this pattern when:
- A problem needs more than 10 minutes of group discussion (too long for a Decision meeting)
- Multiple perspectives are needed to solve it
- The output is a tangible artifact, not just alignment
- A Decision meeting surfaced a topic that wasn't ready — the Workshop does the pre-work

Do NOT use this pattern when:
- The decision is already clear and just needs sign-off (use Decision)
- You need status updates (use Standup)
- You need to inform people (use Broadcast)
- One person can do the work alone — don't workshop what should be an assignment

## Format

1. **Frame** — facilitator states the problem, the desired output artifact, and the time available.
2. **Pre-read review** — confirm everyone read the background material. If not, give 5 minutes to skim. Don't waste the group re-explaining what's in the doc.
3. **Working session** — the actual collaborative work. Structure depends on the problem:
   - Diverge → Converge (brainstorm options, then narrow)
   - Walk-through → Critique (present a draft, then improve it)
   - Analysis → Synthesis (examine data, then draw conclusions)
4. **Artifact check** — is the output artifact complete? If not, what's missing and who finishes it?
5. **Decision handoff** — if the workshop produces options for a decision, package them for the next Decision meeting. Don't make the decision in the workshop unless all decision-makers are present.

## Duration

60+ minutes. No artificial time cap — substance matters more than the clock. But check progress at the halfway mark. If you're going in circles, the problem isn't well-framed.

## Frequency

As needed. Workshops are scheduled from Decision or Standup meetings when a topic surfaces that needs depth. They are not recurring — each one has a specific purpose and ends when the artifact is done.

## Key Rules

1. **Never more than 6 people.** Every additional person reduces productive contribution. If 10 people "need to be there," the problem isn't scoped tightly enough.
2. **Everyone contributes.** No spectators. If someone is in the room, they're expected to work. If they're just listening, they can read the output.
3. **Output artifact required.** A workshop without a written output is a discussion that could have been an email. Define the artifact before the meeting starts.
4. **Pre-read material required.** Don't waste group time bringing people up to speed. Background context is a pre-read. The workshop is for working, not presenting.
5. **Facilitator controls scope.** When the conversation drifts, the facilitator pulls it back: "That's important but not what we're solving today. Let's note it and stay focused."
6. **No decisions without decision-makers.** If the workshop produces options, package them for the Decision meeting. Making decisions without the right people creates rework.

## When This Meeting Drifts

- **The group converges on a decision prematurely** → "Are the right decision-makers here? If yes, decide. If not, package the options for the Decision meeting."
- **Scope creeps to adjacent problems** → "That's related but not what we're solving today. I'll note it. Back to the artifact."
- **One person dominates** → "I want to hear from everyone. [Name], what's your take?" Rotate input deliberately.
- **The halfway check reveals no progress** → "We're spinning. Let me reframe the problem. Are we solving the right question?"

## Run-of-Show

| Segment | Time | Who | What |
|---------|------|-----|------|
| Frame | 5 min | Facilitator | Problem statement. Desired output. Time check. |
| Pre-read check | 5 min | Facilitator | Confirm prep. Quick skim window if needed. |
| Working session | 40-90 min | Group | Structured collaborative work. Facilitator keeps focus. |
| Halfway check | 2 min | Facilitator | Are we on track? Reframe if circling. |
| Artifact review | 10 min | Group | Is the output complete? What's missing? |
| Decision handoff | 5 min | Facilitator | Package options for Decision meeting, or decide if able. |

**Total: 67-117 minutes** (flex based on problem complexity)

## Quality Check

After the meeting, ask:
1. Is there a written artifact? If not, the workshop failed its primary purpose.
2. Did everyone contribute meaningfully? If two people did all the talking, the group was too large or the problem wasn't collaborative.
3. Was the scope maintained? If the conversation wandered into three different topics, the facilitator didn't control it.
4. Were more than 6 people present? If yes, question whether everyone needed to be there.
5. Did it produce inputs for a Decision meeting? If the workshop was about making choices, the handoff should be clean: here are the options, here's the analysis, here's the recommendation.
