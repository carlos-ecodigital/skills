# Pattern: Review

## Purpose

Evaluate past performance against targets. Grade honestly. Decide what to adjust, continue, or kill. Reviews look backward to inform forward decisions.

Use this pattern when:
- A defined period has ended (sprint, month, quarter) and results need to be scored
- Targets were set and now need to be measured
- Underperformance needs to be diagnosed and addressed
- Resources need to be reallocated based on what's working and what isn't

Do NOT use this pattern when:
- You need to make a single decision (use Decision)
- You need to explore or solve a problem (use Workshop)
- You need to share direction or context (use Broadcast)
- Results aren't in yet — don't review what you can't measure

## Format

1. **Scorecard presentation** — RED / AMBER / GREEN for each objective or KPI. Numbers, not narratives. The scorecard is distributed as a pre-read.
2. **GREEN items** — one line each. Acknowledge, move on. Do not let people present their wins at length.
3. **AMBER items** — brief diagnosis. Is it trending toward RED or GREEN? What's the intervention?
4. **RED items** — deep-dive. Root cause. Options. Decision: fix, adjust target, or kill.
5. **Kill/adjust decisions** — explicitly decide what to stop doing, what to change, what to double down on.
6. **Set next targets** — based on what you learned, set targets for the next period.
7. **Close** — summarize decisions and new targets.

Time goes to REDs. If you spend most of the meeting on GREEN items, you're celebrating instead of managing.

## Duration

30-90 minutes. 30 min for a sprint review. 60-90 min for monthly or quarterly reviews. Duration scales with the number of RED items, not the number of objectives.

## Frequency

Monthly, quarterly, or per-sprint. Match the review cadence to the target-setting cadence.

## Key Rules

1. **No presenting good news at length.** GREEN gets one line. Time is for REDs and decisions.
2. **Scorecard is a pre-read.** Everyone walks in having seen the numbers. The meeting is for diagnosis and decisions, not data presentation.
3. **Honest grading.** AMBER is not "almost GREEN." AMBER is "at risk." RED is "failed or will fail without intervention." If everything is GREEN, either targets were too easy or grading is dishonest.
4. **Every RED requires a decision.** Diagnosis without action is a waste of the room's time. Fix it, adjust the target, or kill the initiative.
5. **Kill things.** The hardest part of a review is stopping work that isn't delivering. If you never kill anything, your reviews aren't working.
6. **Forward-looking targets are mandatory.** A review that only looks backward is an autopsy. Set the next period's targets before leaving.

## When This Meeting Drifts

- **Someone presents a GREEN item at length** → "That's great — one line in the scorecard. Let's get to the REDs."
- **A RED item turns into a brainstorming session** → "We're in Workshop mode. Do we need a Workshop to fix this, or can we decide the intervention now?"
- **The group debates targets instead of reviewing results** → "Targets come after we've graded. Let's finish scoring, then set next period."
- **No one wants to grade honestly (everything AMBER)** → "AMBER means at risk. Is this genuinely at risk, or is it RED and we're being polite? Let's call it."

## Run-of-Show

| Segment | Time | Who | What |
|---------|------|-----|------|
| Scorecard overview | 5 min | Facilitator | Show RED/AMBER/GREEN summary. Pre-read assumed. |
| GREEN items | 5 min | Owners (1 line each) | Acknowledge. Move on. |
| AMBER items | 10-15 min | Owners | Trending which way? Intervention needed? |
| RED deep-dives | 15-40 min | Owners + Group | Root cause → Options → Decision (fix/adjust/kill) |
| Kill/adjust decisions | 5-10 min | Leader | What stops? What changes? What doubles down? |
| Next period targets | 5-10 min | Leader + Group | Set measurable targets for next period |
| Close | 3 min | Facilitator | Recap decisions and new targets |

**Total: 48-88 minutes** (flex based on RED count)

## Quality Check

After the meeting, ask:
1. Was every RED item resolved with a decision? If any RED left without an action, the review was incomplete.
2. Did GREEN items take more than 5 minutes total? If yes, the meeting drifted into celebration mode.
3. Was anything killed or significantly adjusted? If not, the review may have lacked courage.
4. Are next period's targets set and measurable? If vague ("do better"), the target-setting failed.
5. Was the scorecard honest? If everything is GREEN, question the calibration.
