# Pattern: Decision

## Purpose

A small group makes pre-identified choices. Every item on the agenda has a decision to be made. You walk in with options; you walk out with commitments.

Use this pattern when:
- Specific decisions are blocked and need resolution
- Pre-work has been done: options identified, trade-offs analyzed, recommendations prepared
- The right decision-makers are in the room

Do NOT use this pattern when:
- You need to explore a problem space (use Workshop)
- The group hasn't done pre-work — no options, no analysis, no recommendation (reschedule)
- You need status updates from everyone (use Standup or async)
- You need to inform a large group (use Broadcast)

## Format

1. **Agenda review** — confirm the decisions on the table. Cut anything that isn't ready.
2. **Per decision item:**
   a. Owner presents: context (30 sec), options (1 min), recommendation (30 sec)
   b. Discussion: clarifying questions, concerns, alternatives
   c. Decision: the decision-maker decides. Record it.
   d. Commitment: "Disagree and commit" — once decided, everyone rows together
3. **Recap** — read back all decisions made. Confirm owners and deadlines.

Every agenda item has: a decision owner, pre-prepared options, a recommendation, and a time box. If any of these are missing, the item is not ready for this meeting.

## Duration

30-60 minutes. Each decision item gets 5-10 minutes max. If it needs more than 10 minutes, it's not ready — schedule a Workshop to do the pre-work.

## Frequency

Weekly or as needed. Weekly for recurring decision backlogs. Ad-hoc for urgent decisions that can't wait.

## Key Rules

1. **No item enters without pre-prepared options.** "We need to discuss X" is not a decision agenda item. "We need to choose between A, B, or C for X" is.
2. **No item without a decision owner.** Someone is accountable for presenting the options and implementing the outcome.
3. **Time-boxed.** Each item gets a fixed number of minutes. When time's up, either decide or defer to a Workshop.
4. **Disagree and commit.** Discussion happens here. Lobbying after the meeting is not allowed. Once the room decides, it's decided.
5. **No spectators.** Everyone in the room should have a stake in at least one decision. If you're just observing, read the minutes instead.
6. **Pre-read required.** Decision briefs distributed 24+ hours before the meeting. If you haven't read the brief, you listen — you don't slow down the group.

## Run-of-Show

| Segment | Time | Who | What |
|---------|------|-----|------|
| Agenda check | 2 min | Facilitator | Confirm items. Cut anything not ready. |
| Decision item 1 | 5-10 min | Owner | Context → Options → Rec → Discussion → Decision |
| Decision item 2 | 5-10 min | Owner | Context → Options → Rec → Discussion → Decision |
| Decision item 3 | 5-10 min | Owner | Context → Options → Rec → Discussion → Decision |
| (repeat as needed) | 5-10 min each | Owners | Same structure |
| Recap | 3-5 min | Facilitator | Read back all decisions, owners, deadlines |

**Total: 30-60 minutes** (3-6 decision items typical)

## Quality Check

After the meeting, ask:
1. Did every agenda item result in a clear decision? If any item ended with "let's think about it more," the pre-work was insufficient.
2. Were options pre-prepared for every item? If someone presented a problem without options, the process failed upstream.
3. Did any item take more than 10 minutes? If yes, it wasn't ready — should have been a Workshop first.
4. Can you state each decision in one sentence? If not, the decision wasn't crisp enough.
5. Does every decision have an owner and a deadline? If not, it will drift.
