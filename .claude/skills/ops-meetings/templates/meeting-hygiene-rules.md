# Digital Energy Meeting Rules

*Applies to ALL recurring internal meetings.*

---

## Before the Meeting

1. **Read the pre-read / agenda.** If there is one, read it. If you show up unprepared, you can't participate in decisions.
2. **Test your audio.** Before the meeting starts, not during. If it doesn't work, fix it and rejoin. The meeting does not pause for you.
3. **Join 1 minute early.** The meeting starts at the scheduled time regardless of who has joined.

## During the Meeting

4. **Camera ON.** Non-negotiable for meetings with <20 people. Energy doesn't transmit through black screens. Exceptions: poor bandwidth (notify the group).
5. **Mute when not speaking.** Auto-mute on entry. Unmute only when you have something to contribute.
6. **No echo.** If you're in the same room as another participant, use one device only.
7. **No side conversations.** If something needs a separate discussion, say "let's take this offline" and schedule it.
8. **Stick to the timebox.** Each agenda item has a time allocation. When time's up, the facilitator moves on. Unfinished items get a separate session.

## After the Meeting

9. **No WhatsApp for meeting content.** Action items, files, and decisions go to ClickUp or email. WhatsApp is for "I'm running 2 min late" only.
10. **Action items have owners and deadlines.** "We should look into X" is not an action item. "[Name] will deliver [X] by [day]" is.

## Hard Limits

11. **Substance over clock.** Remote teams need real time together. The test isn't duration — it's whether every segment produced a decision, unblocked someone, or built shared understanding that couldn't happen async. After each meeting, check: did we produce tangible outputs (decisions, action items, resolved blockers)? If not, the meeting needed better prep, not more time.
12. **If you're not adding value, leave.** It is not rude to leave a meeting when you can't contribute. It is rude to make someone stay and waste their time.
13. **Meetings are for decisions. Updates are for writing.** If something can be communicated in a 5-line email, it should be.
