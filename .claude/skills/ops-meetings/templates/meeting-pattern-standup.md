# Pattern: Standup

## Purpose

Rapid-fire execution tracking. Surface blockers fast. Keep the team synchronized on who's doing what today. No strategy, no deep discussion, no problem-solving.

Use this pattern when:
- A team is in active execution mode and needs daily or near-daily sync
- Blockers need to surface quickly so they can be resolved offline
- The team needs to know what everyone else is working on to avoid collisions

Do NOT use this pattern when:
- You need to solve a problem (use Workshop)
- You need to make a decision (use Decision)
- You need strategic alignment (use Broadcast)
- The team is <3 people (just talk to each other)

## Format

1. **Dashboard or board review** — look at the shared tracker (Kanban, pipeline, CRM, sprint board). Numbers first, not narratives.
2. **Round-robin** — each person, 2-3 minutes max:
   - What I completed since last standup
   - What I'm working on today
   - What's blocking me (one sentence — no solutions here)
3. **Blocker triage** — facilitator notes blockers. Assigns offline resolution. Does NOT solve them in the meeting.
4. **Close** — any urgent announcements (<30 seconds each). Done.

The facilitator's job is to cut people off. Politely, firmly, consistently. "Great — let's take that offline."

## Duration

15 minutes maximum. Hard stop. If it takes longer, either you have too many people or someone is presenting instead of reporting.

## Frequency

Daily or 2-3x per week. Daily during sprints or high-intensity execution. 2-3x/week for steady-state operations.

## Key Rules

1. **No strategy discussion.** Strategy happens in Broadcast or Decision meetings. This is execution tracking.
2. **No rabbit holes.** If a topic takes >30 seconds to explain, it's a sidebar — schedule it offline.
3. **Blockers get one sentence, not a debate.** "I'm blocked on X because of Y. Need Z to unblock." That's it. Solutions happen offline.
4. **2-3 minutes per person, enforced.** The facilitator cuts off at 3 minutes. No exceptions. No "just one more thing."
5. **Standing up is optional, brevity is not.** The name is a metaphor for speed. Whether you physically stand is irrelevant.
6. **Skip if nothing changed.** If someone has no updates and no blockers, they say "No changes" and pass. Don't fill time.

## Run-of-Show

| Segment | Time | Who | What |
|---------|------|-----|------|
| Board review | 2-3 min | Facilitator | Key numbers. Pipeline state. Anything red. |
| Round-robin | 8-10 min | Each person | Done / Doing / Blocked. 2-3 min each. |
| Blocker triage | 2-3 min | Facilitator | Note blockers. Assign offline owners. |
| Close | 1 min | Facilitator | Urgent announcements. Hard stop. |

**Total: 13-17 minutes** (cap at 15)

## Quality Check

After the meeting, ask:
1. Did it finish in 15 minutes? If not, the facilitator didn't enforce time boxes.
2. Were blockers surfaced? If the team says "no blockers" every day, either execution is perfect (unlikely) or people aren't being honest.
3. Were blockers resolved in the meeting? If yes, the facilitator failed — resolution happens offline.
4. Did anyone present instead of report? "Let me walk you through..." is presenting. "I did X, doing Y, blocked on Z" is reporting.
5. Does the team know what everyone else is doing today? If not, the sync failed.
