# Digital Energy -- Competitive Positioning

How Digital Energy compares to the alternatives each buyer persona would consider. Use this to frame DE's differentiation in all marketing materials.

---

## Positioning Framework

**Core question (per April Dunford):** "What would our buyers do if we didn't exist?"

The answer differs by segment. DE doesn't compete head-to-head with any single category — it sits at an intersection that no incumbent fully occupies.

---

## 1. Grower Alternatives

| Alternative | What It Is | Grower Gets | Grower Gives Up | DE Advantage |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Status quo (natural gas)** | Continue gas-fired heating via existing WKK or ketels | Familiar, no change, no commitment | Exposed to gas price volatility, ETS2 cost increase, no future-proofing | DE eliminates gas dependency; zero CAPEX from grower; CPI-indexed pricing vs. commodity-linked |
| **Geothermal (aardwarmte)** | Drill geothermal well on or near property | Very low marginal heat cost once operational | EUR 10-25M CAPEX; 3-5 year development; geological risk (failed wells); requires own financing | DE: zero CAPEX from grower, faster deployment, no geological risk |
| **Biomass boiler** | Wood chips or pellet combustion | Renewable heat source | Fuel supply chain risk; emission permits; storage space; higher O&M | DE: no fuel procurement, no emissions, no permits for grower |
| **Heat pump (electricity)** | Electric heat pump using grid power | Electrification; potential subsidy | High electricity cost at scale; grid capacity needed for heat pump; CAPEX | DE provides heat directly — no grower CAPEX or electricity consumption |
| **Do nothing** | Wait and see | No commitment | Gas costs rise (ETS2); competitive disadvantage vs. early adopters; grid connection depreciates | DE turns idle grid capacity into revenue; early movers get priority in pipeline |

**Category frame:** DE positions as **digital cogeneration** (digitale cogeneratie) — the successor to gas-fired CHP. Same dual-output principle (energy input → two products), fundamentally different technology (compute replaces gas combustion). Growers who had WKK understand this instantly.

**Key differentiator for growers:** DE is the only digital cogeneration option that requires zero capital from the grower, uses their existing grid connection productively via colocation and cable pooling, and produces both AI compute revenue and thermal energy. The grower's existing CHP/e-boiler assets are freed from heat duty, becoming available for grid balancing (mFRR, aFRR) — turning an energy cost into an energy revenue.

## 2. District Heating Alternatives

| Alternative | What It Is | Utility Gets | Utility Gives Up | DE Advantage |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Industrial waste heat (existing)** | Purchase heat from factories, refineries | Large volume, established contracts | Single-source dependency; supply risk if industry closes/relocates; commodity-linked pricing | DE: 24/7 baseload from AI compute (always running); multi-decade infrastructure; CPI pricing |
| **Geothermal** | Deep geothermal wells | Very low marginal cost | EUR 10-25M per well; geological risk; 3-5 year development | DE: zero drilling risk; faster timeline; complementary source |
| **Biomass CHP** | Biomass combustion for heat and power | Dual revenue (heat + electricity) | Fuel supply risk; emission permits; SDE++ dependency; political opposition | DE: no combustion, no emissions, no fuel chain |
| **Large-scale heat pump** | Electric heat pump at district scale | Flexible source | Massive grid capacity needed; electricity cost; CAPEX | DE: zero additional grid for heat; recycled thermal energy is free input |
| **Gas-fired central heating** | Natural gas boilers for network | Low CAPEX, familiar | Wcw compliance risk; ETS2 exposure; end-of-life technology | DE: future-proof, regulatory-aligned, carbon-free |

**Key differentiator for district heating:** DE provides baseload recycled thermal energy (AI compute runs 24/7/365) at cost-based pricing, aligned with Wcw reform, with no geological or combustion risk.

## 3. Neocloud / Enterprise Alternatives

| Alternative | What It Is | Buyer Gets | Buyer Gives Up | DE Advantage |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Hyperscaler cloud (AWS/Azure/GCP)** | Public cloud GPU instances | Global scale, managed services, instant provisioning | Premium pricing (2-5x dedicated); no data sovereignty guarantee; vendor lock-in; queue for GPUs | DE: 30-50% lower cost at scale; EU data sovereignty; no lock-in; dedicated capacity |
| **Nordic colocation (Sweden/Finland)** | Low-cost power in Scandinavia | Cheap electricity (EUR 20-40/MWh); green energy | High latency to major European hubs; limited network connectivity; 2-3 year build timeline | DE: AMS-IX proximity, sub-2ms to Frankfurt/London, immediate grid access, faster deployment |
| **Frankfurt/Amsterdam incumbent DC** | Equinix, Digital Realty, CyrusOne | Established facilities, interconnection | Amsterdam moratorium limits new capacity; high cost ($200+/kW/month); limited liquid cooling readiness | DE: new-build liquid cooling native; sub-hyperscale permits; heat revenue subsidizes operating costs |
| **Self-build** | Develop own data center facility | Full control | 3-5 year timeline; EUR 8-15M/MW CAPEX; grid connection wait; permitting complexity | DE: secured grid, permitted sites, operational in 12-18 months |
| **Edge / on-premises** | GPU servers in own facility | Lowest latency, full control | Power and cooling constraints; no scale; high per-unit cost | DE: high-density liquid cooling; scalable; professional operations |

**Key differentiator for neoclouds/enterprises:** DE offers secured grid capacity in a market where power is the bottleneck, with new-build liquid cooling (40-140 kW/rack), at faster timelines than self-build, and energy recycling revenue that subsidizes operating costs.

## 4. Industrial Heat Alternatives

| Alternative | What It Is | Buyer Gets | Buyer Gives Up | DE Advantage |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Natural gas boilers** | Conventional gas-fired steam/hot water | Proven, reliable, scalable | Gas price volatility; ETS2 carbon costs from 2027; no ESG improvement | DE: zero-carbon heat; CPI-indexed pricing; ESG-reportable reduction |
| **Electric boiler** | Direct electric heating | Simple electrification | Massive grid capacity; high electricity cost at scale | DE: no additional grid draw for heat; recycled energy is free feedstock |

| **Hydrogen boiler** | Green H2 combustion | Zero-carbon, high-temp | Hydrogen cost and availability uncertain; infrastructure immature | DE: available now; proven technology; no hydrogen supply chain dependency |

**Key differentiator for industrial heat:** DE provides zero-carbon, cost-stable recycled thermal energy today (not 2030+), requiring zero CAPEX from the buyer, with measurable ESG impact for reporting.

## DE's Unique Position — Why No Direct Competitor Exists

No single company in the Netherlands combines:
1. **Secured grid connections** in congested zones (scarcity asset)
2. **AI Factory / DEC development** capability (compute revenue)
3. **Energy recycling** to adjacent users (thermal production revenue)
4. **BESS co-location** for revenue stacking (grid services revenue)
5. **Non-recourse project finance** structuring (institutional capital access)
6. **Dutch regulatory expertise** (Omgevingswet, Energiewet, Wcw, cable pooling)

Traditional data center developers don't recycle energy. Heat companies don't do compute. BESS developers don't integrate with data centers. Project finance advisors don't develop assets. DE does all six as a vertically integrated digital cogeneration platform.
