# Digital Energy -- Channel-Specific Tone Adaptations

The general voice rules (see `voice-rules.md`) apply everywhere. These channel-specific adaptations override or extend them for specific contexts.

| Channel | Tone Adjustment | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **LinkedIn (executive)** | Authoritative but conversational. First-person from CEO/founders. Short paragraphs. One insight per post. | "We just signed our third grower in Zuid-Holland. Here's what surprised us about the process..." |
| **LinkedIn (company)** | Professional, third-person. Data-led. | "Digital Energy has secured 25.5 MW of grid capacity in Noord-Holland, sufficient for ~21.25 MW of AI compute." |
| **Cold email** | Direct, respectful of time, problem-first. No fluff. Under 150 words body. | "Your greenhouse consumes [X] m3 gas annually. We can replace 60-80% of that with recycled thermal energy from co-located AI compute." |
| **Investor materials** | Precise, conservative, three-scenario. No superlatives. | Use "indicative," "subject to definitive agreements," "base case." |
| **Grower-facing (NL)** | Bilingual Dutch/English. Simple. Concrete. Empathetic to their concerns. | "Uw gasrekening kan met 60-80% omlaag. Wij plaatsen een Digital Energy Center op uw terrein — digitale cogeneratie: compute én warmte uit één energiebron." |
| **Conference/speaking** | Bold claims backed by specific data. Story-driven. | Lead with the problem (grid scarcity, stranded heat), show the solution through a specific project story. |
| **Press releases** | Standard inverted pyramid. Boilerplate at end. Quote from CEO. | Factual, no superlatives, include deal metrics. |
| **Technical/neocloud** | High technical density. Infrastructure jargon acceptable. PUE, kW/rack, NVLink, liquid cooling. | "40-140 kW/rack density with direct liquid cooling. PUE target 1.2. Available H2 2027." |
