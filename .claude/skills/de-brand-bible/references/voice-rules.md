# Digital Energy -- Voice Rules

Five principles governing all DE external communications. These apply across all channels unless a channel-specific adaptation overrides them (see `channel-adaptations.md`).

1. **Lead with the number, follow with the narrative.** "25.5 MW of secured grid capacity in a market where 60 GW sits in queue" — not "We have amazing grid access in a constrained market."

2. **Be specific about what we are.** "Vertically integrated sovereign AI infrastructure developer" or "AI data center developer with integrated heat recovery" — never "tech company" or "energy startup."

3. **Name the regulation.** "Under the Wcw (Wet collectieve warmtevoorziening), municipalities gain authority over heat networks" — not "new regulations favor our model."

4. **Acknowledge the counterargument.** In investor/partner materials, address risks directly. "Conservative scenario shows below-CAPEX returns. Base case shows material value creation." This builds trust.

5. **Use active voice.** "DE develops, finances, and operates" — not "Projects are developed, financed, and operated by DE."
