# Digital Energy -- Terminology Standards

## Preferred Terms

| Preferred Term | Avoid | Context |
|---|---|---|
| Digital Energy Center (DEC) | — | Primary name for DE installations. Always use when naming our facilities. |
| AI Factory | — | Known-audience shorthand for DEC. Use when audience knows NVIDIA's "AI Factory" concept — website, neocloud, enterprise materials. |
| Data center (comparative only) | Using "data center" for DE facilities | Only when (a) differentiating DE from traditional DCs ("Unlike traditional data centers..."), (b) regulatory/zoning contexts where the legal term applies, (c) explaining to cold audiences who need the familiar term first. Never use as a standalone label for DE. |
| Energy recycling / energierecycling | Waste heat, excess heat, surplus heat, heat recovery | The process of capturing and upgrading compute thermal output into usable heat. Replaces "waste heat" in all forward-looking content. "Waste heat" may appear in technical contexts when describing raw server output before processing, but never as DE's value proposition. |
| Thermal production | Heat recovery, heat capture | What the Thermal Factory delivers — upgraded, usable heat at 70-80°C. Distinguish from energy recycling: recycling = process, thermal production = output. |
| Digital cogeneration / digitale cogeneratie | Waste heat supplier, heat provider | DE's model for growers: CHC is the digital successor to CHP (gas cogeneration). Compute + heat from one energy input. Primary positioning category for growers — maps to their CHP experience. |
| Colocation / co-locatie | "Build on", "install at" | The physical arrangement of DEC on grower/industrial land. Use alongside "digital cogeneration" for growers. Echoes "cable pooling" — dual-use of infrastructure. |
| Super-Factory | — | The federated network of DECs behaving as one sovereign compute fabric. Use in investor, strategic, and scale-narrative contexts. Definition: "a distributed network of DECs operating as a unified AI infrastructure platform." |
| Grower | Farmer, client, customer | Dutch greenhouse operators |
| Energy Partner | Investor, partner, stakeholder | Grid-rights holders like Lodewijk |
| Grid connection / netaansluiting | Power supply, electricity access | The secured utility connection |
| ProjectBV | SPV, project vehicle | Dutch BV holding each project (use ProjectBV in external materials) |
| Sovereign AI | Local AI, national AI | European-controlled AI compute infrastructure |
| Revenue stacking | Multiple income streams | BESS: arbitrage + FCR + aFRR combined |
| Transportschaarste | Grid congestion (acceptable in EN materials) | TenneT's formal designation for capacity scarcity |

## Framing Rules

| Use | Avoid | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Permitting by design / permit fast-track | Zoning arbitrage, zoning bypass, zoning workaround, agricultural zoning bypass | "Arbitrage" implies exploitation. DECs qualify for agricultural/energy infrastructure zoning because they ARE energy infrastructure serving agriculture. This is the system working as intended, not a loophole. |
| "Cogeneration is the most efficient way to produce useful energy" | — | Foundational thermodynamic claim. CHP proved it for gas. CHC proves it for digital. Should appear in any explanation of the CHC model. |
| "Energy maker, not taker" | "Green data center", "sustainable data center" | Rejects the data center category entirely. DE produces energy value, not consumes it. |
| "Manufactured, not constructed" | "Built" (when emphasizing speed/modularity) | Implies industrial process, not construction project. Use for DEC deployment narrative. |

## Visual Identity Notes

- **Primary color:** Not yet formally defined. Use deep navy/dark blue with energy-green accents in digital materials.
- **Logo usage:** Digital Energy wordmark. No icon-only usage in formal materials.
- **Document format:** All formal proposals use structured markdown with numbered sections, tables for key data, and clear hierarchy (H1 -> H2 -> H3).
- **Chart/graph style:** Clean, minimal, data-forward. No decorative elements. Label every axis. Source every chart.
