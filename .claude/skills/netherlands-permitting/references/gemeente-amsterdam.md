# Gemeente Amsterdam: Data Center & Energy Project Permitting

## 1. Municipal Authority

### College van B&W (Mayor and Executive Board)
- Bevoegd gezag (competent authority) for omgevingsvergunningen (environmental permits) within Amsterdam
- Delegates execution to directie Ruimte en Duurzaamheid (Spatial Planning and Sustainability directorate)
- Datacenterbeleid (data center policy) adopted by gemeenteraad (municipal council), executed by B&W

### Omgevingsdienst Noordzeekanaalgebied (OD NZKG)
- Executes Bal/Bkl enforcement in Amsterdam, Zaanstad, Haarlemmermeer on behalf of gemeenten
- Mandated for milieubelastende activiteiten (environmentally impactful activities): geluid (noise), luchtkwaliteit (air quality), bodem (soil)
- Conducts AERIUS-beoordelingen (AERIUS assessments) and advises on stikstof (nitrogen)

### Veiligheidsregio Amsterdam-Amstelland
- Brandveiligheid (fire safety) advice for large buildings
- BESS-installaties (battery storage installations): PGS 37 assessment
- Noodstroomaggregaten (emergency generators): advies over veiligheidsafstanden (safety distance advice)

## 2. Datacenterbeleid Amsterdam (Data Center Policy)

### Historical Timeline
- **2019:** Amsterdam (and Haarlemmermeer) imposed one-year vestigingsstop (moratorium) on new datacenters
- **2020:** Moratorium lifted; replaced by Beleidskader Datacenters (Data Center Policy Framework)
- **2020-2024:** Restrictive permitting continued with restwarmte (waste heat) and sustainability conditions
- **January 2026:** Controversy — Microsoft obtained permits for datacenter complex split into three separate aanvragen (applications), each individually below hyperscale threshold. Renewed political debate.

### Current Policy Framework (2025-2026)

**Locatiebeleid (Location Policy):**
- New datacenters only in aangewezen bedrijventerreinen (designated industrial areas):
  - **Westpoort:** Primary datacenter zone (Equinix, Digital Realty, CyrusOne present)
  - **Sloterdijk:** Limited remaining capacity
  - **Amstel III / Zuidoost:** Emerging opportunity area (former kantoorlocatie / office area)
- NOT permitted in: woongebieden (residential areas), gemengd gebruik (mixed-use zones), groengebieden (green areas), or havengebied (port area) unless specifically designated

**Omvangsbeperkingen (Size Restrictions):**
- Policy discourages hyperscale datacenters (> 10 ha / > 70 MW)
- Applications will be tested against proportionaliteitsbeginsel (proportionality principle)
- Cumulative impact assessment: total energy/heat/water footprint of all datacenters in area considered

**Duurzaamheidseisen (Sustainability Requirements):**
- Restwarmtebenutting (waste heat utilization): mandatory plan for making warmte available
- PUE target: < 1.2 for new facilities (above national minimum)
- Waterefficiency (water efficiency): WUE reporting, preference for droge koeling (dry cooling)
- Groene stroom (green electricity): 100% hernieuwbare energie (renewable energy) requirement or credible transition plan
- Circulair bouwen (circular construction): material passport, recycled materials targets
- Beeldkwaliteit (visual quality): architectural design review, landschappelijke inpassing (landscape integration)

### Omgevingsplan Amsterdam
- Transitional omgevingsplan incorporates former bestemmingsplannen (zoning plans)
- Datacenters classified within bedrijfsbestemming (business/industrial zoning)
- Milieucategorie (environmental category) requirements per zone
- Beeldkwaliteitsplan (visual quality plan): gemeente can impose additional architectural requirements
- Verkeersafwikkeling (traffic management): construction traffic plan required for large developments

## 3. Westpoort Warmte (Waste Heat Network)

### Structure
- Joint venture: Vattenfall (operator) + Gemeente Amsterdam
- Warmtenet (heat network) connecting Westpoort industrial area to woonwijken (residential areas)
- Sources: afvalverbrandingsinstallatie (waste incineration plant) AEB, datacenter restwarmte, potentially others
- Temperature: hogere temperatuur (higher temperature) network (~90°C) and lagere temperatuur (lower temperature) expansion planned

### Datacenter Integration
- Existing datacenters in Westpoort connected or in negotiation for warmtelevering (heat delivery)
- Typical DC waste heat: 25-40°C — requires warmtepomp (heat pump) for network-compatible temperature
- Amsterdam requires new datacenters to commit to warmtelevering before vergunningverlening (permit issuance)
- Warmteleveringsovereenkomst (heat supply agreement): standardized framework being developed
- Wcw (Wet collectieve warmte / Collective Heat Act, from mid-2026): Amsterdam's warmtekavel system may formalize obligations

### DEC Relevance
- Amsterdam's warmtenet infrastructure creates demand for DC waste heat
- BUT: DEC's co-location model (DC + greenhouse) is less relevant in Amsterdam context — few greenhouses in Amsterdam
- Amsterdam is primarily a neocloud/enterprise customer location, not a DEC co-location site
- Lessons from Amsterdam warmte integration applicable to DEC warmtenet design elsewhere

## 4. Stikstof (Nitrogen) in Amsterdam

### Proximity to Natura 2000
- Ilperveld, Varkensland, Oostzanerveld: directly north of Amsterdam
- Kennemerland-Zuid: west of Amsterdam
- Markermeer & IJmeer: east of Amsterdam
- High achtergrondbelasting (background load) means even small NOx additions trigger AERIUS concerns

### Practical Impact
- Diesel noodstroomaggregaten (emergency generators): NOx emissions often > 0.00 mol/ha/jaar
- Bouwfase (construction phase): werktuigen (construction equipment), vrachtverkeer (truck traffic)
- Operational fase: generator testing hours, verkeersbewegingen (traffic movements)
- Amsterdam applies strict intern salderen (internal offsetting) approach
- Many projects require extern salderen (external offsetting) or ADC-toets

## 5. Building Permits (Bbl)

### Gevolgklasse (Consequence Class) for Datacenters
- Most datacenters: gevolgklasse 2 or 3 (due to size, height, structural complexity)
- Gevolgklasse 3: verplichte omgevingsvergunning bouwtechnische activiteit (mandatory building permit for technical construction activity)
- Gevolgklasse 2: potentially omgevingsvergunning required depending on risicoprofiel (risk profile)
- Wkb (Wet kwaliteitsborging / Quality Assurance Act): kwaliteitsborger (quality assurer) required for gevolgklasse 1 only; gevolgklasse 2/3 uses traditional vergunningsprocedure (permit procedure)

### Welstandscommissie (Architectural Review Committee)
- Amsterdam has active welstandscommissie reviewing exterior design
- Beeldkwaliteitsplan (visual quality plan) per stadsdeel (district) or bedrijventerrein
- Relevant for datacenter facade, omheining (fencing), landschappelijke inpassing
- Industrial zones have relaxed but not absent welstandseisen (architectural requirements)

## 6. Water & Infrastructure

### Waternet
- Amsterdam's combined waterbedrijf (water company) and waterschapstaak (water authority function)
- Drinkwatervoorziening (drinking water supply) AND rioolbeheer (sewer management) AND waterbeheer (water management)
- Vergunning grondwateronttrekking (groundwater extraction permit): Waternet mandate
- Lozing op riolering (sewer discharge): meldingsplicht (notification) to Waternet
- Warmwaterlozing (warm water discharge): temperature limits for oppervlaktewater (surface water)

### Hoogtebeperkingen (Height Restrictions)
- Schiphol luchtvaartzone (Schiphol airport zone): affects building heights in parts of Amsterdam
- Not typically a constraint for datacenter design (low-rise buildings)
- BUT: koeltorens (cooling towers) or schoorstenen (chimneys) may exceed limits in some zones
