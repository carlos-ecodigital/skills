# Gemeente Haarlemmermeer: Data Center & Energy Project Permitting

## 1. Municipal Authority

### College van B&W (Mayor and Executive Board)
- Bevoegd gezag (competent authority) for omgevingsvergunningen within Haarlemmermeer
- Haarlemmermeer is home to Schiphol Airport and a major datacenter cluster around Schiphol-Rijk and Hoofddorp
- Close coordination with Schiphol Group on hoogtebeperkingen (height restrictions) and geluidszones (noise zones)

### Omgevingsdienst Noordzeekanaalgebied (OD NZKG)
- Executes Bal/Bkl enforcement in Haarlemmermeer (as part of Amsterdam metropolitan arrangement)
- Milieubelastende activiteiten (environmentally impactful activities) enforcement

## 2. Datacenterbeleid Haarlemmermeer

### Historical Context
- Haarlemmermeer is part of the Amsterdam moratorium (2019) — participated in joint vestigingsstop (settlement freeze)
- Post-moratorium: developed own datacenterbeleidsvisie (data center policy vision)
- Province Noord-Holland datacenterbeleid designates Haarlemmermeer as one of three permitted gemeenten

### Current Policy

**Permitted Locations:**
- **Schiphol Trade Park (STP):** Primary designated datacenter area, purpose-built bedrijventerrein near A4/A5
- **De President:** Business park near Hoofddorp, some datacenter presence
- **Beukenhorst:** Near Hoofddorp station, mixed office/datacenter
- Scattered smaller datacenters in various bedrijventerreinen

**Restrictions:**
- Size limits per kavel (plot): gemeente controls via gronduitgifte (land allocation) and erfpacht (ground lease) conditions
- Energieverbruik (energy consumption) per hectare: not formalized as hard cap but weighed in ruimtelijke afweging (spatial assessment)
- Restwarmte (waste heat) commitment required for new large facilities
- Beeldkwaliteitsplan (visual quality plan): architectural review for Schiphol Trade Park specifically

**Schiphol Airport Constraints:**
- Luchthavenindelingsbesluit (LIB / Airport Zoning Decree): restricts bouwhoogte (building height), bird-attracting functions (e.g., wateropslag / water retention)
- Geluidszones (noise zones): parts of Haarlemmermeer under high Lden contours — affects gevoelige bestemmingen (sensitive uses) but not typically datacenters
- Obstakelvrijheid (obstacle clearance): koeltorens, antennemasten (cooling towers, antenna masts) require toets (assessment)

## 3. Energy & Grid

### Transportschaarste (Grid Congestion)
- Haarlemmermeer served by Liander (DSO) and TenneT (TSO)
- Severe congestieproblematiek (congestion issues): new large connections face multi-year wachttijden (wait times)
- Schiphol Trade Park: dedicated onderstation (substation) planned but timeline uncertain
- Cable pooling and niet-vast transportrecht (non-firm transport rights) increasingly used by datacenter developers

### Duurzame Energie (Sustainable Energy)
- Solar parks along A4 corridor
- Wind turbines restricted due to Schiphol proximity (hoogtebeperkingen / height restrictions)
- Green hydrogen potential via infrastructure near Tata Steel corridor

## 4. Warmtetransitie (Heat Transition)

### Haarlemmermeer Warmtevisie
- Large nieuwbouwopgave (new construction task): thousands of new woningen (homes) planned in Hoofddorp, Nieuw-Vennep
- Aardgasvrije wijken (gas-free neighborhoods) policy: new developments without gas connection
- Warmtevraag (heat demand) growing rapidly — potential off-takers for datacenter waste heat
- No existing large-scale warmtenet, but feasibility studies ongoing for Hoofddorp-area network

### DEC Relevance
- Unlike Amsterdam (established warmtenet), Haarlemmermeer has greenfield warmtenet opportunity
- DC waste heat to new residential developments: attractive for gemeente warmtetransitie targets
- BUT: limited glastuinbouw in Haarlemmermeer — DEC co-location model would need adaptation
- Primary value: neocloud/enterprise customer base (Schiphol corridor connectivity) rather than grower co-location

## 5. Stikstof (Nitrogen)

### Local Context
- Schiphol's own stikstofproblematiek (nitrogen issues) dominates the area
- Additional industrial/datacenter NOx compounds existing overbelasting (overload)
- AERIUS-berekening must account for cumulative impact with Schiphol operations
- Natura 2000 areas affected: Kennemerland-Zuid, Botshol (south), Ilperveld (north)

## 6. Building Permits

### Bbl Specifics
- Schiphol Trade Park: specifieke bebouwingsvoorschriften (specific building regulations) in omgevingsplan
- Maximum bouwhoogtes (building heights): typically 15-25 m, but constrained further by LIB in some zones
- Parkeereis (parking requirement): per bedrijventerrein regulation
- Verkeersafwikkeling (traffic management): concern during bouwfase (construction phase) — A4/A5 capacity

### Welstand (Architectural Review)
- Schiphol Trade Park has dedicated beeldkwaliteitsplan
- Emphasis on zakelijke uitstraling (business appearance), representatieve gevels (representative facades)
- Data centers: specific attention to blinde gevels (blank walls), groenbekleding (green cladding), parkeeroplossingen (parking solutions)

## 7. Grond & Water (Soil & Water)

### Bodemgesteldheid (Soil Conditions)
- Haarlemmermeer is droogmakerij (reclaimed lake / polder): -4 to -5 m NAP
- Slappe grond (soft soil): heipalen (driven piles) required for all significant structures
- Hoge grondwaterstand (high groundwater table): affects kelder (basement) construction, bronbemaling (dewatering)
- Relevant for datacenter foundation design: significant palenplan (piling plan) needed

### Waterschap
- Hoogheemraadschap van Rijnland: waterbeheer (water management) in Haarlemmermeer
- Waterberging (water retention): new developments must compensate for verharding (hardening) with watercompensatie
- Grondwateronttrekking (groundwater extraction): provincial + waterschap coordination
