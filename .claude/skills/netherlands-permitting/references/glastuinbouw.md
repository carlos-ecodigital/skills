# Greenhouse (Glastuinbouw) Permitting in the Netherlands

## 1. Regulatory Framework

Greenhouse horticulture (glastuinbouw) is one of the Netherlands' most economically significant agricultural sectors, concentrated in Zuid-Holland (Westland, Oostland), Noord-Holland, Noord-Brabant, and Gelderland. The permitting framework under the Omgevingswet (Environment and Planning Act) treats glastuinbouw as a distinct milieubelastende activiteit (environmentally impactful activity) with sector-specific rules.

### Key Legislation

| Instrument | Scope |
|---|---|
| **Bal (Besluit activiteiten leefomgeving) paragraaf 3.6.2** | Core glastuinbouw (greenhouse horticulture) activity rules |
| **Bal Art. 4.62** | Gewasbeschermingsmiddelen (crop protection products) preparation/transport via fixed pipelines |
| **Bal Art. 4.75** | Assimilatieverlichting (assimilation lighting) rules |
| **Bal Art. 4.76-4.78** | Lozing (water discharge) from substraatteelt (substrate cultivation) and grondgebonden teelt (soil-bound cultivation) |
| **Bal Art. 4.89** | Groenafval (green waste) composting and storage |
| **Bal Art. 5.4.1** | Energiebesparingsmaatregelen (energy sustainability measures) |
| **Bal Ch. 3.2.1** | Stookinstallaties (combustion/heating installations, including WKK/CHP) |
| **Bbl (Besluit bouwwerken leefomgeving)** | Building rules for new greenhouse construction |
| **Bkl (Besluit kwaliteit leefomgeving) Art. 8.71-8.72** | EU ETS and CO2-sectorsysteem (sector CO2 system) coordination |
| **Omgevingsplan (municipal environment plan)** | Local spatial zoning — agrarisch bestemming (agricultural zoning designation) |

## 2. Core Activity: Glastuinbouw Under Bal

### Permit Status
**No omgevingsvergunning (environmental permit) required** for the core glastuinbouw activity itself.

### Meldingsplicht (Notification Obligation)
Businesses must submit general data to the bevoegd gezag (competent authority) **minimum 4 weeks** before starting:
- Name and address of operator
- Location boundaries (kadastraal / cadastral)
- Start date of the activity
- Description of the greenhouse operation

### Scope Exclusions
Rules do NOT apply to:
- Cultivation at households (hobbykas / hobby greenhouse)
- Educational institutions (school greenhouses)
- Research institutions (proefstations / research stations — separate rules)
- Community gardens (volkstuinen)

### Associated Activities
Glastuinbouw operations typically involve multiple overlapping milieubelastende activiteiten (environmentally impactful activities) from Bal Chapter 3, each with their own rules:

| Activity | Bal Reference | Permit Type |
|---|---|---|
| Stookinstallatie (combustion installation / WKK-CHP) | 3.2.1 | Depends on capacity |
| Koelinstallatie (cooling installation) | 3.2.5 | Algemene regels (general rules) |
| Gasopslagtanks (gas storage tanks) | 3.2.7 | Algemene regels or melding (notification) |
| Vloeistofopslagtanks (liquid storage tanks) | 3.2.8 | Algemene regels or melding |
| Gevaarlijke stoffen opslag (hazardous substance storage) | 3.2.9 | Depends on quantity |
| Gewasbeschermingsmiddelen opslag (crop protection product storage) | 3.2.9 | Veiligheidsvoorschriften (safety rules) apply |

## 3. Lichtemissie / Assimilatieverlichting (Light Emission / Assimilation Lighting)

### The Problem
Assimilation lighting (SON-T, LED grow lights) in greenhouses causes significant lichtuitstraling (light emission/radiation) that affects:
- Omwonenden (neighboring residents): sleep disturbance, lichthinder (light nuisance)
- Natuur (nature): disruption of nocturnal wildlife, bird migration
- Landschap (landscape): "lichtvervuiling" (light pollution) visible over large distances

### Bal Art. 4.75: Assimilatieverlichting Rules

**Horizontal light emission limits:**
- Maximum lichtuitstraling measured at gevel (facade) of geluidgevoelige gebouwen (noise-sensitive buildings)
- Standards based on distance and time of day

**Afschermingsplicht (screening obligation):**
- Verduisteringsschermen (blackout screens) required during specific hours
- Provincial and municipal rules often impose stricter requirements
- Typical screening hours: zonsondergang tot zonsopgang (sunset to sunrise, varies by season)

### Provincial Screening Rules

Each province with significant glastuinbouw has its own lichtemissie policy:

**Zuid-Holland:**
- Glastuinbouwconcentratiegebieden (greenhouse concentration areas): Westland, Oostland, B-driehoek
- Strict screening hours enforced by Omgevingsdienst (environmental enforcement service) Haaglanden / DCMR
- 100% afscherming (screening) required during donkere uren (dark hours, varies by month)
- New installations must use LED with directional light management

**Noord-Holland:**
- Fewer concentration areas; stricter for geïsoleerde kassen (isolated greenhouses)
- Provinciale omgevingsverordening (provincial environmental ordinance) may impose additional requirements

**Noord-Brabant:**
- Greenhouses often in mixed agricultural/residential areas (gemengd agrarisch-woongebied)
- Higher sensitivity to light emission from omwonenden (neighbors)
- Stricter enforcement in overgangsgebieden (transition zones)

### Enforcement
- Omgevingsdienst (environmental enforcement service) conducts inspecties (inspections)
- Handhaving (enforcement) actions: last onder dwangsom (penalty order under coercion), bestuursdwang (administrative enforcement)
- Monitoring via satellite imagery and ground-level lichtmetingen (light measurements)

## 4. WKK (Warmtekrachtkoppeling / Combined Heat and Power) in Greenhouses

### Role of WKK
Many greenhouses operate WKK installations (aardgas-fired / natural-gas-fired CHP) to produce:
- Warmte (heat) for greenhouse warming
- Elektriciteit (electricity) for lighting and equipment (or teruglevering / grid export)
- CO2 for crop growth (CO2-dosering from rookgas / flue gas)

### WKK Permits

**As Stookinstallatie (combustion installation, Bal Ch. 3.2.1):**
- Thermisch vermogen (thermal capacity) determines permit level
- Small WKK (< 1 MW thermal): algemene regels (general rules), meldingsplicht (notification obligation)
- Medium WKK (1-50 MW thermal): additional emissie-eisen (emission requirements)
- Large WKK (> 50 MW thermal): omgevingsvergunning milieubelastende activiteit (environmental permit for impactful activity)

**Emissie-eisen (Emission Requirements):**
- NOx: emissiegrenswaarden (emission limits) per Bal (mg/Nm3)
- CO: emissiegrenswaarden
- SO2: limits for gasgestookte installaties (gas-fired installations)
- Monitoring: continue or periodieke emissie-metingen (continuous or periodic emission measurements)

### Stikstof (Nitrogen Deposition) Issue

**Critical Issue for Glastuinbouw:**
Since the landmark Raad van State (Council of State) ruling on the PAS (Programma Aanpak Stikstof / Nitrogen Action Program) in 2019, NOx emissions from WKK installations require AERIUS-berekening (AERIUS calculation) for stikstofdepositie (nitrogen deposition) on Natura 2000-gebieden (Natura 2000 protected areas).

**When Natuurvergunning (Nature Permit) is Needed:**
- New WKK installation or capacity increase
- Significante wijziging (significant change) in operation
- AERIUS calculation shows stikstofdepositie > 0.00 mol/ha/jaar on any Natura 2000-gebied

**Sources of NOx Emission:**
- WKK/gasmotor (gas engine) exhaust
- Ketelinstallaties (boiler installations)
- Vrachtwagens en personenauto's (trucks and personal vehicles) visiting the site
- All sources must be included in AERIUS-berekening

**Mitigation Options:**
- Intern salderen (internal offsetting): reduce other emissions on same site
- Extern salderen (external offsetting): purchase emission rights from closing businesses
- ADC-toets (ADC test): demonstrate no alternatives (Alternatieven), dwingende redenen van groot openbaar belang (compelling reasons of overriding public interest), and compenserende maatregelen (compensation measures)
- Switch to all-electric (no WKK): eliminates NOx source entirely

## 5. CO2-Dosering (CO2 Dosing) and CO2-Sectorsysteem (Sector CO2 System)

### CO2 Supply for Crop Growth
Greenhouses require elevated CO2 concentrations (800-1200 ppm) for optimale gewasgroei (optimal crop growth). Sources:
- WKK rookgas (flue gas): primary source for many telers (growers)
- OCAP-leiding (OCAP pipeline): pure CO2 from Rotterdam industry
- Vloeibaar CO2 (liquid CO2) delivery

### CO2-Sectorsysteem (Sector CO2 System)
The glastuinbouw sector operates under a collective CO2-emissieplafond (CO2 emission ceiling):
- Convenant (sector agreement) between government and Glastuinbouw Nederland
- Total sector CO2 emissions capped (annually adjusted)
- Individual growers' emissions tracked via energieverbruiksgegevens (energy usage data)
- Bkl Art. 8.71-8.72: vergunningsvoorschriften (permit conditions) cannot frustrate ETS trading or CO2-kostenegalisatie (CO2 cost equalization)

### EU ETS for Large Greenhouse Operators
- Greenhouses with aggregate verbrandingscapaciteit (combustion capacity) > 20 MW thermal fall under EU ETS
- Must hold and surrender emissierechten (emission allowances / EUAs)
- Monitoring and reporting per EU MRR (Monitoring and Reporting Regulation)
- Annual verificatie (verification) by geaccrediteerde verificateur (accredited verifier)
- Smaller operators may participate in opt-in-regeling (opt-in scheme)

## 6. Lozingen (Water Discharges)

### Substraatteelt (Substrate Cultivation)

**Bal Art. 4.76-4.78: Drainwater/Spuiwater (Drain/Flush Water) Discharge:**
- Recirculatieplicht (recirculation obligation): drain water must be collected and recirculated
- Minimum recirculatiepercentage (recirculation percentage, varies by gewastype / crop type)
- Discharge only after zuivering (treatment) if recirculation is technically not feasible
- Lozingsnormen (discharge limits) for nutriënten (nutrients): stikstof (nitrogen), fosfaat (phosphate), and gewasbeschermingsmiddelen (crop protection products)

### Grondgebonden Teelt (Soil-Bound Cultivation)
- Different lozingsregels (discharge rules, less strict on recirculation)
- Nutriëntenuitspoeling (nutrient leaching) prevention measures
- Drainagewater (drain tile water) management

### Gewasbeschermingsmiddelen (Crop Protection Products)

**Bal Art. 4.62: Vast Leidingwerk (Fixed Pipeline Transport):**
- Specific rules for bereiding (preparation) and transport via fixed pipelines
- Morsbeveiligingsmaatregelen (spill prevention measures)
- Spoelwater (rinsing water) collection

**Zuiveringsplicht (Treatment Obligation):**
- Since 2018: zuivering (treatment) of discharge water containing gewasbeschermingsmiddelen required
- Zuiveringsinstallatie (treatment installation) must remove minimum 95% of werkzame stoffen (active substances)
- BZG-certificaat (Bewijsstuk Zuiveringsinstallatie Glastuinbouw / proof of treatment installation certificate) required
- Applies to both substraatteelt (substrate) and grondgebonden teelt (soil-bound) cultivation

### Waterschap (Water Authority) Permits
- Lozing op oppervlaktewater (discharge to surface water): waterschapsvergunning (water authority permit) for discharge
- Lozing op riolering (discharge to sewer): meldingsplicht (notification obligation) to gemeente (municipality)
- Grondwateronttrekking (groundwater extraction): provinciale vergunning (provincial permit) for deep well water extraction

## 7. Spatial Planning for Greenhouses

### Omgevingsplan (Municipal Environment Plan) Zoning

**Agrarisch Bestemming (Agricultural Zoning):**
- Greenhouses permitted in areas zoned for agrarisch gebruik (agricultural use)
- Glastuinbouwconcentratiegebied (greenhouse concentration area): designated areas specifically for kassenconcentraties (greenhouse clusters)
- Expansion within existing agrarisch bestemming: generally permitted

**New Greenhouse or Major Expansion:**
- Must fit within omgevingsplan (or obtain bopa / buitenplanse omgevingsplanactiviteit or plan amendment)
- Bouwhoogtebeperkingen (height restrictions, typical: 8-12 meters for modern greenhouses)
- Bebouwingspercentage (footprint limitations) per zone
- Parkeer- en ontsluitingseisen (parking and access road requirements)

**Omgevingsplan Wijziging (Environment Plan Amendment):**
- Required if greenhouse development not anticipated in current plan
- May trigger m.e.r.-beoordeling (EIA screening) for large expansions
- Provinciale omgevingsverordening (provincial environmental ordinance) may restrict greenhouse development in certain areas

### Glasplafond (Provincial Glass Ceilings)
Some provinces maintain a maximum total glasoppervlakte (greenhouse area / glasplafond):
- New development requires demonstrating net area does not exceed ceiling
- Sloopregeling (demolition scheme): demolish old glass elsewhere to create ruimte (space)
- Greenport-beleid (Greenport policy): concentration in designated areas, discourage verspreide ontwikkeling (scattered development)

## 8. Aardwarmte (Geothermal Energy) for Greenhouses

### Growing Trend
Geothermal heating is increasingly adopted in glastuinbouw to:
- Reduce gasafhankelijkheid (gas dependency) and CO2-uitstoot (CO2 emissions)
- Lower energiekosten (energy costs, once operational)
- Maintain CO2-voorziening (CO2 supply) via OCAP or purchased vloeibaar CO2 (liquid CO2)

### Permit Requirements
Same as general aardwarmte framework (see [district-heating.md](district-heating.md) section 5):
- Opsporingsvergunning (exploration permit, Mijnbouwwet / Mining Act)
- Winningsvergunning (production permit) with approved winningsplan (production plan)
- Omgevingsvergunning for boring (drilling) and bovengrondse installaties (surface installations)
- SodM (Staatstoezicht op de Mijnen / State Supervision of Mines) safety oversight

### Consortium Model
Many greenhouse geothermal projects use a coöperatief model (cooperative/consortium model):
- Multiple telers (growers) form a coöperatie (cooperative)
- Shared investment in geothermal doublet(s)
- Heat distributed via lokaal warmtenet (local heat network) between participating greenhouses
- SDE++ subsidie for geothermie diep (deep geothermal) category

## 9. Energy Performance and Sustainability

### BENG (Bijna Energieneutraal Gebouw / Nearly Zero-Energy Building) for New Greenhouses
- New greenhouse construction subject to BENG requirements
- Bal Art. 5.4.1: energiebesparingsmaatregelen (energy sustainability measures) for existing operations
- LED-verlichting (LED lighting) transition reduces energieverbruik (energy consumption)
- WKO (warmte-koude opslag / thermal energy storage) for seasonal energy storage

### Glastuinbouw Nederland Sector Agreements
- Kas als Energiebron (Greenhouse as Energy Source) program
- Sectordoelstelling (sector target): klimaatneutraal (climate-neutral) by 2040
- Innovatiesubsidies (innovation subsidies) for energy-efficient technologies
- Kennisdeling (knowledge sharing) through proefstations (research stations: WUR, Delphy)

### GACS (Gebouwautomatiserings- en Controlesysteem / Building Automation and Control System) Requirement (Bbl, from 2026)
- Mandatory from 1 January 2026 for installations > 290 kW
- From 2030: mandatory for installations > 70 kW
- Klimaatcomputers (climate computers) in greenhouses typically already satisfy GACS requirements

## 10. DEC-Relevant: DC Waste Heat for Greenhouses

### Opportunity
Data center afvalwarmte (waste heat) at 25-55°C can be upgraded via warmtepomp (heat pump) to greenhouse-usable temperatures (40-90°C depending on gewastype / crop type). This is central to DEC's co-location model.

### Key Considerations
- **Temperature match:** Tomaten (tomatoes) and paprika's (bell peppers) need 18-22°C air, 40-55°C buisrailverwarming (pipe rail heating); sierteelt (ornamental horticulture) and tropische gewassen (tropical crops) need higher temperatures
- **CO2-gat (CO2 gap):** When DC waste heat replaces WKK, the grower loses their primary CO2-bron (CO2 source) from rookgas (flue gas) — must source externally via OCAP-leiding (OCAP pipeline), zuiver CO2 (pure CO2), or biomassa (biomass)
- **Seizoensafstemming (seasonal matching):** Greenhouse heat demand peaks in winter; DC heat output is steady year-round — requires buffer/ATES for mismatch management
- **Contractueel (contractual):** Warmteleveringsovereenkomst (heat supply agreement) needed between DC operator and teler (grower) — pricing in €/GJ, beschikbaarheidsgaranties (availability guarantees), meetinrichting (metering arrangement)

### Permitting Implications
- Warmtenet (heat network) connecting DC to greenhouse requires omgevingsvergunning if crossing public land
- Under Wcw (Wet collectieve warmte / Collective Heat Act, from mid-2026): warmtekavel (heat concession zone) may apply if supplying multiple afnemers (consumers)
- AERIUS-berekening (AERIUS calculation) impact: replacing WKK with external heat eliminates on-site NOx-emissie — significant stikstof benefit
- SDE++ subsidie may apply for warmtebenutting (heat utilization) category

## 11. Typical Greenhouse Permitting Timeline

| Phase | Duration | Activities |
|---|---|---|
| **Locatiekeuze (site selection)** | 1-3 months | Omgevingsplan check, provincial glasplafond, infrastructure |
| **Vooroverleg (pre-consultation)** | 1-2 months | Vooroverleg with gemeente, waterschap, provincie |
| **Ruimtelijke ordening (spatial planning)** | 0-12 months | Bopa or plan amendment if needed |
| **Meldingen (notifications)** | 4+ weeks | Melding glastuinbouw, melding stookinstallatie |
| **Bouwvergunning (building permit)** | 2-8 months | Omgevingsvergunning bouwen (if gevolgklasse 2/3) |
| **Milieu (environmental)** | 2-6 months | WKK permit, watervergunning, natuurvergunning (stikstof) |
| **Netaansluiting (grid connection)** | 3-24 months | Elektriciteits- en gasaansluiting (electricity and gas connection) |
| **Aardwarmte (geothermal)** | 12-24 months | If aardwarmte: Mijnbouwwet permits + boring (drilling) |
| **Bouw (construction)** | 6-18 months | Glass installation, klimaatsystemen (climate systems), WKK, infrastructure |
| **Totaal (total)** | 12-30 months | Depending on complexity and warmtebron (heat source) |
