# District Heating (Warmtenetten) Permitting in the Netherlands

## 1. Legislative Framework

### Current and Upcoming Legislation

| Law | Status | Scope |
|---|---|---|
| **Warmtewet** (2014) | In force; being replaced by Wcw | Consumer protection, maximum tariffs, leveringszekerheid |
| **Wcw (Wet collectieve warmte)** | Adopted Dec 2025; effective mid-2026 (phased) | Complete overhaul: warmtekavel, public ownership, cost-based tariffs |
| **Wgiw (Wet gemeentelijke instrumenten warmtetransitie)** | Adopted alongside Wcw; effective 1 Jan 2026 | Municipal tools to phase out natural gas per neighborhood |
| **Mijnbouwwet** | In force | Aardwarmte (geothermal) exploration and extraction |
| **Omgevingswet** | In force since 1 Jan 2024 | Environmental and spatial planning permits |

## 2. Wcw (Wet collectieve warmte) -- The New Heat Act

### Fundamental Changes
The Wcw replaces the Warmtewet and introduces a completely new regulatory framework for collective heat supply. The key elements:

### 2.1 Warmtekavel System

**Definition:** A warmtekavel (heat concession zone) encompasses a collective heat system that functions as a single integrated unit.

**Designation Process:**
1. Municipal executive (college van B&W) establishes warmtekavel boundaries
2. Municipality designates the warmtebedrijf (heat company) for each kavel
3. Existing heat companies receive designations for current service areas
4. For new areas, municipality solicits interest and selects operator

**Duration:**
- Existing systems: 14-30 years from Wcw entry into force
- New designations: terms set by municipality (typically 20-30 years)

**Cross-Municipal Kavels:**
- Municipalities can cooperate via Wet gemeenschappelijke regelingen
- Joint kavels for heat networks crossing municipal boundaries

### 2.2 Public Ownership Requirement

**Primary Rule:**
- New warmtebedrijven must have >50% ownership (direct or indirect) by public entities
- Public entities: State, province, municipality, or other public body
- Applies to newly designated heat companies

**Transition Period (10 years post-entry):**
- Private operators may still receive warmtekavel designations if no public entity or warmtegemeenschap (heat community) seeks to operate that specific zone
- Private designations run 20-30 years

**Alternative Structures:**
- Joint ventures between private warmteleverancier and public netwerkbedrijf
- Private regional warmtetransportbeheerder (heat transport manager) permitted under limited exceptions (Art. 5.12 Wcw, restricted activities only)

### 2.3 Tariff Regulation

**Transition from NMDA Principle:**
- Old system: "Niet Meer Dan Anders" (no more than otherwise) -- gas-price-linked maximum tariff
- 2025 maximum: EUR 43.79/GJ (including BTW)
- Reference dates: tariff calculated from 3 reference dates in September, October, November of prior year

**Under Wcw (from mid-2026):**
- Transition to cost-based tariff regulation (kostengebaseerde tariefregulering)
- ACM (Autoriteit Consument & Markt) regulates maximum tariffs
- Separate tariffs for:
  - Kleinverbruikers (small consumers / residential)
  - Grootverbruikers (large consumers / commercial)
  - Small systems (< 1,500 connections: lighter regulation)

### 2.4 Sustainability Obligations

- Warmtebedrijven must improve system sustainability over time
- Public reporting on sustainability performance (consumer bills and open disclosure)
- Government may establish greenhouse gas emission standards via AMvB
- Differentiation between existing and new systems
- Sustainability roadmap (verduurzamingsplan) required from designated warmtebedrijf

### 2.5 Small System Exemptions

Systems serving maximum 1,500 consumers benefit from:
- Lighter regulatory burden
- Simplified tariff regime
- Reduced administrative obligations
- Lower barrier for community-led initiatives (warmtegemeenschappen)

## 3. Wgiw (Wet gemeentelijke instrumenten warmtetransitie)

### Municipal Powers
The Wgiw gives municipalities legal instruments to:
- Determine per neighborhood (wijk) which alternative to natural gas is most suitable
- Set a termination date for natural gas distribution per neighborhood
- Require connection to collective heat systems in designated areas
- Enforce disconnection from gas network after transition date

### Wijkuitvoeringsplan (Neighborhood Implementation Plan)
- Municipality develops wijkuitvoeringsplan per neighborhood
- Specifies heat alternative (warmtenet, all-electric, hydrogen, etc.)
- Sets timeline for transition
- Participatie with residents and building owners mandatory
- Linked to municipality's warmtetransitievisie (heat transition vision)

## 4. Permits for Heat Infrastructure

### 4.1 Spatial Planning

**Warmteleidingen (Heat Pipelines):**
- Underground pipelines: typically vergunningsvrij if within existing public infrastructure
- Above-ground infrastructure (pompstations, warmteoverdrachtstations): omgevingsvergunning required
- May require omgevingsplan change if not anticipated in current plan

**Warmtebronnen (Heat Sources):**
- WKK installations: omgevingsvergunning for milieubelastende activiteit
- Biomassa-installatie: omgevingsvergunning + potential MER
- Geothermie: separate Mijnbouwwet permits (see section 5)
- Industriele restwarmte: depends on source facility's existing permits

### 4.2 Construction Permits (Bbl)

**Pompstations and Warmteoverdrachtstations:**
- Gevolgklasse determination based on size and structural complexity
- Brandveiligheid requirements for enclosed stations
- Geluidsisolatie if near residential areas

**Heat Pipeline Infrastructure:**
- Trenching/excavation: graafvergunning from gemeente
- WIBON/KLIC melding: mandatory notification to prevent cable/pipe damage
- Precautionary measures for existing underground infrastructure

### 4.3 Environmental Permits (Bal)

**WKK (Combined Heat and Power):**
- Stookinstallatie: meldingsplicht or vergunningsplicht depending on capacity
- NOx, SO2, CO emissions: Bal emission limits
- Geluid (noise): limits at nearest geluidgevoelig gebouw
- Stikstof: AERIUS calculation required; may need Natura 2000 ontheffing

**Biomass Installations:**
- Omgevingsvergunning milieubelastende activiteit
- Stricter air quality requirements (particulate matter, dioxins)
- Potential MER trigger depending on thermal capacity
- Sustainability criteria for biomass fuel (SDE++ requirement)

**Industrial Waste Heat:**
- Primary facility handles environmental permits
- Heat extraction infrastructure may require separate meldingen
- Interface agreements between heat source and warmtebedrijf

### 4.4 Water Permits

**Groundwater:**
- Open-loop systems using aquifer thermal energy storage (WKO): provincial permit
- Closed-loop boreholes: meldingsplicht to province
- Grondwaterbeschermingsgebied restrictions

**Surface Water:**
- Lozingsvergunning for discharge of heat to oppervlaktewater (waterschap permit)
- Temperature impact assessment
- Ecologische beoordeling for sensitive water bodies (KRW)

### 4.5 Municipal Road and Utility Permits

**Graafvergunning (Excavation Permit):**
- Required from gemeente for all pipeline construction in public roads
- APV (Algemene Plaatselijke Verordening) rules apply
- Coordination with other utility works (riolering, elektra, gas, telecom)

**WIBON/KLIC (Cable and Pipeline Information Exchange):**
- Mandatory KLIC-melding before any excavation
- Minimum 3 working days before start
- Information on existing underground infrastructure
- Grondroerder (excavator) has zorgplicht to prevent damage

**Verkeersbesluit (Traffic Decision):**
- Required if pipeline construction requires road closures
- Verkeersmaatregelen (traffic management) plan
- Coordination with gemeente verkeersdienst and politie

## 5. Aardwarmte (Geothermal) Permits

### Mijnbouwwet Framework
Geothermal energy extraction in the Netherlands is governed by the Mijnbouwwet, with SodM (Staatstoezicht op de Mijnen) as the safety regulator.

### 5.1 Exploration Phase

**Opsporingsvergunning (Exploration Permit):**
- Issued by Ministry of Economic Affairs (EZK) / Minister
- Application via RVO
- Geological assessment of geothermal potential
- Financial capability demonstration
- Exclusivity for defined area and period

**Seismisch Onderzoek (Seismic Survey):**
- Requires separate permit if surface vibration testing needed
- SodM oversight
- Environmental impact assessment for survey activities

### 5.2 Extraction Phase

**Winningsvergunning (Extraction Permit):**
- Issued by Minister of EZK via RVO
- Requires approved winningsplan (extraction plan)
- SodM safety assessment
- Long-term reservoir management plan

**Winningsplan:**
- Detailed geological model
- Production forecast (flow rates, temperature decline over time)
- Subsidence risk assessment (bodemdaling)
- Seismicity risk assessment (geinduceerde seismiciteit)
- Monitoring plan

**Omgevingsvergunning:**
- Building permit for drilling rig and surface installations
- Environmental permit for drilling activities (noise, emissions, waste)
- Watervergunning for drilling fluids discharge

### 5.3 Safety and Monitoring

**SodM Requirements:**
- Well integrity: design, construction, testing per SodM standards
- Blow-out prevention equipment
- Continuous monitoring: pressure, temperature, flow rate
- Regular well integrity testing
- Annual reporting to SodM and Minister
- Seismicity monitoring if in induced seismicity risk area

## 6. Heat Transition Subsidies

### SDE++ for Warmtenetten
- Restwarmte (waste heat utilization) is an SDE++ category
- Geothermie diep (deep geothermal) is an SDE++ category
- WKK can qualify under biomass or renewable heat categories
- Requirements: haalbaarheidsstudie, omgevingsvergunning (where applicable), transportindicatie

### SAH (Subsidieregeling Aardwarmte)
- Specific subsidy for geothermal drilling risk mitigation
- Covers portion of drilling costs if well is unsuccessful (misboring)
- Application to RVO before drilling starts

### ISDE
- For smaller heat installations (heat pumps, solar thermal)
- Available to households and businesses
- Fixed amount per installation type

### PAW (Programma Aardgasvrije Wijken)
- Subsidy program for pilot gas-free neighborhoods (proeftuinen)
- Municipalities apply for funding to support wijkuitvoeringsplannen
- Covers preparation and coordination costs
- First tranches supported 64 proeftuinen across the Netherlands

## 7. ACM Regulation

### Current Role (Under Warmtewet)
- Sets maximum tariffs annually
- Monitors leveringszekerheid (security of supply)
- Handles consumer complaints
- Enforces transparency obligations

### Future Role (Under Wcw)
- Cost-based tariff regulation for all system sizes
- Methodology decisions (methodebesluit) for tariff calculation
- Quality and reliability supervision
- Dispute resolution between warmtebedrijf and gemeente
- Enhanced transparency and reporting requirements

## 8. Typical District Heating Permitting Timeline

| Phase | Duration | Activities |
|---|---|---|
| **Warmtetransitievisie** | Municipality-driven | Municipal heat transition vision per wijk |
| **Warmtekavel designation** | 6-12 months | Municipal process to establish kavel and designate warmtebedrijf |
| **Feasibility** | 3-6 months | Technical design, business case, heat source selection |
| **Spatial planning** | 3-12 months | Omgevingsplan check/amendment for above-ground infrastructure |
| **Environmental permits** | 3-8 months | Milieubelastende activiteit (WKK/biomassa), watervergunning |
| **Graafvergunningen** | 1-3 months per section | Municipal excavation permits for pipeline routes |
| **Geothermal permits** | 12-24 months | If aardwarmte: opsporingsvergunning, winningsvergunning |
| **Grid / energy connection** | Variable | Power for heat pumps, WKK grid export |
| **Construction** | 12-36 months | Pipeline installation, source construction, customer connections |
| **Total** | 24-48 months | From designation to first heat delivery |
