# Province of Zuid-Holland: Data Center & Energy Project Permitting

## 1. Provincial Authority Structure

### Gedeputeerde Staten (GS / Provincial Executive)
- Bevoegd gezag (competent authority) for provincial environmental permits and ontheffing (exemption) from provinciale omgevingsverordening
- Oversees DCMR Milieudienst Rijnmond and Omgevingsdienst Haaglanden for enforcement
- Decides on reactieve aanwijzing (reactive instruction) against municipal omgevingsplannen

### Key Omgevingsdiensten (Environmental Enforcement Services)
- **DCMR Milieudienst Rijnmond:** Rotterdam, Drechtsteden, Europoort — heavy industry, Seveso/BRZO, Rijnmond cluster
- **Omgevingsdienst Haaglanden (ODH):** Den Haag, Westland, Delft — glastuinbouw, residential area enforcement
- **Omgevingsdienst Zuid-Holland Zuid (OZHZ):** Drechtsteden, Hoeksche Waard
- **Omgevingsdienst West-Holland (ODWH):** Leiden, Alphen aan den Rijn area

## 2. Data Center Policy

### Provincial Position
Zuid-Holland has **no dedicated datacenterbeleid (data center policy)** comparable to Noord-Holland's strategy. However, provincial policy frameworks constrain datacenter development through:

**Ruimtelijke ordening (spatial planning):**
- Province designates bedrijventerreinen (industrial areas) in omgevingsverordening
- Large-scale datacenters (> 5 MVA) generally directed to existing zware industrie (heavy industry) zones
- Haven-industrieel complex (port-industrial complex) Rotterdam: Maasvlakte, Europoort, Botlek — large power availability
- Provinciale verstedelijkingsstrategie (urbanization strategy): prefers compact development, discourages scattered industrial use

**Energy considerations:**
- Province prioritizes restwarmtebenutting (waste heat utilization) for new large energy consumers
- Warmtelinq (heat pipeline Rotterdam-Den Haag): provincial infrastructure supporting waste heat transport
- New large consumers expected to participate in warmtetransitie (heat transition)

### Key Datacenter Locations in Zuid-Holland
| Locatie (Location) | Kenmerken (Characteristics) |
|---|---|
| Maasvlakte / Europoort | Heavy industry zone, large grid capacity, limited warmtevraag nearby |
| Rotterdam Stadshavens | Mixed-use redevelopment, warmtenet proximity, space constraints |
| Den Haag / Forepark | Government cluster, limited expansion, noise-sensitive surroundings |
| Westland / Oostland | Glastuinbouwgebied — warmte integration opportunity with greenhouses |
| Drechtsteden | Emerging industrial area, moderate grid capacity |

## 3. Glastuinbouw (Greenhouse Horticulture) Province

### Zuid-Holland as Greenhouse Capital
Zuid-Holland contains the Netherlands' largest glastuinbouwconcentratie (greenhouse concentration):
- **Westland:** ~2,200 ha greenhouse, largest contiguous greenhouse area globally
- **Oostland (Lansingerland, Pijnacker-Nootdorp, Bleiswijk):** ~1,800 ha
- **B-driehoek (Barendrecht, Ridderkerk, Hendrik-Ido-Ambacht):** smaller cluster

### Provincial Glastuinbouwbeleid (Greenhouse Policy)
- Greenport Zuid-Holland: provincial economic development program for glastuinbouw
- Herstructureringsbeleid (restructuring policy): modernize old glass, concentrate in designated areas
- Glasplafond (glass ceiling): total greenhouse area controlled
- Sloopregeling (demolition scheme): demolish old glass to create ruimte (space) for new development
- Energietransitie glastuinbouw: province promotes geothermie (geothermal), restwarmte, all-electric

### Warmte & Glastuinbouw
- **Warmtelinq:** Major warmtetransportleiding (heat transport pipeline) from Rotterdamse haven (Rotterdam port) through South Holland to The Hague — carries industrial restwarmte
- **Geothermie clusters:** Multiple aardwarmtedoubletten (geothermal doublets) operational in Westland and Oostland
- **OCAP-leiding (OCAP pipeline):** Pure CO2 from Shell Pernis to greenhouse cluster — enables WKK-vrije (WKK-free) greenhouse operation
- **DEC opportunity:** Datacenter waste heat supplementing or replacing WKK in adjacent greenhouses, with CO2 sourced from OCAP

## 4. Provinciale Omgevingsverordening (Provincial Environmental Ordinance)

### Instructieregels (Instruction Rules) Relevant for DEC

**Bedrijventerreinen (Industrial Areas):**
- Province classifies industrial zones by milieucategorie (environmental category, 1-6)
- Datacenters typically milieucategorie 3.2-4.2 depending on size and generators
- Must locate in zone matching or exceeding milieucategorie
- Adjacent glastuinbouw: milieucategorie 2-3 (compatible with many datacenter operations)

**Agrarisch Gebied (Agricultural Area):**
- Glastuinbouwconcentratiegebieden designated for greenhouse use
- Non-agricultural functions (datacenter) require bopa or planwijziging (plan amendment)
- Province may support co-location if primair agrarisch karakter (primarily agricultural character) maintained and warmtebenutting (heat utilization) demonstrated

**Groene Ruimte (Green Space):**
- Provinciale groenstructuur (provincial green structure) protected
- Compensatieplicht (compensation obligation) for loss of groengebied (green area)
- Relevant for site selection near greenhouse areas which often border groengebieden

**Externe Veiligheid (External Safety):**
- Province sets aandachtsgebieden (attention areas) for BESS and industrial risks
- DCMR advises on QRA (Quantitative Risk Assessment) for Rijnmond area
- PGS 37-1/37-2 battery storage near residential areas

## 5. Stikstof (Nitrogen) in Zuid-Holland

### Provincial Approach
- Province administers extern salderen (external offsetting) with provincial beleidsregel
- Reële afname (real decrease) requirement: offsetting source must demonstrably reduce emissions
- Latente ruimte (latent headroom) in permits cannot be used for extern salderen

### Key Natura 2000 Areas
- **Solleveld & Kapittelduinen** (dunes near Den Haag/Monster) — very close to Westland greenhouses
- **Voornes Duin** (dunes Voorne) — near Maasvlakte
- **Biesbosch** — near Drechtsteden
- **Grevelingen** — Zeeland/Zuid-Holland border
- WKK and noodstroomaggregaten (emergency generators) emissions must clear AERIUS for all nearby Natura 2000

### DEC Stikstof Strategy
- Co-location near Westland: existing WKK-emissies (WKK emissions) from greenhouses create baseline
- Replacing WKK with DC waste heat REDUCES stikstofdepositie — can serve as intern salderen
- New DC diesel generators ADD stikstof — must be minimized or mitigated
- Net-zero stikstof argument: DC + heat pump replacing WKK = lower total NOx than status quo

## 6. Warmtetransitie (Heat Transition)

### Warmtelinq Infrastructure
- Transport pipeline: Maasvlakte → Vlaardingen → Den Haag (operational)
- Extension planned: Leiden, potentially further
- Capacity: industrial waste heat from Rotterdamse haven and potentially datacenters
- Temperature: high-temperature warmte (>70°C) in main transport line

### Provinciale Warmtecoördinatie (Provincial Heat Coordination)
- Province coordinates gemeentelijke warmtetransitievisies (municipal heat transition plans)
- Stimulates warmte-uitwisseling (heat exchange) between bedrijventerreinen and woonwijken (residential areas)
- SDE++ for geothermie in glastuinbouw: province supports applications
- Wcw (Wet collectieve warmte / Collective Heat Act) implementation: province role in warmtekavel coordination

### DEC Warmte Opportunity
- Westland/Oostland greenhouses: proven warmtevraag (heat demand), existing infrastructure
- Den Haag warmtevraag: new aardgasvrij (gas-free) wijken (neighborhoods) need warmtebronnen (heat sources)
- Provincial framework supports restwarmtebenutting as permitting condition for large energy consumers

## 7. Water & Soil

### Waterschappen (Water Authorities)
- Hoogheemraadschap van Delfland: Westland, Den Haag, Delft — covers main glastuinbouw area
- Hoogheemraadschap van Schieland en de Krimpenerwaard
- Hoogheemraadschap van Rijnland: Leiden area
- Waterschap Hollandse Delta: Hoeksche Waard, Voorne-Putten

### Bodemdaling (Land Subsidence)
- Significant bodemdalingsproblematiek (land subsidence issues) in veenweidegebieden (peat meadow areas)
- Affects foundation design: heipalen (driven piles) to draagkrachtige laag (bearing layer)
- Relevant for site selection: avoid worst slappe grond (soft soil) areas

### Grondwaterbescherming (Groundwater Protection)
- Duingebieden (dune areas): strict grondwaterbeschermingszone (groundwater protection zone)
- Relevant for sites near coast (Maasvlakte, Monster, Hoek van Holland)

## 8. DEC-Specific Considerations

### Westland Co-Location Scenario
- Adjacent to largest greenhouse concentration globally
- Proven warmtevraag from telers (growers)
- OCAP CO2-leiding (pipeline) present — solves CO2-gat (CO2 gap) when WKK replaced
- Space constraints: high land prices, limited available bedrijventerrein
- Lichtemissie (light emission) rules strict — datacenter exterior lighting must comply with same norms as greenhouse afscherming
- Stedin = regionaal systeembeheerder (DSO) — severe transportschaarste in Zuid-Holland (full capacity announced Dec 2024)

### Rotterdam Port Area
- Large grid capacity (TenneT HS/EHS direct connections possible)
- Limited warmtevraag nearby (industrial, not residential or greenhouse)
- Seveso/BRZO proximity: QRA considerations for BESS if near Seveso installations
- DCMR as omgevingsdienst: experienced with zware industrie (heavy industry) permitting
