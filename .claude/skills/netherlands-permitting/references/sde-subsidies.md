# SDE++ and ISDE Subsidy Permitting Requirements

## 1. SDE++ Overview

The SDE++ (Stimulering Duurzame Energieproductie en Klimaattransitie) is the Netherlands' primary operating subsidy for renewable energy and CO2 reduction. It compensates the difference between the production cost of renewable energy/CO2 reduction and the market price of energy, providing a basisbedrag (base amount) guaranteed over 12-15 years.

### 2025 Round
- Application window: 7 October -- 6 November 2025
- Processing: ongoing as of February 2026 (7/80 Moleculen, 33/121 LT-warmte, 6/20 HT-warmte, 98/189 Elektriciteit, 11/58 CCS/CCU)

### 2026 Round
- Expected: Fall 2026
- Preliminary budget: EUR 8 billion

## 2. SDE++ Categories (Domains)

| Domain | Key Technologies |
|---|---|
| **Elektriciteit** (Renewable Electricity) | Zon-PV (solar PV), wind op land, waterkracht |
| **LT-warmte** (Low-Temperature Heat) | Geothermie ondiep, zonthermie, aquathermie, restwarmte, WKO |
| **HT-warmte** (High-Temperature Heat) | Geothermie diep, elektrische boilers, biomassa verbranding |
| **Moleculen** (Molecules/Green Gas) | Biomassa vergisting, groen gas, waterstof |
| **CCS/CCU** (Carbon Capture) | CO2 afvang en opslag/hergebruik |

### Technology-Specific Notes

**Zon-PV (Solar PV):**
- Since 2025: haalbaarheidsstudie mandatory for ALL categories, including < 1 MW
- Quarter-hour pricing (kwartieruren) used for calculation from October 2025 (replacing hourly)
- Rooftop vs ground-mounted distinction affects permit requirements

**Geothermie:**
- SDE++ category for diep geothermie (deep geothermal, > 500m)
- Requires opsporingsvergunning (Mijnbouwwet) as prerequisite
- Long realisatietermijn due to drilling timeline

**Biomassa WKK:**
- Sustainability criteria for biomass fuel (NTA 8080 / SBP/FSC certification)
- Combined heat and power: both electricity and heat production subsidized
- Requires warmteafnemer (heat buyer) declaration

**Restwarmte (Waste Heat):**
- Utilization of industrial/data center waste heat for district heating
- New since 2025: verklaring warmteafnemer (heat buyer declaration) required as attachment
- Helps RVO assess project feasibility

**Groen Gas:**
- From 2025: value of Garanties van Oorsprong (GvOs) factored into subsidy amount
- Biomethane injection into gas grid

**CCS/CCU:**
- CO2 storage abroad (EU, Iceland, Norway, Liechtenstein) now permitted
- Large industrial CCS projects eligible

## 3. Application Requirements

### 3.1 Haalbaarheidsstudie (Feasibility Study)

**Mandatory for all categories since 2025** (previously exempted small zon-PV).

**Content Requirements (per RVO Model Haalbaarheidsstudie):**
1. **Projectbeschrijving** (Project description) -- detailed, not brief
2. **Technisch ontwerp** (Technical design) -- capacity, technology, configuration
3. **Locatie** (Location) -- map/drawing, kadastrale gegevens, omgevingsplan status
4. **Planning** (Timeline) -- milestones, realisatietermijn compliance
5. **Financieringsplan** (Financing plan) -- CAPEX, OPEX, financing structure
6. **Organisatie** (Organization) -- project team, EPC contractor, O&M
7. **Risico-analyse** (Risk analysis) -- technical, financial, permit, grid risks
8. **Energieopbrengst** (Energy yield) -- production estimate with methodology

**Common Rejection Reasons:**
- Too brief project description (RVO requires detailed narratives)
- Incomplete financing plan
- Unrealistic energy yield calculations
- Missing risk analysis for permit and grid connection

**Shorter version:** Available for installations < 1 MW but must still cover all core elements.

### 3.2 Vergunningen (Permits)

**Major simplification since 2025:**
- Vergunning voor technische bouwactiviteit (technical building permit) **no longer required** with SDE++ application for most categories
- This saves significant time in the pre-application phase

**Permits Still Required (depending on project type):**

| Permit | When Required | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Omgevingsvergunning (spatial) | Ground-mounted systems, new structures | Must be irrevocable (onherroepelijk) or at minimum granted |
| Watervergunning | Systems near water, discharge, groundwater | Waterschap permit |
| Natuurvergunning | If Natura 2000 or soortenbescherming affected | Via Omgevingswet |
| Opsporingsvergunning | Geothermal projects | Mijnbouwwet, via RVO/Minister EZK |
| Graafvergunning | Underground cabling/piping | NOT required with SDE++ application |

**Key Rule:** Partial permits for:
- Underground cabling (kabels en leidingen)
- Fencing (hekwerk)
- Underground piping (leidingwerk)
...do **not** need to be submitted with the SDE++ application.

### 3.3 Transportindicatie (Grid Connection Indication)

- Required from the netbeheerder (TenneT, Liander, Stedin, Enexis)
- Confirms that grid connection is technically feasible
- Not a binding agreement; actual connection agreement (ATO) comes later
- In areas with transportschaarste: transportindicatie may be conditional or delayed
- Cable pooling arrangements can help satisfy this requirement

### 3.4 Bankgarantie (Bank Guarantee)

- Required after SDE++ beschikking (grant decision) is issued
- Guarantees project realization; forfeit if project not completed in time
- Amount varies by technology and capacity (typically EUR 10-25 per kW)
- Must be issued by recognized financial institution
- Released upon successful commissioning and first production

## 4. Application Process

### Step-by-Step

1. **Preparation** (months before opening)
   - Complete haalbaarheidsstudie
   - Obtain required permits
   - Secure transportindicatie from netbeheerder
   - Prepare bankgarantie commitment

2. **Draft Application** (2 weeks before opening)
   - Digital application environment opens ~2 weeks before official opening
   - Prepare application in draft (concept-aanvraag)
   - Upload all attachments
   - Verify completeness

3. **Submission** (during application window)
   - Tick "Verklaring en ondertekening" (declaration and signature)
   - Click "Ondertekenen en verzenden" (sign and submit)
   - Application is definitief (final) upon submission

4. **Assessment** (up to 13 weeks)
   - RVO assesses completeness and substance
   - May request additional information (hersteltermijn)
   - Ranking by subsidy intensity (EUR/ton CO2 or EUR/kWh)

5. **Decision** (beschikking)
   - Positive: subsidy granted with specific terms
   - Negative: rejection with reasoning
   - Beschikking includes: basisbedrag, realisatietermijn, maximum subsidy

6. **Realization**
   - Build installation within realisatietermijn
   - Install gross production meter (bruto productiemeter) with EAN-code
   - Commission and start production
   - Submit bankgarantie (if not already)

### Realisatietermijn (Realization Deadline)

Technology-specific deadlines for project completion after beschikking:

| Technology | Typical Realisatietermijn |
|---|---|
| Zon-PV (< 1 MW) | 1.5 years |
| Zon-PV (>= 1 MW) | 3 years |
| Wind op land | 4 years |
| Geothermie | 4-5 years |
| Biomassa | 4 years |
| CCS/CCU | 4-5 years |

**Extension:** Possible in exceptional circumstances (force majeure, permit delays) but not guaranteed. Must be requested before deadline expires.

**Consequence of Missing Deadline:** Subsidy beschikking revoked; bankgarantie forfeited.

## 5. Monitoring and Reporting

### During Subsidy Period (12-15 years)

**EAN-code and Production Meter:**
- Each productie-installatie (production installation) requires a unique EAN-code
- Gross production meter (bruto productiemeter) required
- Meter readings reported to RVO (typically via CertiQ for electricity, Vertogas for green gas)

**Annual Reporting:**
- Production data submitted via RVO's digital system
- Subsidy payments calculated based on actual production x (basisbedrag - correctiebedrag)
- Correctiebedrag (correction amount) reflects market energy prices

**Compliance Obligations:**
- Installation must match beschikking specifications
- Material changes require RVO approval
- Annual declaration of continued operation
- Biomass sustainability reporting (SBP/FSC documentation)

## 6. ISDE (Investeringssubsidie Duurzame Energie)

### Overview
ISDE provides investment subsidies for smaller-scale sustainable energy installations, complementing the SDE++ (which is an operating subsidy).

### Eligible Technologies
- Warmtepompen (heat pumps): air-source, ground-source, hybrid
- Zonneboilers (solar water heaters)
- Isolatiemaatregelen (insulation) when combined with heat pump
- Small wind turbines (specific categories)
- Pellet stoves (specific categories)

### Key Differences from SDE++

| Aspect | SDE++ | ISDE |
|---|---|---|
| Type | Operating subsidy (exploitatiesubsidie) | Investment subsidy (investeringssubsidie) |
| Scale | Large (> 15 kWp typical) | Small (residential/SME) |
| Payment | Per kWh/GJ over 12-15 years | One-time lump sum |
| Application | Before installation | After installation (within 12 months) |
| Permits | Required before/with application | Usually not required |
| Budget | EUR 8+ billion per round | ~EUR 400 million/year |

### Application Process
- Apply via rvo.nl after installation is operational
- Submit within 12 months of installation date
- Provide installer invoice, product specifications
- Payment within 8 weeks of approval

## 7. Interaction Between SDE++ and Other Permits

### Sequencing Strategy
```
1. Haalbaarheidsstudie (months 1-3)
2. Omgevingsvergunning application (months 2-8)
3. Transportindicatie from netbeheerder (months 2-6)
4. Bankgarantie arrangement (months 4-6)
5. SDE++ application (during fall application window)
6. Beschikking (13 weeks after close)
7. Final permits, grid connection agreement (parallel)
8. Construction and commissioning (within realisatietermijn)
```

### Risk: Permit Timing
- If omgevingsvergunning is under bezwaar/beroep at time of SDE++ application: risk of rejection
- RVO prefers onherroepelijke vergunning (irrevocable permit) but may accept granted-but-not-yet-irrevocable
- Strategy: apply for permits well before SDE++ round opens
