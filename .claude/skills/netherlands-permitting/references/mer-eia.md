# Environmental Impact Assessment (MER/m.e.r.) in the Netherlands

## 1. Legal Framework Under the Omgevingswet

### Legislation
- **Omgevingswet afdeling 16.4** -- procedural rules for milieueffectrapportage
- **Omgevingsbesluit hoofdstuk 11** -- detailed MER provisions
- **Omgevingsbesluit Bijlage V** -- list of MER-plichtige and m.e.r.-beoordelingsplichtige activities
- **Omgevingsregeling** -- technical/administrative requirements for MER content

### Key Change from Old System
Under the old Wet milieubeheer + Besluit milieueffectrapportage (Besluit mer):
- Two separate lists: C-list (MER-plicht) and D-list (m.e.r.-beoordeling)
- D-list had drempelwaarden (thresholds) below which a vormvrije m.e.r.-beoordeling applied

Under the Omgevingswet + Omgevingsbesluit:
- **Single list: Bijlage V** combining both categories
- **Drempelwaarden largely removed** for m.e.r.-beoordeling
- **One unified procedure** for project-m.e.r.-beoordeling (replacing two procedures)
- More activities subject to m.e.r.-beoordeling (broader scope)

## 2. Types of MER

### Plan-MER (Strategic Environmental Assessment)
**Required for:**
- Omgevingsvisie (national, provincial, municipal)
- Omgevingsplan changes that are kaderstellend (framework-setting) for future MER-plichtige activities
- Programs that set frameworks for future project decisions

**When is an omgevingsplan change "kaderstellend"?**
- When it creates spatial room for activities listed in Bijlage V
- Example: rezoning agricultural land to industrial for data center development
- Example: designating new glastuinbouwconcentratiegebied

### Project-MER (Environmental Impact Statement)
**Required for:**
- Activities listed in Bijlage V kolom 2 that meet the specified criteria
- Also called besluit-m.e.r. (decision MER)

### Project-m.e.r.-Beoordeling (EIA Screening)
**Required for:**
- Activities listed in Bijlage V kolom 1 where kolom 2 states "niet van toepassing" or where thresholds in kolom 2 are not met
- Bevoegd gezag determines whether significant environmental effects are likely
- If yes: full MER required; if no: project proceeds without MER

## 3. How to Read Bijlage V

### Column Structure (Left to Right)

| Column | Content | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Kolom 1** | Project description | Identify if your project appears in the list |
| **Kolom 2** | m.e.r.-plicht criteria (if applicable) | Check if direct MER obligation applies |
| **Kolom 3** | m.e.r.-beoordelingsplicht criteria | If kolom 2 doesn't apply, m.e.r.-beoordeling applies |
| **Kolom 4** | Relevant besluit (decision) | Which permit decision triggers the MER |

### Decision Logic
```
1. Is your project in kolom 1?
   No  --> No MER obligation
   Yes --> Continue to step 2

2. Does kolom 2 apply and are thresholds met?
   Yes --> Direct project-MER obligation (m.e.r.-plicht)
   No  --> Continue to step 3

3. Kolom 3 applies: m.e.r.-beoordeling required
   Bevoegd gezag assesses significance of environmental effects
   Significant --> Full MER required
   Not significant --> No MER needed (reasoned decision required)
```

### Categories in Bijlage V (~98 projects in 13 categories)
Key categories relevant to energy and infrastructure:

| Category | Example Activities |
|---|---|
| **A: Landbouw en visserij** | Intensieve veehouderij, glastuinbouw expansion |
| **B: Winning delfstoffen** | Aardwarmte (geothermie), gas extraction |
| **C: Energie-industrie** | Thermische centrales, windturbines, hoogspanningsleidingen |
| **D: Productie/verwerking metalen** | Steel, aluminum production |
| **E: Minerale industrie** | Cement, glass production |
| **F: Chemische industrie** | Chemical plants, refineries |
| **G: Voedingsmiddelen** | Food processing above thresholds |
| **H: Textiel/leer/hout/papier** | Large industrial processing |
| **I: Rubber en kunststof** | Polymer production |
| **J: Infrastructuur en ruimte** | J10: industrieterrein; J11: stedelijk ontwikkelingsproject |

| **M: Overig** | Seveso installations, military |

## 4. Sector-Specific MER Triggers

### Data Centers
**No standalone data center category in Bijlage V.**

Triggers via:
- **J10 (Industrieterrein)**: if omgevingsplan creates space for industrial site development where data center will be located
- **J11 (Stedelijk ontwikkelingsproject)**: for large data centers in urban/suburban settings; no fixed threshold but m.e.r.-beoordeling required
- **C (Energie-industrie)**: if thermal power generation on-site (emergency generators aggregating to significant capacity)
- **Plan-MER**: almost always when gemeente rezones for large data center

### BESS (Battery Energy Storage Systems)
**No standalone BESS/EOS category in Bijlage V.**

Triggers via:
- **Hazardous substance storage**: if lithium-ion storage exceeds Seveso/Bevi thresholds
- **C (Energie-industrie)**: for very large BESS (> 100 MW) connected to energy infrastructure
- **J10 (Industrieterrein)**: if new industrial area created for BESS park
- **Plan-MER**: when omgevingsplan change creates space for BESS development

### District Heating
Triggers via:
- **C (Energie-industrie)**: for large WKK or biomass heating installations
- **B (Winning delfstoffen)**: for aardwarmte (geothermal) extraction
- **J10/J11**: for large warmtenet infrastructure projects requiring plan changes
- **Plan-MER**: for municipal heat transition plans (warmtetransitievisie) that are kaderstellend

### Greenhouses (Glastuinbouw)
Triggers via:
- **A (Landbouw)**: for large new greenhouse complexes
- **Plan-MER**: when glastuinbouwconcentratiegebied is designated or expanded
- **Associated activities**: WKK, aardwarmte, water extraction may independently trigger MER

### Wind Energy
- **Kolom 2 (m.e.r.-plicht)**: windpark >= 15 turbines or >= 10 MW capacity; or single turbine >= 15 MW
- **Kolom 3 (m.e.r.-beoordeling)**: windpark >= 3 turbines or >= 2 MW capacity

### Solar Energy
- **No direct MER trigger** for ground-mounted solar
- **J10/J11**: may apply if large solar park requires plan change
- **Plan-MER**: if omgevingsplan change is kaderstellend

## 5. MER Procedure

### Project-MER Procedure (m.e.r.-plicht)

**Step 1: Notification and Scope (Kennisgeving en Reikwijdte)**
- Initiator notifies bevoegd gezag of intent to prepare MER
- Bevoegd gezag publishes kennisgeving
- Public can submit zienswijzen (views) on scope
- Commissie m.e.r. advises on scope (reikwijdte en detailniveau)
- Results in: Notitie Reikwijdte en Detailniveau (NRD)

**Step 2: MER Preparation**
- Initiator prepares MER rapport
- Content: description of project, alternatives, environmental effects, mitigation measures
- Must address effects on all relevant environmental themes
- Commissie m.e.r. may provide interim advice

**Step 3: Public Review and Commissie m.e.r. Assessment**
- MER submitted with permit application
- Public review period (zienswijzen)
- Commissie m.e.r. provides toetsingsadvies (review advice)
- Bevoegd gezag must wait for Commissie m.e.r. advice before deciding

**Step 4: Decision**
- Bevoegd gezag considers MER, zienswijzen, and Commissie m.e.r. advice
- Decision on omgevingsvergunning incorporating MER findings
- Must motivate how MER and advice have been taken into account

### m.e.r.-Beoordeling Procedure

**Step 1: Aanmeldingsnotitie (Notification)**
- Initiator submits aanmeldingsnotitie to bevoegd gezag
- Describes project, location, potential environmental effects
- Not a full MER; but substantive enough to enable assessment

**Step 2: Beoordeling (Screening Decision)**
- Bevoegd gezag assesses whether significant environmental effects are likely
- Criteria: nature, scale, location sensitivity, cumulation with other projects
- Must be reasoned decision (gemotiveerd besluit)
- Timeline: within 6 weeks of receiving complete notification

**Step 3: Outcome**
- **Significant effects expected**: full project-MER required
- **No significant effects**: project proceeds; screening decision published
- Screening decision can be challenged in beroep against final permit decision

## 6. Commissie voor de Milieueffectrapportage

### Role
- Independent advisory body (not a decision-maker)
- Provides scoping advice (reikwijdte en detailniveau)
- Reviews MER quality (toetsingsadvies)
- Can flag deficiencies requiring supplement
- Advice is not binding but bevoegd gezag must motivate any deviation

### Timeline Impact
- Scoping advice: typically 6-8 weeks
- Toetsingsadvies: typically 6-8 weeks after receiving MER
- If MER supplement needed: additional 2-4 months
- Total MER procedure addition to permit timeline: 6-18 months

## 7. Natura 2000 and Species Protection

### Stikstofdepositie (Nitrogen Deposition)

**Legal Context:**
The 2019 Raad van State ruling invalidating the PAS (Programma Aanpak Stikstof) created a major permitting bottleneck. Any project causing stikstofdepositie > 0.00 mol/ha/jaar on a Natura 2000-gebied requires assessment.

**AERIUS Calculator:**
- National calculation tool for nitrogen deposition
- Mandatory for all projects with NOx or NH3 emissions
- Input: emission sources (location, height, emission rate)
- Output: depositie per hectare per Natura 2000-gebied
- Result > 0.00 mol/ha/jaar: further assessment required

**Relevant Sources of Emissions:**
- WKK/boiler installations (NOx)
- Emergency diesel generators (NOx)
- Construction traffic and equipment (NOx)
- Agricultural activities (NH3)
- Vehicle movements to/from site (NOx)

**If Depositie > 0.00 mol/ha/jaar:**
1. **Intern salderen** (internal offsetting): reduce existing emissions on same site
2. **Extern salderen** (external offsetting): acquire emission rights from closing sources
3. **ADC-toets** (Alternatives-Compelling Interest-Compensation):
   - No realistic alternatives exist
   - Compelling public interest (dwingende reden van groot openbaar belang)
   - Compensation measures for Natura 2000 damage
4. **Ecologische beoordeling**: assess whether depositie actually causes significant effect

### Soortenbescherming (Species Protection)
- Omgevingswet (incorporating Wnb provisions): protection of beschermde soorten
- Quickscan flora en fauna: initial assessment of protected species presence
- If protected species found: nader onderzoek (detailed survey)
- Ontheffing (exemption): possible under strict conditions
- Timing restrictions: construction may be limited during breeding/hibernation seasons
- Mitigatie and compensatie measures required

### Natura 2000 Passende Beoordeling (Appropriate Assessment)
- Required if significant effects on Natura 2000 cannot be excluded
- Detailed ecological assessment of:
  - Stikstofdepositie impact
  - Verstoring (disturbance): noise, light, vibration
  - Hydrologische effecten (water level, quality changes)
  - Habitatverlies (habitat loss)
- Results feed into vergunning decision (Natura 2000-activiteit, Omgevingswet Art. 5.1)

## 8. Other Environmental Assessment Topics

### PFAS / Bodemkwaliteit (Soil Quality)
- PFAS normen (standards) for grondverzet (soil movement)
- Bodemonderzoek (soil investigation) required before construction
- NEN 5740 protocol for soil sampling
- If contamination found: bodemsanering (remediation) may be needed
- Bkl omgevingswaarden for bodemkwaliteit

### Geluid (Noise)
- Bkl omgevingswaarden for geluid at geluidgevoelige gebouwen
- WHO guidelines referenced for health-based noise standards
- Industrial noise: Bal general rules + potential maatwerkvoorschrift
- Construction noise: separate temporary permit conditions (APV)

### Externe Veiligheid (External Safety)
- QRA (Quantitative Risk Assessment) for installations with dangerous substances
- Plaatsgebonden risico (PR): max 10^-6/year at kwetsbare objecten
- Groepsrisico (GR): assessment against orienteringswaarde
- Verantwoordingsplicht: bevoegd gezag must justify acceptance of groepsrisico
- Relevant for BESS (PGS 37), fuel storage, chemical processes

### Watertoets (Water Assessment)
- Required for omgevingsplan changes and large projects
- Assessment of impact on waterhuishouding (water management)
- Waterschap advises on water-related aspects
- Topics: waterberging, waterafvoer, waterkwaliteit, grondwater

## 9. Legal Remedies

### Bezwaar (Objection)
- Against m.e.r.-beoordeling decision: no separate bezwaar
- Challenged together with underlying permit decision
- Timeline: 6 weeks after permit decision

### Beroep (Appeal)
- MER-related issues can be raised in beroep against permit
- Rechtbank (court) reviews MER adequacy
- Raad van State (Council of State) as highest administrative court
- Common grounds: inadequate alternatives assessment, incomplete scope, deficient monitoring

### Voorlopige Voorziening (Interim Relief)
- Urgent cases: request suspension of permit pending beroep
- Voorzieningenrechter assesses interests
- Can effectively halt construction during legal proceedings
