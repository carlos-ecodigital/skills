# Follow-Up Cadences

Tier-based follow-up timing and personalization requirements for W5.

---

## Tier A (Hot) — 24-Hour Protocol

- **Hour 0-4:** Route to counter-party-intel W2 (quick profile)
- **Hour 4-8:** Route to sales-intake Mode A or B
- **Hour 8-16:** Route to ops-outreachops — personalized follow-up email
  - MUST reference specific conversation topic
  - MUST include whatever we promised to send
  - MUST propose specific next step with date
- **Hour 16-24:** Email sent (after CEO approval)
- **Day 3-5:** Second touch — new value-add (case study, article, intro offer)
- **Day 10-14:** Third touch — alternative channel (LinkedIn message, mutual contact re-intro)
- **If no response after touch 3:** Move to Tier B cadence

## Tier B (Warm) — 48-72 Hour Protocol

- **Hour 0-24:** Route to counter-party-intel W2 (quick profile)
- **Hour 24-48:** Route to ops-outreachops — structured follow-up email
  - Reference event + conversation (lighter personalization)
  - Include relevant DE collateral matching their interest area
  - Propose "let's find time" (less specific than Tier A)
- **Hour 48-72:** Email sent
- **Day 7-10:** Second touch if no response
- **Day 21:** Final touch or move to nurture

## Tier C (Cool) — 1-Week Protocol

- **Day 1-3:** HubSpot record created (already done in W3)
- **Day 3-7:** ops-outreachops — lightweight "nice to meet you" email
- **Day 30:** Add to relevant nurture sequence

## Tier V (Vendor) — Evaluation Track

- **Day 1-3:** vendor-lifecycle evaluation record created
- **Day 3-7:** If relevant to active procurement → vendor-negotiation engagement strategy
- **Day 7-14:** Request capabilities deck / pricing
- **Ongoing:** Track in vendor pipeline

---

## Follow-Up Personalization Rules

- **Tier A:** MUST reference specific conversation detail ("you mentioned X")
- **Tier B:** Reference event + their general interest area ("at [Event], we discussed X")
- **Tier C:** Generic but warm ("great connecting at [Event]")
- **If introduction chain exists:** ALWAYS reference introducer ("X suggested we connect")
- **If they visited our booth:** Acknowledge their interest ("thanks for stopping by our booth")
- **If inbound:** Acknowledge their outreach ("thanks for reaching out about X")

---

## Escalation Rules

- Tier A follow-up not sent within 24hr → escalate to Carlos
- Promise-we-made not fulfilled by deadline → escalate to owner + Carlos
- Promise-they-made not received by expected date → gentle ping

---

## Email Subject Line Patterns

| Tier | Pattern | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| A | "[Specific topic] -- following up from [event]" | "Liquid cooling for your Amsterdam expansion -- following up from Datacloud" |
| B | "Great connecting at [event] -- [value prop]" | "Great connecting at Datacloud -- AI colocation with built-in heat recovery" |
| C | "Good to meet you at [event]" | "Good to meet you at Datacloud" |
| V | "Re: [category] capabilities for Digital Energy" | "Re: Cooling system capabilities for Digital Energy" |

- Never use generic "Following up" or "Touching base" -- these get ignored
- Always reference the specific conversation or event in the subject

---

## Attachment Guidance

| Tier | Collateral | When to Attach |
|------|-----------|----------------|
| A | Tailored one-pager matching their use case + any promised materials | Always -- include in first email |
| B | General capability deck or relevant case study | First email only if conversation was substantive |
| C | None in first email -- add in second touch if they respond | Keep lightweight |
| V | RFQ template or capability requirements doc | Only if we initiated the vendor conversation |

---

## CC/BCC Rules

- **CC Carlos:** When follow-up is to a CEO/C-suite contact that Carlos met personally. When the contact explicitly referenced Carlos by name.
- **CC Jelmer:** When follow-up involves technical details, specs, or vendor coordination.
- **Never CC both** unless the contact explicitly engaged with both.
- **BCC:** Never use BCC for follow-ups -- it breaks trust if discovered.

---

## Out-of-Office Handling

- If OOO reply received: reschedule the next cadence step to OOO return date + 1 business day
- If OOO with delegate: do NOT contact the delegate unless they are also in our pipeline
- Update HubSpot note: "OOO until [date]. Next touch rescheduled."
- For Tier A with urgent timeline: check if they provided a mobile number -- consider WhatsApp if relationship supports it

---

## "They Responded But Declined" Protocol

- **Soft decline** ("not the right time", "maybe later"): Move to nurture. Add tag `soft-decline-[date]`. Revisit in 90 days.
- **Hard decline** ("not interested", "wrong fit"): Close respectfully. Archive in HubSpot with reason. Do NOT add to nurture unless they explicitly say "reach out later."
- **Redirect** ("talk to my colleague X"): Treat as a warm referral. Create new contact record for X with intro chain from original contact. Tier based on context.
- Always respond graciously to any decline -- the relationship outlasts the deal.
