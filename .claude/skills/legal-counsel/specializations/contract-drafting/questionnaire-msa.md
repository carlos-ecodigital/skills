# Master Services Agreement -- Intake Questionnaire

This questionnaire gathers all information needed to generate a complete MSA (Master Services Agreement) with a modular SOW (Statement of Work) framework. Questions are grouped into 7 parts. Present in 3 conversational batches.

**Key design principle:** An MSA establishes the umbrella legal terms; individual SOWs define scope, fees, and timelines for each engagement. The MSA questionnaire focuses on governance and risk allocation. SOW-specific questions are handled at SOW creation time.


## Part A: Parties and Relationship (6 questions)

**A1.** What is the full legal name and entity type of the service provider?
*Include entity type suffix (BV, AS, Ltd, LLC). Must match registered name.*

**A2.** What is the provider's jurisdiction of incorporation and registration number?

**A3.** What is the full legal name and entity type of the client?
*Include entity type suffix. Must match registered name.*

**A4.** What is the client's jurisdiction of incorporation and registration number?

**A5.** Is this a new relationship, or is the MSA replacing an existing agreement?
*If replacing, identify the prior agreement so that transition provisions can be included.*

**A6.** Will the MSA cover services to the client's Affiliates (group-wide)?
*If yes, specify whether Affiliates can issue SOWs directly or only through the named client entity. Consider liability implications (each Affiliate liable for its own SOWs, or parent guarantee).*


## Part B: Scope Framework (5 questions)

**B1.** What broad categories of services will be covered under this MSA?
*E.g., consulting, technology/IT development, managed services, maintenance, EPC, energy infrastructure. The MSA scope should be broad enough to accommodate all anticipated SOWs.*

**B2.** How will individual engagements be documented?
*Standard: SOW (Statement of Work) signed by both parties under the MSA. Alternatives: work order, change order, purchase order. Specify the required form and approval process.*

**B3.** Is there a preferred SOW template or minimum content requirements?
*Recommended minimum: engagement title, scope of work, deliverables, acceptance criteria, fees, timeline, key personnel, specific terms (if deviating from MSA defaults).*

**B4.** What is the document hierarchy in case of conflict?
*Standard: MSA prevails over SOW unless the SOW expressly states otherwise. Alternative: SOW prevails (less common). Exception: DPA always prevails for data protection matters.*

**B5.** What standard of performance applies?
*Options: "reasonable skill and care" (default, lower standard), "Good Industry Practice" (higher standard), "fitness for purpose" (strict liability -- unusual for services). This becomes the MSA default; individual SOWs can set higher standards.*


## Part C: Commercial Defaults (6 questions)

**C1.** What is the default payment structure across SOWs?
*Options: time and materials (with rate card in MSA schedule), fixed fee (per SOW), milestone-based (per SOW), retainer. The MSA typically establishes default terms; SOWs can override.*

**C2.** What is the default rate card or pricing mechanism?
*Provide the rate card to include as a schedule, or specify: "rates to be agreed per SOW." If a rate card exists, specify annual escalation mechanism (CPI, fixed %, or negotiated).*

**C3.** What are the invoicing and payment terms?
*Default: invoiced monthly in arrears, payable within 30 days. Specify: frequency, advance vs. arrears, payment window, electronic invoicing requirements.*

**C4.** What currency are fees denominated in?
*If multi-currency, specify the default and how exchange rate risk is allocated.*

**C5.** What is the annual fee review mechanism?
*Options: CPI-linked escalation, fixed annual increase (e.g., 3%), mutual agreement, or no automatic increase.*

**C6.** Are there volume discount or minimum commitment provisions?
*E.g., if aggregate annual fees exceed EUR {{AMOUNT}}, a discount of {{PERCENTAGE}} applies. Minimum commitment: client guarantees a minimum level of spending.*


## Part D: IP and Data Protection Defaults (5 questions)

**D1.** What is the default IP ownership model?
*Options: (a) client-owns all foreground IP, (b) provider-retains with licence to client, (c) joint ownership, (d) to be agreed per SOW. Most MSAs establish a default that SOWs can override.*

**D2.** Does the provider have pre-existing IP (Background IP) that will be used across engagements?
*If yes, identify it. The MSA should include a standing licence for the client to use Background IP in connection with the deliverables.*

**D3.** Will services involve processing personal data?
*If yes (or likely across multiple SOWs): a DPA should be included as an MSA schedule, with SOW-specific data processing particulars appended per engagement.*

**D4.** What is the default confidentiality duration post-termination?
*Standard: 3-5 years. Trade secrets: indefinite. This becomes the MSA default.*

**D5.** Are there any specific data security or compliance standards required?
*E.g., ISO 27001, SOC 2 Type II, NEN 7510 (healthcare), specific encryption standards. These may apply across all SOWs or only specific ones.*


## Part E: Risk Allocation Defaults (6 questions)

**E1.** What is the aggregate liability cap?
*Options: fixed amount, multiple of fees per SOW or annually, total MSA fees. Specify whether the cap is per-SOW or aggregate across all SOWs.*

**E2.** Should the consequential damages exclusion be mutual?
*Standard: yes, mutual exclusion of indirect/consequential loss. Identify any carve-outs beyond the standard (fraud, death/personal injury, uncapped by law).*

**E3.** Are there additional liability carve-outs?
*Common carve-outs (not subject to the cap): breach of confidentiality, data protection breach, IP infringement, wilful misconduct. Each is negotiable.*

**E4.** What indemnification provisions are required?
*Standard: provider indemnifies for IP infringement and negligence. Client indemnifies for client materials. Additional: regulatory fines, third-party claims. Subject to cap or carved out?*

**E5.** What insurance does the provider need to maintain?
*Minimum types and amounts: professional indemnity (PI/E&O), public liability, employers' liability, cyber liability. Specify minimum coverage amounts and run-off period.*

**E6.** What force majeure events should be included or excluded?
*Standard list + consider: pandemic/epidemic (include/exclude given COVID precedent), cyberattack, sanctions, supply chain disruption.*


## Part F: Term and Termination (5 questions)

**F1.** What is the MSA initial term?
*Common: 12, 24, or 36 months. The MSA term governs the umbrella agreement; individual SOWs can have shorter terms within the MSA term.*

**F2.** What happens to active SOWs if the MSA terminates?
*Options: (a) active SOWs terminate with the MSA, (b) active SOWs continue until their own expiry/termination (MSA terms survive for those SOWs), (c) transition period applies.*

**F3.** What is the termination for convenience notice period?
*Specify for MSA level and for individual SOW level separately. Common: 90 days for MSA, 30 days for SOW.*

**F4.** What is the cure period for material breach?
*Common: 30 days. Consider whether the period should differ for payment breaches (shorter, e.g., 14 days) vs. performance breaches (longer, e.g., 45 days).*

**F5.** Is transition assistance required on termination?
*If yes: mandatory or optional, duration, at MSA rates or agreed rates, scope (knowledge transfer, data migration, parallel running).*


## Part G: Governance and Disputes (4 questions)

**G1.** What governance structure is required?
*E.g., steering committee, quarterly business reviews, escalation ladder, designated relationship managers. Governance provisions are important for long-term MSAs.*

**G2.** What is the governing law?
*Always ask -- never assume. If provider and client are in different jurisdictions, negotiate.*

**G3.** What dispute resolution mechanism is preferred?
*Options: escalation to senior management > mediation > courts or arbitration. Specify institution and seat for arbitration.*

**G4.** What additional schedules are needed?
*Common MSA schedules: (1) rate card, (2) SOW template, (3) SLA framework, (4) DPA, (5) insurance requirements, (6) approved subcontractors, (7) key personnel, (8) acceptable use policy.*
