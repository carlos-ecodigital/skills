# Share Purchase Agreement -- Intake Questionnaire

This questionnaire gathers all information needed to generate a complete, jurisdiction-aware Share Purchase Agreement (SPA). Questions are grouped into 9 parts. Present in 4 conversational batches.

**Key design principle:** An SPA is the most complex commercial agreement type. The questionnaire is comprehensive to ensure all critical provisions are captured. For simpler transactions (single seller, no earn-out, no locked-box), many questions can be adaptively skipped.


## Part A: Parties (6 questions)

**A1.** What is the full legal name, entity type, jurisdiction, and registration number of the buyer?
*If the buyer is a NewCo or SPV, identify the ultimate parent and whether a parent guarantee is expected.*

**A2.** What is the full legal name, entity type, jurisdiction, and registration number of each seller?
*List all sellers. If multiple sellers, specify whether they are jointly and severally liable or severally liable only (proportion basis).*

**A3.** Are there any guarantors (seller's parent, buyer's parent)?
*Guarantors may be required to back warranty claims, purchase price obligations, or earn-out payments.*

**A4.** Is there a management seller (founder/CEO selling shares while remaining with the target)?
*If yes, the SPA may need separate treatment for the management seller's warranties, non-compete, and service agreement.*

**A5.** What is the full legal name, entity type, jurisdiction, and registration number of the target company?
*The company whose shares are being purchased. Include: principal business activity, number of employees, key jurisdictions of operation.*

**A6.** Does the target have subsidiaries?
*If yes, list all material subsidiaries (name, jurisdiction, ownership %). The SPA warranties will cover the entire group.*


## Part B: Transaction Structure (7 questions)

**B1.** What percentage of the target's shares is being acquired?
*100%, majority stake (specify %), minority stake (specify %). Partial acquisitions require additional provisions (SHA, reserved matters, pre-emption).*

**B2.** What share classes exist in the target?
*Ordinary shares, preference shares, convertible instruments, options/warrants. Specify whether all classes are being acquired.*

**B3.** What is the headline purchase price?
*Enterprise value, equity value, and price per share. Specify currency. If the price is indicative/subject to adjustment, state the formula.*

**B4.** What is the price mechanism?
*Options:*
- *Locked-box: fixed price based on reference balance sheet date; no post-completion adjustment; leakage protection*
- *Completion accounts: price adjusted post-completion for net debt, working capital, cash; specify targets and methodologies*
- *Combination: locked-box with limited adjustment mechanisms*

**B5.** Is there deferred consideration?
*If yes: amount, payment schedule, security (escrow, bank guarantee, parent guarantee), acceleration triggers.*

**B6.** Is there earn-out consideration?
*If yes: metric (revenue, EBITDA, EBIT, specific milestones), measurement period, calculation methodology, cap and floor, buyer's conduct of business obligations during earn-out period, dispute resolution for earn-out calculations.*

**B7.** Is there an escrow or holdback?
*Percentage of purchase price held in escrow (typically 5-15%), release schedule, claims procedure, residual release timeline.*


## Part C: Conditions Precedent (5 questions)

**C1.** What are the conditions precedent to completion?
*Common CPs: regulatory/competition clearance, third-party consents (landlords, customers, licensors), financing condition, no material adverse change, completion of reorganisation, shareholder approvals.*

**C2.** Is competition/merger clearance required?
*Identify filing jurisdictions: ACM (NL), Konkurransetilsynet (NO), European Commission, CMA (UK), FTC/DOJ (US), others. Specify thresholds and expected timeline.*

**C3.** Is there a material adverse change (MAC) condition?
*If yes: define MAC (quantitative threshold, carve-outs for general economic/market conditions, industry-wide changes, changes in law). MAC conditions are heavily negotiated.*

**C4.** What is the long-stop date for satisfaction of conditions?
*Date by which all CPs must be satisfied, failing which either party may terminate. Typically 3-6 months. Specify consequences of non-satisfaction (walk-away, reverse break fee).*

**C5.** Which party bears the risk and cost of obtaining regulatory clearances?
*Standard: buyer files; buyer bears filing fees; both cooperate; seller provides information. Specify: "hell or high water" commitment (buyer must divest to obtain clearance) or "reasonable best efforts."*


## Part D: Pre-Completion Covenants (4 questions)

**D1.** What conduct of business restrictions apply between signing and completion?
*Standard: target operates in the ordinary course; no material contracts, disposals, distributions, borrowing, or headcount changes without buyer consent. Specify materiality thresholds.*

**D2.** Are there specific pre-completion actions required?
*E.g., group reorganisation, spin-off of non-target assets, settlement of inter-company balances, termination of related-party agreements, IP assignment.*

**D3.** What access does the buyer have to the target between signing and completion?
*Standard: reasonable access to management, premises, books and records for integration planning. Specify limitations (no customer/supplier contact without consent).*

**D4.** For locked-box: what leakage protections are required?
*Define "leakage" (dividends, management fees, bonuses, inter-company charges, guarantees in favour of seller). Identify permitted leakage (scheduled items). Specify remedy: EUR-for-EUR refund with interest.*


## Part E: Completion Mechanics (4 questions)

**E1.** Where will completion take place?
*Physical location (notary's office for Dutch transactions) or virtual completion (via electronic document exchange). For Dutch share transfers: notarial deed (akte van levering) required.*

**E2.** What are the completion deliverables?
*Standard seller deliverables: signed transfer deed, share certificates, board resignations, statutory registers, powers of attorney, release of security. Standard buyer deliverables: purchase price payment, parent guarantee (if applicable).*

**E3.** How is the purchase price paid at completion?
*Wire transfer to seller's account (or escrow agent). Specify: same-day funds, payment timing relative to other completion steps.*

**E4.** Is simultaneous signing and completion intended, or split signing and completion?
*Simultaneous: no gap between signing and closing (no CPs). Split: signing first, completion after CPs are satisfied. Split is standard for regulated transactions.*


## Part F: Warranties and Indemnities (7 questions)

**F1.** What scope of seller warranties is expected?
*Standard categories: title and capacity, accounts, tax, material contracts, employees, IP, real property, litigation, compliance, insurance, environment, data protection. Specify any areas of particular concern.*

**F2.** Are there specific tax warranties and a tax indemnity?
*Standard for all M&A transactions. The tax indemnity covers pre-completion tax liabilities. Specify: tax indemnity on a EUR-for-EUR basis or subject to the warranty cap?*

**F3.** What is the warranty cap (aggregate limit)?
*Common ranges: 10-30% of purchase price for business warranties; 100% for title and capacity and tax indemnity. Specify separate caps for different warranty categories.*

**F4.** Are there individual claim thresholds (de minimis) and basket (aggregate threshold)?
*De minimis: claims below EUR {{AMOUNT}} are disregarded. Basket: claims are only payable once aggregate qualifying claims exceed EUR {{AMOUNT}} (tipping basket: all claims payable once threshold reached; excess-only basket: only amounts above threshold).*

**F5.** What is the warranty claims period?
*Standard: 18-24 months for business warranties; 7 years for tax warranties; statute of limitations for title/capacity. Specify for each category.*

**F6.** Are there specific indemnities (beyond warranties)?
*Specific indemnities cover known risks identified in DD: pending litigation, environmental remediation, regulatory investigation, specific tax exposure, employee claims. These are typically uncapped or separately capped.*

**F7.** Is warranty and indemnity (W&I) insurance being considered?
*W&I insurance shifts warranty risk from seller to insurer. Impact on SPA: reduced seller liability cap (typically EUR 1), seller-friendly warranty package, buyer-side policy. Specify: buyer-side or seller-side, and whether synthetic (no seller recourse) or traditional.*


## Part G: Restrictive Covenants (4 questions)

**G1.** What non-compete restrictions apply to the seller?
*Scope: business activities covered. Duration: typically 2-3 years (jurisdictions may limit enforceability). Geography: relevant markets. Exceptions: passive investments below {{PERCENTAGE}}.*

**G2.** What non-solicitation restrictions apply?
*No-poach of target employees and no-solicitation of target customers/suppliers. Duration: typically 12-24 months. Scope: key employees, key customers.*

**G3.** Is the management seller entering into a new service/employment agreement?
*If yes, the SPA should be conditional on (or simultaneous with) execution of the employment agreement. Non-compete may be in the employment agreement rather than the SPA.*

**G4.** Are there any ongoing commercial arrangements between seller and target post-completion?
*If yes: transitional services agreement (TSA), supply agreement, licence agreement. These should be agreed and appended to the SPA or executed at completion.*


## Part H: Post-Completion Provisions (3 questions)

**H1.** What is the completion accounts process (if applicable)?
*Preparation: buyer prepares draft completion accounts within {{DAYS}} days. Review: seller reviews within {{DAYS}} days. Dispute: independent accountant determines disputed items. Final adjustment: payment within {{DAYS}} days of determination.*

**H2.** Are there specific integration or transition provisions?
*E.g., access to seller's systems for {{MONTHS}} months, shared services during transition, data migration, re-branding timeline.*

**H3.** What post-completion information rights does the seller retain?
*For earn-out: access to target's management accounts, budget, business plan. For deferred consideration: confirmation of continued business operation. For tax: access to records for historic tax periods.*


## Part I: Governing Law and Disputes (3 questions)

**I1.** What is the governing law of the SPA?
*Typically the target's jurisdiction. For Dutch targets: Dutch law. For Norwegian targets: Norwegian law.*

**I2.** What dispute resolution mechanism is preferred?
*Options: courts (with exclusive jurisdiction), arbitration (ICC, LCIA, NAI, NCC). For completion accounts disputes: expert determination. For M&A, arbitration is increasingly common for cross-border transactions.*

**I3.** Is the disclosure letter process understood?
*The seller prepares a disclosure letter qualifying the warranties against known matters. This is a critical SPA deliverable. Specify: general disclosures permitted, specific disclosures only, or combination.*
