# Service Agreement -- Pre-Signing Checklist

Run this checklist before the agreement is executed. Each item should be verified and confirmed. Flag items with [ATTENTION REQUIRED] if an issue is identified.


## 1. Party Verification

- [ ] Full legal entity names match current company registry records (Bronnoysundregistrene, Companies House, KvK, etc.)
- [ ] Entity types correctly stated (AS, Ltd, BV, LLC, GmbH, etc.)
- [ ] Registration/company numbers verified as current and active
- [ ] Registered addresses are current (check against registry)
- [ ] Signatory has authority to bind the entity (verify: board resolution, power of attorney, delegation of authority, or statutory authority as managing director/daglig leder)
- [ ] Entity is in good standing / not dissolved, struck off, or in insolvency proceedings
- [ ] If either party is an individual (sole proprietor/enkeltpersonforetak), confirm no entity-specific clauses apply incorrectly
- [ ] VAT/MVA registration numbers verified (if tax clauses reference VAT registration)


## 2. Structural Completeness

- [ ] All sections present per the Section Structure Table in SKILL.md
- [ ] All `{{PLACEHOLDER}}` variables replaced with actual values (search for remaining `{{` -- none should remain)
- [ ] All `[REVIEW REQUIRED]` items resolved (search for `[REVIEW` -- none should remain)
- [ ] All internal cross-references correct (verify clause numbers match -- e.g., "as defined in Clause 10.1" actually points to the correct clause)
- [ ] All defined terms used consistently throughout the agreement
- [ ] Every capitalised term is defined in Section 2 (Definitions) -- no undefined capitalised terms
- [ ] No defined terms in Section 2 that are never used in the agreement (remove unused definitions)
- [ ] Clause numbering is sequential with no gaps or duplicate numbers
- [ ] Table of contents (if included) matches actual clause structure
- [ ] All Schedules referenced in the main body are actually attached
- [ ] All Schedules attached are actually referenced in the main body
- [ ] Document hierarchy clause (Interpretation section) correctly reflects the Schedules included


## 3. Commercial Terms Verification

- [ ] Fee amounts / rates match the commercially agreed terms (cross-check with proposal, email confirmation, or term sheet)
- [ ] Payment terms (net 30, net 60, etc.) match commercial agreement
- [ ] Currency correctly specified and consistent throughout
- [ ] Late payment interest rate is stated and is legally permissible in the governing law jurisdiction
- [ ] Liability cap amount is commercially agreed and proportionate to contract value
- [ ] Insurance minimum amounts are achievable / affordable (verify with provider's broker if needed)
- [ ] Expense caps and pre-approval thresholds are realistic
- [ ] Fee escalation mechanism (if any) is clearly defined with a formula or reference index
- [ ] Term length and renewal periods match business requirements
- [ ] Notice periods are practical (long enough for planning, short enough for flexibility)
- [ ] Termination for convenience (if included) has appropriate notice period and financial consequences


## 4. Legal Review Points

- [ ] Governing law clause is consistent with dispute resolution mechanism (e.g., Norwegian law + Norwegian courts/arbitration, not Norwegian law + English courts)
- [ ] Limitation of liability passes the reasonableness test for the chosen jurisdiction (UCTA 1977 for English law; avtaleloven § 36 for Norwegian law)
- [ ] Consequential damages exclusion is clear and the included/excluded heads of loss are appropriate
- [ ] Liability cap carve-outs are specific and limited to genuinely uncappable items (death/personal injury, fraud)
- [ ] Entire agreement clause includes fraud carve-out (cannot exclude liability for own fraud)
- [ ] Force majeure clause reviewed for post-COVID appropriateness (pandemics expressly included/excluded as agreed)
- [ ] Force majeure clause addresses cyber-attacks (included/excluded as agreed)
- [ ] Data protection clauses compliant with applicable legislation (GDPR Art. 28 requirements if processor)
- [ ] DPA (if included) contains all mandatory GDPR Art. 28(3) provisions
- [ ] IP ownership is clearly and unambiguously allocated with no gaps between background IP and foreground IP
- [ ] IP assignment clause uses "present assignment of future rights" formulation (if English law and client-owns model)
- [ ] Termination consequences cover all scenarios (breach, convenience, insolvency, FM, change of control)
- [ ] Survival clause lists all necessary surviving provisions by clause number
- [ ] Indemnification procedure is workable (notice periods, control of defence, cooperation obligations)
- [ ] No conflicting provisions between main body and Schedules
- [ ] Warranties are not inadvertently duplicated or contradicted between sections
- [ ] Set-off rights (if included) are appropriately limited


## 5. Jurisdiction-Specific Checks

### Norway
- [ ] Avtaleloven compliance: no provisions that would be unreasonable under § 36
- [ ] Markedsforingsloven considerations for B2B relationships (§§ 25-26)
- [ ] MVA treatment correct (25% standard, 15% food, 12% transport/hotel, 0% export)
- [ ] Arbeidsgiveravgift implications considered if provider's personnel work on client's premises
- [ ] Aandsverkloven moral rights provisions addressed (cannot fully waive in Norway)
- [ ] Personopplysningsloven supplements to GDPR considered
- [ ] Forsinkelsesrenteloven rate used for late payment interest (or contractual rate stated)
- [ ] Verneting (jurisdiction) correctly specified (e.g., Oslo tingrett)

### England and Wales
- [ ] UCTA 1977 reasonableness test applied to exclusion/limitation clauses (B2B)
- [ ] No oral modification clause enforceable post-MWB Business Exchange v Rock Advertising [2018]
- [ ] Misrepresentation Act 1967 s.3 considered for entire agreement clause
- [ ] Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998 rate referenced or contractual rate stated
- [ ] Electronic signature validity under Electronic Communications Act 2000 and eIDAS
- [ ] If agreement is a deed: witness requirements met, "executed as a deed" language included

### Netherlands
- [ ] Ingebrekestelling (notice of default) requirements addressed -- BW Art. 6:82
- [ ] Boeteclausule (penalty clause) subject to judicial moderation under BW Art. 6:94
- [ ] BW Art. 6:233 and 6:234 compliance for general terms and conditions (algemene voorwaarden)
- [ ] Dutch mandatory law provisions cannot be excluded by choice of foreign law (BW Art. 6:247)

### United States
- [ ] State-specific requirements identified (governing law state)
- [ ] UCC Article 2 applicability assessed (if any goods component)
- [ ] Delaware law considerations (if Delaware-incorporated entities)
- [ ] ESIGN Act / UETA compliance for electronic signatures
- [ ] Choice of law clause includes waiver of jury trial (if desired and enforceable)
- [ ] Arbitration clause complies with Federal Arbitration Act (if arbitration selected)

### Germany
- [ ] AGB-Kontrolle (standard terms review) under §§ 305-310 BGB
- [ ] Schriftform (written form) requirements met
- [ ] Limitation of liability complies with German mandatory provisions (cannot exclude liability for Vorsatz/intent)

### Other Jurisdictions
- [ ] All `[LOCAL LAW NOTE]` flags reviewed and resolved with local counsel
- [ ] Mandatory local law provisions identified and respected


## 6. Execution Formalities

- [ ] Correct number of counterparts prepared (one per party plus any required for filing/registration)
- [ ] Electronic signature provisions match jurisdiction requirements (eIDAS for EU/EEA, ESIGN/UETA for US, esignaturloven for Norway)
- [ ] Witness requirements checked -- deeds in English law require witnesses; most other jurisdictions do not for simple contracts
- [ ] Date format consistent throughout (DD/MM/YYYY for European; MM/DD/YYYY for US; or written out: 10 February 2025)
- [ ] Signature blocks include: company name, signature line, printed name, title, date
- [ ] All Schedules physically attached (or incorporated by reference with clear identification)
- [ ] Page numbering continuous and correct
- [ ] Headers/footers consistent with agreement title and date
- [ ] Version control: confirm this is the final agreed version (not a draft)


## 7. Energy/BESS/JV Additional Checks (if applicable)

- [ ] Performance guarantee metrics are technically achievable (verified by independent engineer or technical adviser)
- [ ] Guaranteed capacity, RTE, and availability figures align with equipment manufacturer specifications
- [ ] Delay LD rate and cap at market-standard levels (daily rate 0.05-0.10%; cap 10-15% of contract price)
- [ ] Performance LD calculation methodology is clear and workable
- [ ] Aggregate LD cap at market standard (typically 20-25% of contract price)
- [ ] Battery degradation warranty aligned with (or exceeds) OEM warranty terms
- [ ] SOH milestones at Year 1, 5, 10, 15, 20 are realistic for the battery chemistry
- [ ] Grid connection responsibility clearly allocated between parties
- [ ] Curtailment risk allocation is commercially agreed and clearly drafted
- [ ] Commissioning process (mechanical completion, provisional acceptance, final acceptance) is clear with defined timeframes
- [ ] Decommissioning security/bond amount is adequate and mechanism for updating is defined
- [ ] Environmental remediation obligations are clear and baseline survey completed (or scheduled)
- [ ] JV governance: deadlock resolution mechanism is functional and has been tested against hypothetical scenarios
- [ ] JV: reserved matters list is complete and thresholds are appropriate
- [ ] JV: exit mechanisms (tag-along, drag-along, put/call) are clearly defined with fair valuation methodology
