**BETWEEN:**

**BACKGROUND:**

(B) The Parties wish to record the terms on which Confidential Information will be disclosed and received.

The Parties agree as follows:


## 1. Definitions and Interpretation

1.1 In this Agreement, unless the context requires otherwise:

"**Affiliate**" means, in relation to a Party, any entity that directly or indirectly controls, is controlled by, or is under common control with that Party, where "control" means the ownership of more than 50% of the voting rights or equivalent ownership interest;

"**Confidential Information**" means all information (whether technical, commercial, financial, or otherwise) disclosed by the Discloser {{/ a Party}} to the Recipient {{/ the other Party}} in connection with the Purpose, whether in writing, orally, electronically, or by inspection, that:
(a) is marked as confidential or proprietary; or
(b) by its nature or the circumstances of disclosure, would reasonably be understood to be confidential,

including {{SPECIFIC_CATEGORIES}};

"**Disclosing Party**" means the Party disclosing Confidential Information;

"**Receiving Party**" means the Party receiving Confidential Information;

"**Representatives**" means, in relation to a Party, its Affiliates, and its and their respective directors, officers, employees, agents, and professional advisers.

1.2 In this Agreement: (a) headings do not affect interpretation; (b) "including" means "including without limitation"; (c) words in the singular include the plural and vice versa; (d) references to legislation include amendments and re-enactments.


## 2. Confidentiality Obligations

2.1 The Receiving Party shall:
(a) keep all Confidential Information strictly confidential;
(b) not disclose Confidential Information to any person except as permitted under Clause 3;
(c) use Confidential Information solely for the Purpose;
(d) apply no less than reasonable care to protect Confidential Information (and no less than the care it applies to its own confidential information of a similar nature).

2.2 The Receiving Party shall not, without the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party, copy, reproduce, or reduce to writing any Confidential Information except as reasonably necessary for the Purpose.


## 3. Permitted Disclosures

3.1 The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to its Representatives who:
(a) have a genuine need to know for the Purpose;
(b) are informed of the confidential nature of the information; and
(c) are bound by obligations of confidentiality no less restrictive than those in this Agreement (whether by professional duty or written undertaking).

3.2 {{[ALTERNATIVE -- financing sources] The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to its bona fide financing sources and potential co-investors, provided they are bound by obligations of confidentiality no less restrictive than those in this Agreement.}}

3.3 The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to the extent required by Applicable Law, regulation, court order, or the rules of any relevant regulatory authority or stock exchange, provided that (where legally permitted) the Receiving Party:
(a) gives the Disclosing Party prior written notice as soon as reasonably practicable;
(b) consults with the Disclosing Party regarding the scope and manner of disclosure; and
(c) discloses only the minimum information required to comply.

3.4 The Receiving Party shall be responsible for any breach of this Agreement by its Representatives.


## 4. Exclusions

4.1 The obligations in Clause 2 do not apply to information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate:
(a) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party or its Representatives;
(b) was already in the lawful possession of the Receiving Party before disclosure, without restriction as to use or disclosure;
(c) was independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Confidential Information; or
(d) was received from a third party who was not, to the Receiving Party's knowledge, under any obligation of confidentiality in respect of that information.


## 5. No Obligation to Disclose

5.1 Nothing in this Agreement obliges either Party to disclose any particular information to the other. The Disclosing Party makes no representation or warranty, express or implied, as to the accuracy, completeness, or reliability of any Confidential Information disclosed.


## 6. No Implied Rights

6.1 No licence or right is granted under this Agreement to the Receiving Party in respect of any intellectual property rights of the Disclosing Party. All Confidential Information remains the property of the Disclosing Party.

## {{7/8}}. Return and Destruction

{{7/8}}.1 Upon the earlier of: (a) the Disclosing Party's written request, or (b) the expiry or termination of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall promptly:
(a) return to the Disclosing Party all documents, materials, and other tangible items containing Confidential Information; and
(b) destroy all copies, notes, summaries, and analyses derived from Confidential Information in the Receiving Party's possession or control,
and certify such return or destruction in writing within {{RETURN_DAYS}} Business Days.

{{7/8}}.2 The Receiving Party may retain copies of Confidential Information to the extent required by Applicable Law or its internal compliance policies, provided such retained copies remain subject to the confidentiality obligations in this Agreement.

## {{12/13}}. Remedies

{{12/13}}.1 Each Party acknowledges that a breach of this Agreement may cause the Disclosing Party irreparable harm for which damages would not be an adequate remedy. Accordingly, the Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek injunctive or other equitable relief from any court of competent jurisdiction, without the need to prove actual loss and without prejudice to any other rights or remedies.


## {{13/14}}. General

{{13/14}}.1 **No amendment** to this Agreement shall be effective unless in writing and signed by both Parties.

{{13/14}}.2 **Waiver:** failure to exercise a right is not a waiver of that right. A waiver is effective only if in writing.

{{13/14}}.3 **Severability:** if any provision is held invalid, the remaining provisions continue in full force. The invalid provision shall be replaced by a valid provision that most closely reflects the Parties' original intention.

{{13/14}}.4 **Entire agreement:** this Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties in relation to its subject matter and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, and agreements. Nothing in this Clause limits liability for fraud.

{{13/14}}.5 **Assignment:** neither Party may assign this Agreement without the prior written consent of the other Party, except to an Affiliate (provided the assignor remains liable).

{{13/14}}.6 **Counterparts:** this Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which constitutes an original. Electronic signatures shall have the same legal effect as handwritten signatures.

{{13/14}}.7 **Notices:** any notice under this Agreement shall be in writing and delivered to the address specified above (or as updated in writing). Notices are deemed delivered on the Business Day sent (email) or 2 Business Days after posting (recorded mail).
