# NDA / NCNDA -- Intake Questionnaire

This questionnaire gathers all information needed to generate a complete, jurisdiction-aware Non-Disclosure Agreement (or Non-Circumvention and Non-Disclosure Agreement). Questions are grouped into 5 parts. During intake, present in 2-3 conversational batches.


## Part A: Parties and Direction (6 questions)

**A1.** What is the full legal name of the first party (the "Discloser" in one-way, or "Party A" in mutual)?
*Include entity type suffix (e.g., BV, AS, Ltd, LLC). Must match registered name.*

**A2.** What is the first party's jurisdiction of incorporation and registration number?
*Country/state and company registry number (KvK, organisasjonsnummer, Companies House, etc.).*

**A3.** What is the full legal name of the second party (the "Recipient" in one-way, or "Party B" in mutual)?
*Include entity type suffix. Must match registered name.*

**A4.** What is the second party's jurisdiction of incorporation and registration number?

**A5.** Is this NDA mutual (both parties disclose) or one-way (only one party discloses)?
*Mutual is standard for commercial negotiations. One-way is appropriate when only one party will share confidential information (e.g., investor receiving information for evaluation).*

**A6.** Should this agreement include non-circumvention provisions (making it an NCNDA)?
*Non-circumvention prevents a party from bypassing the discloser to deal directly with the discloser's contacts, clients, or suppliers. Common in broker/intermediary, deal origination, and joint venture exploration contexts.*


## Part B: Scope of Confidential Information (5 questions)

**B1.** What is the purpose of the disclosure?
*Describe the reason for sharing confidential information: evaluating a potential transaction, joint venture exploration, commercial partnership, technology evaluation, due diligence, etc. The NDA will restrict use to this stated purpose.*

**B2.** What types of confidential information will be shared?
*Categories to consider: financial data, business plans, customer lists, pricing, technical specifications, trade secrets, source code, IP, personal data, regulatory information. Listing categories helps define scope and tailor protections.*

**B3.** Are there specific exclusions from confidential information that should be stated?
*Standard exclusions (public domain, prior knowledge, independent development, third-party disclosure) are always included. This question is about additional contract-specific exclusions.*

**B4.** Will the disclosed information include personal data (triggering GDPR/data protection obligations)?
*If yes, a separate DPA or data protection annex may be needed.*

**B5.** Should the definition of confidential information be broad (all information disclosed in connection with the purpose) or narrow (only information marked as confidential)?
*Broad definitions provide better protection but require careful exclusion drafting. Narrow definitions are easier to manage but risk gaps. A hybrid approach (marked information + information that a reasonable person would consider confidential) is common.*


## Part C: Term and Duration (4 questions)

**C1.** What is the effective date of the NDA?
*If the parties have already been exchanging information, consider whether the NDA should be backdated to cover prior disclosures.*

**C2.** What is the term of the NDA (the period during which disclosures may be made)?
*Common terms: 12 months, 24 months, "until completion or abandonment of the Purpose." If no natural end date, specify a fixed term.*

**C3.** How long should confidentiality obligations survive after the NDA terminates?
*Standard: 2-5 years. Trade secrets: indefinite. Technology: 3-5 years. Financial information: 2-3 years.*

**C4.** Are there any time-sensitive milestones (e.g., exclusivity deadlines, bid submission dates)?
*If the NDA supports a transaction, alignment with deal timeline is important.*


## Part D: Permitted Disclosures and Restrictions (5 questions)

**D1.** Who may receive confidential information within the recipient's organisation?
*Standard: employees, officers, directors, professional advisers who need to know. Consider whether to extend to: Affiliates, contractors, potential financing sources, potential co-investors.*

**D2.** Is the recipient permitted to share information with its financing sources or potential co-investors?
*Common in M&A and project finance contexts. If yes, specify that they must be bound by equivalent confidentiality obligations.*

**D3.** Are there specific standstill or non-solicitation provisions required?
*Standstill: prevents the recipient from acquiring shares/assets of the discloser without consent. Non-solicitation: prevents poaching of employees. Common in M&A NDAs.*

**D4.** Should there be restrictions on making announcements about the existence of the NDA or the discussions?
*Particularly important for listed companies (market abuse regulation), sensitive transactions, or competitive situations.*

**D5.** If this is an NCNDA: what is the scope of non-circumvention?
*Specify: which contacts/relationships are protected, duration of non-circumvention (often longer than confidentiality term), geographic scope, what constitutes "circumvention."*


## Part E: Governing Law and Remedies (4 questions)

**E1.** What is the governing law?
*If parties are in different jurisdictions, the disclosing party's jurisdiction is typical but negotiable.*

**E2.** What dispute resolution mechanism is preferred?
*Options: courts of {{jurisdiction}} with exclusive jurisdiction, or arbitration under {{institution}} rules. For cross-border NDAs, arbitration is often preferred.*

**E3.** Should the NDA include an express acknowledgment that damages are an inadequate remedy and that the discloser is entitled to injunctive relief?
*Standard and recommended. This expedites interim relief applications in most jurisdictions.*

**E4.** Are there any additional provisions required?
*Examples: no implied licence to IP, no obligation to disclose any particular information, return/destruction deadline, specific document marking requirements, no warranty as to accuracy of information.*


## Batch Presentation Order

| Batch | Parts | Questions |
|---|---|---|
| Batch 1 | A + B | Parties, direction, purpose, scope (11 questions) |
| Batch 2 | C + D + E | Term, disclosures, restrictions, governing law (13 questions) |

For simple bilateral NDAs, a single batch may suffice. For NCNDA or M&A NDAs, use 3 batches (split D and E).
