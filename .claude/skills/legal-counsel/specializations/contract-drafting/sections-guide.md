# Service Agreement -- Sections Drafting Guide

Detailed drafting guidance for each section of a professional Service Agreement. For each section: purpose, required elements, key sub-clauses, and drafting notes with jurisdiction-specific considerations.


## Cover Page / Title Block

**Purpose:** Identify the agreement and its parties at a glance.

**Must include:**
- Title: "Service Agreement" (or "Master Services Agreement", "Professional Services Agreement", etc.)
- Date of the agreement (execution date or effective date)
- Party names with defined short-form references
- Optional: confidentiality marking, agreement reference number

**Drafting notes:**
- Use "dated {{AGREEMENT_DATE}}" rather than "dated as of" (which can create uncertainty about actual signing date)
- Include "BETWEEN" and "AND" connectors for two-party agreements
- If effective date differs from execution date, state both clearly


## Section 1: Preamble and Recitals

**Purpose:** Identify the parties, provide context for the agreement, and transition to operative provisions.

**Must include:**
- Full legal names and entity types
- Jurisdiction of incorporation / registration
- Registered address
- Company registration number
- Defined short-form names (e.g., the "**Provider**" and the "**Client**")

**Key sub-clauses:**
- 1.1 Party identification block (WHEREAS / Background / Recitals)
- 1.2 Background statements (3-5 recitals):
  - (A) Provider's business description
  - (B) Client's business description
  - (C) Client's desire to engage Provider for the Services
  - (D) Provider's agreement to perform the Services on the terms set out
  - (E) Additional context (e.g., prior relationship, RFP, tender)
- 1.3 Operative transition: "The Parties agree as follows:" or "IT IS AGREED as follows:"

**Drafting notes:**
- Recitals are interpretive aids, not operative. Avoid placing obligations in recitals.
- Norwegian practice: "BAKGRUNN" or "Background" heading; no "WHEREAS" (Anglo-American convention).
- English law: recitals can be used to resolve ambiguity but do not create legal obligations (Investors Compensation Scheme v West Bromwich [1998]).
- Dutch law: preamble ("overwegingen") commonly used and given interpretive weight.


## Section 2: Definitions and Interpretation

**Purpose:** Define capitalised terms used throughout the agreement to ensure consistency and reduce ambiguity.

**Must include:**
- Alphabetical list of defined terms
- Interpretation rules (headings, singular/plural, gender, statutory references, business days, time)

**Key sub-clauses:**
- 2.1 Definitions (alphabetical). Standard defined terms include:
  - "**Agreement**" -- this agreement including all Schedules
  - "**Affiliate**" -- entity controlling, controlled by, or under common control with a party (define "control" threshold, typically 50%+)
  - "**Applicable Law**" -- all laws, regulations, codes applicable to the performance of the Services
  - "**Business Day**" -- day other than Saturday, Sunday, or public holiday in {{JURISDICTION}}
  - "**Change Control**" -- procedure for varying the Services (cross-ref to clause)
  - "**Client**" -- the entity receiving the Services
  - "**Client Materials**" -- materials provided by Client for use in performing Services
  - "**Commencement Date**" -- date on which Services commence
  - "**Confidential Information**" -- defined in the Confidentiality section (cross-ref)
  - "**Data Protection Legislation**" -- GDPR, local data protection laws (specify)
  - "**Deliverables**" -- specific outputs of the Services
  - "**Effective Date**" -- date this Agreement comes into effect
  - "**Fees**" -- amounts payable for the Services
  - "**Force Majeure Event**" -- defined in the Force Majeure section (cross-ref)
  - "**Intellectual Property Rights**" or "**IPR**" -- patents, copyright, trademarks, design rights, database rights, trade secrets, know-how, and all similar rights
  - "**Key Personnel**" -- named individuals material to Service delivery
  - "**Losses**" -- damages, losses, costs, expenses, liabilities (include or exclude consequential)
  - "**Party**" / "**Parties**" -- Provider and/or Client
  - "**Personnel**" -- employees, agents, subcontractors of a party
  - "**Provider**" -- the entity providing the Services
  - "**Services**" -- as described in Clause 3 and the applicable Schedule
  - "**Term**" -- as defined in Clause 5

- 2.2 Interpretation rules:
  - (a) Headings do not affect interpretation
  - (b) References to clauses and Schedules are to this Agreement
  - (c) "Including" means "including without limitation"
  - (d) Singular includes plural and vice versa
  - (e) References to legislation include amendments, re-enactments, and subordinate legislation
  - (f) "Writing" includes email (unless otherwise specified)
  - (g) A "person" includes an individual, body corporate, partnership, or unincorporated association
  - (h) Document hierarchy: in case of conflict, the main body prevails over Schedules, unless a Schedule expressly states otherwise (e.g., DPA may take precedence for data protection matters)

**Drafting notes:**
- Define terms on first use in the operative body using bold and quotation marks: the "**Services**"
- Do not define terms that are not used, and do not use terms that are not defined
- Norwegian law: avoid importing English-law concepts (e.g., "estoppel") without adaptation
- Dutch law: consider Haviltex standard for interpretation (subjective intent over objective text)


## Section 3: Scope of Services

**Purpose:** Define what the Provider will do (and, critically, what they will not do).

**Must include:**
- Description of Services (or cross-reference to Schedule)
- Standard of performance (reasonable skill and care / fitness for purpose)
- Client dependencies and cooperation obligations
- Change control mechanism

**Key sub-clauses:**
- 3.1 The Provider shall perform the Services as described in Schedule 1 (Scope of Work / Statement of Work)
- 3.2 Standard of performance: "with reasonable skill, care and diligence" or "in accordance with Good Industry Practice"
- 3.3 Key Personnel: Provider shall ensure named individuals are assigned; no substitution without Client's prior written consent
- 3.4 Client obligations: provide access, information, decisions, and approvals in a timely manner; Client delays entitle Provider to relief
- 3.5 Exclusions: services not included (expressly stated to manage scope creep)
- 3.6 Change control: either party may request a change; changes require written agreement; Provider to provide impact assessment (cost, time, quality) before implementation
- 3.7 Subcontracting: Provider may / may not subcontract without Client's prior written consent; Provider remains responsible for subcontractor performance

**Drafting notes:**
- "Reasonable skill and care" is the standard implied by common law (Supply of Goods and Services Act 1982, s.13 for English law). "Fitness for purpose" is a higher, strict-liability standard.
- Norwegian law: implied standard under kjopsloven § 17 / avtaleloven is professional standard ("faglig forsvarlig"). Express clause recommended.
- Always include a change control mechanism -- scope disputes are the most common source of service agreement conflicts.


## Section 4: Service Standards and SLAs

**Purpose:** Define measurable performance standards, measurement methodology, and consequences of underperformance.

**Must include:** (conditional -- only if SLAs are required)
- KPIs / metrics
- Measurement methodology and reporting
- Service credits or other remedies
- Escalation procedure

**Key sub-clauses:**
- 4.1 Service levels: defined in Schedule 3 (SLA Schedule) with target and minimum levels
- 4.2 Measurement: Provider to measure and report performance against SLAs monthly/quarterly
- 4.3 Service credits: if minimum level is not met, Client entitled to service credits (percentage of monthly fees, typically 5-15% per SLA breach, capped at aggregate per month)
- 4.4 Earnback: Provider may earn back service credits in subsequent period if performance exceeds target (optional -- incentivises recovery)
- 4.5 Persistent failure: if minimum levels are not met for {{CONSECUTIVE_MONTHS}} consecutive months, Client may terminate for material breach
- 4.6 Root cause analysis: Provider to conduct and report within {{RCA_DAYS}} Business Days of any SLA failure
- 4.7 Continuous improvement: Provider to propose improvements annually

**Drafting notes:**
- Service credits are not liquidated damages in English law (they are a price adjustment mechanism). This distinction matters for limitation of liability.
- Avoid making service credits the sole and exclusive remedy for SLA breaches -- the Client should retain the right to terminate for persistent underperformance.
- Norwegian law: service credits are acceptable; boeteklausule provisions (avtaleloven § 36) may apply if credits are disproportionate.


## Section 5: Term and Duration

**Purpose:** Define when the agreement starts, how long it lasts, and how it renews.

**Must include:**
- Commencement date
- Initial term
- Renewal mechanism
- Notice for non-renewal

**Key sub-clauses:**
- 5.1 Commencement: this Agreement commences on the Commencement Date and continues for the Initial Term
- 5.2 Initial Term: {{INITIAL_TERM}} from the Commencement Date
- 5.3 Renewal: automatically renews for successive periods of {{RENEWAL_PERIOD}} unless either Party gives written notice of non-renewal at least {{NON_RENEWAL_NOTICE}} before the end of the then-current term
- 5.4 Long-stop date: this Agreement shall in any event expire on {{LONG_STOP_DATE}} unless the Parties agree in writing to extend (optional)
- 5.5 Early termination: without prejudice to Clause 16 (Termination)

**Drafting notes:**
- Be precise about whether the term is "12 months" or "1 year" (leap year implications).
- Auto-renewal (evergreen) clauses are enforceable in most jurisdictions but may be subject to notice requirements.
- Norwegian law: no specific legislation restricting auto-renewal for B2B contracts, but markedsforingsloven §§ 22 and 25 apply to unfair contract terms.


## Section 6: Compensation and Payment

**Purpose:** Define how much, how often, and how the Client pays for the Services.

**Must include:**
- Fee structure (fixed, time-based, milestone, retainer)
- Invoicing procedure
- Payment terms
- Late payment consequences
- Fee review mechanism

**Key sub-clauses:**
- 6.1 Fees: Client shall pay the Fees as set out in Schedule 2 (Rate Card / Fee Schedule)
- 6.2 Invoicing: Provider shall issue invoices {{INVOICE_FREQUENCY}} in arrears / in advance / upon milestone completion
- 6.3 Payment terms: Client shall pay each invoice within {{PAYMENT_TERMS}} of receipt of a valid invoice
- 6.4 Late payment interest: overdue amounts accrue interest at {{LATE_PAYMENT_RATE}} per annum (or the rate under the Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998 for English law; Forsinkelsesrenteloven for Norwegian law)
- 6.5 Disputed invoices: Client may withhold payment of genuinely disputed amounts provided it notifies Provider within {{DISPUTE_NOTICE_DAYS}} days, pays undisputed amounts, and the parties use good faith efforts to resolve
- 6.6 Fee review: Fees may be reviewed annually; increases capped at {{FEE_ESCALATION}} (CPI, fixed %, or negotiated)
- 6.7 Set-off: Client may / may not set off amounts owed to Provider against amounts owed by Provider (specify -- Provider typically resists broad set-off rights)
- 6.8 Suspension: Provider may suspend Services if invoices remain unpaid for {{SUSPENSION_PERIOD}} days after becoming due (with prior written notice)

**Drafting notes:**
- Norwegian law: forsinkelsesrente (late payment interest) is set by Finansdepartementet every 6 months. Contractual rate can be higher but must not be unreasonable (avtaleloven § 36).
- English law: Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998 provides a statutory rate of 8% + Bank of England base rate. Contractual rate can replace this.
- Always specify currency and whether amounts are exclusive of VAT/taxes.


## Section 7: Expenses and Reimbursement

**Purpose:** Define which expenses are reimbursable and the approval process.

**Must include:** (conditional -- only if reimbursable expenses exist)
- Categories of reimbursable expenses
- Pre-approval requirements
- Documentation requirements
- Caps

**Key sub-clauses:**
- 7.1 Reimbursable expenses: travel, accommodation, subsistence as agreed in writing
- 7.2 Pre-approval: expenses exceeding {{EXPENSE_THRESHOLD}} require Client's prior written approval
- 7.3 Documentation: Provider to submit receipts and expense reports with each invoice
- 7.4 Cap: total reimbursable expenses shall not exceed {{EXPENSE_CAP}} per {{PERIOD}} without prior written approval
- 7.5 Travel policy: Provider shall comply with Client's travel and expense policy (if provided)


## Section 8: Taxes

**Purpose:** Allocate responsibility for taxes and address VAT/GST, withholding tax, and cross-border implications.

**Must include:**
- VAT/GST treatment
- Withholding tax gross-up
- Tax compliance obligations

**Key sub-clauses:**
- 8.1 VAT exclusive: all Fees are expressed exclusive of VAT (or MVA / GST). Provider shall add VAT to invoices at the applicable rate.
- 8.2 Withholding tax: if Client is required by law to withhold tax from payments, Client shall (a) deduct the required amount, (b) pay the net amount to Provider, (c) pay the withheld amount to the relevant tax authority, and (d) provide Provider with a certificate of tax withheld. Provider may / Client shall gross up so Provider receives the full Fee amount (specify -- gross-up is negotiable).
- 8.3 Tax indemnity: each Party is responsible for its own income taxes arising from this Agreement.
- 8.4 Cross-border: if Services are provided across jurisdictions, the Parties shall cooperate to minimise withholding tax exposure and shall apply for reduced rates under applicable tax treaties.
- 8.5 Reverse charge: for B2B cross-border services within the EU/EEA, the reverse charge mechanism applies (Client self-assesses VAT).

**Drafting notes:**
- Norwegian MVA: standard rate 25%. Cross-border B2B services: reverse charge (snudd avregning) applies -- Provider invoices without MVA; Client self-assesses.
- Withholding tax gross-up: commercially significant -- a 25% WHT without gross-up reduces Provider's effective fee substantially. Always negotiate explicitly.
- Reference `norwegian-tax-law/references/treaty-wht-rates.md` for treaty rates if Norwegian counterparty.


## Section 9: Intellectual Property

**Purpose:** Allocate ownership of IP created during the engagement and license pre-existing IP.

**Must include:**
- Background IP identification and ownership
- Foreground IP ownership allocation
- License grants
- Moral rights
- Third-party IP

**Key sub-clauses:**
- 9.1 Background IP: each Party retains ownership of its pre-existing IPR ("**Background IP**")
- 9.2 Foreground IP ownership: choose one model:
  - **(a) Client-owns**: all IPR in Deliverables and work product created in performing the Services ("**Foreground IP**") shall vest in the Client. Provider hereby assigns (by way of present assignment of future rights) all Foreground IP to the Client. (For English law, use "assigns with full title guarantee".)
  - **(b) Provider-retains**: Provider retains ownership of all Foreground IP and grants Client a perpetual, irrevocable, non-exclusive, royalty-free licence to use the Foreground IP for the Client's business purposes.
  - **(c) Joint ownership**: Foreground IP jointly owned -- specify exploitation rights (each party may exploit independently, or only with consent)
- 9.3 Licence of Background IP: Provider grants Client a non-exclusive, royalty-free licence to use Provider's Background IP solely to the extent necessary to receive the benefit of the Services and use the Deliverables
- 9.4 Third-party IP: Provider warrants that the Services and Deliverables do not infringe third-party IPR (cross-ref to indemnity in Clause 14)
- 9.5 Moral rights: Provider shall procure waiver of moral rights by its Personnel to the fullest extent permitted by Applicable Law (note: moral rights cannot be fully waived under Norwegian law -- aandsverkloven § 3)
- 9.6 Source code escrow: if applicable, Provider to deposit source code with an independent escrow agent; release triggers defined (insolvency, material breach, discontinuation of support)

**Drafting notes:**
- English law: present assignment of future rights is effective upon creation (no further formality). Requirement for writing: Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988, s.90(3).
- Norwegian law (aandsverkloven): assignment of copyright is valid but moral rights (right of attribution, right against derogatory treatment) cannot be fully waived. A contractual commitment not to exercise moral rights is permissible but unenforceable if unreasonable.
- Joint ownership is generally avoided -- exploitation rights become complex and disputes arise frequently.


## Section 10: Confidentiality

**Purpose:** Protect sensitive information exchanged between the parties.

**Must include:**
- Definition of Confidential Information
- Confidentiality obligations
- Exclusions
- Permitted disclosures
- Duration
- Return / destruction

**Key sub-clauses:**
- 10.1 Definition: "**Confidential Information**" means all information (technical, commercial, financial) disclosed by one Party to the other in connection with this Agreement, whether in writing, orally, or by inspection, that is marked as confidential or would reasonably be understood to be confidential.
- 10.2 Obligations: the Receiving Party shall (a) keep Confidential Information confidential, (b) not disclose to third parties except as permitted, (c) use only for the purposes of this Agreement, (d) apply the same degree of care as for its own confidential information (but not less than reasonable care)
- 10.3 Exclusions: information that (a) is or becomes publicly available without breach, (b) was already known to the Receiving Party, (c) was independently developed, (d) was received from a third party without restriction
- 10.4 Permitted disclosures: to (a) Affiliates, (b) employees/advisers who need to know and are bound by confidentiality obligations, (c) as required by law, regulation, or court order (with prior notice to the Disclosing Party where legally permitted)
- 10.5 Duration: obligations continue for {{CONFIDENTIALITY_DURATION}} after termination (typically 2-5 years; trade secrets: indefinitely)
- 10.6 Return / destruction: upon termination or request, the Receiving Party shall return or destroy all Confidential Information and certify destruction in writing

**Drafting notes:**
- Consider whether an existing NDA supersedes or is superseded by the Agreement's confidentiality clause.
- Norwegian law: no specific statute on trade secrets in B2B contracts (markedsforingsloven § 28-29 provides some protection). Contractual confidentiality provisions are enforceable.
- Duration of indefinite confidentiality for trade secrets is standard and enforceable.


## Section 11: Data Protection and Privacy

**Purpose:** Address personal data processing obligations where the Services involve personal data.

**Must include:** (conditional -- only if personal data is processed)
- Roles: controller / processor / joint controllers
- DPA cross-reference
- GDPR Art. 28 requirements
- Sub-processor obligations
- International transfers
- Data breach notification

**Key sub-clauses:**
- 11.1 Roles: Provider acts as data processor on behalf of Client (data controller) in respect of Personal Data processed in performing the Services
- 11.2 DPA: the Parties shall comply with the Data Processing Agreement set out in Schedule 4 (DPA), which forms part of this Agreement
- 11.3 The DPA shall include all provisions required by GDPR Art. 28(3): subject matter, duration, nature and purpose, type of personal data, categories of data subjects
- 11.4 Sub-processors: Provider shall not engage sub-processors without Client's prior written consent (specific or general with objection right)
- 11.5 International transfers: Provider shall not transfer Personal Data outside the EEA without appropriate safeguards (adequacy decision, SCCs, BCRs)
- 11.6 Data breach: Provider shall notify Client without undue delay (and in any event within {{BREACH_NOTIFICATION_HOURS}} hours) upon becoming aware of a Personal Data breach
- 11.7 Data return: upon termination, Provider shall return or delete all Personal Data as instructed by Client

**Drafting notes:**
- Norwegian law: Personopplysningsloven supplements the GDPR. Datatilsynet (Norwegian DPA) is the supervisory authority.
- Always include a standalone DPA as a Schedule -- embedding data protection terms in the main body risks non-compliance with Art. 28 requirements.
- UK GDPR: post-Brexit, separate SCCs and UK addendum may be required for UK-EEA transfers.


## Section 12: Representations and Warranties

**Purpose:** Provide contractual assurances about each party's status, authority, and performance.

**Must include:**
- Mutual warranties
- Provider-specific warranties
- Client-specific warranties
- Remedies for breach

**Key sub-clauses:**
- 12.1 Mutual warranties: each Party warrants that (a) it is duly incorporated and validly existing, (b) it has full power and authority to enter into and perform this Agreement, (c) this Agreement constitutes a legal, valid, and binding obligation, (d) it is not subject to insolvency proceedings
- 12.2 Provider warranties: Provider warrants that (a) it will perform the Services with reasonable skill and care, (b) the Services and Deliverables will conform to the Specification, (c) it has and will maintain all necessary licences, permits, and consents, (d) its Personnel have the requisite qualifications and experience
- 12.3 Client warranties: Client warrants that (a) it will provide accurate information and timely cooperation, (b) it has all necessary rights to the Client Materials
- 12.4 Remedies: if a warranty is breached, the non-breaching Party may require the breaching Party to remedy within a reasonable time; if the breach is not remedied, the non-breaching Party may exercise its termination rights

**Drafting notes:**
- Warranties are distinct from conditions and innominate terms in English law. Breach of warranty gives rise to a damages claim but not necessarily a right to terminate.
- Norwegian law: the distinction between warranties and conditions is less rigid. The concept of "vesentlig mislighold" (material breach) determines termination rights.
- Avoid over-promising: "best efforts" warranties create enforcement issues. Prefer "reasonable skill and care" or "Good Industry Practice."


## Section 13: Limitation of Liability

**Purpose:** Cap each party's financial exposure and exclude certain heads of loss.

**Must include:**
- Aggregate liability cap
- Consequential damages exclusion
- Carve-outs (items not subject to cap)
- Mutual application

**Key sub-clauses:**
- 13.1 Aggregate cap: subject to Clause 13.3, each Party's total aggregate liability under or in connection with this Agreement shall not exceed {{LIABILITY_CAP}} (options: fixed amount, multiple of fees paid/payable in the 12-month period preceding the claim, or fees paid under the Agreement)
- 13.2 Consequential damages exclusion: neither Party shall be liable for indirect, special, incidental, or consequential loss or damage, including loss of profit, loss of revenue, loss of data, loss of goodwill, loss of anticipated savings, or loss of business opportunity, whether or not foreseeable
- 13.3 Carve-outs: the limitations in Clauses 13.1 and 13.2 shall not apply to: (a) death or personal injury caused by negligence, (b) fraud or fraudulent misrepresentation, (c) breach of Clause 10 (Confidentiality) [negotiable], (d) breach of Clause 11 (Data Protection) [negotiable], (e) IP infringement indemnity under Clause 14 [negotiable], (f) any liability that cannot be limited by Applicable Law
- 13.4 Mutual: the limitations apply equally to both Parties (or specify asymmetric caps if commercially agreed)

**Drafting notes:**
- English law: UCTA 1977 s.2(2) -- exclusion of liability for negligence must be reasonable. s.3 -- exclusion of liability for breach of contract must be reasonable when dealing on written standard terms. Watford Electronics v Sanderson [2001] EWCA Civ 317 provides guidance on B2B reasonableness.
- Norwegian law: avtaleloven § 36 provides a general fairness control. Exclusions that are unreasonable can be set aside or adjusted.
- The consequential damages exclusion should list specific heads of loss (not just "indirect or consequential") to reduce ambiguity.
- Always include the fraud carve-out (cannot exclude liability for own fraud) and the death/personal injury carve-out (cannot exclude by law).


## Section 14: Indemnification

**Purpose:** Allocate specific risks through indemnification obligations and define the claims procedure.

**Must include:**
- IP indemnity (Provider indemnifies Client)
- Negligence / third-party claims
- Client indemnity (for Client Materials)
- Indemnification procedure
- Cap cross-reference

**Key sub-clauses:**
- 14.1 Provider IP indemnity: Provider shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Client from losses arising from any third-party claim that the Services or Deliverables infringe that third party's IPR
- 14.2 Client indemnity: Client shall indemnify Provider from losses arising from any third-party claim resulting from Client Materials or Client's use of Deliverables outside the scope of the licence granted
- 14.3 Procedure: the indemnified Party shall (a) promptly notify the indemnifying Party, (b) grant sole control of the defence and settlement, (c) provide reasonable cooperation. Failure to notify promptly does not relieve the indemnifying Party except to the extent prejudiced.
- 14.4 Mitigation: Provider's IP infringement remedies: (a) obtain a licence, (b) modify or replace the infringing element, (c) if neither (a) nor (b) is commercially practicable, terminate and refund pre-paid fees for the infringing element
- 14.5 Cap: indemnification obligations are subject to the aggregate liability cap in Clause 13 (or carve-out specified)

**Drafting notes:**
- "Indemnify and hold harmless" is tautological in English law but conventional. Under US law, the distinction between "indemnity" (reimbursement) and "hold harmless" (no-loss obligation) may have separate legal significance in some states.
- Norwegian law: "skadeslosholde" (hold harmless) is the standard term. The concept functions similarly to English law indemnities.
- Always cross-reference the limitation of liability to avoid inadvertently creating uncapped indemnities.


## Section 15: Insurance

**Purpose:** Require parties to maintain insurance adequate to cover their obligations.

**Must include:** (conditional -- only if insurance requirements specified)
- Types of insurance
- Minimum amounts
- Run-off period
- Certificates and evidence

**Key sub-clauses:**
- 15.1 Provider shall maintain: (a) professional indemnity (PI) / errors & omissions (E&O): {{PI_AMOUNT}}, (b) public/general liability: {{PL_AMOUNT}}, (c) employers' liability: {{EL_AMOUNT}}, (d) cyber liability: {{CYBER_AMOUNT}} (if applicable)
- 15.2 Run-off: Provider shall maintain PI insurance for {{RUNOFF_PERIOD}} years after termination (claims-made basis)
- 15.3 Evidence: Provider shall provide certificates of insurance upon request (annually and upon renewal)
- 15.4 Claims-made vs occurrence: specify which basis applies for each policy type
- 15.5 Additional insured: Client may require being named as additional insured on Provider's public liability policy (negotiable)

**Drafting notes:**
- Insurance amounts should be proportionate to contract value and risk profile.
- Run-off period for PI: typically 2-6 years; matches limitation period for breach of contract claims.
- Norwegian law: yrkesskadeforsikringsloven requires employers' liability insurance for all Norwegian employers.


## Section 16: Termination

**Purpose:** Define the circumstances and process for ending the agreement before the natural expiry of the Term.

**Must include:**
- Termination for material breach (with cure period)
- Termination for insolvency
- Termination for convenience
- Other termination triggers

**Key sub-clauses:**
- 16.1 Termination for material breach: either Party may terminate by written notice if the other commits a material breach and (a) the breach is not remediable, or (b) the breach is remediable and the breaching Party fails to remedy within {{CURE_PERIOD}} days of written notice specifying the breach
- 16.2 Termination for insolvency: either Party may terminate immediately upon written notice if the other (a) becomes insolvent, (b) has a receiver, administrator, or liquidator appointed, (c) enters into any voluntary arrangement with creditors, (d) ceases or threatens to cease to carry on business
- 16.3 Termination for convenience: {{CONVENIENCE_PARTY}} may terminate for any reason by giving {{CONVENIENCE_NOTICE}} written notice
- 16.4 Termination for persistent underperformance: Client may terminate if Provider fails to meet minimum SLAs for {{CONSECUTIVE_MONTHS}} consecutive months (cross-ref to Clause 4)
- 16.5 Termination for change of control: either Party may terminate within {{COC_NOTICE_DAYS}} days of becoming aware of a change of control of the other Party
- 16.6 Termination for prolonged force majeure: either Party may terminate if a Force Majeure Event continues for more than {{FM_TERMINATION_DAYS}} consecutive days

**Drafting notes:**
- "Material breach" is the most litigated term in contract law. Consider defining it or providing non-exhaustive examples.
- Norwegian law: the right to terminate for breach arises when the breach constitutes "vesentlig mislighold" (material/fundamental breach). A cure period of 30 days is standard.
- English law: ipso facto insolvency termination clauses may be restricted in certain contexts (Corporate Insolvency and Governance Act 2020 for supply contracts).


## Section 17: Consequences of Termination

**Purpose:** Define what happens after termination -- payments, data return, transition, and survival.

**Must include:**
- Outstanding payments
- Return of confidential information and data
- Transition assistance
- IP handover
- Survival of provisions

**Key sub-clauses:**
- 17.1 Outstanding payments: Client shall pay all Fees for Services performed up to the termination date within {{POST_TERM_PAYMENT_DAYS}} days
- 17.2 Return of materials: each Party shall return or destroy the other's Confidential Information and certify destruction
- 17.3 Data return: Provider shall return all Client Data in a commonly used format within {{DATA_RETURN_DAYS}} days and delete all copies within {{DATA_DELETE_DAYS}} days (subject to legal retention requirements)
- 17.4 Transition assistance: Provider shall provide reasonable transition assistance for up to {{TRANSITION_PERIOD}} at Provider's then-current rates (or at the rates in this Agreement -- specify)
- 17.5 IP handover: all Foreground IP (if Client-owns model) to be fully transferred; work-in-progress materials delivered
- 17.6 Survival: the following clauses survive termination or expiry: Clause 2 (Definitions), Clause 6 (to the extent of accrued payment obligations), Clause 9 (IP), Clause 10 (Confidentiality), Clause 13 (Limitation of Liability), Clause 14 (Indemnification), Clause 17 (Consequences), Clause 19 (Dispute Resolution), Clause 20 (Governing Law)

**Drafting notes:**
- Always list surviving clauses by number -- "provisions which by their nature should survive" creates uncertainty.
- Transition assistance should be mandatory (not optional) for critical services to avoid business disruption.


## Section 18: Force Majeure

**Purpose:** Excuse non-performance for events beyond a party's reasonable control.

**Must include:**
- Definition (non-exhaustive list of events)
- Notification and mitigation obligations
- Suspension of obligations
- Termination threshold
- Payment obligations carve-out

**Key sub-clauses:**
- 18.1 Definition: a "**Force Majeure Event**" means an event beyond a Party's reasonable control, including (non-exhaustive): natural disasters, war, terrorism, civil unrest, government action, embargo, fire, flood, epidemic or pandemic, failure of utility services, cyberattack (if agreed), strike or industrial action (if agreed)
- 18.2 Notification: the affected Party shall notify the other within {{FM_NOTICE_DAYS}} Business Days, specifying the event and its expected duration
- 18.3 Mitigation: the affected Party shall use reasonable endeavours to mitigate the impact and resume performance
- 18.4 Suspension: the affected Party's obligations are suspended during the Force Majeure Event (except payment obligations)
- 18.5 Termination: if the Force Majeure Event continues for more than {{FM_TERMINATION_DAYS}} consecutive days, either Party may terminate by written notice
- 18.6 Payment: Client is not obliged to pay for Services not performed during the Force Majeure Event

**Drafting notes:**
