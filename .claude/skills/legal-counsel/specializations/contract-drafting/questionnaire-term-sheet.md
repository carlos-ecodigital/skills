# Term Sheet -- Intake Questionnaire

This questionnaire gathers all information needed to generate a term sheet for any transaction type: investment, acquisition, joint venture, financing, or commercial deal. Questions are grouped into 4 parts. Present in 2 conversational batches.

**Key design principle:** A term sheet is a concise, tabular document. Questions focus on extracting the key commercial terms without the procedural detail of a full agreement.


## Part A: Transaction Overview (6 questions)

**A1.** What type of transaction is this term sheet for?
*Options: equity investment (seed/Series A/B/C/growth), convertible instrument (SAFE/convertible note/CLN), share acquisition, asset acquisition, joint venture, project finance, debt facility, commercial licensing, or other.*

**A2.** Identify all parties and their roles.
*E.g., "Investor: [Fund BV], Issuer: [Target AS]" or "Buyer: [AcquireCo Ltd], Seller: [Founder Name]." Include entity types and jurisdictions.*

**A3.** What is the target or subject of the transaction?
*Brief description: company name, jurisdiction, business activity, stage (for investments). For project finance: asset type, location, capacity.*

**A4.** What is the proposed valuation or pricing?
*For investment: pre-money valuation, price per share, discount rate (convertibles). For acquisition: enterprise value, equity value, price per share. For project finance: total project cost, gearing. For debt: facility amount.*

**A5.** What is the proposed investment/acquisition amount?
*Total amount, currency, and breakdown if multiple tranches or investors.*

**A6.** What is the target timeline?
*Key dates: DD completion, signing, conditions satisfaction, closing/disbursement. Express as "within X days/weeks of term sheet execution."*


## Part B: Structure and Economics (8 questions)

**B1.** What is the proposed instrument or deal structure?
*For equity: ordinary shares, preferred shares (participating/non-participating), share classes. For convertibles: SAFE, convertible note, CLN, conversion triggers. For acquisition: share purchase vs asset purchase. For JV: equity split, contribution structure.*

**B2.** What are the key economic terms?
*Depends on transaction type:*
- *Investment: liquidation preference (1x/2x, participating/non-participating), anti-dilution (full ratchet/weighted average), dividend rights*
- *Acquisition: price mechanism (locked-box/completion accounts), earn-out, escrow/holdback*
- *JV: profit/loss sharing, contribution obligations, distribution waterfall*
- *Debt: interest rate, tenor, amortisation, security package*

**B3.** What are the key governance provisions?
*Board composition, reserved matters (veto rights), information rights, observer rights, consent thresholds. Who has what level of control?*

**B4.** What are the conditions precedent?
*DD completion, regulatory approvals, third-party consents, financing, key person commitment, IP assignment, etc.*

**B5.** Are there any specific protective provisions or restrictions?
*Pre-emption rights (right of first refusal), drag-along, tag-along, lock-up period, non-compete, non-solicit, transfer restrictions.*

**B6.** What exit provisions are contemplated?
*Redemption rights, IPO provisions, trade sale mechanics, put/call options, buy-sell (shotgun) mechanisms. Specify trigger events and timing.*

**B7.** Are there ESOP/option pool provisions?
*Size of option pool (% of fully diluted), treatment at completion (pre/post-money), vesting schedule for key employees.*

**B8.** What warranties and indemnities are expected at this stage?
*High-level indication: "standard warranties for a [jurisdiction] [transaction type]" or specific concerns (tax, IP, regulatory, environmental). Detail deferred to definitive agreements.*


## Part C: Process and Exclusivity (4 questions)

**C1.** Is there an exclusivity (no-shop) period?
*Duration (typically 30-60 days for term sheets). During this period, the target/seller agrees not to solicit or negotiate with other parties.*

**C2.** Which provisions are binding?
*Standard binding: exclusivity, confidentiality, costs, governing law. Standard non-binding: all commercial terms. Confirm any variations.*

**C3.** Is there a break fee or cost cap?
*Break fee if one party walks away. Cost cap: maximum legal/advisory fees to be borne by one party (common for investors to cap target's costs).*

**C4.** What is the long-stop date?
*Date by which definitive agreements must be signed, failing which the term sheet lapses and exclusivity ends.*


## Part D: Legal Framework (3 questions)

**D1.** What is the governing law?
*For the term sheet itself and for the anticipated definitive agreements (may differ).*

**D2.** What dispute resolution mechanism applies to the binding provisions?
*Courts or arbitration. For term sheets, courts are more common.*

**D3.** Are there any transaction-specific legal considerations?
*E.g., foreign investment screening (FDI), competition clearance, sector-specific regulation (energy, financial services, data), tax structuring preferences, holding structure requirements.*


## Batch Presentation Order

| Batch | Parts | Questions |
|---|---|---|
| Batch 1 | A + B | Transaction overview, structure, economics (14 questions) |
| Batch 2 | C + D | Process, exclusivity, legal framework (7 questions) |
