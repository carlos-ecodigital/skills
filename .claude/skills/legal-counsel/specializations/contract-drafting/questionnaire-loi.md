# Letter of Intent / Heads of Terms -- Intake Questionnaire

This questionnaire gathers all information needed to generate a complete LOI (Letter of Intent), MOU (Memorandum of Understanding), or Heads of Terms for a proposed transaction. Questions are grouped into 5 parts. Present in 2-3 conversational batches.

**Key design principle:** An LOI contains a mix of binding and non-binding provisions. The questionnaire must identify which provisions the parties intend to be binding.


## Part A: Parties and Transaction (6 questions)

**A1.** What is the full legal name and entity type of the proposing party?
*The party initiating the LOI or making the proposal.*

**A2.** What is the full legal name and entity type of the receiving party?
*The party to whom the proposal is addressed.*

**A3.** Are there additional parties (e.g., guarantors, shareholders, co-investors)?
*If yes, identify each and their role in the proposed transaction.*

**A4.** What type of transaction is proposed?
*Options: share acquisition (100% or partial), asset acquisition, joint venture, investment (equity or convertible), merger, commercial partnership, licensing arrangement, real estate transaction, project development, or other.*

**A5.** Provide a brief description of the target (for M&A) or the subject matter (for other transactions).
*E.g., "acquisition of 100% of the shares in [Target BV], a Dutch battery energy storage developer." Include: business description, jurisdiction, key assets or activities.*

**A6.** What is the proposed timeline for completing the transaction?
*E.g., "signing of definitive agreements within 90 days of LOI execution." Key dates: DD period, signing, conditions satisfaction, completion/closing.*


## Part B: Key Commercial Terms (8 questions)

**B1.** What is the proposed purchase price, investment amount, or economic arrangement?
*For M&A: enterprise value and equity value (or price per share). For investment: amount, valuation, and instrument type. For JV: each party's contribution. Include currency.*

**B2.** What is the proposed price mechanism?
*Options: locked-box (fixed price, no post-completion adjustment), completion accounts (price adjusted for net debt, working capital, cash), earn-out (contingent consideration), deferred consideration, or combination.*

**B3.** What is the proposed deal structure?
*Share purchase, asset purchase, merger, subscription, convertible instrument, asset contribution to JV. State whether the buyer/investor is forming a new entity (NewCo) or using existing.*

**B4.** What are the key conditions precedent to completion?
*Common CPs: satisfactory due diligence, regulatory approvals (ACM, EC, CFIUS), landlord/counterparty consents, financing, shareholder approval, no MAC. List all anticipated conditions.*

**B5.** Is there an earn-out or deferred consideration component?
*If yes: metric (revenue, EBITDA, specific milestones), measurement period, cap, floor, payment timing.*

**B6.** What are the key warranty/indemnity expectations at this stage?
*E.g., "standard representations and warranties for a Dutch share purchase," or "seller to provide tax indemnity." Detail can be deferred to the SPA.*

**B7.** Are there any specific post-completion obligations?
*E.g., non-compete, transitional services, key employee retention, seller's ongoing involvement.*

**B8.** What is the proposed financing structure (if relevant)?
*Cash on hand, debt financing (committed/uncommitted), equity co-investment. Is the transaction conditional on financing?*


## Part C: Exclusivity and Confidentiality (4 questions)

**C1.** Is the proposing party seeking an exclusivity (lock-out) period?
*Duration: typically 30-90 days. Scope: the receiving party agrees not to solicit, encourage, or negotiate with third parties during the exclusivity period. This is typically a binding provision.*

**C2.** Is there an existing NDA in place, or should confidentiality be included in the LOI?
*If no NDA exists, the LOI should include a binding confidentiality provision. If an NDA exists, reference it and confirm it remains in effect.*

**C3.** Should the LOI include a break fee or cost undertaking?
*Break fee: a payment by one party if the transaction does not proceed. Cost undertaking: agreement to bear own costs (or to reimburse the other party's costs if transaction is aborted). Both are binding provisions.*

**C4.** Are there any standstill restrictions required?
*Prevents the receiving party from acquiring shares/assets without consent during the exclusivity period. Common in public M&A.*


## Part D: Binding vs Non-Binding (4 questions)

**D1.** Which provisions should be legally binding?
*Standard binding provisions: exclusivity, confidentiality, costs, governing law, dispute resolution. Standard non-binding provisions: commercial terms (price, structure, conditions). Confirm with the parties.*

**D2.** Is the LOI intended to create an obligation to negotiate in good faith?
*A good faith negotiation obligation may be binding in civil law jurisdictions (NL, NO) but is generally unenforceable in common law jurisdictions (UK, limited in US). Specify the intent.*

**D3.** Should the LOI be countersigned or acknowledged?
*Countersigned: both parties sign, creating a bilateral agreement. Acknowledged: one party signs, the other acknowledges receipt (less formal).*

**D4.** Is there a long-stop date for the LOI itself?
*The date by which definitive agreements must be signed, failing which the LOI lapses. Typically aligned with the exclusivity period.*


## Part E: Governing Law and Process (3 questions)

**E1.** What is the governing law?
*Same considerations as any cross-border agreement. For M&A, the target's jurisdiction is common.*

**E2.** What dispute resolution mechanism is preferred for the binding provisions?
*Courts or arbitration. For LOIs, court jurisdiction is more common than arbitration (the LOI is preliminary).*

**E3.** Are there any regulatory or competition law filings anticipated?
*E.g., ACM (Netherlands), Konkurransetilsynet (Norway), European Commission, CFIUS (US). Timing of filings may affect the overall transaction timeline.*


## Batch Presentation Order

| Batch | Parts | Questions |
|---|---|---|
| Batch 1 | A + B | Parties, transaction type, commercial terms (14 questions) |
| Batch 2 | C + D + E | Exclusivity, binding status, governing law (11 questions) |
