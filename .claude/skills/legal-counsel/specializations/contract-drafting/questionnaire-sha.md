# Shareholders' Agreement -- Intake Questionnaire

This questionnaire gathers all information needed to generate a complete, jurisdiction-aware Shareholders' Agreement (SHA). Questions are grouped into 8 parts. Present in 3-4 conversational batches.

**Key design principle:** An SHA governs the relationship between shareholders in a company. It supplements (and may override) the articles of association. The questionnaire covers governance, economics, transfer restrictions, and exit mechanisms.


## Part A: Parties and Company (6 questions)

**A1.** What is the full legal name, entity type, jurisdiction, and registration number of the company?
*The company whose shares the shareholders hold. Include: business description, date of incorporation, current stage (startup, growth, mature).*

**A2.** List all shareholders with their current shareholding and share class.
*For each shareholder: name, entity type (or individual), jurisdiction, number of shares, percentage, share class (ordinary, preference, founder). Include option/warrant holders if relevant.*

**A3.** Are there different share classes with different rights?
*If yes, specify for each class: voting rights, dividend rights, liquidation preference, conversion rights, anti-dilution protection.*

**A4.** Is this SHA being entered into in connection with a specific event?
*E.g., new investment round (Series A/B/C), joint venture formation, management buyout, post-acquisition, founder agreement. Context determines which provisions are priority.*

**A5.** Is there an existing SHA being replaced or amended?
*If replacing: identify what is changing. If first SHA: note that the company's articles may need to be amended to align.*

**A6.** Are there any investors with special rights (e.g., lead investor, majority shareholder, institutional investor)?
*Identify any shareholders with preferential treatment: board seats, veto rights, information rights, anti-dilution, liquidation preference. This drives governance provisions.*


## Part B: Governance (8 questions)

**B1.** What is the board composition?
*Number of directors, appointment rights per shareholder (e.g., "Investor A appoints 2, Founders appoint 2, 1 independent"). Chair and casting vote provisions.*

**B2.** What matters require board approval (beyond day-to-day management)?
*Common reserved matters: annual budget, expenditure above threshold, borrowing, hiring key employees, litigation, related-party transactions. Specify approval threshold (simple majority, unanimous, specific director consent).*

**B3.** What matters require shareholder approval beyond statutory requirements?
*Shareholder reserved matters: share issuance/dilution, change of articles, dividend declarations, disposal of material assets, change of business, winding up, change of auditors. Specify approval threshold (simple majority, 75%, unanimous, or investor consent).*

**B4.** What is the quorum for board meetings and shareholder meetings?
*Board: typically majority of directors including at least one investor-appointed and one founder-appointed. Shareholders: specify minimum percentage.*

**B5.** What deadlock resolution mechanism applies?
*Options: escalation to senior principals, mediation, expert determination, buy-sell (shotgun/Russian roulette), put/call, dissolution. Specify the mechanism and trigger.*

**B6.** What information rights do shareholders have?
*Standard: monthly management accounts, annual audited accounts, annual budget, quarterly KPI reports. Enhanced (for investors): access to management, observer rights at board meetings, inspection rights.*

**B7.** What approval is required for related-party transactions?
*Transactions between the company and a shareholder (or their affiliates) typically require approval of the non-interested shareholders or independent directors. Specify threshold and process.*

**B8.** Are there any specific management or operational provisions?
*E.g., appointment of CEO/CFO, business plan approval, key employee incentive schemes, non-compete obligations on shareholders, commitment of services by shareholders (common in JVs).*


## Part C: Capital and Funding (5 questions)

**C1.** What is the initial share capital structure?
*Total authorised shares, issued shares, nominal value, share premium. For each class: number, rights, restrictions.*

**C2.** What are the future funding obligations or expectations?
*Is additional equity required? Committed funding rounds, pro-rata follow-on rights, pay-to-play provisions, convertible instruments.*

**C3.** What are the pre-emption (anti-dilution) rights on new share issuances?
*Standard: existing shareholders have the right to subscribe for new shares pro-rata to their existing holding. Exceptions: employee option pool, conversion of existing instruments.*

**C4.** What is the dividend policy?
*Options: no dividends until exit (reinvestment), minimum distribution of available profits, fixed distribution percentage, priority return to preference shareholders. Specify: cumulative or non-cumulative preference.*

**C5.** Is there a distribution waterfall?
*For JVs and PE-backed companies: specify priority of distributions (return of capital, preferred return, catch-up, profit share). Reference equity-structures.md for detailed waterfall mechanics.*


## Part D: Transfer Restrictions (6 questions)

**D1.** Are share transfers restricted?
*Standard: no transfer without board/shareholder consent and compliance with SHA transfer provisions. Exceptions: transfers to Affiliates, family trusts, estate planning vehicles.*

**D2.** Do pre-emption rights (right of first refusal) apply on transfers?
*Standard: a selling shareholder must first offer shares to existing shareholders pro-rata before selling to a third party. Specify: price determination (same terms as third-party offer, fair market value, formula).*

**D3.** Is there a tag-along (co-sale) right?
*Tag-along: if a majority shareholder sells, minority shareholders can sell their shares on the same terms. Protects minority from being left with a new controlling shareholder.*

**D4.** Is there a drag-along right?
*Drag-along: if shareholders holding {{PERCENTAGE}}%+ agree to a sale, they can require all other shareholders to sell on the same terms. Specify: threshold (typically 75%+), minimum price, process.*

**D5.** Is there a lock-up period?
*Period during which shareholders cannot transfer shares. Common: 12-36 months post-investment or post-IPO. Specify: applies to all shareholders or specific classes (e.g., founders only).*

**D6.** Are there compulsory transfer (good leaver/bad leaver) provisions?
*For management shareholders: bad leaver (termination for cause, resignation) transfers at lower value (cost/nominal); good leaver (death, disability, redundancy) transfers at fair market value. Specify leaver definitions and valuation methodology.*


## Part E: Exit Mechanisms (5 questions)

**E1.** What exit mechanisms are contemplated?
*Options: trade sale, IPO, management buyout, buy-sell mechanism, put option, call option, orderly wind-down. Specify which are available and to whom.*

**E2.** Is there a put option (right to require the company or other shareholders to buy shares)?
*If yes: trigger events (time-based, event-based), valuation method, payment terms, funding mechanism.*

**E3.** Is there a call option (right to require a shareholder to sell shares)?
*If yes: trigger events, valuation method, payment terms. Common in JVs and PE-backed structures.*

**E4.** What is the valuation methodology for share transfers?
*Options: fair market value by independent valuer, formula-based (revenue multiple, EBITDA multiple, NAV), last funding round price, agreed process. Specify who appoints the valuer and how disputes are resolved.*

**E5.** Are there IPO-related provisions?
*Qualified IPO definition (minimum valuation, recognised exchange), conversion of preference shares, lock-up post-IPO, registration rights (for US). Specify if any shareholder can trigger or veto an IPO.*


## Part F: Non-Compete and IP (4 questions)

**F1.** Are shareholders subject to non-compete obligations?
*Common in JVs and founder SHAs. Specify: scope (competing business activities), duration (during shareholding + {{MONTHS}} months post-exit), geography, exceptions (passive investments below {{PERCENTAGE}}%).*

**F2.** Are shareholders subject to non-solicitation obligations?
*No-poach of company employees and no-solicitation of company customers. Duration: during shareholding + {{MONTHS}} months post-exit.*

**F3.** How is IP contributed by shareholders treated?
*Options: assigned to the company, licensed to the company (exclusive/non-exclusive), retained by shareholder with company licence. Specify for each shareholder's background IP.*

**F4.** Who owns IP created by the company?
*Standard: all IP created by the company or its employees vests in the company. Confirm: no shareholder has a claim to company-created IP.*


## Part G: Confidentiality and Dispute Resolution (3 questions)

**G1.** What confidentiality obligations apply to shareholders?
*Standard: all company information is confidential. Permitted disclosures: professional advisers, Affiliates, regulatory/legal requirements. Duration: during shareholding + {{YEARS}} years post-exit.*

**G2.** What is the governing law?
*Typically the company's jurisdiction of incorporation. For a Dutch BV: Dutch law. For a Norwegian AS: Norwegian law.*

**G3.** What dispute resolution mechanism is preferred?
*For SHAs, arbitration is common (confidentiality). Specify: institution (NAI, ICC, NCC, LCIA), seat, number of arbitrators, language. Deadlock resolution may use a different mechanism (expert determination).*


## Part H: Miscellaneous (3 questions)

**H1.** Does the SHA need to align with or override the company's articles of association?
*If the SHA and articles conflict, the SHA governs between the parties, but the articles govern vis-a-vis third parties and the company. Consider amending the articles to align. For Dutch BVs: the notarial deed of incorporation may need amendment.*

**H2.** Are there any specific regulatory or compliance provisions required?
*E.g., energy sector regulations, financial services licensing, foreign ownership restrictions, UBO registration, sanctions compliance.*

**H3.** Are there any additional provisions or concerns not covered above?
*E.g., shareholder loans (interest rate, repayment, subordination), management fees, service agreements between shareholders and company, insurance (D&O, key person), specific reporting to investors.*


## Batch Presentation Order

| Batch | Parts | Questions |
|---|---|---|
| Batch 1 | A + B | Parties, company, governance (14 questions) |
| Batch 2 | C + D | Capital, funding, transfer restrictions (11 questions) |
| Batch 3 | E + F | Exit mechanisms, non-compete, IP (9 questions) |
| Batch 4 | G + H | Confidentiality, disputes, miscellaneous (6 questions) |

For simple two-shareholder SHAs, Batches 3 and 4 can be combined into a single batch.
