# NDA Review Checklist

25-item checklist for reviewing counterparty-drafted NDAs. Each item includes DE's acceptable position and a default priority tag (DEFEND / NEGOTIATE / ACCEPT) per the NDA Policy Positions. Priority can be upgraded based on clause-interaction analysis.

**Usage:** Run through all 25 items. Score each GREEN / AMBER / RED. Tag non-Green items with their priority. Feed results into the Three-Layer Analysis (clause interaction, devil's advocate, precedent matching).

---

## A. Parties and Authority

### 1. Entity Name Verification
**Check:** Full legal entity name matches current company registry (Companies House, Handelsregister, KvK, Brønnøysundregistrene).
**DE Position:** Entity name must be exact. Mismatches create enforceability risk.
**Red Flags:** Name does not match registry. Entity type omitted or incorrect (e.g., "Stelia" instead of "Stelia Ltd"). Abbreviated names without full legal name stated.
**Default Priority:** DEFEND
**Redline:** Request correction to match registered name before signing.

### 2. Entity Status
**Check:** Counterparty entity is active, not dissolved, struck off, or in insolvency proceedings.
**DE Position:** Only sign with active, solvent entities.
**Red Flags:** Entity shown as dissolved/dormant on registry. Recent insolvency filings. Entity incorporated very recently (< 6 months) with no visible operations.
**Default Priority:** DEFEND
**Redline:** Do not sign. Investigate counterparty status.

### 3. Signatory Authority
**Check:** Person signing has authority to bind the entity (director, Verwaltungsrat, officer with POA, authorised signatory).
**DE Position:** Signing authority must be verifiable. For DE: Carlos Reuven, as Verwaltungsrat / Director.
**Red Flags:** Signatory title not stated. Signatory is not a director or officer. No authority confirmation available.
**Default Priority:** DEFEND
**Redline:** Request confirmation of signatory authority or signature by a known director.

### 4. Correct DE Entity
**Check:** DE is correctly identified as Digital Energy Group AG (CHE-408.639.320, Zug, Switzerland).
**DE Position:** The AG is the correct signing entity unless a specific subsidiary is relevant to the deal.
**Red Flags:** Wrong entity name. Wrong registration number. Wrong jurisdiction.
**Default Priority:** DEFEND
**Redline:** Correct before signing.

## B. Confidential Information Definition

### 5. CI Scope
**Check:** Is the definition of Confidential Information appropriately scoped?
**DE Position:** Broad definitions are acceptable in mutual NDAs (both parties benefit equally). In one-way NDAs where DE is the Recipient only, the scope should be reasonable and bounded.
**Red Flags:** One-sided breadth (covers everything the Discloser has ever created). "All information whatsoever" without Purpose limitation. Definition includes "ideas, concepts, or know-how" without qualification.
**Default Priority:** ACCEPT (mutual broad) / NEGOTIATE (one-sided broad)
**Redline:** Narrow to "information disclosed in connection with the Purpose" or add Purpose-based qualification.

### 6. Temporal Scope
**Check:** Does the CI definition cover information disclosed before the NDA date?
**DE Position:** "Before or after the date of this Agreement" is acceptable and standard in UK practice. It protects pre-NDA discussions.
**Red Flags:** No temporal scope at all (ambiguous). CI definition that excludes pre-NDA disclosures when parties have already been talking.
**Default Priority:** ACCEPT
**Redline:** Not typically needed. If temporal scope is missing, note for awareness.

### 7. Derived Information
**Check:** Does CI include information derived from or based on disclosed information (analyses, compilations, findings)?
**DE Position:** Including derived information is standard and appropriate.
**Red Flags:** Derived information explicitly excluded (weakens protection). No mention of derived information in a technically complex deal.
**Default Priority:** ACCEPT
**Redline:** Not typically needed. If missing in a technical deal, NEGOTIATE addition.

### 8. Catch-All Sweep
**Check:** Is any "including but not limited to" catch-all reasonable in scope?
**DE Position:** Standard drafting practice. Accept catch-all lists.
**Red Flags:** Catch-all that explicitly includes "personal opinions," "verbal remarks," or "informal communications" — could capture casual conversation. Catch-all so broad it captures information unrelated to the Purpose.
**Default Priority:** ACCEPT (standard) / NEGOTIATE (unreasonable breadth)
**Redline:** Narrow catch-all to "information disclosed in connection with the Purpose."

## C. Exclusions from Confidential Information

### 9. Standard Four Exclusions Present
**Check:** Does the NDA include all four standard exclusions: (a) public domain, (b) prior knowledge, (c) independent development, (d) third-party receipt without breach?
**DE Position:** All four must be present. Missing exclusions are a serious deficiency.
**Red Flags:** Any of the four missing. "Public domain" exclusion qualified with "through no fault" (acceptable) vs unqualified (too broad). Independent development exclusion missing entirely.
**Default Priority:** DEFEND (if any missing)
**Redline:** Insert missing exclusion(s) using standard language from DE's clause library.

### 10. Burden of Proof on Exclusions
**Check:** Who bears the burden of proving an exclusion applies?
**DE Position:** "Can demonstrate" or "demonstrable" placing burden on the Receiving Party is standard and acceptable. "Demonstrable from written records" (Stelia-style) is stricter but acceptable.
**Red Flags:** No burden-of-proof language (ambiguous). Burden on the Discloser to prove information was NOT in the exclusion (reverse burden).
**Default Priority:** ACCEPT
**Redline:** Not typically needed.

### 11. Compilation Carve-Out
**Check:** Does the NDA treat compilations of otherwise public information as still confidential?
**DE Position:** Acceptable and common. A compilation can have value even if individual elements are public.
**Red Flags:** None — this is a protective provision that benefits both parties.
**Default Priority:** ACCEPT
**Redline:** Not needed.

## D. Confidentiality Obligations

### 12. Standard of Care
**Check:** What standard of care applies? "Reasonable care," "same care as own confidential information," or both?
**DE Position:** Either standard is acceptable. The "same care as own" standard is slightly stronger but standard.
**Red Flags:** "Best endeavours" or "all reasonable endeavours" (higher standard than market). No standard of care specified at all.
**Default Priority:** ACCEPT
**Redline:** Not typically needed.

### 13. Use Restriction Tied to Purpose
**Check:** Is use of CI restricted to a defined Purpose?
**DE Position:** Purpose must be clearly defined. Use restriction must reference the Purpose. Without this, the CI definition is meaningless.
**Red Flags:** No defined Purpose. Purpose defined so broadly it provides no restriction ("any and all business purposes"). Use restriction missing or not tied to the Purpose.
**Default Priority:** DEFEND (if Purpose undefined or use unrestricted)
**Redline:** Define Purpose clearly: "evaluating a potential commercial relationship between the Parties in relation to [description]."

### 14. Breach Notification
**Check:** Does the NDA require notification of actual or suspected breach?
**DE Position:** Mutual breach notification is standard and appropriate.
**Red Flags:** One-way notification obligation (only Recipient must notify). No notification obligation at all. Notification required for "suspected" breach with very tight timeline (e.g., 24 hours).
**Default Priority:** NEGOTIATE (if one-way)
**Redline:** Make notification obligation mutual.

## E. Permitted Disclosures

### 15. Representative Scope
**Check:** Does the definition of Representatives include professional advisers (lawyers, accountants)?
**DE Position:** Must include professional advisers. DE needs to share CI with its legal and financial advisers for deal evaluation. Financing sources should also be included (see item 16).
**Red Flags:** Representatives limited to "employees and directors" only — excludes professional advisers. Representatives include "agents" without qualification (too broad).
**Default Priority:** NEGOTIATE (if advisers excluded)
**Redline:** Add "professional advisers" to the definition of Representatives.

### 16. Financing Source Carve-Out
**Check:** Is the Recipient permitted to share CI with bona fide financing sources?
**DE Position:** DE needs this for fundraising and project finance activities. If missing, flag and negotiate.
**Red Flags:** No financing source carve-out. Financing sources included but without requirement for equivalent confidentiality obligations.
**Default Priority:** NEGOTIATE (if missing)
**Redline:** Add: "The Receiving Party may disclose Confidential Information to its bona fide financing sources, provided such sources are bound by confidentiality obligations no less restrictive than those in this Agreement."

### 17. Legal Compulsion Procedure
**Check:** Does the NDA allow disclosure required by law/court order, with prior notice and consultation?
**DE Position:** Legal compulsion carve-out is essential. Must include: (a) carve-out for legally required disclosures, (b) prior written notice to Discloser where legally permitted, (c) consultation on scope of disclosure.
**Red Flags:** No legal compulsion carve-out at all. Carve-out without any notice requirement. "Required by law" without "where permitted by law" notice qualifier.
**Default Priority:** DEFEND (if carve-out missing entirely)
**Redline:** Add standard legal compulsion clause with notice and consultation.

## F. Return and Destruction

### 18. Compliance Retention Exception
**Check:** Can the Recipient retain CI to comply with legal, regulatory, or professional obligations?
**DE Position:** Compliance retention exception is required. DE may be required to retain certain information for regulatory, tax, or compliance purposes.
**Red Flags:** No retention exception whatsoever. Blanket "return or destroy all copies" without any carve-out. Retention exception limited to "regulatory authority requirements" but not legal hold or compliance archives.
**Default Priority:** NEGOTIATE (if missing)
**Redline:** Add: "The Receiving Party may retain copies of Confidential Information to the extent required by applicable law, regulation, or its internal compliance policies, provided such retained copies remain subject to the confidentiality obligations of this Agreement."

### 19. Electronic Copies
**Check:** Is the return/destruction obligation practical for electronic copies, including automated backups?
**DE Position:** Blanket "no copies in any form including electronic copies on computer systems or backup drives" is impractical. Modern IT generates automated backups that cannot be selectively purged.
**Red Flags:** Requirement to purge "all electronic copies including backup drives and cloud storage." No carve-out for automated backup systems. Requirement to certify destruction of copies that may exist on backup tapes not under DE's operational control.
**Default Priority:** NEGOTIATE (if blanket electronic prohibition)
**Redline:** Add: "Notwithstanding the foregoing, the Receiving Party shall not be required to purge Confidential Information from automated backup or archival systems, provided that such information is not accessed or used except as required by applicable law, and the confidentiality obligations of this Agreement continue to apply."

## G. Term and Duration

### 20. Term Structure
**Check:** Does the NDA have a fixed term, or are obligations perpetual (continuing while CI is held)?
**DE Position:** Both are acceptable for pure NDAs without restrictive covenants. Perpetual (e.g., "obligations continue for as long as CI remains confidential") is standard in UK practice. For NDAs with NC, standstill, or non-solicitation: require a fixed term.
**Red Flags:** Perpetual term combined with restrictive covenants (NC, standstill). No term and no termination mechanism.
**Default Priority:** ACCEPT (pure NDA) / NEGOTIATE (if restrictive covenants are perpetual)
**Redline:** For NDAs with covenants: "This Agreement shall remain in force for [24/36] months from the date hereof, unless terminated earlier by either Party on [30] days' written notice."

### 21. Survival Period
**Check:** How long do confidentiality obligations survive termination?
**DE Position:** 2-5 years is standard. Indefinite for trade secrets is acceptable. Perpetual survival without trade secret qualification is acceptable for pure NDAs.
**Red Flags:** Survival period less than 2 years (insufficient protection for DE). No survival clause at all (obligations may terminate with the NDA).
**Default Priority:** ACCEPT
**Redline:** Not typically needed.

## H. Remedies and Indemnity

### 22. Injunctive Relief Acknowledgement
**Check:** Does the NDA include an acknowledgement that damages may be inadequate and that equitable/injunctive relief is available?
**DE Position:** Standard and recommended. This expedites interim relief applications.
**Red Flags:** Absent entirely. Clause that limits remedies to damages only. Clause waiving right to seek injunctive relief.
**Default Priority:** NEGOTIATE (if absent) / DEFEND (if injunctive relief waived — Critical Red)
**Redline:** Add: "Each Party acknowledges that a breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm for which damages would not be an adequate remedy. Accordingly, the non-breaching Party shall be entitled to seek injunctive or other equitable relief without the need to prove actual loss."

### 23. Indemnity Terms
**Check:** Is the indemnity mutual? What is the costs basis? Is it capped?
**DE Position:** Mutual indemnity is acceptable. "Indemnity basis" costs: ALWAYS NEGOTIATE to "reasonable and documented costs." One-way uncapped indemnity: DEFEND (RED).
**Red Flags:** One-way indemnity (DE indemnifies but counterparty does not). "Indemnity basis" costs (full actual costs without assessment). No cap on indemnity for an NDA. Indemnity scope includes "indirect, consequential, or special damages."
**Default Priority:** ALWAYS NEGOTIATE costs basis / DEFEND one-way or uncapped
**Redline:** Change "on an indemnity basis" to "reasonable and documented costs" or "on the standard basis." If one-way: make mutual.

## I. Governing Law and Jurisdiction

### 24. Law-Jurisdiction Alignment
**Check:** Does the governing law match the jurisdiction for disputes?
**DE Position:** Matching pairs are standard (English law + English courts, Dutch law + Dutch courts). Mismatches (English law + Swiss courts) are unusual and may cause practical issues.
**Red Flags:** Governing law and jurisdiction are different countries without obvious reason. Arbitration seat in a different country from the governing law.
**Default Priority:** ACCEPT (matching pairs)
**Redline:** Not typically needed. If mismatched, NEGOTIATE alignment.

### 25. Exclusive vs Non-Exclusive Jurisdiction
**Check:** Is the jurisdiction clause exclusive or non-exclusive?
**DE Position:** For NDAs specifically, accept exclusive foreign jurisdiction. The probability of litigating an NDA is low, and the commercial relationship matters more than jurisdictional flexibility.
**Red Flags:** Exclusive jurisdiction in a jurisdiction where DE has no presence and enforcement would be impractical. Mandatory arbitration for NDA disputes (overkill).
**Default Priority:** ACCEPT (for NDAs)
**Redline:** Not typically needed. If desired: change "exclusive" to "non-exclusive" or add: "Nothing in this Clause prevents either Party from seeking interim or injunctive relief from any court of competent jurisdiction."
