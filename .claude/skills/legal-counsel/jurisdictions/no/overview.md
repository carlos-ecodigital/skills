# Norway -- Legal System Overview

## Legal System

Norway follows a **Scandinavian civil law** system. Unlike continental European civil law (e.g., Netherlands, Germany), Norwegian law relies on a combination of codified statutes and customary principles (sedvane), with significant weight given to legislative preparatory works (forarbeider) for statutory interpretation. Supreme Court (Hoyesterett) precedent carries strong persuasive authority.

## Key Principles

### Avtalefrihet (Freedom of Contract)
Parties may freely determine their contractual terms, subject to mandatory law (preseptorisk lovgivning). Avtaleloven (Contract Act, 1918) provides the general framework.

### Avtaleloven § 36 (General Reasonableness Clause)
The overriding fairness mechanism in Norwegian contract law:
- A contract may be set aside or adjusted in whole or in part if it would be **urimelig** (unreasonable) or contrary to **god forretningsskikk** (good business practice) to enforce it
- Applies to all contracts, including negotiated commercial agreements
- Courts consider: content of the agreement, the parties' positions, circumstances at the time of formation, subsequent events, and other factors
- Comparable to Dutch Art. 6:248 BW (redelijkheid en billijkheid) but codified as a standalone provision

### Lojalitetsplikt (Duty of Loyalty)
Norwegian contract law imposes a general duty of loyalty between contracting parties. This unwritten principle requires parties to take reasonable account of each other's interests, including during negotiations and performance.

## EEA Membership

Norway is not an EU member but participates in the internal market through the EEA Agreement. Key implications:
- EU directives and regulations transposed into Norwegian law (with some exceptions)
- EFTA Surveillance Authority (ESA) and EFTA Court oversight
- No EU customs union membership (separate customs/trade policy)
- No participation in EU Common Agricultural Policy or Common Fisheries Policy
- EU financial services directives generally apply (MiFID II, AIFMD, etc.)
