# Norway -- Dispute Resolution

## Court System

### Alminnelige domstoler (Ordinary Courts)

| Level | Court | Jurisdiction |
|---|---|---|
| First instance | Tingrett (district court) | General civil and criminal; 23 tingretter |
| Appeal | Lagmannsrett (court of appeal) | 6 courts; full re-examination of facts and law |
| Supreme Court | Hoyesterett | Final appeal; leave required (ankeutvalget); focuses on legal issues of general importance |

### Forliksradet (Conciliation Board)
- Mandatory first step for most civil claims under NOK 200,000 (with exceptions)
- Lay judges; informal procedure
- Can render binding judgment if both parties consent or claim is under threshold
- Claim must be filed with forliksradet before tingrett in most cases -- tvisteloven § 6-2

### Smaakravsprosess (Small Claims Procedure)
- Claims under NOK 250,000 -- tvisteloven kap. 10
- Simplified procedure, limited costs
- Generally no appeal on facts (only law)

## Voldgift (Arbitration)

### Voldgiftsloven (Arbitration Act) -- Lov 2004-05-14 nr. 25
- Based on UNCITRAL Model Law
- Parties may agree to arbitration for disputes they can freely dispose of
- Arbitral awards are final and enforceable; limited grounds for setting aside

### Common Arbitration Institutions

| Institution | Common Use |
|---|---|
| NCC (Nordic Arbitration Centre / Oslo Chamber of Commerce) | Domestic and Nordic commercial disputes |
| ICC (International Chamber of Commerce) | International commercial disputes |
| SCC (Stockholm Chamber of Commerce) | Common for Scandinavian cross-border disputes |
| LCIA (London) | Anglo-Norwegian disputes |
| UNCITRAL ad hoc | Tailored to parties' needs |

### Typical Arbitration Clauses
- Norwegian commercial contracts commonly include arbitration clauses for disputes above a certain threshold
- Smaller claims often routed through ordinary courts with arbitration reserved for larger disputes
- Energy sector: ICC or NCC arbitration standard for project finance and EPC disputes

## Mekling (Mediation)

- Courts actively encourage mekling (mediation) -- tvisteloven § 8-1: court may order rettsmekling (court-annexed mediation)
- Rettsmekling: conducted by a judge (not the trial judge); non-binding; widely used and successful
- Private mediation: no specific statutory framework; contractual mediation clauses enforceable
- Common in construction disputes, commercial disputes, and employment matters

## Namsmannen (Enforcement Authority)

- **Tvangsfullbyrdelse** (enforcement) of judgments and arbitral awards
- Namsmannen (enforcement officer) handles: utlegg (attachment), tvangssalg (forced sale), fravikelse (eviction)
- Hjemmel: tvangsfullbyrdelsesloven (Enforcement Act)
- Midlertidig forfoying (interim measures/injunctions): available from courts on application -- tvisteloven kap. 32-34

## Foreign Judgment Recognition

| Source | Mechanism |
|---|---|
| EU/EEA (Lugano Convention) | Lugano Convention 2007: recognition and enforcement similar to Brussels regime; no exequatur for Lugano II judgments |
| Nordic countries | Nordic Enforcement Convention: simplified recognition of Nordic judgments |
| Other countries | No general statutory mechanism; foreign judgments may be used as evidence; enforcement requires Norwegian proceedings based on the original claim |

## Arbitral Award Enforcement

- **New York Convention (1958):** Norway is a signatory; foreign arbitral awards enforceable via tingrett
- Limited grounds for refusal (Art. V New York Convention)
- Norwegian courts are generally pro-arbitration

## Foreldelse (Limitation Periods)

See `contract-law.md` for limitation periods. Key difference from NL: Norwegian general limitation is **3 years** (not 5 years as in Netherlands).

## Key Procedural Features

- **Advokattvang** (mandatory legal representation): required for lagmannsrett and Hoyesterett; not required for tingrett or forliksrad (but common in practice)
- **No discovery:** Norway has limited disclosure; parties must produce specific documents on request if relevant -- tvisteloven § 26-5
- **No jury for civil cases**
- **Sakskostnader** (costs): loser-pays principle, but court has discretion; costs typically based on actual reasonable costs (unlike NL fixed tariff system)
