# Netherlands -- Legal System Overview

## Legal System

The Netherlands follows a **civil law** system based on the Burgerlijk Wetboek (BW / Civil Code). Unlike common law jurisdictions, Dutch law relies primarily on codified statutes rather than judicial precedent, though Hoge Raad (Supreme Court) case law has significant interpretive authority.

## Key Principles

### Contractsvrijheid (Freedom of Contract)
Parties may freely determine their contractual terms, subject only to mandatory law (dwingend recht) and public policy (openbare orde / goede zeden, Art. 3:40 BW).

### Redelijkheid en billijkheid (Reasonableness and Fairness, Art. 6:248 BW)
The overriding principle of Dutch contract law:
- **Aanvullende werking (supplementary function, lid 1):** fills gaps in the contract
- **Derogerende werking (derogating function, lid 2):** a contractual provision does not apply insofar as this would be onaanvaardbaar (unacceptable) under the circumstances
- Even well-drafted, heavily negotiated contracts may be adjusted by courts

### Haviltex Interpretation Standard
Contract interpretation follows the Haviltex standard (HR 13 March 1981): the meaning of contractual provisions depends on what parties could reasonably expect from each other, considering all circumstances -- not just the literal text. Contrast with English law's objective/textual approach.

### Notarieel stelsel (Notarial System)
Security over shares, real property, and certain rights requires a notariele akte (notarial deed). This is mandatory for:
- Oprichting (incorporation) of BV, NV, Stichting, Cooperatie
- Aandelenlevering (share transfer) in a BV or NV
- Hypotheek (mortgage) creation
- Vestiging zakelijk recht (creation of right in rem) such as opstal or erfpacht

### Kadaster (Land Registry)
Registration system for rights in rem over onroerend goed (immovable property) and zakelijke rechten. Registration determines priority of rights.

## Burgerlijk Wetboek -- Structure

| Boek (Book) | Onderwerp (Subject) | Key Provisions |
|---|---|---|
| Boek 1 | Personen- en familierecht (Persons and Family Law) | Rarely relevant for commercial transactions |
| Boek 2 | Rechtspersonen (Legal Entities) | BV, NV, Stichting, Cooperatie, Vereniging -- see `corporate-law.md` |
| Boek 3 | Vermogensrecht (Patrimonial Law) | Property rights, pledge, assignment -- see `property-law.md` |
| Boek 4 | Erfrecht (Succession Law) | Rarely relevant for commercial transactions |
| Boek 5 | Zakelijke rechten (Rights in Rem) | Opstal, erfpacht, hypotheek -- see `property-law.md` |
| Boek 6 | Verbintenissen (Obligations) | Contract law, tort, unjust enrichment -- see `contract-law.md` |
| Boek 7 | Bijzondere overeenkomsten (Specific Contracts) | Sale, lease, construction, agency -- see `contract-law.md` |
| Boek 8 | Verkeersmiddelen en vervoer (Means of Transport) | Rarely relevant for general commercial work |
| Boek 10 | IPR (Private International Law) | Choice of law, jurisdiction |

## Wet op het financieel toezicht (Wft / Financial Supervision Act)

### Fund Manager Licensing

| Regime | Threshold | Supervisor | Requirements |
|---|---|---|---|
| Full AIFMD license (vergunning) | Above thresholds or voluntary | AFM | Full compliance: depositaris, risk management, reporting, remuneration policy |
| Light regime / registration (Art. 2:66a Wft) | <EUR 100M AuM (with leverage) or <EUR 500M AuM (no leverage, closed-end, no redemption within 5 years) | AFM registration | Simplified: registration, AIFMD Annex IV reporting, no depositary required |

### Prospectus Requirements

| Scenario | Obligation | Authority |
|---|---|---|
| Public offering of securities > EUR 8M | Mandatory AFM-approved prospectus | AFM |
| Offer exclusively to gekwalificeerde beleggers (qualified investors) | Exempt | N/A |
| Offer to < 150 natural or legal persons per member state | Exempt | N/A |
| Total consideration < EUR 8M in rolling 12 months | Exempt | N/A |
| Minimum denomination EUR 100,000 per unit | Exempt | N/A |

Source: Verordening (EU) 2017/1129 (Prospectus Regulation); Art. 5:2 Wft.

### DNB Prudential Oversight

- **Bankvergunning (banking license):** required for entities accepting opvorderbare gelden (repayable funds) from the public (Art. 2:11 Wft)
- **Securitisation Regulation (EU 2017/2402):** applies to securitisation SPVs; STS framework for favourable treatment
- **DORA (Digital Operational Resilience Act, eff. 17 Jan 2025):** applies to financial entities and their ICT third-party service providers

## Faillissementsbestendigheid (Bankruptcy Remoteness)

Key structuring principle in Dutch structured and project finance:
- Use stichtingen (foundations) as orphan entities to hold shares or assets
- Limited-recourse mechanisms in SPV structures
- Stichting administratiekantoor (STAK) separates legal ownership from economic interest via certificaten van aandelen (depository receipts)

## Substance Requirements

For treaty benefits, EU Directive compliance, and anti-abuse:
- Adequate NL substance required under ATAD/ATAD2 implementation (eff. 1 Jan 2019)
- **Minimum indicators:** NL-resident directors with relevant expertise, NL bank accounts, NL board meetings (at least 50% held in NL), decision-making in NL, adequate office space, bookkeeping in NL
- **OECD MLI** impact: PPT (principal purposes test) applies to most Dutch treaties
- Relevant for holding structures to ensure deelnemingsvrijstelling and treaty WHT reductions
