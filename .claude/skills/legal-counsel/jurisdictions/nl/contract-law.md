# Netherlands -- Contract Law

## Governing Framework

Dutch contract law is primarily codified in **Boek 6 BW (Verbintenissen / Obligations)** and **Boek 7 BW (Bijzondere overeenkomsten / Specific Contracts)**.

## Boek 6 BW -- Key Provisions

### Ingebrekestelling (Notice of Default, Art. 6:82 BW)

- **Written notice** (schriftelijke aanmaning) with redelijke termijn (reasonable term) required before claiming schadevergoeding (damages) or ontbinding (termination)
- **Exceptions (Art. 6:83 BW):** no ingebrekestelling needed if:
  - Fatale termijn (fixed deadline) expired
  - Performance is blijvend onmogelijk (permanently impossible)
  - Debtor has declared it will not perform (mededeling)
- **Drafting note:** always include explicit notice requirements in contracts to override default rules; specify notice method, cure period, and consequences

### Boeteclausule (Penalty / Liquidated Damages, Art. 6:91 BW)

- **Enforceable** as agreed between parties
- Subject to **rechterlijke matiging (judicial reduction, Art. 6:94 BW)** if penalty is buitensporig (disproportionate) to actual loss
- **No cumulation** of penalty with schadevergoeding unless expressly agreed (Art. 6:92 lid 2 BW default)
- **Drafting note:** document reasoning for penalty amounts in negotiations; draft as genuine pre-estimate of loss; explicitly state whether penalty is exclusive or cumulative with damages

### Overmacht (Force Majeure, Art. 6:75 BW)

- Narrow interpretation: tekortkoming (breach) must be **niet toerekenbaar** (not attributable) to the debtor
- Not attributable if: not due to schuld (fault), and not krachtens wet, rechtshandeling, of verkeersopvattingen (by law, legal act, or common opinion) for debtor's account
- **Transportschaarste (grid congestion):** widely foreseeable in NL; NOT likely to qualify as overmacht in new contracts
- **Pandemie (pandemic):** no longer novel; unlikely FM defence after 2020
- **Drafting note:** include explicit contractual FM definitions listing qualifying events, notice requirements, mitigation obligations, and consequences (suspension vs. termination)

### Redelijkheid en billijkheid (Good Faith, Art. 6:248 BW)

- **Aanvullende werking (lid 1):** fills gaps in contract
- **Derogerende werking (lid 2):** provision does not apply if onaanvaardbaar (unacceptable) under circumstances
- Particularly relevant for: hardship, changed circumstances, and long-term contracts
- Courts will consider all circumstances including: the nature and contents of the agreement, the way it was formed, the parties' mutual positions, and societal views

### Onvoorziene omstandigheden (Unforeseen Circumstances, Art. 6:258 BW)

- Court may modify or (partially) dissolve contract if circumstances arise that were not foreseen at formation
- Circumstances must be of such nature that the counterparty cannot expect unaltered maintenance of the agreement
- High threshold: risk allocation in the contract is assessed first
- **Drafting note:** include hardship / material adverse change clauses to provide contractual mechanism before resorting to Art. 6:258

### Ontbinding (Termination, Art. 6:265 BW)

- Right to terminate (ontbinden) contract upon tekortkoming (breach) unless breach is too insignificant (gezien haar bijzondere aard of geringe betekenis)
- Requires ingebrekestelling first unless exception applies (Art. 6:83 BW)
- **Gevolgen:** mutual obligations to return performance (ongedaanmaking, Art. 6:271 BW); right to schadevergoeding preserved

### Dwaling (Mistake, Art. 6:228 BW)

- Contract voidable if party formed an incorrect assumption at formation, and:
  - Other party should have informed (schending mededelingsplicht)
  - Other party gave incorrect information
  - Both parties shared the same incorrect assumption
- Not available if risk was accepted or should have been known through normal due diligence

### Algemene voorwaarden (General Terms and Conditions, Art. 6:231--247 BW)

- **Vernietigbaar (voidable):** if counterparty was not given reasonable opportunity to take cognisance (Art. 6:233 sub b BW)
- **Zwarte lijst (black list, Art. 6:236 BW)** and **grijze lijst (grey list, Art. 6:237 BW):** certain terms presumed unreasonable in B2C contracts
- B2B: no black/grey list, but Art. 6:248 BW derogating function still applies
- **Battle of forms (Art. 6:225 lid 3 BW):** first reference prevails unless second party explicitly rejects first party's terms

## Boek 7 BW -- Specific Contracts

### Aanneming van werk (Contract for Work, Art. 7:750 BW)

Governs EPC/construction contracts.

- **Oplevering (delivery/completion, Art. 7:758 BW):** triggers risk transfer from aannemer (contractor) to opdrachtgever (employer)
- After oplevering, aannemer discharged from liability for gebreken (defects) that opdrachtgever knew or should have discovered
- **Verborgen gebreken (hidden defects):** 5-year verjaringstermijn (limitation) from ontdekking (discovery); absolute limit 20 years from oplevering (Art. 7:761 BW)
- **Waarschuwingsplicht (duty to warn, Art. 7:754 BW):** aannemer must warn for errors in opdrachtgever's design/instructions
- **Standard conditions:** UAV 2012 / UAV-GC 2005 often incorporated for larger projects

### Huur (Lease, Art. 7:201 BW)

- **Bedrijfsruimte (Art. 7:290 BW):** retail/hospitality -- minimum 5+5 year terms, renewal rights, huurbescherming (tenant protection)
- **Overige bedrijfsruimte (Art. 7:230a BW):** office, industrial -- less protection; court may grant ontruimingsbescherming up to 3 years
- **Preference:** use recht van opstal over huur for installations on third-party land to avoid huurbescherming and create zakelijk recht (right in rem)

### Koop (Sale, Art. 7:1 BW)

- Conformiteitseis (conformity requirement, Art. 7:17 BW): delivered goods must conform to the agreement
- Klachtplicht (duty to complain, Art. 7:23 BW): buyer must notify seller within bekwame tijd (reasonable time) of discovering non-conformity

## Verjaring (Limitation Periods)

| Claim Type | Period | Basis |
|---|---|---|
| General contractual claims | 5 years from claim becoming due | Art. 3:307 BW |
| Tort claims | 5 years from knowledge of damage + liable party | Art. 3:310 BW |
| Hidden defects (construction) | 5 years from discovery; max 20 years from oplevering | Art. 7:761 BW |
| Absolute maximum | 20 years from event | Art. 3:306 BW |
| Tax claims (belastingschuld) | 5 years from assessment | Art. 4:104 Awb |

## Interpretation Principles

1. **Haviltex** (HR 13 March 1981): subjective/objective test -- what parties could reasonably expect from each other in all circumstances
2. **CAO-norm** (HR 17 September 1993): for contracts with effect on third parties (e.g., collective agreements) -- objective/linguistic interpretation prevails
3. **DSM/Fox** (HR 20 February 2004): entire spectrum from Haviltex to CAO-norm; court determines appropriate standard based on circumstances
