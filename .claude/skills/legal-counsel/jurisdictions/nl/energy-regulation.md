# Netherlands -- Energy Regulation

## Energiewet / Energy Act (eff. 1 Jan 2026)

Replaces Elektriciteitswet 1998 and Gaswet. Official text: wetten.overheid.nl/BWBR0050714.

## Key Changes

| Subject | Old Situation | New Situation (Energiewet) |
|---|---|---|
| Cable pooling (gedeelde aansluiting) | Limited to production; min 2 MVA; max 2 parties | All user types (production + consumption); min 100 kVA; up to 4 parties; BESS explicitly included |
| ACM anticipatory approval | N/A | ACM may grant approvals for grid operator investments ahead of formal demand |
| Energiegemeenschappen (energy communities) | No formal framework | Legal basis for cooperatieve energiegemeenschappen and burgergemeenschappen; right to share energy |
| Grid operator obligations | Limited transparency | Enhanced transparency on connection timelines; publication of available capacity |
| Prosumer rights | Basic teruglevering (feed-in) | Expanded rights: dynamic tariffs, data access, energy sharing |
| Leveringsvergunning (supply license) | Separate E and G licenses | Single integrated vergunning |
| Meetverantwoordelijkheid (metering) | Varied | Clearer framework: netbeheerder or erkende meetverantwoordelijke |
| Systeemintegratie | Ad hoc | Explicit provisions for flexibility services and storage |

## Grid Connection (Aansluitplicht)

- **Netbeheerder** must connect within redelijke termijn (reasonable term)
- **Transportschaarste (grid congestion):** ~60 GW battery storage in connection queue vs ~20 GW peak demand; average wait up to 10 years in congested areas; over 12,000 companies waiting
- **TenneT TDTR (Transportduurgerechtigden Transmissienet Regime):** 6 GW capacity granted for installations with >85% hour availability
- **Non-firm aansluiting:** contractual arrangement subject to curtailment during congestion; developing in practice

## Cable Pooling (Gedeelde Aansluiting)

Under Energiewet (eff. 1 Jan 2026):
- All installation types eligible (production, consumption, storage)
- Minimum **100 kVA** (reduced from 2 MVA)
- Up to **4 parties** per connection
- BESS and electrolysers explicitly included
- Requires ACM approval

**Use case:** BESS co-located with solar/wind behind single grid connection; dramatically reduces waiting time and connection costs.

## SDE++ (Stimulering Duurzame Energieproductie en Klimaattransitie)

- Operating subsidy for renewable energy and CO2-reduction technologies
- EUR 100M allocated for BESS in recent rounds
- Administered by RVO
- Typically 15-year subsidy period
- Basisbedrag (base amount) minus basisenergieprijs (base energy price) = subsidy per unit

## Congestion Management

- **Congestiemanagement (congestion management):** netbeheerder may curtail feed-in/offtake during congestion
- **Verplicht (mandatory):** grid operators required to implement congestion management under Energiewet
- **Market-based mechanisms:** day-ahead/intraday redispatch; compensation for curtailed parties
- BESS can participate in congestion management as flexibility service

## Ancillary Services Market

| Service | Description | Typical Revenue (NL) |
|---|---|---|
| FCR (Frequency Containment Reserve) | Primary frequency control; response <30s | ~EUR 13/MW/h |
| aFRR (Automatic Frequency Restoration Reserve) | Secondary frequency control; response <5min | Variable; merit order |
| mFRR (Manual Frequency Restoration Reserve) | Tertiary reserve; response <15min | Variable |
| EPEX SPOT day-ahead/intraday | Energy arbitrage | NL spread 83--121 EUR/MWh (2024/2025) |
