# Netherlands -- Dispute Resolution

## Court System

### Ordinary Courts (Gewone rechtspraak)

| Level | Court | Jurisdiction |
|---|---|---|
| First instance | Rechtbank (district court) | Civil, criminal, administrative; 11 arrondissementen |
| First instance (commercial) | Handelskamer (commercial chamber) | Claims > EUR 25,000; complex commercial disputes |
| First instance (smaller claims) | Kantonrechter (subdistrict judge) | Claims up to EUR 25,000; employment; lease |
| Appeal | Gerechtshof (court of appeal) | 4 courts; full re-examination of facts and law |
| Supreme Court | Hoge Raad | Cassation only (law, not facts); no leave requirement for civil |

### Specialised Courts

| Court | Jurisdiction |
|---|---|
| Ondernemingskamer (Enterprise Chamber, Amsterdam) | Corporate disputes: enqueterecht (inquiry proceedings), uitkoop (squeeze-out), geschillenregeling (share dispute resolution) |
| Netherlands Commercial Court (NCC) | English-language commercial proceedings; part of Amsterdam rechtbank and gerechtshof; opt-in by agreement; court fees EUR 15,000 (first instance) / EUR 20,000 (appeal) |
| Intellectual Property Court | Patent and IP disputes; The Hague |

## Kort geding (Summary Proceedings)

- Urgent interim relief from voorzieningenrechter (summary proceedings judge)
- **Timeline:** hearing typically within 1--3 weeks of application
- **Standard:** voorlopig oordeel (provisional assessment); balance of interests (belangenafweging)
- **Types of relief:** gebod (order to act), verbod (injunction), voorlopige voorziening (provisional measure)
- Decision is voorlopig (provisional): does not bind in bodemprocedure (main proceedings)
- Widely used in commercial disputes for injunctions, interim payments, disclosure orders

## Arbitrage (Arbitration)

### NAI (Nederlands Arbitrage Instituut)

- Seat: Rotterdam
- Rules: NAI Arbitration Rules (most recent version)
- Language: Dutch (default) or English by agreement
- **Speed:** typical proceedings 9--15 months
- **Costs:** registration fee EUR 2,475 + arbitrator fees based on amount in dispute

### ICC (International Chamber of Commerce)

- Most common international arbitration for NL cross-border disputes
- Seat in NL frequently chosen (Amsterdam, The Hague)
- ICC Rules; administrative charges + arbitrator fees (ad valorem scale)

### NCC (Netherlands Commercial Court)

- Not technically arbitration but English-language litigation
- Attractive alternative for parties wanting court proceedings in English
- Full appeal to NCC Court of Appeal (Gerechtshof Amsterdam)

### Other Arbitration Institutions

| Institution | Common Use |
|---|---|
| LCIA (London) | Common in Anglo-Dutch disputes |
| ICSID (Washington) | Investment treaty arbitration |
| PCA (The Hague) | State-to-state and investor-state |
| UNCITRAL ad hoc | Tailored to parties' needs |

## Mediation

- No mandatory mediation in NL, but courts actively encourage it
- MfN (Mediatorsfederatie Nederland) register of certified mediators
- Mediationclausule (mediation clause) increasingly common in commercial contracts
- **Effect of mediation clause:** Dutch courts generally respect but do not stay proceedings pending mediation (unlike some common law jurisdictions)

## Enforcement

### Domestic Enforcement

- Uitvoerbaar bij voorraad (provisionally enforceable): most court judgments can be enforced pending appeal if declared uitvoerbaar bij voorraad
- Executie (enforcement) by deurwaarder (bailiff): beslag (attachment), executoriale verkoop (forced sale)
- **Conservatoir beslag (prejudgment attachment):** available ex parte from voorzieningenrechter; extremely common in NL; used to preserve assets pending proceedings

### Foreign Judgment Recognition

| Source | Mechanism |
|---|---|
| EU Member State | Brussels Ia Regulation (EU 1215/2012): automatic recognition; no exequatur required |
| EFTA (Norway, etc.) | Lugano Convention: similar to Brussels regime |
| Non-EU/EFTA | Bilateral treaty (few) or Art. 431 Rv: fresh proceedings but can be simplified if foreign judgment meets Dutch standards |

### Arbitral Award Enforcement

- **New York Convention (1958):** NL is signatory; foreign arbitral awards enforceable via exequatur procedure at rechtbank (Art. 1075 Rv)
- Grounds for refusal: limited (Art. V New York Convention)
- NL courts pro-arbitration; enforcement generally straightforward

## Verjaring (Limitation Periods) -- Dispute Context

| Type | Period | Start |
|---|---|---|
| Contractual claims | 5 years | From date claim is due and claimant becomes aware (Art. 3:307/310 BW) |
| Tort (onrechtmatige daad) | 5 years | From knowledge of damage + liable party |
| Absolute maximum | 20 years | From the event causing damage |

- **Stuiting (interruption):** by written demand (schriftelijke aanmaning), commencement of proceedings, or acknowledgment of claim (Art. 3:317 BW)
- Stuiting restarts the limitation period

## Key Procedural Features

- **Verplichte procesvertegenwoordiging (mandatory legal representation):** advocaat required for proceedings before rechtbank and gerechtshof (not kantonrechter)
- **No discovery:** NL has limited disclosure; Art. 843a Rv provides for specific document production on conditions (rechtmatig belang, bepaald bescheid, rechtsbetrekking)
- **No jury trials**
- **Loser pays (proceskostenveroordeling):** but based on fixed tariff (liquidatietarief), not actual costs -- often substantially below actual costs
