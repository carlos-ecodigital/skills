# United States -- Jurisdiction Overview

> **Status:** Phase 3 stub. To be expanded with full US jurisdiction content.

## Legal System

**Federal common law** system with **50 state jurisdictions**. The US Constitution establishes the federal framework; most commercial law is state law. Key uniform laws (UCC, Uniform LLC Act) create cross-state consistency but adoption varies.

Delaware dominates corporate law (67% of Fortune 500 incorporated there). New York governs most major commercial contracts. California leads on employment and data privacy.

## Court Structure

| Level | Federal | State (varies) |
|---|---|---|
| Trial | US District Courts (94 districts) | Superior/Circuit/District Courts |
| Appeal | US Circuit Courts of Appeals (13 circuits) | State Appellate Courts |
| Final | US Supreme Court | State Supreme Courts |

**Key specialist courts:** Bankruptcy Courts, Tax Court, Court of International Trade, Delaware Court of Chancery (corporate/equity).

## Key Legislation (Commercial)

| Area | Primary Source |
|---|---|
| Contract | UCC (goods), Restatement (Second) of Contracts, state common law |
| Corporate | DGCL (Delaware), MBCA (model), state corporation statutes |
| Employment | FLSA, Title VII, ADA, FMLA, state laws (at-will default) |
| Tax | Internal Revenue Code (IRC), Treasury Regulations |
| Data privacy | CCPA/CPRA (CA), sectoral (HIPAA, GLBA, COPPA), no federal omnibus |
| Securities | Securities Act 1933, Exchange Act 1934, Dodd-Frank |
| Competition | Sherman Act, Clayton Act, FTC Act |
| IP | Patent Act (35 USC), Copyright Act (17 USC), Lanham Act (15 USC) |

## Key Principles

- **Freedom of contract** -- very strong, particularly in Delaware (contractarian philosophy)
- **Parol evidence rule** -- prior/contemporaneous oral evidence excluded if written contract integrated
- **Choice of law** -- parties generally free to choose governing state law; enforced if reasonable nexus
- **Discovery** -- extremely broad pre-trial discovery (depositions, interrogatories, document requests) -- distinguishes US litigation from all other jurisdictions
- **Contingency fees and class actions** -- drive litigation dynamics, absent in European systems
