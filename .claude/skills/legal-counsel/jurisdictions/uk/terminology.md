# United Kingdom -- Legal Terminology

Key English legal terms encountered in NDA review. Explanations for non-UK lawyers.

## Costs and Indemnity

| Term | Meaning | NDA Context |
|---|---|---|
| **Indemnity basis** (costs) | Costs assessment where doubt is resolved in favour of the receiving party. No proportionality requirement. Higher recovery than standard basis. | DE policy: always negotiate away. See redline-playbook.md #5. |
| **Standard basis** (costs) | Default costs assessment. Must be proportionate and reasonable. Doubt resolved against the receiving party. | DE's preferred fallback if "reasonable and documented costs" is rejected. |
| **Indemnify / indemnity** | Obligation to compensate for loss arising from a specified event. Creates a primary obligation (not dependent on proving breach). | Common in NDA remedies clauses. Check: mutual or one-way? Capped or uncapped? |
| **Hold harmless** | Contractual protection against liability to third parties. Often used alongside indemnity. | "Indemnify and hold harmless" — the two together cover both direct loss and third-party claims. |

## Remedies and Enforcement

| Term | Meaning | NDA Context |
|---|---|---|
| **Injunction** (interim) | Court order restraining a party from doing something, pending trial. American Cyanamid test applies. | Primary NDA enforcement tool. See contract-law.md Equitable Remedies section. |
| **Injunction** (final) | Permanent restraint ordered after trial. | End-state remedy for NDA breach — permanent prohibition on use/disclosure. |
| **Undertaking** | A promise to the court (not just to the other party). Breach = contempt of court. | Applicant for interim injunction must give an undertaking in damages. Extremely serious. |
| **Without prejudice** | Communications made in a genuine attempt to settle cannot be used as evidence in court. | Common in negotiation correspondence. Do not mark NDA redline emails "without prejudice" — they are commercial negotiations, not settlement offers. |
| **Without prejudice save as to costs** | WP communications that can be referred to on the question of costs (similar to Part 36). | Tactical tool in NDA dispute settlement. |
| **Specific performance** | Court order requiring a party to perform a contractual obligation. Discretionary. | Rarely relevant for NDAs (the obligation is negative — don't disclose — so injunction is the remedy). |

## Contract Formation and Terms

| Term | Meaning | NDA Context |
|---|---|---|
| **Consideration** | Something of value given in exchange for a promise. Required for binding simple contracts. | Mutual NDA: mutual promises = good consideration. Amendment without consideration may be unenforceable (unless by deed). |
| **Deed** | Formal legal instrument signed, witnessed, and delivered. Does not require consideration. 12-year limitation. | NDAs are almost never deeds. But if executed as a deed, limitation doubles from 6 to 12 years. |
| **Boilerplate** | Standard clauses found at the end of most commercial contracts (entire agreement, severability, waiver, assignment, notices). | In English law, boilerplate is enforceable and meaningful — not decorative. Missing boilerplate = real gaps. |
| **Entire agreement clause** | Clause stating the contract is the complete agreement between the parties, superseding all prior discussions. | Effective in English law to exclude pre-contractual representations (contrast with NL). |
| **Recitals** | Introductory paragraphs (usually "WHEREAS...") setting out context and background. | Not operative terms but may be used to interpret ambiguous operative clauses. |

## Legislation Referenced in NDA Review

| Abbreviation | Full Name | Relevance |
|---|---|---|
| **UCTA** | Unfair Contract Terms Act 1977 | Controls exclusion/limitation clauses. Reasonableness test for standard-form NDAs. |
| **CRA** | Consumer Rights Act 2015 | B2C only — not relevant to DE's commercial NDAs. |
| **TPA 1999** | Contracts (Rights of Third Parties) Act 1999 | Allows non-parties (e.g., Affiliates) to enforce NDA terms. Check if included or excluded. |
| **CA 2006** | Companies Act 2006 | Director authority (s.40), company execution (s.44), registered office (s.1139). |
| **CPR** | Civil Procedure Rules | Govern English court proceedings. Part 36 (offers), Part 44 (costs). |

## Corporate and Registry

| Term | Meaning | NDA Context |
|---|---|---|
| **Companies House** | UK company registry. Public records of companies, LLPs, and partnerships. | Primary source for counterparty verification (Phase 1, Step 1.2). |
| **Company number** | Unique 8-digit identifier assigned at incorporation. | Cross-reference against NDA registration number (if stated). |
| **Company secretary** | Corporate officer with filing responsibilities. Mandatory for PLCs, optional for Ltd. | May be NDA signatory — has authority to sign on behalf of company. |
| **Registered number** | Same as company number. Sometimes prefixed with jurisdiction indicator (e.g., SC for Scotland). | |
| **Persons with significant control (PSC)** | UBO equivalent — persons with >25% shares, >25% votes, or significant influence. | Useful context for counterparty profiling. |
| **Confirmation statement** | Annual filing at Companies House confirming company details are current. | Overdue confirmation statement = possible distress signal. |

## Disclosure and Proceedings

| Term | Meaning | NDA Context |
|---|---|---|
| **Disclosure** | English law equivalent of discovery. Obligation to reveal relevant documents to the other side in litigation. | More extensive than civil law discovery. Standard disclosure: documents on which you rely, documents that adversely affect your case, and documents supporting the other side's case. |
| **Privilege** | Legal professional privilege (solicitor-client communications for advice or litigation) protected from disclosure. | NDA review communications with external counsel are privileged. |
| **Part 36 offer** | Formal settlement offer under CPR Part 36 with automatic costs consequences if rejected. | Powerful tactical tool in NDA disputes. See dispute-resolution.md. |
