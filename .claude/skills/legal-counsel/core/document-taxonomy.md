## Jurisdiction Sensitivity Guide

- **Low**: Core terms are universal; minimal local law adaptation needed. Examples: NDA, LOI, commercial term sheets.
- **Low-Moderate**: Core terms are universal but liability caps, dispute resolution, and governing law clauses need jurisdiction review. Examples: MSA, service agreements, license agreements.
- **Moderate**: Significant provisions are jurisdiction-dependent (corporate governance, tax treatment, IP ownership). Examples: SHA, JV agreements, convertible instruments.
- **Moderate-High**: Most provisions require jurisdiction-specific drafting. Examples: SPA (notarial requirements in NL, different warranty regimes UK vs US), employment agreements.
- **High**: Almost entirely governed by mandatory local law. Examples: employment agreements, real estate leases, regulatory filings, tax returns.
