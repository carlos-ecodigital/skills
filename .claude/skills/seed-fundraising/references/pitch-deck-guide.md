# Pitch Deck Guide -- Seed Round for Digital Infrastructure / AI / Energy

## 1. Overview

A seed-stage pitch deck should be 12-15 slides, designed to earn 3-5 minutes of investor attention. VCs review hundreds of decks per month. Clarity, specificity, and visual simplicity win. The deck is NOT the pitch -- it's a visual aid for a conversation. The founder's narrative carries the weight.

## 2. The 12-Slide Canonical Structure

| Slide # | Title | Purpose | Archetype | Time |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Cover | First impression, name, tagline, contact | -- | 5s |
| 2 | Problem | Why this matters NOW | Musk | 30-45s |
| 3 | Solution | What you've built / are building | Musk + Lochmiller | 30-45s |
| 4 | Market Opportunity | TAM/SAM/SOM with conviction | Wood + Altman | 30-45s |
| 5 | Business Model | How you make money | Altman | 30s |
| 6 | Traction | What you've achieved so far | Lochmiller + Altman | 45-60s |
| 7 | Technology / Product | What's different and defensible | Lochmiller + Musk | 30-45s |
| 8 | Competition | Honest landscape + your positioning | Wood + Liebreich | 30s |
| 9 | Team | Why YOU can execute this | Lochmiller | 30s |
| 10 | Go-to-Market | How you'll acquire customers and grow | Altman | 30s |
| 11 | Financial Ask | How much, what for, what milestones | Altman + Musk | 30-45s |
| 12 | Vision | Where this goes in 5-10 years | Musk + Wood | 15-30s |

**Total presentation time: 6-8 minutes** (leaves time for Q&A in a 30-minute meeting).

## 3. Slide-by-Slide Guidance

### Slide 1: Cover
- Company name and logo
- One-sentence descriptor (max 10 words): e.g., "Co-locating AI compute and heat recovery on Dutch industrial sites"
- Founder name(s) and contact
- "Seed Round -- [Month Year]"
- **Design**: Clean. No clutter. Brand colors.

### Slide 2: Problem
**Purpose**: Make the investor FEEL the problem before understanding it intellectually.

**Musk Mode Applied**:
- Lead with the human/economic cost of the problem
- Use visual contrast: congested grid vs. unused industrial capacity
- Quantify the pain: "60 GW of projects waiting for grid connection in the Netherlands. Average wait: 10 years."
- Frame as systemic failure, not niche complaint

**Infrastructure-specific problems to frame**:
- Grid congestion blocking energy transition and AI deployment
- Waste heat from data centers vented to atmosphere (100% thermal waste)
- European dependence on US hyperscalers for AI compute sovereignty
- Grower energy costs threatening Dutch greenhouse competitiveness

**Common mistakes**: Too abstract. Too many problems listed. No emotional hook.

### Slide 3: Solution
**Purpose**: Show your solution as the inevitable answer to the problem on the previous slide.

**Structure**:
- One clear statement of what you do
- 3 key differentiators (bullets, not paragraphs)
- Visual: product photo, site photo, system diagram, or architecture
- Bridge from problem to solution should feel like a revelation, not a sales pitch

**For DE-type companies**:
- "We co-locate AI data centers on existing grid connections at Dutch industrial and agricultural sites"
- Differentiator 1: Existing 25.5 MW grid connection (no 10-year queue)
- Differentiator 2: 97% waste heat recovery to adjacent growers/district heating
- Differentiator 3: BESS provides standalone revenue from Day 1

### Slide 4: Market Opportunity
**Purpose**: Prove this is a big enough market to warrant venture-scale returns.

**Wood Mode Applied**:
- TAM/SAM/SOM with clear methodology (top-down and bottom-up)
- Show the cost curve trajectory: where is this market going?
- Identify the inflection point: why NOW?

**Framework**:
| Level | Definition | Sizing Approach |
|---|---|---|
| TAM | Total market if every possible customer bought | Top-down: total European DC + BESS + heat market |
| SAM | Serviceable portion given your geography and segment | NL + adjacent markets, your customer segments |
| SOM | Realistically capturable in next 3-5 years | Bottom-up: # of sites x revenue per site |

**Market data** (cross-reference `_shared/market-data.md` and `references/sector-thesis.md`):
- European AI compute: EUR 20B+ EIB AI Gigafactories program
- NL BESS: SDE++ EUR 100M allocation, 6 GW TDTR capacity
- District heating: Wcw mandating 50%+ public heat infrastructure
- Dutch greenhouse sector: 9,000+ hectares, EUR 8B+ energy spend

### Slide 5: Business Model
**Purpose**: Show how revenue flows. Keep it simple.

**Altman Mode Applied**:
- Revenue model in one clear diagram or table
- Unit economics: what does one site generate?
- Show multiple revenue streams if applicable (but keep it digestible)

**For infrastructure companies**:
| Revenue Stream | Model | Indicative Unit Economics |
|---|---|---|
| AI Colocation | $/kW/month or $/rack/month | EUR X per MW/month |
| Heat Supply | EUR/MWh to growers/district heating | EUR 15/MWh baseload |
| BESS Arbitrage | Revenue stacking: FCR + aFRR + day-ahead | EUR Y per MW/year |
| Grid Services | Congestion management, flexibility | Emerging revenue |

### Slide 6: Traction
**Purpose**: The most important slide. This is where investors decide to lean in or lean out.

**Lochmiller Mode Applied**:
- Lead with your strongest metric
- Show velocity: "Achieved X in Y months"
- Use timeline format if milestones tell a compelling story
- Include: LOIs, pilot results, permits, revenue, partnerships, grid connections

**Traction hierarchy** (most to least convincing):
1. Revenue (best)
2. Signed contracts / binding LOIs
3. Pilot customers with results
4. Partnerships with named counterparties
5. Permits / regulatory approvals
6. Pipeline / non-binding LOIs
7. Waitlist / expressions of interest

### Slide 7: Technology / Product
**Purpose**: Show what you've built is real, differentiated, and defensible.

- Architecture diagram or product screenshot
- Key technical specs that matter to investors (not engineering details)
- What's the moat? (grid access, proprietary integration, data advantage, regulatory position)
- IP status if relevant (patents, trade secrets)

**For infrastructure**: Show the physical system. Site photos. Architecture diagrams. PUE numbers. Heat recovery efficiency.

### Slide 8: Competition
**Purpose**: Show awareness of alternatives while positioning your unique advantage.

**Framework**: 2x2 matrix or comparison table. NOT a magic quadrant where you're in the top-right corner (investors see through this).

**Honest positioning**:
| Competitor Type | Examples | Their Strength | Your Advantage |
|---|---|---|---|
| Traditional DC operators | Equinix, Digital Realty | Scale, brand, customer base | Grid access, waste heat revenue, lower PUE |
| Neocloud providers | Crusoe, Lambda | GPU expertise, AI-native | European sovereignty, heat integration |
| BESS pure-play | Fluence, Gresham House | Storage expertise | Integrated model: BESS + DC + heat |
| District heating | Vattenfall, Ennatuurlijk | Heat network ownership | Waste heat supply at lower cost |

### Slide 9: Team
**Purpose**: Why THIS team can execute THIS plan.

- Founder photos, names, roles
- 2-3 bullet points per founder: relevant experience, domain expertise, prior exits
- Advisors / board members if they add credibility
- Key hires planned (shows you know what's missing)

**What investors look for**: Founder-market fit. Do these people understand energy, infrastructure, AND technology? Have they built something before?

### Slide 10: Go-to-Market
**Purpose**: How you'll acquire the first 10 customers, then the next 100.

- Current GTM motion (how are you getting customers today?)
- Near-term plan (next 12-18 months)
- Customer acquisition cost if known
- Channel strategy: direct sales, partnerships, inbound

### Slide 11: Financial Ask
**Purpose**: Clear, specific ask with accountability.

**Structure**:
- Amount raising: "EUR X million seed round"
- Instrument: SAFE / convertible / priced round
- Use of proceeds pie chart or table (hiring, infrastructure, development, operations)
- Key milestones this round enables (Series A triggers)
- Timeline to next round

**Milestone examples**:
| Milestone | Target | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| First BESS COD | X MW operational | Q[X] 2026 |
| First heat delivery | X MW thermal to growers | Q[X] 2026 |
| First DC phase operational | X MW IT load | Q[X] 2027 |
| Revenue run rate | EUR X million ARR | By Series A |

### Slide 12: Vision
**Purpose**: Leave the investor thinking big.

- Where does this company go in 5-10 years?
- What's the endgame? (platform, portfolio, market leader position)
- How big can this be?
- One memorable closing line

## 4. Design Principles

| Principle | Guideline |
|---|---|
| Text per slide | Max 30 words. Fewer is better. |
| Font size | Minimum 24pt for body text |
| Color palette | 2-3 colors maximum. Match brand. |
| Charts | One chart per slide max. Label clearly. |
| Images | High-resolution. Real photos > stock photos. |
| Transitions | None. No animations. |
| Format | 16:9 aspect ratio. PDF for sending. |
| File size | Under 10MB for email delivery |

## 5. Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Too many slides | Cut to 12. Everything else goes to appendix. |
| Wall of text | One idea per slide. Use the speaker notes. |
| Burying traction | Move traction to slide 6 or earlier. |
| Vague market sizing | Bottom-up SOM + top-down TAM. Show the math. |
| No clear ask | State exact amount, instrument, and use of proceeds. |
| Generic competition slide | Acknowledge real competitors. Show honest differentiation. |
| Missing team slide | Even if team is small, show who's building this. |
| Financial projections in deck | Save detailed financials for the data room. Show milestones + ask. |

## 6. Appendix Slides (Optional, for Q&A)

Prepare but don't present:
- Detailed financial projections (3-5 year summary)
- Cap table (current and pro forma)
- Detailed technical architecture
- Customer case studies / testimonials
- Regulatory landscape summary
- Detailed competitive analysis
