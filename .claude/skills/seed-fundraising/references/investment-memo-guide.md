# Investment Memorandum Guide -- Seed Round

## 1. Purpose

The investment memorandum (IM) is the most comprehensive single document in a fundraise. It provides the depth that a pitch deck cannot: full market analysis, detailed financials, risk assessment, and legal structure. Institutional investors (infrastructure funds, pension funds, family offices) expect an IM. Seed-stage VCs may not require one, but having one signals seriousness and preparation.

## 2. When to Produce an IM

| Investor Type | IM Required? | Depth Expected |
|---|---|---|
| Seed VC (Tier 1) | Optional but impressive | 10-15 pages |
| Infrastructure fund | Required | 20-30 pages |
| Family office | Usually required | 15-25 pages |
| Pension fund / insurer | Required | 25-30 pages + appendices |
| Angel / syndicate | Not required | Executive summary suffices |
| Strategic investor | Case-by-case | 15-25 pages |

## 3. Structure (20-25 Page Standard)

### Section 1: Executive Summary (1-2 pages)
Standalone summary covering: company, problem, solution, market, traction, team, ask. This is the executive summary from `executive-summary-guide.md` embedded as the opening section.

### Section 2: Company Overview (2-3 pages)
- Legal entity: Digital Energy Group AG (Zug, Switzerland)
- Operating structure: Dutch project BVs for each site
- Founded: [date]; incorporated: [date]
- Registered offices and key operational locations
- Corporate structure diagram (holding + operating entities)
- Brief history and key milestones timeline

### Section 3: The Problem (1-2 pages)
- Market pain quantified with third-party data
- Why existing solutions fail (incumbents, structural barriers)
- Why the timing is right (regulatory, technological, economic inflection)
- **Archetype**: Musk mode for urgency; Liebreich mode for institutional evidence

### Section 4: The Solution (2-3 pages)
- Product/service description with technical detail
- How it works (system architecture, process flow)
- Key differentiators (minimum 3, each substantiated)
- IP and proprietary technology (if applicable)
- Product roadmap: current state, near-term development, long-term vision

### Section 5: Market Opportunity (2-3 pages)
- TAM/SAM/SOM with dual methodology (top-down + bottom-up)
- Market growth projections with sources
- Cost curve analysis (Wright's Law application)
- Regulatory drivers and tailwinds
- **Archetype**: Wood mode for platform analysis; data tables with sources

### Section 6: Business Model (2-3 pages)
- Revenue model by stream (detailed per-unit economics)
- Pricing strategy and benchmarks
- Customer acquisition model
- Gross margin analysis
- Path to profitability (or path to Series A metrics)
- **Archetype**: Altman mode for clarity

### Section 7: Traction and Operational Progress (1-2 pages)
- Milestone timeline (graphical preferred)
- Key metrics table (MRR, MW, customers, pipeline)
- Customer testimonials or case studies (anonymized if needed)
- Partnerships and strategic relationships
- **Archetype**: Lochmiller mode for execution proof

### Section 8: Competitive Landscape (1-2 pages)
- Direct and indirect competitors
- Competitive positioning matrix
- Barriers to entry / moat analysis
- Why competition validates the market rather than threatens it
- **Archetype**: Wood + Liebreich for honest analysis

### Section 9: Team (1-2 pages)
- Founder biographies (professional, not personal)
- Key team members and roles
- Advisory board with relevance explained
- Organizational chart (current and planned)
- Key hires planned with this round's capital

### Section 10: Financial Overview (4-6 pages — restructured for dual audience)

**Dual-audience framing:** VCs read Section 10.1 (platform growth). Infrastructure investors read 10.2-10.5 (per-site yield). Both audiences need all five subsections for a complete picture.

#### Section 10.1: Platform P&L (1-2 pages)
- Historical financials (if any): P&L summary
- 3-5 year consolidated projections: revenue by stream, costs, EBITDA, cash flow
- Key assumptions clearly stated with sources
- Platform-level unit economics summary
- **Audience:** Primary for VCs (growth trajectory, margin expansion)

#### Section 10.2: Per-Site Unit Economics (1-2 pages)
- Per-site revenue breakdown: BESS arbitrage, BESS ancillary (FCR/aFRR), DC colocation, heat supply, grid services
- Per-site CAPEX breakdown (line items)
- Per-site OPEX (O&M, insurance, grid charges, land lease, personnel)
- Per-site DCF with unlevered IRR
- Aggregation: how per-site economics build to platform P&L
- **Audience:** Primary for infrastructure investors (per-asset yield)

#### Section 10.3: Financial Model Audit Status (0.5 pages)
- Model structure (integrated vs. separate platform + project models)
- Audit status: audited / in review / planned
- Auditor identity if completed (Forvis Mazars, Operis, BDO, Gridlines)
- Key findings and model integrity statement
- Cross-reference to detailed model in data room (folder 03a/03b)

#### Section 10.4: Debt Sizing & DSCR Analysis (1 page)
- Target gearing per site
- DSCR base case vs. P75 downside vs. P90 stress
- Earningsstripping impact on deductible interest (from intake S2)
- LLCR and PLCR outputs
- Debt sizing conclusion: maximum debt quantum supportable
- **Source:** `project-financing` produces this subsection
- Cross-reference to debt sizing model in data room (folder 03c)

#### Section 10.5: Breakeven Analysis (0.5 pages)
- Revenue breakeven: at what utilization / price level does DSCR = 1.0x?
- Equity breakeven: at what revenue does equity IRR reach target?
- Sensitivity tornado chart: top 5 variables driving equity IRR (+/- 20% impact)
- **Archetype**: Wood + Liebreich for data rigor

### Section 11: Use of Proceeds (1 page)
- Allocation table (hiring, infrastructure, development, operations, working capital)
- Milestone mapping: which proceeds enable which milestones
- Runway calculation: months of operation funded
- Series A trigger metrics

### Section 12: Investment Structure (1-2 pages)
- Instrument: SAFE / convertible note / priced round
- Key terms: valuation cap, discount, pro rata rights
- Cap table: pre-money and pro-forma post-money
- Governance: board composition, investor rights
- Jurisdiction-specific considerations (Swiss AG + Dutch BV)

### Section 12A: Capital Structure & Financing Strategy (1-2 pages)
- Current capital structure: equity raised, SAFEs/notes outstanding, grants
- Proposed capital structure post-seed: equity + planned project debt
- Debt evolution across project lifecycle: construction facility → term loan → refinancing
- Target gearing per site and portfolio-level gearing trajectory
- Cash waterfall: Revenue → OPEX → Senior debt service → DSRA → MRA → Tax → Equity distributions
- Hedging strategy: interest rate exposure, energy price exposure
- **Source:** `project-financing` produces this section using intake S10 and S12 data
- **Audience:** Critical for infrastructure investors; informative for VCs

### Section 13: Risk Factors (2-3 pages — expanded)
- Market risks (demand, pricing, competition)
- Technology risks (performance, obsolescence)
- Regulatory risks (permitting, policy changes, grid)
- Execution risks (construction, hiring, partnerships)
- Financial risks (capital needs, revenue timing)
- Mitigation strategies for each risk category
- **Archetype**: Liebreich mode -- honest, dual-framework analysis

### Section 14: Appendices (as needed)

**Standard Appendices (A-F):**
- Appendix A: Detailed financial model summary
- Appendix B: Technical specifications
- Appendix C: Market research excerpts
- Appendix D: Letters of intent / support letters
- Appendix E: Regulatory permits and status
- Appendix F: Comparable transactions (include infrastructure M&A multiples: EV/MW, EV/EBITDA, EV/Contracted Revenue — not just funding rounds)

**Infrastructure-Specific Appendices (G-K) — NEW:**
- **Appendix G: Asset Portfolio Summary** — Per-site one-pager for each site: location, MW, grid connection, permit status, CAPEX, revenue mix, key metrics. Source: `project-financing` using intake S8 data.
- **Appendix H: Insurance Programme** — Coverage map: construction (CAR/EAR), operational (PAR/ISR/All-Risk), machinery breakdown, third-party liability, cyber. Key exclusions. Annual cost. Broker identity. Source: intake S10.29-35.
- **Appendix I: ESG & SFDR Assessment** — SFDR Article 8/9 classification, EU Taxonomy alignment, Scope 1/2/3 emissions, CO2 avoided calculation, corporate governance summary. Source: intake S15.21-25.
- **Appendix J: Tax Structuring Memo** — CH-NL treaty benefits, earningsstripping impact, TP documentation status, conditional WHT assessment, exit tax treatment. Source: intake S2.
- **Appendix K: Bankability Checklist** — 8-category assessment: EPC, Revenue, Technology, Grid, Permits, Insurance, Model, Sponsor. Pass/fail per category with specific deficiencies. Source: `project-financing` due-diligence-checklist.md Section 9.

**Sensitivity Analysis Methodology:**
- Use **tornado charts** for top 5 variables driving equity IRR (not just scenario tables)
- Variables typically include: revenue price/volume, degradation rate, OPEX escalation, construction cost, interest rate
- Show +/- 20% impact on equity IRR for each variable
- Present as visual tornado chart with clear axis labels

## 4. Writing Principles

| Principle | Guideline |
|---|---|
| Tone | Professional, evidence-based. Not promotional. |
| Data density | Every claim backed by a source or verifiable data point |
| Length | 20-25 pages body + appendices. Never exceed 30 pages body. |
| Formatting | Consistent headers, numbered sections, table of contents |
| Visuals | Charts, diagrams, and tables preferred over prose |
| Disclaimers | Standard "confidential" header; "not investment advice" footer |
| Versioning | "Version X.X -- [Date] -- Confidential" on cover page |

## 5. Infrastructure-Specific IM Conventions

Infrastructure IMs differ from SaaS/consumer IMs in several ways:

| Aspect | SaaS/Consumer IM | Infrastructure IM |
|---|---|---|
| Revenue certainty | MRR/ARR projections | Contracted vs. merchant split |
| Asset detail | Product screenshots | Site locations, MW capacity, grid connections |
| Regulatory section | Brief | Extensive (permits, grid, environmental) |
| Construction risk | N/A | Detailed: EPC, timeline, cost overruns |
| Financing structure | Equity only | Equity + project debt (non-recourse) |
| Return metrics | Revenue multiple, growth rate | IRR, cash yield, MOIC, DSCR |
| Exit scenarios | Acquisition, IPO | Trade sale, refinancing, secondary, platform IPO |

## 6. Quality Checks Before Distribution

- [ ] All financial figures consistent across sections
- [ ] Market data sourced and dated (no figures older than 18 months)
- [ ] Legal entity names and structures accurate
- [ ] Cap table adds to 100%
- [ ] Risk section is honest (investors will find issues you don't disclose)
- [ ] Formatted consistently (fonts, headers, spacing)
- [ ] PDF renders correctly on different devices
- [ ] "Confidential" watermark present
- [ ] Table of contents hyperlinked
- [ ] Appendix referenced from body where relevant
