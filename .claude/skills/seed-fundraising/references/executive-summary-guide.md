# Executive Summary Guide -- Seed Round

## 1. Purpose

The executive summary is the single most-read document in a fundraise. It's what investors see first in a cold email, what gets forwarded between partners, and what investment committee members scan before a pitch meeting. It must stand alone -- conveying the full story in one page (or two at most).

## 2. One-Page Format (Primary)

Target: 400-600 words. Fits on a single A4/Letter page with reasonable margins and font size.

### Structure

| Section | Length | Content |
|---|---|---|
| Header | 2 lines | Company name, tagline, round details |
| The Problem | 2-3 sentences | Market pain with quantified evidence |
| The Solution | 2-3 sentences | What you do and why it's different |
| Market Opportunity | 2-3 sentences | TAM/SAM/SOM with methodology |
| Traction | 3-4 bullet points | Strongest proof points |
| Business Model | 2-3 sentences | Revenue streams and unit economics |
| Team | 2-3 sentences | Founder backgrounds, key hires |
| The Ask | 2-3 sentences | Amount, instrument, use of proceeds, milestones |
| Contact | 1 line | Founder name, email, phone |

### Writing Principles (Altman Mode)

- **Lead with strength**: Whatever is most impressive goes first. If it's traction, lead with traction. If it's the team, lead with the team. Don't follow the template if your story is stronger in a different order.
- **Numbers over adjectives**: "25.5 MW grid connection" not "significant grid capacity"
- **Specificity signals competence**: "EUR 15/MWh waste heat to 12 hectares of greenhouse" not "we supply heat to agricultural customers"
- **One read = complete understanding**: An investor should understand your company after 60 seconds of reading

## 3. Two-Page Format (Expanded)

Use for data rooms, formal investor packages, or when requested. Same sections as one-page, with expanded detail:

| Section | Additional Detail |
|---|---|
| Market Opportunity | Include cost curve data (Wood mode), regulatory tailwinds |
| Traction | Expanded timeline with milestones achieved |
| Business Model | Per-site unit economics table |
| Competitive Landscape | Brief positioning (2-3 sentences) |
| Financial Summary | High-level projections: Year 1-3 revenue, key metrics |
| Risk Factors | Top 3 risks with mitigation (Liebreich mode) |

## 4. Audience Adaptation

| Investor Type | Emphasis | Tone |
|---|---|---|
| VC (early stage) | Growth rate, market size, team, vision | Altman + Musk: clear narrative + bold vision |
| Infrastructure fund | Cash flow profile, contracted revenue, asset quality | Liebreich: institutional, measured, data-heavy |
| Family office | Alignment with values, return profile, founder relationship | Liebreich + Musk: mission + evidence |
| Strategic investor | Synergy, technology integration, market access | Lochmiller: operational detail + partnership value |
| Angel investor | Founder story, personal conviction, early entry opportunity | Musk + Altman: vision + clean terms |

## 5. Formatting Rules

| Rule | Guideline |
|---|---|
| Font | Professional serif or sans-serif. 10-11pt body. |
| Margins | 2cm minimum all sides |
| Header | Company logo + "Confidential" |
| Footer | Page number + "Seed Round [Date]" |
| Bold | Use for key metrics and company name only |
| Bullets | Max 5 per section |
| Paragraphs | Max 3 sentences each |

## 6. Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Fix |
|---|---|
| Reads like marketing copy | Use specific numbers, not superlatives |
| Too long | If it's more than 2 pages, cut it |
| Missing the ask | Always include round size, instrument, use of proceeds |
| No traction section | Even pre-revenue companies have proof points (permits, LOIs, pilots) |
| Generic problem statement | Make it specific to your market and geography |
| Burying the lead | First sentence should be your strongest claim |
