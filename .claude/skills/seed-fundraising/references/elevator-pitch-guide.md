# Elevator Pitch Guide -- Seed Round

## 1. Purpose

The elevator pitch is the verbal version of your executive summary, compressed to 30-90 seconds. It's used at conferences, introductions, investor events, and the opening of formal pitch meetings. The goal is not to close a deal -- it's to earn the next conversation.

## 2. Three Formats

### 30-Second Pitch (Conference / Hallway)

**Structure**: Problem + Solution + Proof + Hook

> "[Company] solves [specific problem] by [solution mechanism]. We've already [strongest proof point]. We're raising [amount] to [next milestone].

**Example for Digital Energy:**
> "Digital Energy co-locates AI data centers on Dutch industrial sites with existing grid connections -- turning the 10-year grid queue into a 12-month deployment. We recover 97% of waste heat and sell it to adjacent greenhouses at EUR 15 per megawatt-hour. We're raising EUR [X] million to bring our first three sites online."

### 60-Second Pitch (Warm Introduction)

**Structure**: Context + Problem + Solution + Traction + Model + Ask

| Element | Duration | Archetype | Content |
|---|---|---|---|
| Context | 5s | Liebreich: relatable framing | "You know how [relatable context]..." |
| Problem | 10s | Musk: existential urgency | Quantified pain point |
| Solution | 15s | Lochmiller: operational proof | What you do, why it's different |
| Traction | 15s | Lochmiller: execution velocity | Strongest 2-3 proof points |
| Model | 5s | Wood: unit economics | How you make money |
| Ask | 10s | Altman: clear and direct | What you're raising and why |

### 90-Second Pitch (Investor Event / Demo Day)

**Structure**: Hook + Problem + Solution + Market + Traction + Team + Ask + Vision

This is the full narrative arc compressed. Every sentence earns the next 10 seconds of attention.

| Element | Duration | Archetype |
|---|---|---|
| Hook | 5s | Musk: surprising fact or provocative question |
| Problem | 15s | Musk: existential urgency + quantified pain |
| Solution | 15s | Lochmiller: what you've built, how it works |
| Market | 10s | Wood: size + trajectory + inflection point |
| Traction | 15s | Lochmiller: execution proof, velocity |
| Team | 10s | Altman: why this team, relevant credentials |
| Ask | 10s | Altman: clear amount, milestones |
| Vision | 10s | Musk: where this goes, why it matters |

## 3. Hooks by Sector

### BESS / Energy Storage
- "The Netherlands has 60 gigawatts of projects waiting for grid connections -- and an average wait of 10 years."
- "Last year, Dutch electricity prices went negative for 423 hours. We get paid every single one of those hours."
- "Battery costs have fallen 90% since 2010. We're positioned at the inflection point."

### AI Data Centers
- "Europe runs 90% of its AI workloads on American hyperscaler infrastructure."
- "Every megawatt of AI compute generates a megawatt of heat. Right now, 100% of that heat is wasted."
- "There's a moratorium on new data centers in Amsterdam -- and 10-year grid queues everywhere else."

### Waste Heat / District Heating
- "Dutch greenhouses spend EUR 8 billion a year on energy. We can cut that by 40%."
- "The new Dutch Heat Act requires municipalities to build district heating. We're the cheapest source."
- "A 10 MW data center produces enough heat for 25 hectares of greenhouse."

## 4. Delivery Principles

| Principle | Guideline |
|---|---|
| Speed | Speak slightly slower than conversational. Urgency comes from content, not pace. |
| Eye contact | Address the person, not the room (for 1-on-1) or scan the room (for group). |
| Specificity | Use exact numbers: "25.5 MW" not "about 25 megawatts." |
| Confidence | State facts, don't hedge. "We have" not "we're hoping to." |
| Pause | After the hook and after the ask. Let it land. |
| No jargon | If you must use a technical term, define it immediately. |
| Ask for the meeting | End with a clear next step: "Can I send you the deck?" or "I'd love 20 minutes next week." |

## 5. Adapting by Audience

| Audience | Emphasize | De-emphasize |
|---|---|---|
| VC partner | Growth, market size, moat | Technical detail |
| Infrastructure fund LP | Cash yield, asset quality, contracted revenue | Growth multiple |
| Energy executive | Grid dynamics, regulatory, partnerships | Venture metrics |
| Tech executive | AI compute, sovereignty, PUE | Heat economics |
| Government official | Jobs, sovereignty, sustainability | Financial returns |
| Fellow founder | Technical approach, market insight | Financial projections |

## 6. Practice Framework

1. Write it out word-for-word (even though you won't read it)
2. Time yourself. Cut until it fits the format.
3. Record yourself. Listen for filler words, hedging, and unclear transitions.
4. Pitch to 3 people who don't know your company. Ask them to repeat back what you do.
5. If they can't explain it accurately, the pitch isn't clear enough.
