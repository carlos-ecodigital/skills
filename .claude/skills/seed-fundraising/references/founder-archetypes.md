# Founder Archetypes -- Communication Styles for Fundraising

Five elite fundraiser archetypes, each grounded in documented public philosophies and communication patterns. Use these as voice/style layers when generating fundraising deliverables.

---

## 1. The Visionary -- Elon Musk Mode

### Core Philosophy
Start with the problem humanity faces, not the product you built. Break every assumption to first principles. Make investors feel the urgency of the mission, then show them your solution is the inevitable answer.

### Communication Principles

**First-Principles Cost Decomposition:**
Strip every cost assumption to raw materials and physics. Musk's rocket example: raw materials cost ~2% of a rocket's market price, meaning 98% is inefficiency. For energy infrastructure: what does a kWh of storage actually cost in raw lithium, iron phosphate, and aluminum? What does a MW of compute actually cost in silicon, copper, and concrete? Start there, reason upward.

**Problem-First Narrative Arc:**
1. The Obstacle -- what's blocking progress (grid congestion, energy waste, compute scarcity)
2. The Vision -- vivid picture of the world if this is solved
3. The Product -- revealed as the bridge between obstacle and vision
4. The Proof -- demos, metrics, operational data
5. The Call -- specific ask with urgency

**Emotional Framing Techniques:**
- Visual contrast: show the "before" (smoke, waste, congestion) and "after" (clean, efficient, abundant)
- Existential stakes: this isn't about returns, it's about whether Europe has sovereign AI capability
- Honesty about risk: "This will be hard. Many will fail. That's precisely why the return profile exists."
- David vs. Goliath: position the company against entrenched incumbents who said it couldn't be done

### Vocabulary Patterns
- "From first principles..."
- "The physics allows this"
- "It's just a matter of engineering and will"
- "Critical to [civilization / energy security / European sovereignty]"
- "A maniacal sense of urgency"
- "Now, not eventually"
- "The material cost of X is Y -- everything above that is inefficiency"

### Sentence Structure
- Short declarative sentences. Minimal conjunctions.
- Technical specifics grounded in accessible analogies.
- Slow build to bold conclusion. Strategic pauses.
- One idea per slide. Minimal text. Let visuals carry weight.

### When to Apply
- **Pitch deck**: Problem slide, vision slide, technology differentiation slide
- **Elevator pitch**: The opening hook (existential urgency + clear solution)
- **Investor meetings**: Q&A on technical feasibility and long-term vision
- **Executive summary**: Opening paragraph framing the problem

### Energy Infrastructure Adaptation
- Grid congestion as the "stranded energy" problem (60 GW queue vs 20 GW peak demand)
- First-principles on waste heat: every MW of compute = 1 MW thermal, currently wasted
- First-principles on BESS cost: $/kWh broken to cathode materials, cell-to-pack costs, BOS
- The "multiplanetary" equivalent: European AI sovereignty requires European compute infrastructure

## 2. The Strategist -- Sam Altman Mode

### Core Philosophy
The best pitch is a great company. Make something people want. Growth solves all problems. Lead with your strongest proof point, not your mission statement. Keep the story so clear a child could follow it.

### Communication Principles

**Product-First Pragmatism:**
Don't pitch -- explain. Let the company speak for itself. If your company isn't in good enough shape to attract capital, no amount of pitch polish will fix that. The most effective fundraising happens when the company is growing and you tell investors clearly what you're doing and why.

**Clarity Over Accuracy:**
Better to be 80% accurate and 100% clear than the reverse. Investors don't have time to decode complexity. Bunch information into numbered groups ("We have four competitive advantages..."). Tell them what you'll cover, cover it, summarize.

**Growth as the Organizing Principle:**
Frame everything around trajectory. "We launched in X, we grow at Y% monthly, we have Z customers." Growth rate is the most important metric because it proves market demand and execution capability simultaneously.

**Network and Ecosystem Thinking:**
Show how your company connects to larger trends and creates network effects. How does waste heat recovery connect to Wcw regulation, which connects to municipal sustainability mandates, which connects to grower economics? Platform thinking, not product thinking.

### Vocabulary Patterns
- "Make something people want"
- "Growth solves all problems"
- "Anything but yes is a no -- believe the no, not the reason"
- "Focus and intensity"
- "The most important thing is..."
- "Here's what we've built, here's what's working, here's what we need"
- "Clean terms" (for deal structure)

### Sentence Structure
- Direct, conversational, zero jargon.
- Numbers front and center: "X customers, Y% growth, Z months runway."
- Structured lists: "Three things matter here: first... second... third..."
- Conclusion-first paragraphs. Lead with the answer, then support it.

### When to Apply
- **Pitch deck**: Market size slide, traction slide, business model slide, team slide
- **Executive summary**: Entire document (clarity and concision are Altman's signature)
- **Investor targeting**: Identifying the right investors, understanding their psychology
- **Cap table**: Clean terms, appropriate dilution, founder-friendly structures
- **Financial projections**: Growth rate framing, milestone-driven narrative

### Energy Infrastructure Adaptation
- Frame grid congestion as market demand signal: "60 GW of projects waiting = massive unmet demand"
- Growth metrics: MW under management, heat customers connected, revenue run rate
- Ecosystem framing: BESS + DC + heat recovery = platform with network effects
- Clean term advocacy: use standard instruments (SAFE or priced round), avoid complex structures at seed

## 3. The Thesis Builder -- Cathie Wood Mode

### Core Philosophy
Disruptive innovation follows predictable cost curves. Identify the platforms where costs are declining exponentially, then invest at the inflection point. Communicate your thesis transparently, with data, and hold conviction through volatility.

### Communication Principles

**Wright's Law as Central Framework:**
For every cumulative doubling of units produced, costs fall by a constant percentage. Batteries: 28% cost decline per doubling. This is the most powerful tool for convincing investors that today's expensive technology will be tomorrow's commodity. Show the curve, show where we are on it, show the inevitable destination.

**Five Converging Platforms:**
Frame investments at the intersection of multiple innovation waves: AI + energy storage + grid modernization + electrification + data infrastructure. When platforms converge, the compounding effects create outsized returns.

**Radical Transparency:**
Publish your thesis. Share your reasoning. Explain your buy/sell logic. Institutional investors respect intellectual honesty more than sales polish. Give away the research, let the conviction speak.

**Long-Term Horizon:**
7+ year investment horizons. Build models projecting 5-10 years of technology adoption. Work backward to identify where value concentrates. Portfolios that look uncomfortable short-term become obvious through a longer lens.

### Vocabulary Patterns
- "Wright's Law predicts..."
- "Cost decline curve"
- "Platform convergence"
- "Escape velocity" (when a technology captures 15-30% market share)
- "Creative destruction"
- "Good deflation" (costs fall because technology improves, not because demand collapses)
- "Stay on the right side of change"
- "Nascent stage of an early technology"

### Sentence Structure
- Data-heavy declarative statements: "Battery costs have declined 90% since 2010."
- Conditional projections: "If deployment doubles again by 2028, costs will reach $X/kWh."
- Historical parallels: "This is where solar was in 2012 -- three years before it crossed grid parity."
- Research-paper tone: measured, evidence-based, but with underlying conviction.

### When to Apply
- **Investment memorandum**: Market analysis, technology thesis, competitive landscape
- **Sector thesis**: Full market sizing with cost curve projections
- **Pitch deck**: Market opportunity slide, technology slide, financial projections slide
- **Data room**: Market research, third-party validation, cost benchmarks

### Energy Infrastructure Adaptation
- BESS cost curve: 28% decline per cumulative doubling. Currently at ~$117/kWh global, ~$177/kWh Europe. Project forward.
- AI compute cost curve: GPU performance/$ improving ~2x every 18 months
- Convergence thesis: BESS + AI compute + waste heat recovery + grid flexibility = platform
- Wright's Law applied to DE's specific cost advantages: waste heat monetization reduces effective $/MW

## 4. The Pragmatist -- Michael Liebreich Mode

### Core Philosophy
The energy transition is happening, but it's harder than evangelists claim and easier than skeptics believe. Ground your analysis in evidence, acknowledge both bull and bear cases, and present a pragmatic path that sophisticated institutional investors can underwrite.

### Communication Principles

**Dual-Framework Analysis:**
Present the "Five Horsemen" (barriers) alongside the "Five Superheroes" (enablers). Investors trust advisors who acknowledge challenges rather than cheerleading. Show you've stress-tested the thesis.

Bears (Horsemen): poor economics beyond solar/wind/batteries, inadequate grid, critical mineral supply, political inertia, regulatory capture.
Bulls (Superheroes): exponential deployment growth, systems solutions, great-power competition driving investment, demand reduction, primary energy fallacy.

**Evidence-Based Measured Conviction:**
Never oversell. Use "indicative," "base case," "subject to verification." Quote specific sources, dates, and data points. Sophisticated investors (pension funds, insurers, sovereign wealth) respond to intellectual rigor, not enthusiasm.

**Institutional-Grade Communication:**
Structure everything for professional investment committees. Executive summary first, detailed analysis supporting. Clean formatting. Source citations. Risk factors alongside opportunities.

### Vocabulary Patterns
- "The evidence suggests..."
- "On balance, the data supports..."
- "Legitimate concerns include..."
- "Pragmatic" / "achievable" / "affordable"
- "Recalibrate expectations"
- "Speedbumps, not showstoppers"
- "The tortoise strategy wins" (steady compounding beats hype cycles)

### Sentence Structure
- Balanced clause structure: "While [challenge], [opportunity]."
- Specific data citations: "Wind and solar now make up 93% of new global capacity additions."
- Measured qualifiers: "likely," "on current trajectory," "if current trends continue."
- Academic/policy tone, but accessible to non-specialists.

### When to Apply
- **Investment memorandum**: Risk analysis, regulatory landscape, market context
- **Data room**: Structuring for institutional due diligence
- **Pitch to infrastructure funds/pension funds**: Entire communication style
- **Sector thesis**: Balanced market analysis with both upside and downside scenarios
- **Financial projections**: Scenario analysis, stress testing, banker's case

### Energy Infrastructure Adaptation
- Dutch grid congestion: present both the constraint (10-year queue) and the opportunity (existing connections = moat)
- Regulatory landscape: Energiewet, Wcw, Omgevingswet as tailwinds with execution complexity
- Institutional framing: SFDR Article 8/9 alignment, EU Taxonomy eligibility, pension fund investment criteria
- BESS economics: honest about merchant risk vs. contracted revenue, with mitigation strategies

## 5. The Builder -- Chase Lochmiller Mode

### Core Philosophy
Velocity is the ultimate advantage. The capital formation process works when you have a great idea that solves a big problem and you can demonstrate execution. Don't just tell investors what you'll build -- show them what you've already built, faster than anyone thought possible.

### Communication Principles

**Execution Velocity as Proof:**
Speed of delivery IS the proof point. Industry builds data centers in 3-5 years? You did it in 15 months. Supply chain has 100-week lead times? You vertically integrated and cut it to 22 weeks. Velocity is not just a metric -- it's the moat.

**Stranded Resource as Opportunity:**
Identify resources that exist in abundance but are economically inaccessible. Stranded natural gas. Curtailed renewables. Waste heat. Grid connections in congested markets. The market failure is "last-mile" utilization, not generation. Your company solves this.

**Good Storytelling + Tangible Business:**
Fundraising requires good storytelling, but the story must be grounded in a tangible business. "I was excited about doing something entrepreneurial where I could build a tangible business." Show the physical infrastructure, the operational metrics, the customer contracts.

**Win-Win-Win Framing:**
Position every deal as multi-stakeholder value creation. Growers win (lower heat costs). Grid wins (congestion relief). Investors win (attractive risk-adjusted returns). Regulators win (policy objectives met). When everyone wins, deals close faster.

### Vocabulary Patterns
- "Velocity is the ultimate advantage"
- "Speed is the only moat"
- "Stranded [energy / resources / capacity]"
- "Vertical integration"
- "Modular" / "scalable" / "replicable"
- "We built it in X months vs. industry standard of Y years"
- "Win, win, win"
- "Tangible business" / "real infrastructure"

### Sentence Structure
- Achievement-first: "We delivered X in Y months." (not "We plan to deliver...")
- Contrast with industry: "Industry standard: 3 years. Our execution: 15 months."
- Concrete numbers: MW deployed, customers connected, revenue generated.
- Colloquial confidence, not academic formality.

### When to Apply
- **Pitch deck**: Traction slide, team slide, competitive advantage slide
- **Elevator pitch**: Leading with operational proof ("We've already...")
- **Investor outreach**: Cold emails and warm intros emphasizing execution track record
- **Financial projections**: Unit economics grounded in actual operational data
- **Due diligence**: Operational documentation, construction timelines, customer testimonials

### Energy Infrastructure Adaptation
- Grid connections as "stranded assets": existing 25.5 MW utility connections in a 10-year queue market
- Construction velocity: BESS COD in 6-12 months, DC phases in 18 months
- Vertical integration: own grid connections, own heat infrastructure, own customer relationships
- Revenue proof: actual BESS arbitrage revenue, actual heat supply contracts, actual DC LOIs
- Scalability narrative: proven template at Site 1, replicable across 12+ sites
