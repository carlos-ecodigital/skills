## CONTRADICTION-001 — Narrow-deep vs. TAM-ceiling

**Apparent contradiction:**
- **PG-006** (How to Get Startup Ideas): "Narrow-and-deep beats broad-and-shallow" — pre-PMF, intensity of demand from a small group is what works
- **ALTMAN-009** (How to Convince Investors): "TAM sets upper bound on company size... market needn't be large today, but must show credible expansion sequence"

**Tension:** PG-006 implies smaller-is-better; ALTMAN-009 implies bigger-is-better. Both are by the same author.

**Resolution:** Stage-conditional logic.
- **Pre-PMF (YC application stage):** Apply PG-006. Narrow-deep is the YC-fundable shape. Q-PROG-5/6 answers prioritize intensity of demand.
- **Post-PMF (Series A onward):** Apply ALTMAN-009. Expansion path is what justifies large valuation. Investors look for the ceiling.

**Skill behavior:** Q-IDEA-3 ("how much could you make?") application strong answer: lead with narrow-deep beachhead, then describe credible expansion. NOT lead with TAM. NOT exclusively narrow without expansion path.

## CONTRADICTION-002 — Hale "X for Y" conditional vs. PG "no analogies"

**Apparent contradiction:**
- **HALE-003**: "X for Y" framework valid when 3 conditions hold (X recognized, Y need clear, Y market large)
- **PG via /howtoapply**: Avoid "we are the Airbnb of X" entirely in the description — "just describe what you do literally"

**Tension:** Hale conditional permits; PG forbids in product description specifically.

**Resolution:** Question-conditional.
- **Q-CO-2 / Q-CO-7 (product description fields):** Apply PG. Avoid the construction entirely. Use HALE-006 noun-verb-object format instead.
- **Q-IDEA-1 / Q-IDEA-2 (where comparison is appropriate to explain positioning):** Apply HALE-003 conditional. "X for Y" permitted only if all 3 hold.

**Skill behavior:** Anti-pattern detection flags "X for Y" in Q-CO-2 / Q-CO-7 unconditionally. Flags in Q-IDEA-1 / Q-IDEA-2 only when HALE-003 conditions don't hold.

## CONTRADICTION-003 — Sincere uncertainty (Seibel) vs. no "I don't know" in writing (Lior rejection evidence)

**Apparent contradiction:**
- **SEIBEL-005** (interviews page): "Be earnest, sincere, straightforward and natural." Conversational uncertainty is acceptable.
- **ANTI-005** (Lior Neu-ner rejection): "I don't have a good answer to this" in interview was rejection-shaped.

**Tension:** Seibel says be sincere about uncertainty; rejection evidence shows uncertainty kills.

**Resolution:** Form-vs-conversation distinction.
- **In interview (oral conversation):** Sincere uncertainty about a small thing, paired with confident discussion of what you DO know, is acceptable.
- **In submitted application (written form):** No uncertainty allowed. Application is the founder's best work; gaps must be closed before submission.
- **The Lior failure:** He had no answer to a CORE question (customer acquisition). That's not "small uncertainty," that's structural unpreparedness.

**Skill behavior:** Skill flags any hedged language ("we think," "we believe," "TBD") in drafts and demands resolution. Applicants resolve via either gap-closure (real progress) or honest acknowledgment paired with concrete plan ("we have not yet onboarded paying users; our 30-day plan is X with named target Y").

## CONTRADICTION-004 — "Apply early" (advantage) vs. "Submit with working demo" (substance)

**Apparent contradiction:**
- **ARC-006** (YC official): "Apply early for slight advantage"
- Same source: "Submit with working demo if possible"

**Tension:** Early submission may be before demo is ready.

**Resolution:** Substance-over-timing for any meaningful tradeoff.
- Slight timing advantage doesn't compensate for missing demo (Q-CO-4 is REQUIRED in Summer 2026).
- Submit on the deadline with working demo > submit early without demo.

**Skill behavior:** Default recommendation is on-time submission with all required artifacts present. Early submission is a tiebreaker, not a strategy.

## CONTRADICTION-005 — Polish > clarity vs. Polish ≠ substance

**Apparent contradiction:**
- **ARC-005** (Buchheit): "Perfectionism is a disease" — don't over-polish
- **PG-009** (Convince Investors): "If you can't explain concisely, you don't really understand" — clarity is competence

**Tension:** Don't polish vs. polish until clear.

**Resolution:** Polishing for clarity ≠ polishing for prettiness.
- **Polish for clarity:** YES. Tighten word choice, remove jargon, increase specificity. Iterate until brevity gate passes.
- **Polish for prettiness:** NO. Decorating, varying word choice for variety's sake, adding adjectives, "make it sound impressive."

**Skill behavior:** Skill v-raw output is intentionally unpolished but clear. v-polished applies humanizer + executive-comms (clarity polish). Neither version should add prettiness for its own sake.
