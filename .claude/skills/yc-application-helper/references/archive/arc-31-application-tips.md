## Section A: General Tips for Applying to YC

### Tip 1 — Get to the point (Jason Shen, co-founder Ridejoy)
> "each app gets about 5 minutes of each partner's time on average"
- Concrete time budget: ~5 min per partner. Brevity is forced, not preferred.
- 70-100 applications per partner per day.

### Tip 2 — Impress them (concisely) (Joseph Perla, ex-VP Tech Turntable, YC alum)
- Write only impressive and unique things; avoid filler.

### Tip 3 — Avoid marketing speak (Michael Seibel, YC partner, ex-CEO Justin.tv)
> "eliminate jargon, acronyms, marketing speak, and any ambiguous terms"
- Plain language only.

### Tip 4 — Stay away from buzzwords
- Verboten: "revolutionize," "disrupt," "synergy," "innovate," "platform" (Seibel cites "platform" as ambiguous)

### Tip 5 — Excellence not perfection (Paul Buchheit, creator of Gmail, YC partner)
> "Perfectionism is a disease. It stops progress and drives us crazy."

### Tip 6 — Apply early (Y Combinator official guidance)
- Slight advantage from early submission. Submit with working demo if possible.

## Section B: Tips for Being an Awesome Founder

### Tip 7 — Be formidable (Paul Graham, YC co-founder)
> "A formidable person is one who seems like they'll get what they want"
- Justified confidence + determination signal.

### Tip 8 — Be resourceful (Paul Graham)
- "Relentlessly resourceful" — find ways around obstacles, don't be passive.

### Tip 9 — Be naughty (Paul Graham)
- Creative rule-breaking and problem-solving.

### Tip 10 — Have more than one founder (or look for one) (YC selection patterns)
- Co-founders provide mutual support, ideation, shared burden.

### Tip 11 — Don't pretend, be sincere (Lee Lin, Li Zhou, RentHop YC S09)
- Show formidability authentically; not all founders fit "outgoing" stereotype.

### Tip 12 — Be nice (Paul Graham, "Why It's Safe for Founders to Be Nice")
> Mean people fail because "being mean makes them stupid"

### Tip 13 — Show what you have done!
- Showcase individual + team accomplishments; complementary strengths.

### Tip 14 — Show team won't break up (CB Insights "Top 20 Reasons Startups Fail")
- Demonstrate cohesion, ability to persevere.

### Tip 15 — Show your online presence (GitHub, HN, etc.)
- Display active community participation.

### Tip 16 — Move to Silicon Valley (even temporarily) (Paul Graham)
- SV offers superior investor experience and specialized resources.

## Section C: Tips for Selling Your Idea

### Tip 17 — Make something people want (Paul Graham, "Organic Startup Ideas")
> "focus more on the idea and less on the startup itself"
- Start with genuine problems in your life, not market trends.

### Tip 18 — Show traction (David Chen, co-founder Strikingly, YC alum)
- Revenue, users, engagement metrics.

### Tip 19 — Pick a metric (Strikingly YC application example)
- Priority: profit > revenue > usage > users > audience.

### Tip 20 — Show passion for your startup
- Demonstrate 100% commitment; investors gauge persistence after rejection.

### Tip 21 — Describe exactly what your product would do (Jason Chen, Ridejoy)
> "Be specific about the problem you are trying to solve"
- Explain concrete functionality in clear stages.

### Tip 22 — Name the flaws of your product
- Identify weaknesses + preliminary solutions; preempt counterarguments.

### Tip 23 — Present your domain knowledge (Lee, RentHop)
- First-hand expertise, not consultant generalities.

### Tip 24 — Have a distribution plan
- User acquisition: referral, virality, SEO, partnerships.

### Tip 25 — Give figures (Paul Graham, "How to Present to Investors")
> "Numbers stick in people's heads"
- Specific metrics, but limit to 4-5 key data points.

### Tip 26 — Make bottom-up estimates (Zain Shah, YC S13, Watchsend)
- Concrete realistic calculations > aspirational top-down projections.

### Tip 27 — Be smart about execution time
- Show you've started; efficient progress without delays.

### Tip 28 — Present alternative ideas
- Increases acceptance chance; if investors like you, they'll guide better ideas.
- (This corresponds to Q-IDEA-5 in the Summer 2026 form)

## Section D: Tips for Better YC Application

### Tip 29 — Include a YC video (Derek Andersen, Startup Grind, three-time applicant)
- Keep 1 minute; YouTube; only founders speak; no fancy effects/music.

### Tip 30 — Leverage your network — recommendations (Harj Taggar, YC partner, Triplebyte founder)
- Seek high-quality endorsements from close contacts.
- One strong recommendation > ten shallow ones.

### Tip 31 — Get someone to read your application
- Have someone unfamiliar with the product review for clarity.
