## Central thesis

Founders must understand their startup's default trajectory: whether current burn and revenue growth lead to profitability or insolvency.

## The Core Test (verbatim)

> "Assuming their expenses remain constant and their revenue growth is what it has been over the last several months, do they make it to profitability on the money they have left?"

(Trevor Blackwell calculator: growth.tlb.org)

## The Visibility Problem

> "Half the founders I talk to don't know whether they're default alive or default dead."

This blindness stems from founders treating the question as irrelevant until critical.

## The Fatal Pinch

Default dead + slow growth + insufficient runway = no time to correct.

## Primary Thesis on Hiring (the strongest claim)

> "Hiring too fast is by far the biggest killer of startups that raise money."

Why this happens:
- Founders conflate hiring with growth capability
- Successful companies' large staffs reflect *effects* of growth, not causes
- Slow growth typically signals product problems, not staffing gaps
- VCs encourage overhiring due to portfolio-level incentive structures

## The Death Sequence

Moderately appealing product → decent initial growth → easy first funding → founders assume hiring accelerates growth → product remains marginally compelling → expenses spike while growth stalls → runway exhausts → company cannot attract rescue funding.

## Counterexample: Airbnb

> "Airbnb waited 4 months after raising money at the end of Y Combinator before they hired their first employee."

Founders evolved the product rather than scaling operations.

## On Fundraising Assumptions

PG distinguishes plan A (fundraising) from necessary plan B (survival without capital). Growth >5x annually can attract investor interest despite unprofitability — but unreliable.

## Atoms (preliminary)

- **PG-AORD-001 (the test):** Application disclosure of revenue (Q-PROG-8) + spend (Q-EQ-8) + cash (Q-EQ-7) lets partners run this test in their head. Skill must ensure these numbers are present and self-consistent.
- **PG-AORD-002 (founder self-knowledge):** Half of founders don't know their default status. Anti-pattern: applying without having computed this. Skill should cross-check Q-EQ-8 (spend) × Q-EQ-9 (runway) × revenue trajectory and surface implausibilities.
- **PG-AORD-003 (hiring anti-pattern):** Plans-to-hire-team-of-12 immediately after YC = anti-pattern. If application's use-of-proceeds heavily emphasizes hiring vs. product, flag.
- **PG-AORD-004 (Airbnb counterexample):** "Waited 4 months after YC to hire first employee" — partner-canonical example of disciplined post-YC behavior. Quote when Q-EQ-related responses describe modest team plans.
- **PG-AORD-005 (>5x growth):** Growth above 5x annually is the bar that compensates for unprofitability. Q-PROG-8 monthly revenue should be evaluated against this implicit standard.
