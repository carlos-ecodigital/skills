## Application-relevant atoms

### Seibel on knowing your own PMF status (atom: SEIBEL-PMF)
> "You know whether you have product market fit or not... You are the biggest expert in your company."

**Application implication:** When answering Q-PROG-5 ("Are people using your product?") and Q-PROG-6 (active users / paying / revenue), founders must self-honestly assess. Partner detects bullshit if revenue from a single customer or zero active users is dressed up as traction.

### Seibel on business model precision (atom: SEIBEL-MODEL)
Many B2B founders want to be SaaS founders but operate as Enterprise. Strategy must align with pricing and customer type — these factors are "decided for you."

**Application implication:** Q-IDEA-3 ("How will you make money?") and Q-PROG-9 (revenue source breakdown) demand precise model framing. Calling a pre-revenue or 1-customer enterprise sale a "SaaS business" is anti-pattern; partners detect category mismatch.

### Seibel on traction realism (atom: SEIBEL-TRACTION)
Most B2B companies should target $150-250K monthly recurring revenue before Series A. Raising at $1M ARR with minimal runway gives investors "maximum leverage."

**Application implication:** Pre-PMF context for Q-PROG-6/7/8. Partners reading the application know what mature traction looks like; the application should not over-claim traction relative to actual revenue stage.

### Seibel on post-acceptance reality (atom: SEIBEL-PATIENCE)
Twitch (originally Justin.tv) took six years to find product-market fit.
> "If your plan is you already have solved the problem... beware, it might take a lot longer."

**Application implication:** Q-IDEA-1 (why this idea, do you have domain expertise) — application should communicate honest engagement with the problem, not premature certainty. Over-confidence is anti-pattern.
