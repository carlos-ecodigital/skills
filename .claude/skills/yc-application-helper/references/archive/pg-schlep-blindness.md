## Central thesis (verbatim quote)

> "Your unconscious won't even let you see ideas that involve painful schleps."

**Schlep blindness** = unconscious mental block preventing founders from recognizing valuable startup ideas involving tedious, unpleasant work.

## Key arguments

### The reality of business
> "Schleps are not merely inevitable, but pretty much what business consists of. A company is defined by the schleps it will undertake."

### The Stripe example (canonical)
For over a decade, thousands of hackers experienced payment-processing pain firsthand, yet built recipe sites and local-event aggregators instead. Why? Because founders couldn't consciously see the opportunity — it required dealing with banks, fraud prevention, regulations, and security complexities. The scary nature of ambitious ideas makes them valuable because **"everyone else will have been frightened off by the challenges."**

### Two antidotes to schlep blindness
1. **Ignorance** — Young founders lack awareness of obstacles AND awareness of their own limitations; mistakes cancel out. Experienced founders only lack the first.
2. **Perspective shift** — Instead of asking "what problem should I solve?" ask **"what problem do I wish someone else would solve for me?"**

### The closer
> "There's plenty still broken in the world, if you know how to see it."

## Atoms (preliminary)

- **PG-SCHLEP-001 (the test):** Reframe "what should I solve?" → "what problem do I wish someone else would solve for me?" — direct application to Q-IDEA-1 ("Why did you pick this idea?"). Strong answers identify a schlep the founder personally encountered.
- **PG-SCHLEP-002 (defensibility through tedium):** Schlep-laden ideas are valuable because competitors are frightened off. Application implication: Q-IDEA-2 ("what do you understand about your business that they don't?") can answer with "we are willing to do the schlep that competitors avoid."
- **PG-SCHLEP-003 (anti-pattern: glamour-seeking):** Founders gravitating toward unsexy-but-easy ideas are a known YC failure pattern. Skill should detect "consumer social network" + similar low-schlep framings as anti-pattern.
- **PG-SCHLEP-004 (Stripe as canonical):** Quote: "everyone else will have been frightened off by the challenges" — pull as a direct quote when the company facts file shows the company is taking on regulated, infrastructure-heavy, or complex-onboarding work.
