## Core principles (per page)

**Clarity Over Marketing (Paul Graham):**
- Marketing-speak "obscures rather than illuminates"
- YC partners read **~100 applications daily**
- Applicants must be "exceptionally clear and concise" with ideas presented in their opening sentence

**Matter-of-Fact Descriptions:**
Instead of vague claims ("transforming relationships between people and information"), provide concrete descriptions.
- **Example given (PG):** "A database with a wiki-like interface, combined with a graphical UI for controlling who can see and edit what."

## Key Application Questions Highlighted

**Founder achievements (PG: "the most important question on the application"):**
> "Please tell us in one or two sentences about something impressive that each founder has built or achieved."

**Wildcard question (in 2026 form: location/structure to be confirmed in Phase 2):**
> "Please tell us about the time you most successfully hacked some (non-computer) system to your advantage."
- Strong answers can prompt re-evaluation of borderline applications

## What Evaluators Prioritize

1. **Founders over ideas** — most funded startups pivot significantly
2. **Insight over novelty** — how founders plan to differentiate (not just "well-designed")
3. **Awareness of obstacles** — acknowledging challenges with theoretical solutions impresses more than ignoring them
4. **Specificity** — avoid generic claims; provide concrete examples

## Application Best Practices (per page)

- Submit video (statistically increases interview likelihood)
- Disclose flaws rather than conceal them
- Use comparisons to known companies economically
- Edit ruthlessly: "cross out every word you don't need"

## Atoms (preliminary, will be re-passed in Phase 2)

- **HTA-001:** Partners read ~100 apps daily → 5-min average per app per partner. Brevity isn't preference; it's the only way the work gets done.
- **HTA-002 (PG, "most important question"):** "Tell us in one or two sentences about something impressive that each founder has built or achieved." This is the founder-credibility anchor. The skill must enforce concrete specificity here above all other questions.
- **HTA-003 (the wildcard):** "How you successfully hacked some non-computer system" — strong answers can re-open borderline rejections. Treat as recovery surface, not throwaway.
- **HTA-004 (founders > ideas):** Most funded startups pivot. Skill should not over-weight idea perfection at expense of founder credibility.
- **HTA-005 (specificity):** Generic = penalty. Every claim should carry a name, number, or date.
