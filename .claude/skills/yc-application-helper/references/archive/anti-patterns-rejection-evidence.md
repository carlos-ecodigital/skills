## Founders Corner analysis (March 2026)

### Four critical evaluation questions

1. **Commitment Level** — fully all-in vs. hedging with day jobs
   - "Risk tolerance is precisely what YC is betting on"

2. **Problem Comprehension** — articulate the underlying problem with precision
   - Why it exists, who suffers, why unsolved before, why now is right time
   - Partners push hard on "why now"

3. **Demand Evidence** — concrete signals
   - Letters of intent, pilot customers, waitlist that converted at unusual rate
   - NOT imaginary problems

4. **Execution Credibility** — technical ability + identifiable unfair advantages
   - Beyond generic claims

### Key failure modes
- Vagueness over specificity → hedged answers signal weak conviction
- Performance over authenticity → partners detect performance vs. genuine understanding
- Product focus over founder clarity → 60-second video should showcase founders, not product features
- Incomplete customer research → building for problems not stress-tested with real humans

## Atoms (preliminary)

### From W23 rejection
- **ANTI-001 (pivoting in interview):** Pivoting from your authentic product to a "grander" idea = rejection trigger. Application coherence (idea consistency from facts → draft → interview) is essential.
- **ANTI-002 (ceremony over building):** Focus on pitch deck/application polish vs. customer engagement = rejection signal. Skill should not encourage polish-paralysis.
- **ANTI-003 ("lack of social proof and traction"):** Verbatim partner phrasing of common rejection reason. Q-PROG-5/6 with no users + Q-PROG-7/8 with no revenue + no LOIs = high rejection probability. Application must surface evidence even if modest.

### From Lior's rejection
- **ANTI-004 (incumbent-substitute trap):** Building "a better X" against deeply embedded incumbent = rejection. Q-IDEA-2 must articulate paradigm shift, not feature comparison. Quote partners use: "completely new paradigm and no one saw coming."
- **ANTI-005 ("I don't have a good answer"):** Acceptable to say in conversation, fatal to write in application. Q-IDEA-1/Q-PROG-6 require answers; gaps must be flagged for resolution before draft.
- **ANTI-006 (no prototypes/designs):** Q-CO-4 (demo upload required) — applicants without ANY visual evidence of work face disadvantage. Skill must demand at least mockup/design work for the demo.
- **ANTI-007 (solo without co-founder strategy):** FAQ accepts solo founders, but no plan to find a co-founder = rejection-shaped. Q-FOUND-3 ("looking for co-founder?") must have a substantive answer for solos.

### From Founders Corner
- **ANTI-008 (day job hedging):** Risk tolerance is what YC bets on. Q-PROG-2 (full-time?) cannot be hedged.
- **ANTI-009 (problem un-precise):** Q-IDEA-1 must precisely articulate: why problem exists, who suffers, why unsolved, why now. Vagueness on any of these four = anti-pattern.
- **ANTI-010 (imaginary demand):** Q-PROG-5/6 demands concrete demand evidence. "We think users want this" without any LOIs/pilots/waitlist conversion = rejection-shaped.
- **ANTI-011 (product-focused video):** Founder video (Q-VIDEO-1) must showcase founders, not product features. Common error: founders make a product demo and submit as founder video.
- **ANTI-012 (untested problem):** Building for problems not stress-tested with real humans = rejection. Q-IDEA-1 must show direct customer conversation.
- **ANTI-013 (performance signal):** Partners detect rehearsed/performance answers. Application that reads "constructed" loses authenticity points.
