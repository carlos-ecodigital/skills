## Clarity in Explanation

> "If you can't explain your plans concisely, you don't really understand them."

- Investors view unclear explanations as evidence of weak comprehension
- Avoid marketing jargon
- Use the same direct language you'd employ discussing the business internally
- Vague, grandiose language signals incompetence rather than confidence

## What Investors Actually Seek (the 3 dimensions)

1. **Formidable founders** — the dominant factor
   - Most investor decisions happen within minutes
   - Initial impressions difficult to reverse
   - Founder who "seems like they'll get what they want, regardless of obstacles"

2. **Promising market** — credible expansion sequence
   - TAM sets upper bound on company size
   - Market needn't be large *today*; must show plausible expansion path

3. **Evidence of traction** — varies by stage
   - Early: promising experiments
   - Mature: experiments worked

## Demonstrating Market Expertise

- "Know everything about your market"
- When unable to answer directly: explain methodology for discovering the answer; never bluff
- Investors detect inauthenticity if you haven't convinced yourself first

## Demonstrating Execution

- Founder formidability signals execution potential
- **Address rejection head-on:** if other investors said no, candidly explain why those rejections were mistaken
- Conviction grounded in genuine expertise communicates execution likelihood more persuasively than pitch technique
