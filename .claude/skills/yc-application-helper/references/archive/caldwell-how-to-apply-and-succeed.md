## Core Principles

### Application Value (forcing function)
The act of completing a YC application benefits founders regardless of acceptance by forcing examination of product, differentiation, competitive landscape, and equity splits.

### Luck Creation
> "Great founders actively create luck by consistently positioning themselves for fortunate outcomes."

Applying to YC exemplifies proactive opportunity-seeking rather than passive waiting.

### Rejection Resilience
Founders must develop comfort with rejection early — rejection pervades startup life (customers, recruits, etc.). The application itself is a rejection-tolerance practice.

## Common Self-Imposed Barriers (Invalid Excuses)

These are the excuses Caldwell hears that he categorically rejects:

1. Being "too early" — YC funds pre-idea stage; FAQ-004 corroborates ("40% of companies funded are just an idea")
2. Being "too late" — having existing revenue/funding doesn't disqualify; FAQ-005 corroborates
3. Solo founder status — FAQ-001 corroborates ("we regularly accept solo founders")
4. Prior rejection — FAQ-008 corroborates ("strongly encourage you to apply again")
5. "Unique" business YC wouldn't understand
6. Lack of traditional credentials

## Application Essentials

### Professionalism
- Complete all fields
- Eliminate errors
- Follow submission guidelines precisely

### Founder Video (operational spec)
- 1 minute maximum
- All co-founders present
- Deviations suggest inattention or team misalignment

### Clarity Over Complexity
Strong applications communicate:
- What you're building
- Who it's for
- Why
Using simple language without jargon.

## Team & Capability Signals (what partners scan for)

- Domain expertise OR proven execution ability (prototype evidence)
- Clear delineation of who builds what (per ALTMAN-006 skills split)
- Genuine commitment beyond casual exploration (per FAQ-003 + ANTI-008)
- Self-awareness regarding business challenges (per ARC-022)

## Interview Strategy

**Duration:** 10 minutes with 3-4 interviewers (corroborates `archive/yc-interviews-page.md`)

**Approach:** Candid conversation demonstrating business mastery and numbers comprehension — NOT adversarial "winning"

**Tone:** Empathy and trustworthiness matter; YC seeks long-term working relationships

## Reapplication Path

> "~33% of accepted companies previously rejected. Subsequent applications showing measurable progress viewed favorably as evidence of perseverance."

This is concrete data on re-applicant success rates.

## Networking Reality

YC funds strangers; networking-in is ineffective. Focus resources on building the startup itself rather than relationship-cultivation strategies. (Corroborates FAQ-006: "considering all applications equally.")

## Advisor Caution

Skepticism warranted toward "predatory advisors" offering restrictive guidance or demanding unreasonable compensation in exchange for promised access.
