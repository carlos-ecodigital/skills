## Application Process & Eligibility

**Can I apply as a solo founder?**
> "Yes. We regularly accept solo founders." However, YC advises that "one-person startups are tough and you're more likely to succeed with a co-founder."

**Do I need technical skills?**
> "It's important for the founding team to have the skills to build their product themselves, rather than outsourcing it to someone else."

**Can I apply while working or studying full-time?**
> You may apply, but "we expect the founders to commit to working full-time on their company during the batch and afterwards if accepted."

**Am I too early-stage?**
> "No, it would be a mistake to wait to apply. On average, 40% of the companies we fund in each batch are just an idea."

**Can I apply if already funded?**
> "Sure. Each YC batch has many companies who have already raised over $1M."

**Do I need connections at YC?**
> "No. One of YC's core principles is considering all applications equally."

## Application Requirements

**Must I incorporate before applying?**
> "Nope."

**Can I submit multiple applications per batch?**
> "We ask applicants to only submit one application per batch."

**Can I apply again if rejected?**
> "Yes...we strongly encourage you to apply again."

## Deadlines & Timing

> "Late applications are reviewed on a rolling basis, typically within one month."

## Application atoms (preliminary)

- **FAQ-001 (solo founder):** Solo founders are accepted "regularly" but are at structural disadvantage per YC's own framing. Solo facts files must compensate by addressing the co-founder gap explicitly (e.g., named technical advisor, prior solo execution evidence).
- **FAQ-002 (technical skill):** Founding team must include builders. Non-technical founder + outsourced product = explicit anti-pattern. If facts show outsourced build, this is a [GAP] that must be addressed.
- **FAQ-003 (full-time commitment):** Application must commit to full-time post-acceptance. Part-time framing is fatal.
- **FAQ-004 (idea stage):** "40% of companies we fund are just an idea" — idea-only applicants are not penalized for absence of traction; instead, partners weight founder credibility and insight quality more heavily.
- **FAQ-005 (already-funded):** Prior funding ≥$1M is common in YC batches. Skill should not treat already-funded as anti-pattern; surface it normally.
- **FAQ-006 (no connections needed):** "Considering all applications equally" — partners explicitly disclaim relationship advantage. Application content alone determines outcome.
- **FAQ-007 (one app per batch):** Co-founders must coordinate to submit a single application. Multiple applications = automatic disqualification.
- **FAQ-008 (re-apply):** "Strongly encourage you to apply again" — re-applicants need a "what changed" narrative; the question is in the form.
- **FAQ-009 (rolling review for late):** Late submissions reviewed within ~1 month. Mission-critical applications should hit on-time deadline; the "rolling review" path is fallback, not strategy.
