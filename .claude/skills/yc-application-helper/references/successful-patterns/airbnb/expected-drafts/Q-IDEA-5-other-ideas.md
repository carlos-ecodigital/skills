# Q-IDEA-5 — "If you had any other ideas you considered..." (Airbnb worked example)

**Form text (verbatim partner-stated):** "Often when we fund people it's because of something they list here and not in the main application."

**This is the form's own highest-leverage flag.** Skill treats this with the same rigor as the main idea question.

**Atoms applied:** ARC-028 (alternate ideas as conversation surface), HALE-001 (each alternate must satisfy 3-component frame at quality bar), ALTMAN-002 (excitement test on each)

---

## v-raw

**1. Snooth — wine-discovery platform.** Brian's idea pre-Airbnb. We considered it because a friend in the wine industry had specific frustrations with how restaurants stocked unfamiliar bottles. We didn't pursue: (a) we weren't wine-experts, (b) wine commerce required state-by-state regulatory navigation we didn't want, (c) the addressable customer was tiny (sommeliers, not consumers).

**2. Conference-scoped travel marketplace** — early version of Airbnb but ONLY for conference attendees. We discovered this version first (the IDSA experiment). We didn't stick with it because the same trust infrastructure (photos + reviews + escrow) could serve broader travel, and conferences are ~3% of US travel volume. Narrowing to conferences would cap our market at design conferences in SF + ~50 events globally.

**3. A peer-to-peer goods rental marketplace.** Same trust-infrastructure idea applied to "your neighbor's drill / camera / lawn mower." We considered this in early 2008. We didn't pursue because (a) goods rental has lower repeat purchase (you rent a drill once a year), (b) trust unlock is harder (physical theft vs. lodging where the host is present), (c) the 6-month repeat-host cohort we have for Airbnb suggested marketplace was the right model but lodging was the right inventory type.

## Anti-pattern checks

- ❌ Empty / single throwaway — NOT triggered (3 alternates with reasoning each)
- ❌ Same-quality-as-main-idea bar — PASS (each alternate has specific problem + solution + reason-not-pursued)

## Why this is partner-readable

Per the form's own quote: partners often fund based on what's in this section. The partner reading this thinks: "These founders consider ideas seriously and reject them rigorously. The wine idea showed they understand regulatory friction. The conference-narrow idea showed they understand TAM. The goods-rental idea showed they understand repeat-purchase dynamics. They picked Airbnb because they correctly diagnosed which trust-infrastructure problem had the best inventory type."

Anti-pattern equivalent (would FAIL):
> "We considered a few other ideas but Airbnb felt right."

Zero specifics. Zero reasoning. Zero conversation surface. Partner gets nothing.
