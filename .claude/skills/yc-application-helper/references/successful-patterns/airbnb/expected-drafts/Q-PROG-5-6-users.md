**Atoms applied:** ALTMAN-001 (love > like — depth-of-engagement evidence), PG-006 (narrow-deep > broad-shallow), PG-005 (organic-not-sitcom signals), ARC-019 (metric priority: profit > revenue > usage > users > audience), SEIBEL-002 (PMF self-knowledge), SEIBEL-007 (signal filter — survival signal in first 2 minutes), ANTI-003 (lack of social proof / traction), ANTI-010 (imaginary demand)

## v-raw

**Yes** — people are using it. We have ~10,000 active listings and have processed thousands of bookings since launch. We are NOT yet at PMF — we know this because hosts list aggressively but only 30% of listings receive a booking in any given month, and our repeat-guest rate is 18% (low for travel).

**Active users:** hosts ~10,000 listed, ~3,000 active (booking received in last 60 days). Travelers — we don't have monthly-active numbers below booking events; we measure repeat-guest rate.

**Paying:** travelers pay listing prices + 3% guest fee. Hosts receive booking proceeds minus 3% host fee. Both sides are paying customers.

**Highest-paying:** no single dominant customer (marketplace). Highest single-month booking we've processed was $4,200 (whole-apartment 14-night stay during NYC Fashion Week). Our largest concentrated host (Denver during DNC) generated $8,400 in 8 days.

**Specific user love-evidence (organic, not stagecrafted):** "Marie L., a 29-year-old engineer from London, flying in for a startup conference. 'I'd been trying to find a place for two weeks, every hotel was booked, and your platform was the only thing that worked.'" That's the canonical organic user — desperate-now demand, returned 6 weeks later.

**Anti-trumpet: we have not yet figured out** how to make hosts list during off-peak. Our event-overflow demand is real and loud; our steady-state demand is quieter and unproven.
