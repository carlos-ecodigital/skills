# Q-VIDEO-1 — Founder video script (Airbnb worked example, 60-second)

**Form spec:** ~60 seconds. All founders on camera. No music/effects. YouTube-hostable.

**Atoms applied:**
- ARC-029 (Andersen format spec)
- HALE-002 (lead with what, not why or how)
- SEIBEL-005 (be earnest, sincere, natural)
- CALDWELL-004 (1-min, all founders, format-compliance signals)
- CALDWELL-010 (no adversarial / shark-tank register)
- ALTMAN-004 (formidability via specific evidence)
- ANTI-011 (founder-focused, NOT product demo)

---

## v-raw script (~150 spoken words for 60 seconds)

**[0:00-0:08] Open — all 3 founders on camera, conversational**

Brian: We're Brian, Joe, and Nathan.
Joe: We run airbedandbreakfast.com.
Nathan: An online marketplace for renting rooms in someone's home instead of a hotel.

**[0:08-0:25] Origin & traction — Brian leads**

Brian: It started in October 2007, when Joe and I rented out air mattresses on our living room floor to designers attending the IDSA conference. Hotels were sold out. Three guests stayed four days. They came back the next year.

We launched at the Democratic National Convention in Denver. 10,000 listings now.

**[0:25-0:45] Why us — each founder ~7 seconds**

Joe: I designed Critbuns — sold at MoMA at age 23.
Brian: I designed the Pina sport seat for 3DM — 200,000 units shipped in 8 countries by 24.
Nathan: I founded Datamine at Harvard, ran it profitably for three years, sold it.

**[0:45-0:60] Why YC / close**

Brian: We're applying to YC because the trust unlock for peer-to-peer travel — photos plus reviews plus payment escrow — needs the kind of operational discipline YC teaches. We've shown the model works. We want to scale it.

[~150 spoken words. Hits 60 seconds at conversational pace.]

## Production notes

- **Filming location:** Their living room (the actual location of the air-mattress story; sets visual context).
- **Equipment:** One camera, one mic. No tripod required if held steady.
- **Lighting:** Natural light from window.
- **Wardrobe:** Casual — same clothing each founder would wear in a normal work day.
- **Take target:** 3-5 takes. First and last takes usually look most natural.
- **Hosting:** YouTube unlisted. Embed link in application Q-VIDEO-1 field.

## Anti-pattern checks

- ❌ Pitch-deck opener ("Hello, we are here to tell you about...") — NOT triggered
- ❌ Background music — NOT included
- ❌ Fancy effects / transitions — NOT included
- ❌ Product demo over founder voice — NOT triggered (founders speak entire time)
- ❌ Single founder dominating — NOT triggered (each founder gets ~equal time)
- ❌ Memorized-speech cadence (CALDWELL-010) — NOT triggered (conversational)
- ❌ Marketing-speak ("revolutionizing", "platform") — not present

## Why this is partner-readable

A YC partner watches this 60-second video and pattern-matches:
- **Three founders, all comfortable on camera together** = team coordination signal
- **Origin story is concrete** (October 2007, IDSA conference, 3 guests, came back next year) = organic-not-sitcom signal per PG-005
- **Each founder has a specific shipped project at scale** = ALTMAN-004 founder-trait-evidence
- **Close ties to operational discipline** = self-aware about what they need from YC, not just "we want money"
- **No production polish** = Caldwell's "they followed instructions" detail-orientation signal

In 60 seconds, partners see: a coordinated team that has shipped real things, has organic founder-product fit, and knows specifically what they need from the program. That's the structural template.

Anti-pattern equivalent (would FAIL CALDWELL-010 and ARC-029):
- 90 seconds long
- Background music throughout
- Product demo screen-share for first 30 seconds with voiceover
- Single founder talking, others appearing only at end
- Opens with "Hello! We're excited to introduce Airbnb, the future of travel, where..."

Every element of that anti-pattern fails a specific atom.
