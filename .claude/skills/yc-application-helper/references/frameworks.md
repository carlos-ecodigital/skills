# Supporting Frameworks (with full mental model)

**Purpose:** Reference frameworks YC partners explicitly cite when evaluating applications. Each framework's full mental model is preserved (per Phase 2 directive: "frameworks with full mental model, not summaries").

## Default Alive vs. Default Dead (PG)

**Source atom:** PG-007
**The test:** "Assuming expenses remain constant and revenue growth is what it has been over the last several months, do they make it to profitability on the money they have left?"

**The mental model:**
Founders fall into one of two trajectories. Default-alive companies are progressing toward break-even on existing capital. Default-dead companies will run out of money before reaching break-even unless something changes (revenue accelerates or expenses cut).

**Why it matters for applications:**
- Partners reading Q-EQ-7 / Q-EQ-8 / Q-EQ-9 + Q-PROG-8 are running this calculation in their heads
- "Half the founders I talk to don't know whether they're default alive or default dead" — knowing your status signals financial discipline
- Partner-canonical example: Airbnb waited 4 months post-YC before first hire (PG-008)

**Application implication:**
The skill cross-checks runway × monthly burn vs. revenue trajectory. Inconsistencies are flagged before submission.

## Schlep Blindness (PG)

**Source atom:** PG-001 + PG-002
**The reframe:** Instead of "what problem should I solve?" ask **"what problem do I wish someone else would solve for me?"**

**The mental model:**
Founders unconsciously avoid valuable startup ideas requiring tedious, regulated, or unpleasant work. The unconscious filters out these ideas before they reach conscious consideration. Stripe is the canonical example: thousands of programmers experienced payment-processing pain firsthand for a decade, yet didn't see it as a startup opportunity because the regulatory + bank + fraud schlep was filtered out.

**Why it matters for applications:**
- Schlep-laden ideas are *valuable* because competitors are frightened off
- Q-IDEA-2 ("what do you understand about your business that they don't?") strong answer: "we are willing to do [specific schlep] competitors avoid"
- Defensibility through tedium is a form of moat that doesn't require novel technology

**Application implication:**
Q-IDEA-1 strongest answers identify a specific schlep the founder personally encountered. Anti-pattern: "we wanted to build a cool app" (low-schlep filter).

## The Founder 3-Test (PG)

**Source atom:** PG-003
**The test:** Best startup ideas have:
1. Something the founders themselves want
2. That they themselves can build
3. That few others realize are worth doing

**The mental model:**
Founder-product fit is the prerequisite for everything else. Without (1), the founder doesn't dogfood and won't iterate from real pain. Without (2), the founder is dependent on hires for product velocity. Without (3), the market is crowded and execution must be exceptional just to compete.

**Empirical pattern:** Microsoft, Apple, Yahoo, Google, Facebook all began this way.

**Application implication:**
Q-IDEA-1 must demonstrably satisfy all three. Skill validates each Q-IDEA-1 draft for explicit evidence of all 3 components.

## Live in the Future (PG)

**Source atom:** PG-004
**The directive:** "Live in the future, then build what's missing."

**The mental model:**
Great startup ideas are seen by founders who are already users of the future. Bill Gates / Altair, Drew Houston / USB stick — external stimulus hits prepared mind. The "preparation" is heavy use of frontier tools. You don't need to be a researcher pushing boundaries — you can be at the leading edge as a user.

**Application implication:**
Q-IDEA-1 strong answers cite specific "I've been doing X for Y years and noticed Z" evidence. Anti-pattern: framing the opportunity from market-research perspective rather than user-experience perspective.

## Organic vs. Sitcom Ideas (PG)

**Source atom:** PG-005
**The test:** When you describe the idea, do people say "Yeah, maybe I could see using something like that"? If yes, the idea is sitcom — zero actual demand.

**The mental model:**
Real demand sounds intense ("I need this NOW," "I would pay for this today," "I've been looking for this for years"). Fake demand sounds mild and conditional. Fake demand summed across a population is still zero users.

**Application implication:**
Q-PROG-5/6 strong answers cite intense user reactions, not mild interest counts.

## The Three Components of a Startup Idea (Hale)

**Source atom:** HALE-001
**The components:** Problem, Solution, Insight (Unfair Advantage).

**The mental model:**
Without insight, problem+solution alone is commodity — competitors with the same problem-solution pair will beat you on execution speed. The insight is what compounds growth faster than competitors. Problem and solution are visible to everyone; insight is what's hard to see.

**Application implication:**
Q-IDEA-1 strong answer makes all 3 visible: problem (specific, named pain), solution (concrete, demoable), insight (non-obvious, not just "we're better").

## The X-for-Y Conditional (Hale)

**Source atom:** HALE-003
**The 3 conditions, ALL required:**
1. X is a universally recognized billion-dollar company
2. Y genuinely wants/needs that model (clear unmet need)
3. The Y market is substantial enough to support a large business

**The mental model:**
Analogies work when they import clear, scaled concepts. "Buffer for Snapchat" fails because Buffer is smaller than Snapchat — the analogy doesn't elevate. "Airbnb for storage" works because Airbnb is universally recognized AND storage has clear unmet need AND the market is large.

**Application implication:**
Default recommendation: avoid the X-for-Y construction. If used, all 3 conditions must hold. Skill detects "X for Y" usage and verifies.

## The Founder Trait Quartet (Altman)

**Source atom:** ALTMAN-004
**The traits:** Unstoppability, determination, formidability, resourcefulness.

**The mental model:**
These four traits are the empirical pattern across YC-funded founders. Specific tech-stack experience does NOT make this list. The traits are evidenced via behavior (specific named projects shipped through obstacles) — never via adjectives.

**Application implication:**
P-ACC-2 (most-impressive-thing) must communicate at least 2 of the 4 traits via specific evidence. P-ACC-1 (wildcard) is purpose-built for resourcefulness.

## Love > Like (Altman)

**Source atom:** ALTMAN-001
**The principle:** "Make something users love. ... Better small-N who love than large-N who like."

**The mental model:**
Love produces referral, daily use, paid retention, organic word-of-mouth. Like produces sign-ups that don't return. The startup goal at YC stage is to maximize love-density, not user-count breadth.

**Application implication:**
Q-PROG-5/6 strong answers cite named love-evidence (specific user using daily, organic referral, paid retention).

---

## Why-Now = Unrealized Shift (Altman)

**Source atom:** ALTMAN-003
**The principle:** "Major technological shifts are just starting that most people haven't realized yet — big companies are bad at addressing those."

**The mental model:**
The why-now is fundable when it's a shift incumbents are STRUCTURALLY unable to address (not just slow). Structural inability comes from: business-model conflict (cannibalization), organizational conflict (different team needed), or technical conflict (different architecture needed).

**Application implication:**
Q-IDEA-1 why-now strong answers name (a) specific shift, (b) why incumbents are structurally unable to address, (c) why this team noticed first.

## Do Things That Don't Scale (PG / Altman)

**Source atom:** ALTMAN-008
**The principle:** Manual user acquisition early. Watch users use your product. Sit in their office.

**The mental model:**
The data-collection regime that produces real PMF insight requires founder-user contact at the individual level. Automated acquisition (SEO, ads) without prior manual evidence skips the discovery loop and produces no learning.

**Application implication:**
Q-PROG-1 / Q-PROG-5/6 strong answers cite specific manual acquisition evidence. Anti-pattern: "we plan to acquire users via SEO/ads/virality" without prior manual evidence.
