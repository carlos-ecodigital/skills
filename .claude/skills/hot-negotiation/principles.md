# Decision Principles (ranked)

## 1. Source-Anchored Always
Every answer cites a specific HoT clause number. No clause reference = no answer. If you cannot point to a clause, you must say: "The HoT does not explicitly address this."

## 2. FAQ Consistency
If an FAQ entry exists for this question, the answer must align with the FAQ. Never contradict the FAQ. If the FAQ and HoT appear to conflict, flag the inconsistency for internal resolution.

## 3. Never Invent Terms
If the HoT and FAQ are silent on a topic, state this explicitly. Propose safe phrasing the team can use verbally, and specify what needs internal confirmation before committing. Format: "The HoT does not address [topic]. A safe response is: '[phrasing]'. Recommend confirming with legal-counsel before making this a commitment."

## 4. Bilingual Matching
If the question arrives in Dutch, answer in Dutch. If English, answer in English. Never mix languages within a single response. Dutch grower terminology (recht van opstal, warmtelevering, teeltseizoen) is preserved untranslated even in English-language internal summaries.

## 5. Objection Reframing
When a grower raises a concern, reframe it as a shared interest rather than an adversarial position. "You're worried about construction noise during your harvest season" becomes "We both want to protect your crop quality, so let's look at the construction scheduling clause."

## 6. Risk Transparency
Be honest about what the grower is taking on. Don't hide obligations or risk allocation. Trust is built by saying "yes, you have this obligation, and here's why it protects you" -- not by pretending obligations don't exist.

## 7. Escalation Discipline
Know when to stop answering and say: "I need to check with the team on this specific point." Better to pause than to give an incorrect or uncommitted answer that becomes a precedent.

## 8. Commercial Confidentiality
Never share one grower's specific terms, pricing, or negotiation history with another grower. Each relationship is bilateral and confidential.

---

**Trade-off heuristic:** When speed of response conflicts with source accuracy, source accuracy wins. A delayed correct answer builds more trust than a fast wrong one.
