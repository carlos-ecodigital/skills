## 1. Company Profile

Ennaturlijk is a Dutch district heating company operating warmtenetten in Arnhem, Nijmegen, Purmerend, Duiven, and several smaller municipalities in Gelderland. Owned by Alliander (grid operator). Supplies heat to approximately 40,000 households and commercial customers. Currently expanding its network in Arnhem-Zuid as part of the municipality's heat transition plan (Transitievisie Warmte). Ennaturlijk is actively seeking sustainable heat sources to replace gas-fired peak boilers and comply with upcoming Wcw (Wet collectieve warmtevoorziening) requirements.

## 3. What They Need

Sustainable baseload heat source for the Arnhem-Zuid network expansion: 10-25 MWth, supply temperature 40-50C (low-temp network being built), 8,000+ operating hours/year. Must qualify as sustainable heat under Wcw criteria. Ennaturlijk is evaluating multiple sources for a portfolio approach -- DC waste heat would serve as baseload, with geothermal and peak boilers for supplementation.

## 4. Why Now (Trigger)

Three converging drivers: (1) Wcw legislation nearing final implementation -- requires heat network operators to source sustainable heat and obtain warmtekavel designations; (2) Arnhem-Zuid network expansion is in design phase (construction 2026-2027), so supply source decisions must be made now; (3) Ennaturlijk's existing gas boilers face increasing carbon pricing pressure under ETS2 (expected 2027+). Marloes specifically mentioned that DC waste heat is on their evaluation shortlist alongside geothermal.

## 5. How We Fit

Moderate-to-strong fit, conditional on site proximity:
- **Heat capacity:** DE can supply 10-25 MWth baseload from a purpose-built facility
- **Temperature:** 40-50C supply matches Arnhem-Zuid's low-temperature network design
- **Operating hours:** DC operates 24/7/365 -- ideal baseload (8,760 hours/year)
- **Wcw compliance:** DC waste heat qualifies as sustainable under Wcw criteria

Key gaps:
- **Site proximity:** DE does not currently have a site in Arnhem area. Would need to develop one -- requires grid connection, land, and permitting. This is a 2-3 year development timeline.
- **Portfolio positioning:** Ennaturlijk wants DC heat as ONE source in a portfolio. We need to understand their baseload allocation and whether 10 MWth or 25 MWth is the ask.

## 6. Positioning Angle

Lead with **Wcw compliance** + **reliable baseload**. Ennaturlijk's primary driver is regulatory: they MUST source sustainable heat. Secondary: price certainty vs gas/biomass alternatives. The message is: "24/7 sustainable baseload heat that checks your Wcw box, with long-term price certainty."

Key nuance: Ennaturlijk is an infrastructure company, not a single decision-maker. Language should be professional, data-driven, emphasizing reliability, contractual guarantees, and municipal alignment.

## 7. Competitive Landscape

| Alternative | Status | DE Advantage |
|-------------|--------|--------------|
| Geothermal (Arnhem area) | Feasibility study commissioned, 2-3 year timeline if viable | No drilling risk, no CAPEX for Ennaturlijk, more predictable output. But geothermal offers higher temperature. |
| Biomass (existing capacity) | Currently using biomass at Purmerend plant | No fuel cost volatility, no particulate emissions, better public perception. Biomass increasingly politically difficult. |
| Other waste heat (industrial) | Evaluating options from steel/chemical industry | DC heat is more reliable (24/7) and more predictable. Industrial heat supply depends on production levels. |
| Gas boilers (status quo) | Currently operational, being phased out | Wcw non-compliant, increasing carbon cost. Not a long-term option. |

Key differentiator: reliability (24/7/365) + Wcw compliance + no fuel cost exposure. Geothermal is the main competitor -- it offers higher temperature but carries drilling risk and multi-year timeline.

## 8. Obstacles & Risk

| Obstacle | Severity | Mitigation |
|----------|----------|------------|
| No DE site near Arnhem | High | Requires full site development. Identify potential sites near Arnhem-Zuid network. This is a 2-3 year project, which aligns with Ennaturlijk's 2026-2027 construction timeline. |
| Long decision process (municipal governance) | Medium | Accept the timeline. Engage proactively with the municipality. Offer to present to wethouder and relevant committees. |
| Temperature concern (need peak >50C) | Low | Position as baseload (40-50C) with heat pump upgrade option to 70-90C. Ennaturlijk's low-temp network is designed for 40-50C, so this is a good fit. |
| Portfolio approach = smaller allocation | Medium | Clarify: are they looking for 10 MWth or 25 MWth from DC waste heat? Smaller allocation still viable but changes facility sizing. |
| Ennaturlijk is Alliander-owned = corporate governance | Medium | Longer sign-off chain. Prepare for formal procurement process, not handshake deal. |

Red flag: No existing site near Arnhem. This deal requires DE to develop a new location, which adds complexity and timeline risk. However, the 2026-2027 construction timeline provides a window.
